## Answering Your Deepest Questions

### When to Fire a Co-Founder?

**The consensus across 20VC guests:** Fire fast, but fairly. The single biggest predictor of startup failure cited by repeat guests is co-founder conflict left unaddressed. Jason Lemkin (6 appearances) is the most vocal: if trust is broken, it won't fix itself. Steve Blank adds that the most successful founders treat the co-founder decision like any other hiring decision — stop hoping and start acting. Tim Ferriss, drawing from his angel portfolio, says the real failure mode is avoiding the conversation, not the decision itself.

*Sources: [[jason-lemkin]], [[tim-ferriss]], [[steve-blank]], [[disagreements-founder-ceo]]*

### Single Best Piece of Advice for a First-Time Founder Raising Seed?

**"Sell the alpha, not the feature"** — Jen Abel's maxim echoes across 47 seed-round focused episodes. The pattern is consistent: VCs don't back products, they back asymmetric insights. The best seed pitches make the investor feel like they've discovered a secret about the world. Jason Lemkin adds: raise when you don't need to, not when you're desperate. David Frankel (3 appearances) says the #1 mistake is optimizing valuation over finding partners who will help you in the bad times.

*Sources: [[jen-abel]], [[jason-lemkin]], [[david-frankel]], [[safe-agreement]], [[seed-fundraising]]*
