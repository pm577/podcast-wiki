# Frank Rotman

**Role:** Founding Partner at QED Investors

**Key Beliefs:**
- The seed investing model is broken because too many funds are writing small checks without providing real value, leading to a 'lemons problem' where quality startups are not adequately supported. (confidence: 0.8)
- To make money at seed moving forward, investors need to focus on high-conviction bets and provide operational support, rather than spraying small checks. (confidence: 0.8)
- Founders should focus on building a business that can be profitable without relying on continuous fundraising, as the market has shifted. (confidence: 0.8)
- Venture value-add platforms are BS and have failed because they are generic and don't address the specific needs of portfolio companies. (confidence: 0.7)
- The best time to start a company is during a downturn because there is less competition for talent and attention. (confidence: 0.7)
