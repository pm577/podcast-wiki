# Jonathan Becker

**Role:** Founder at Thrive Digital

**Key Beliefs:**
- Performance marketing should be viewed as investing capital with expectation of return; diversify across channels to avoid over-reliance on any single channel.
- You have to make your own luck by taking risks and capitalizing on opportunities, even if there's a chance of embarrassment or failure.
- The biggest opportunity in paid growth is focusing on creatives, with rigorous testing and iteration being the key unlock.
- To test creative assets effectively, isolate a single variable (e.g., copy or image) within a single audience at the ad set level to measure what performs better.
- Attribution is an ongoing investigation, not a single source of truth; anyone claiming a single source of truth is inaccurate.
- AI is shifting work from manual implementation to strategic modeling, validation, and creative leverage — reducing time for mockups to 1% of what it took.
- For early-stage startups, hire in-house expertise first before engaging an agency, as agencies need a point of contact with authority and context.

## Timeline

- **2008–2010** — Started as a web developer and SEO practitioner; transitioned into paid search as clients sought measurable ROI.
- **2013** — Met Uber founder Garrett Camp in a taxi cab at the TED conference; exploited Uber's referral program loophole and landed them as a client after offering to close it.
- **2015–2016** — Pitched and won Snapchat as a client by refusing to answer their RFP questions and instead telling them what they needed to hear.
- **~2013–2023** — Worked with Uber for 10 years; Thrive Digital grew from a walk-in closet consultancy to a 130-person agency managing $500M+ in annual ad spend for companies including Uber, Asana, Square, Masterclass, and Tempur-Pedic.
- **Present** — Founder of Thrive Digital; built internal ETL tool "Thrive Stack" for data-driven attribution and insights.
