# David Frankel

**Key Beliefs:**
- Pro rata is the original sin in VC because it forces investors to make follow-on investments that may not be the best use of capital, and it distorts incentives. (confidence: 0.9)
- LPs are pissed with VCs because many funds have not returned capital and are sitting on unrealized gains, while charging management fees on that capital. (confidence: 0.9)
- Seed fund economics are hard because the math doesn't work unless you have a few massive winners, and most seed funds fail to generate top-quartile returns. (confidence: 0.9)
- Liquidity has died in 2024 because the IPO market is frozen and M&A activity is low, making it hard for VCs to return capital to LPs. (confidence: 0.8)
- Founder Collective focuses on investing in founders who have a deep understanding of a specific problem and are building a solution that is 10x better than existing alternatives. (confidence: 0.8)
