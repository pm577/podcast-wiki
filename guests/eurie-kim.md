# Eurie Kim

**Key Beliefs:**
- To raise a Series B or C, you need three core elements: a large and growing market, a differentiated product with strong retention, and a clear path to profitability or a convincing narrative for future profitability. (confidence: 0.95)
- The funding crunch is real but concentrated in later stages (Series B and beyond), where investors are demanding clear unit economics and a path to profitability before writing large checks. (confidence: 0.9)
- Investors do not want to reprice companies because it creates mark-to-market losses on their books, which can hurt fundraising for their own funds and signal poor judgment. (confidence: 0.9)
- AI investing today is like buying lottery tickets because it's very early, most companies will fail, but the winners will be enormous; investors should be prepared for high failure rates. (confidence: 0.85)
- Seed pricing remains high because there is a lot of capital chasing a limited number of high-quality deals, and investors are willing to pay up for the best teams. (confidence: 0.8)
