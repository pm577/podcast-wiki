# Mike Vernal

**Role:** General Partner at Sequoia

**Key Beliefs:**
- The strength of data moats is overrated today because data becomes commoditized quickly; instead, focus on network effects and user engagement. (confidence: 0.9)
- When hiring, prioritize candidates who have a strong sense of ownership and can operate in ambiguity, rather than just domain expertise. (confidence: 0.9)
- In go-to-market, start with a narrow beachhead and expand from there, rather than trying to capture a broad market initially. (confidence: 0.9)
- Product managers should spend significant time with users to understand their problems deeply, not just rely on data. (confidence: 0.9)
- Network effects are a stronger moat than data; companies should design products that get better as more people use them. (confidence: 0.9)
