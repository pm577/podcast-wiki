# Woody Marshall

**Key Beliefs:**
- Growth investors should focus on companies with strong unit economics and a clear path to profitability, not just top-line growth. (confidence: 0.9)
- Founders should prioritize building a great product and company over short-term financial engineering. (confidence: 0.9)
- The best time to invest in a company is when it has proven product-market fit and is scaling efficiently. (confidence: 0.9)
- The best companies often have a clear, differentiated value proposition that is easy to understand and communicate. (confidence: 0.8)
- Liquidity events (IPOs) should be timed when the company is ready, not forced by investor pressure. (confidence: 0.8)
