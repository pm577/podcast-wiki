# Eric Glyman

**Role:** Co-Founder and CEO at Ramp

**Key Beliefs:**
- To determine what to buy vs build, evaluate whether the component is core to your competitive advantage; if not, buy it to save time and focus on differentiation. (confidence: 0.9)
- Founders should prioritize building a product that solves a real pain point for customers, rather than chasing features that competitors have. (confidence: 0.9)
- You should never take the highest price because it often comes with strings attached, like aggressive terms or misaligned incentives. (confidence: 0.85)
- Working with venture funds that have a long-term perspective and are aligned with your mission is more important than the valuation or check size. (confidence: 0.85)
- When deciding between venture funds and crossover funds, consider that crossover funds may have different time horizons and risk profiles, which can affect their support during downturns. (confidence: 0.8)
