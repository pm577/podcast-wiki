# Tony Conrad

**Role:** Partner at True Ventures

**Key Beliefs:**
- Party rounds (many small investors) can be dangerous because no single investor has enough at stake to provide meaningful support or hold the founder accountable. (confidence: 0.9)
- True founder/product fit is when the founder's personal passion and expertise align perfectly with the problem they are solving, leading to a unique advantage. (confidence: 0.8)
- The best founders are often those who have a deep, almost obsessive understanding of their customer's needs because they themselves are the target user. (confidence: 0.8)
- Investors should look for founders who have a clear vision and are not easily swayed by market trends or investor pressure. (confidence: 0.8)
- Straddling the roles of founder and investor requires extreme discipline and clear boundaries to avoid conflicts of interest and maintain focus. (confidence: 0.7)
