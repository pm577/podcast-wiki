# Vince Hankes

**Role:** Partner at Thrive Capital

**Key Beliefs:**
- Startups building on top of OpenAI should assume model commoditization and build switching costs via proprietary data and user stickiness. (confidence: 0.9)
- The value in AI will accrue to application layer startups that build proprietary data moats and user engagement, not just infrastructure. (confidence: 0.85)
- Investors should focus on startups that have unique data advantages and network effects, as model defensibility is limited. (confidence: 0.8)
- Incumbents have an advantage in AI adoption due to existing data and distribution, but startups can win by focusing on new workflows. (confidence: 0.8)
- Regulation of AI will likely happen, but it will be slow and fragmented, creating opportunities for startups to shape compliance tools. (confidence: 0.75)
