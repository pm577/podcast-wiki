# Linda Lian

**Role:** Co-Founder & CEO at CommonRoom

**Key Beliefs:**
- When giving feedback, start with compassion and clarity: separate the person from the problem, and be specific about the behavior and its impact. (confidence: 0.9)
- Values are not just words on a wall; they are the behaviors you reward and punish. To instill values, you must operationalize them in hiring, firing, and performance reviews. (confidence: 0.9)
- When hiring, look for people who align with your values and have the ability to adapt to a fast-changing environment; cultural fit is as important as skills. (confidence: 0.9)
- Remaining in stealth for 12 months while raising $50M+ allowed CommonRoom to focus on product development without external noise, but it requires strong conviction and investor alignment. (confidence: 0.8)
- To build a community-driven product, you need to deeply understand the community's needs and involve them in the product development process from the start. (confidence: 0.8)
