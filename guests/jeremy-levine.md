# Jeremy Levine

**Role:** Partner at Bessemer Venture Partners

**Key Beliefs:**
- Two golden rules to tell entrepreneurs pre-investment: (1) Be honest about what you know and don't know, and (2) Always be prepared to walk away from a deal that isn't right. (confidence: 0.95)
- The idea that operational VCs can add more value than traditional VCs is bogus; the most important thing is the quality of the partner and their ability to help with strategic decisions, not operational tactics. (confidence: 0.9)
- When investing, focus on the team's ability to execute and the size of the opportunity, not just the current traction. (confidence: 0.9)
- Consumer companies are harder to build now because distribution is more expensive and competitive, but the best companies will still emerge. (confidence: 0.85)
- We are in a fallow period for consumer investing, meaning it's a time when few new consumer companies are being started and funded, but this is cyclical and will eventually change. (confidence: 0.8)
