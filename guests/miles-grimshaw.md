# Miles Grimshaw

**Role:** General Partner at Benchmark

**Key Beliefs:**
- Data can be a trap when early-stage investing because it often leads to false precision and backward-looking decisions; instead, focus on the founder's vision and the size of the opportunity. (confidence: 0.9)
- The five pillars of venture capital are: pattern recognition, judgment, conviction, empathy, and resilience. (confidence: 0.9)
- Missing investments like Figma and Plaid taught the lesson that you must be willing to pay up for exceptional companies and not be too price-sensitive. (confidence: 0.9)
- The new business model for AI is not just about selling a co-pilot; co-pilot is an incumbent strategy. Instead, focus on full automation or new workflows that replace human labor. (confidence: 0.9)
- When investing in AI, look for companies that have proprietary data or a unique workflow that creates a data flywheel, not just a wrapper on top of a foundation model. (confidence: 0.9)
