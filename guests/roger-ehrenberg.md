# Roger Ehrenberg

**Key Beliefs:**
- Founders should be conservative with cash and focus on extending runway, as fundraising will be harder in the new market. (confidence: 0.9)
- In a market downturn, the biggest crunch will be for companies that raised at high valuations without sufficient revenue or path to profitability. (confidence: 0.8)
- Investors should slow deployment and be more selective, focusing on companies with strong fundamentals and clear paths to profitability. (confidence: 0.8)
- M&A and IPO markets will slow down, so companies should not rely on exit via acquisition or IPO in the near term. (confidence: 0.7)
- The right time to be aggressive is when others are fearful; contrarian investing can yield high returns. (confidence: 0.7)
