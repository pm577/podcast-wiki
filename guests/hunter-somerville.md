# Hunter Somerville

**Key Beliefs:**
- LPs assess emerging managers based on their ability to articulate a clear and differentiated investment thesis, and whether they have a repeatable sourcing advantage. (confidence: 0.8)
- LPs are concerned about emerging managers who lack a clear strategy or are too broad in their focus, as it indicates a lack of conviction. (confidence: 0.8)
- LPs are excited by emerging managers who have a strong network and can demonstrate proprietary deal flow, especially in a specific niche. (confidence: 0.8)
- Emerging managers should focus on building a track record of successful investments, even if small, to gain LP confidence. (confidence: 0.8)
- The current state of seed investing is characterized by a large number of funds, making it harder for LPs to differentiate, but also creating opportunities for managers with a unique angle. (confidence: 0.7)
