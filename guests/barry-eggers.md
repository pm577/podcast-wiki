# Barry Eggers

**Key Beliefs:**
- The three ways firms do not succeed in generational transition are: 1) not having a clear succession plan, 2) not investing in the next generation of leaders early enough, and 3) not being willing to let go of control. (confidence: 0.8)
- To scale a global venture firm, you need to have a strong culture, hire great people, and be willing to invest in new geographies and sectors. (confidence: 0.8)
- Enterprise SaaS companies need to focus on go-to-market execution, including sales, marketing, and customer success, to succeed. (confidence: 0.8)
- What makes truly special board members like Jim Goetz is their ability to provide strategic guidance, be a sounding board, and have deep domain expertise. (confidence: 0.7)
- The best founders are those who are relentless, have a clear vision, and are able to attract top talent. (confidence: 0.7)
