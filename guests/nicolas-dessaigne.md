# Nicolas Dessaigne

**Key Beliefs:**
- Founders should not raise more money than they need; over-raising can lead to misallocation of resources and loss of focus. (confidence: 0.9)
- Series B is the hardest round to raise because you need to show a repeatable sales motion and a clear path to $100M ARR, but you don't yet have the revenue to prove it. (confidence: 0.8)
- The right time to move to the US is when you have a product that is ready for the US market and you have a clear go-to-market strategy. (confidence: 0.8)
- After Series B, the only limiting factor should be cash, meaning you should have product-market fit and a scalable go-to-market engine. (confidence: 0.7)
- Investors look for a strong team, a large market, and a product that solves a real pain point when deciding to invest in a Series B. (confidence: 0.7)
