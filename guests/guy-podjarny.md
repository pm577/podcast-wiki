# Guy Podjarny

**Key Beliefs:**
- Being first to market does not matter; what matters is being first to product-market fit. (confidence: 0.9)
- You do not have defensibility on day one; defensibility is built over time through network effects, data, or brand. (confidence: 0.9)
- Great messaging is about clarity and resonance with the target audience, not just being clever. (confidence: 0.9)
- When analyzing market size, focus on the total addressable market (TAM) and present it in a way that shows a clear path to capturing it. (confidence: 0.8)
- Vitamins (nice-to-have products) can survive recessions if they provide clear value, but painkillers (must-have solutions) are more resilient. (confidence: 0.8)
