# Benjamin Lauzier

**Role:** VP of product and growth (former) at Thumbtack (former)

**Key Beliefs:**
- Provide guardrails for what a good experience is in your marketplace, set a clear bar for quality, and provide the right coaching and tools for supply to be successful, then step back and invest in hands-on tactics only to close remaining gaps. (confidence: 0.95)
- If you don't have product-market fit and a reliable growth strategy for at least one side, forget marketplace dynamics — focus on the core exchange of value, deep-dive on one side, and use a crutch for the other. (confidence: 0.95)
- When choosing which side to focus on initially, pick the harder side — which is usually supply 80–90% of the time. (confidence: 0.9)
- Marketplace liquidity is everything: track fill rate (intentful demand converting to transactions) and find a predictive metric (e.g., ETA under 2 minutes for Lyft) to guide supply additions. (confidence: 0.95)
- Pre-product-market fit, don't let intellectually challenging marketplace dynamics distract you; nail the basics of product-market fit first. (confidence: 0.95)
- Avoid giving users excessive filters that fragment supply — use preferences to rank results rather than exclude supply entirely (e.g., Thumbtack's smoke machine checkbox). (confidence: 0.95)
- Leverage your best supply-side users as mentors or recruiters to onboard new supply at a fraction of the cost — Lyft's mentor program ($35/session) and recruiter program ($20/activation) were 10–20x more efficient than in-house teams. (confidence: 0.95)
