# Sheel Mohnot

**Key Beliefs:**
- Overly large GP commits are dangerous because they reduce the fund's ability to be flexible and can lead to misaligned incentives. (confidence: 0.9)
- Biggest mistakes managers make with Fund I include not having enough capital reserves for follow-on investments and not being disciplined about valuation. (confidence: 0.9)
- Missing out on Robinhood and Chime taught Sheel that you need to be willing to invest in companies that seem overvalued at the time if they have strong product-market fit and a large addressable market. (confidence: 0.9)
- Investing in Flexport and ChipperCash taught Sheel the importance of backing founders who have deep domain expertise and a clear vision for transforming an industry. (confidence: 0.9)
- In emerging markets, fintech companies that focus on solving real problems for the unbanked and underbanked have higher survival rates. (confidence: 0.8)
