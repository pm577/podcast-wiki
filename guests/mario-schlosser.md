# Mario Schlosser

**Key Beliefs:**
- I stood aside as CEO because I realized that the company needed a different skill set at that stage—someone with more operational and public company experience. It was the right decision for the company. (confidence: 0.9)
- In a downturn, you need to be honest with your team about the situation and rally them around a clear, focused plan. Transparency builds trust. (confidence: 0.9)
- When the market cap dropped 94%, we had to focus on the fundamentals: unit economics, cash flow, and customer retention. We stopped chasing growth at all costs. (confidence: 0.8)
- The rebound to $5.8BN in revenue came from focusing on the small business and Medicare Advantage segments, where we could differentiate with technology and service. (confidence: 0.8)
- We built a technology platform that automates many administrative tasks in healthcare, which allows us to have lower cost structure and better customer experience. (confidence: 0.7)
