# Daniel Erickson

**Role:** Founder & CEO at Viable Fit

**Key Beliefs:**
- To measure product-market fit scientifically, use the Sean Ellis survey question: 'How would you feel if you could no longer use the product?' with responses on a scale. The percentage who say 'very disappointed' is the PMF score. (confidence: 0.9)
- At pre-seed, analyzing CAC is not useful because the business model is not yet validated; focus on product-market fit instead. (confidence: 0.9)
- Use structured feedback collection (e.g., surveys with open-ended questions) to get quantifiable data on user sentiment and identify areas for improvement. (confidence: 0.9)
- Segment users into cohorts based on behavior (e.g., usage frequency, feature adoption) rather than demographics to get actionable insights. (confidence: 0.85)
- To determine which customer feedback to accept vs reject, look for patterns across multiple users and prioritize feedback that aligns with your product vision and core metrics. (confidence: 0.85)
