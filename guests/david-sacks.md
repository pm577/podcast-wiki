# David Sacks

**Role:** Co-Founder at Craft Ventures

**Key Beliefs:**
- To accurately evaluate CAC, you need to consider the fully loaded cost of sales and marketing, including salaries, overhead, and any tools, not just ad spend. (confidence: 0.9)
- Churn rate is the most important metric for a SaaS business; you should aim for less than 5% monthly churn, and ideally under 2%. (confidence: 0.9)
- The best way to assess founder psychology is to look at how they handle adversity and failure, not just their successes. (confidence: 0.85)
- Burn rate should be evaluated in the context of unit economics; a high burn is acceptable if the unit economics are strong and improving. (confidence: 0.85)
- The best startup boards have a high degree of trust and transparency between founders and investors, and focus on strategic issues rather than operational details. (confidence: 0.8)
