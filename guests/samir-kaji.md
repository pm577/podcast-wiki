# Samir Kaji

**Key Beliefs:**
- IRR is no longer the primary metric for evaluating venture funds; instead, focus on cash-on-cash returns and the quality of the portfolio. (confidence: 0.9)
- To raise a venture fund today, managers must demonstrate a clear strategy, a differentiated network, and a track record of sourcing and adding value. (confidence: 0.85)
- Founders should focus on building a sustainable business model early, as the market is becoming more discerning about unit economics. (confidence: 0.85)
- The explosion of seed financing has led to a more competitive landscape where founders need to differentiate themselves beyond just a good idea. (confidence: 0.8)
- Seed investors should look for startups that have a clear path to revenue and a strong team, rather than just a compelling story. (confidence: 0.8)
