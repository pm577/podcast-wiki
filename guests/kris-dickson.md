# Kris Dickson

**Role:** CFO at Lead Bank

**Key Beliefs:**
- The FDIC guarantee only covers up to $250,000 per depositor, per institution. For amounts above that, there is no automatic guarantee. (confidence: 0.95)
- Depositors with over $250,000 at SVB may not get their full funds back quickly; they become unsecured creditors in the FDIC receivership. (confidence: 0.95)
- Startups should diversify their cash across multiple banks to ensure all deposits are under the $250,000 FDIC limit. (confidence: 0.9)
- Startups should have a contingency plan for payroll and operations if their bank fails, including access to alternative banking relationships. (confidence: 0.9)
- The best outcome for SVB is a sale to a larger, well-capitalized bank that can absorb the deposit base and maintain relationships with tech companies. (confidence: 0.85)
