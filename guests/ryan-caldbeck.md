# Ryan Caldbeck

**Key Beliefs:**
- The business model of VC is broken because the standard 2/20 fee structure incentivizes fund managers to raise larger funds rather than generate superior returns, leading to misalignment with LPs. (confidence: 0.9)
- Founders should focus on unit economics and sustainable growth rather than chasing vanity metrics like top-line revenue, especially in consumer startups. (confidence: 0.9)
- The best funds will use data intelligently by leveraging machine learning to identify patterns in consumer behavior and product-market fit, rather than relying solely on gut instinct. (confidence: 0.85)
- VCs should spend more time on portfolio support and less on sourcing, as the real value creation comes from helping companies scale post-investment. (confidence: 0.85)
- We are not in a consumer bubble, but there is a frothy market for certain categories like direct-to-consumer brands that lack differentiation. (confidence: 0.8)
