# Rafael Illishayev

**Role:** Co-Founder and CEO at GoPuff

**Key Beliefs:**
- 10-15 minute delivery is an unsustainable model because it requires excessive labor and inventory costs that cannot be justified by customer willingness to pay. (confidence: 0.9)
- To become profitable, GoPuff plans to shift from 10-15 minute delivery to 30-45 minute delivery, reducing labor costs and allowing for more efficient routing. (confidence: 0.9)
- Founders should focus on unit economics and profitability rather than growth at all costs, especially in capital-intensive businesses like quick commerce. (confidence: 0.9)
- The European expansion was a mistake because GoPuff tried to replicate the US model without adapting to local market dynamics, leading to high costs and low density. (confidence: 0.85)
- Quick commerce in 5 years will likely consolidate around a few players who achieve profitability by optimizing delivery windows and product assortment, not by promising the fastest delivery. (confidence: 0.8)
