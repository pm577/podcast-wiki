# Ed Sim

**Key Beliefs:**
- Founders should focus on building a sustainable business with strong unit economics rather than chasing hypergrowth at all costs. (confidence: 0.9)
- The three biggest mistakes in growth investing are: 1) investing in companies that are not truly differentiated, 2) over-indexing on growth at the expense of unit economics, and 3) not paying enough attention to the CEO's ability to scale. (confidence: 0.8)
- Companies need three things to go public in 2024: 1) a clear path to profitability, 2) a large addressable market, and 3) a strong management team with public company experience. (confidence: 0.8)
- Investors should prioritize companies with a clear competitive moat and defensible technology, especially in AI and infrastructure. (confidence: 0.8)
- The Figma acquisition by Adobe being blocked has made M&A markets more cautious, as buyers are now worried about regulatory risk. (confidence: 0.7)
