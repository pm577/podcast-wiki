# Joey Zwillinger

**Key Beliefs:**
- When you go public, the market judges you on your ability to generate profits, not just growth. Allbirds was valued on growth potential, but when growth slowed, the market repriced the stock dramatically. (confidence: 0.9)
- Allbirds needs to get to profitability by cutting costs and focusing on core products, not by chasing new categories or growth at all costs. (confidence: 0.85)
- Growth slowed because the company expanded too quickly into new product categories and retail locations without sufficient demand validation. (confidence: 0.85)
- The bull case for Allbirds is that they have a strong brand and loyal customer base, and if they can execute on profitability, the stock could recover. (confidence: 0.8)
- Public markets have written Allbirds off because they see a path to profitability as uncertain and the brand's premium positioning may not be sustainable in a recession. (confidence: 0.8)
