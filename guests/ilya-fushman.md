# Ilya Fushman

**Role:** Partner at Index Ventures

**Key Beliefs:**
- Venture capital is like being on a swim team, not a soccer team: you are individually responsible for your own performance, but you also benefit from the team's success. (confidence: 0.9)
- Marketplaces are hard to build because you need to solve the chicken-and-egg problem, but once you get liquidity, they become very defensible. (confidence: 0.9)
- Founders should be obsessed with their customers and spend a lot of time talking to them to understand their needs and pain points. (confidence: 0.9)
- The key lesson from scaling Dropbox to 400M users is that you need to focus on the product and the user experience, not just on growth at all costs. (confidence: 0.8)
- Excessive amounts of capital can drive valuations too high, which can be dangerous for startups because it sets unrealistic expectations and can lead to misallocation of resources. (confidence: 0.8)
