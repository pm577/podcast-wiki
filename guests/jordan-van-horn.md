# Jordan Van Horn

**Key Beliefs:**
- Founders should personally close the first 10-20 customers to understand the sales process before hiring a sales team. (confidence: 0.95)
- The founder must create the initial sales playbook because they understand the customer problem best and can iterate based on early feedback. (confidence: 0.9)
- A sales playbook is a living document that captures the winning sales motions, objection handling, and ideal customer profile based on real deals. (confidence: 0.9)
- The first sales hire should be a rep, not a leader, because a leader without a proven playbook will struggle to scale a process that doesn't exist yet. (confidence: 0.85)
- When hiring the first sales rep, look for someone who can execute and learn, not necessarily someone with a big network or leadership experience. (confidence: 0.85)
