# Daniel Kan

**Role:** Chief Product Officer at Cruise

**Key Beliefs:**
- When scaling from 40 to 1,500 people, the biggest lesson is that you have to be willing to let go of control and trust your team. You can't do everything yourself. (confidence: 0.9)
- CEOs should not hire themselves out of roles; instead, they should continue to do the job they are best at and hire to complement their weaknesses. (confidence: 0.9)
- When hiring, look for people who are smarter than you and who can challenge your thinking. Avoid hiring people who just agree with everything you say. (confidence: 0.9)
- Continuous learning is critical; you should always be reading, listening to podcasts, and seeking out new information to stay ahead. (confidence: 0.8)
- To build a great product, you need to have a clear vision and be willing to make tough decisions about what not to build. (confidence: 0.8)
