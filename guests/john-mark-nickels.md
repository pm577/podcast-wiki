# John Mark Nickels

**Role:** Senior Director of Product Management at Uber

**Previously:** Head of Product for Commercialization at Waymo, Head of Product (Platform) at DoorDash, Engineering Manager at Groupon, Equity Trader at Getco

**Key Beliefs:**
- Get clear on your objective function by thinking about 'future me' — five years from now, you won't care about a slightly better presentation, but you will care about relationships with loved ones. (confidence: 1.0)
- An awareness and mindfulness that our lives will come to an end punctuates reality in a way that requires rethinking priorities. (confidence: 1.0)
- Leadership is broadly defined as having influence in the world; everyone is a leader because we all have influence on others. (confidence: 1.0)
- Conscious leadership involves becoming more aware of your interior world, background, biases, and inherited belief systems, and taking responsibility for your influence. (confidence: 1.0)
- As a senior leader, be mindful of power dynamics; create space for junior folks to speak up and don't need to win the argument in the meeting. (confidence: 1.0)
- When you're more junior, you have limited opportunities to present to executives, so the pressure is higher; senior leaders should be aware of this asymmetry. (confidence: 0.9)
- Organizations that run on fear can still be effective (carrots and sticks work), but they are not conducive to conscious leadership. (confidence: 0.85)
- Emotions in the workplace contain wisdom — fear signals something needs attention, sadness signals something to let go of, anger signals a boundary crossed, and joy signals something to celebrate. (confidence: 0.95)
- Break out of victim consciousness by shifting from "life happening to me" to taking responsibility for how you see the world and showing up differently. (confidence: 0.95)

## Timeline

- **Pre-2014**: Equity trader at Getco; Engineering Manager at Groupon
- **2014**: Joined Uber (Uber 1.0 / Travis era). Built and launched the first version of Uber Pool.
- **2015–2017**: Led product for infrastructure and algorithms powering Uber's matching and pricing systems (rideshare marketplace tech).
- **2017–2018**: Head of Product for DoorDash Platform
- **2019–2020**: Head of Product for Commercialization at Waymo — autonomous ride-hailing and last-mile delivery
- **2021–present**: Returned to Uber as Senior Director of Product Management, leading product for the Mobility team
