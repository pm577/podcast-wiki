# Rob Go

**Key Beliefs:**
- When raising a fund, the first close is the hardest because you have no track record; you need to find a few anchor LPs who believe in you personally. (confidence: 0.9)
- To get a first meeting with an LP, you need a compelling narrative about your unique edge and why this specific fund is needed in the market. (confidence: 0.9)
- Multi-stage funds have not ruined seed investing because they often lack the focus and speed that dedicated seed funds provide, and founders still value hands-on support. (confidence: 0.85)
- The best time to raise a fund is when you have a strong track record of investments, ideally after a few years of deploying capital and seeing early signs of success. (confidence: 0.85)
- Venture capital is a young person's game because it takes a decade to see returns, so starting early allows you to build a career over multiple fund cycles. (confidence: 0.8)
