# Ryan Petersen

**Key Beliefs:**
- Velocity, not speed, is the most important thing in company building. Speed is how fast you're going; velocity is speed in the right direction. (confidence: 0.9)
- To maintain velocity, companies should focus on a few key metrics and avoid getting distracted by vanity metrics. (confidence: 0.9)
- Money creates inefficiencies in scaling. When you have too much capital, you lose the discipline to make tough decisions and prioritize. (confidence: 0.85)
- Founders who raise mega rounds should be wary of the 'lazy capital' trap and maintain a culture of frugality and focus. (confidence: 0.85)
- Remote work is challenging because it reduces serendipitous interactions and slows down decision-making. (confidence: 0.8)
