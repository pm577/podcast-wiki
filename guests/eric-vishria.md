# Eric Vishria

**Role:** General Partner at Benchmark

**Key Beliefs:**
- Foundation models are becoming commoditized, so startups should not build their entire business on top of a single model; instead, they should focus on proprietary data and user experience. (confidence: 0.9)
- To build a durable AI company, focus on workflow integration and data moats rather than just model performance, because models will improve and become cheaper. (confidence: 0.9)
- The value in AI will shift from chips to models to applications over time, and the biggest opportunity for startups is in applications that have sustainable competitive advantages like data network effects and workflow integration. (confidence: 0.8)
- AI applications that have sustaining value are those that solve a real pain point with a clear ROI, not just those that generate short-term revenue from hype. (confidence: 0.8)
- Investors should look for AI startups that have a clear path to distribution and customer lock-in, not just those with the best technology. (confidence: 0.8)
