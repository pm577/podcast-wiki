# Matt Clifford

**Key Beliefs:**
- LLMs are reaching a stage of diminishing returns, so the next S-curve will come from new architectures or approaches, not just scaling existing models. (confidence: 0.85)
- To stay ahead, startups should focus on applications that leverage AI agents rather than just building better models, as model improvements are plateauing. (confidence: 0.85)
- China has the ability to challenge US AI capabilities due to its large talent pool and government investment, but faces constraints in advanced chip access. (confidence: 0.8)
- AI agents will reshape society by automating complex tasks, leading to significant labor market shifts and requiring new forms of human-AI collaboration. (confidence: 0.8)
- Investors should look for startups that are building the infrastructure for AI agents, such as orchestration and reliability layers, as these will be critical for widespread adoption. (confidence: 0.8)
