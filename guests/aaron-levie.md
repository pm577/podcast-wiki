# Aaron Levie

**Key Beliefs:**
- Founders cannot hedge their bets; they must be all-in on a single strategy to succeed. (confidence: 0.9)
- There are two categories of wrong decisions: mistakes of commission (doing something wrong) and mistakes of omission (not doing something right). Avoid the latter by taking action. (confidence: 0.9)
- Being over-funded is dangerous because it can lead to complacency, lack of focus, and misallocation of resources. (confidence: 0.9)
- Founders should be willing to make decisions quickly and iterate, rather than waiting for perfect information. (confidence: 0.9)
- Hire for adaptability and learning ability rather than just domain expertise, especially in early-stage startups. (confidence: 0.8)
