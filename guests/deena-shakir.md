# Deena Shakir

**Role:** Partner at Lux Capital

**Key Beliefs:**
- Founders should think twice before taking multi-stage money at seed because later-stage investors may push for growth at all costs, which can be detrimental for early-stage companies that need to focus on product-market fit. (confidence: 0.9)
- Becoming a parent made me a better investor because it gave me a longer time horizon and more empathy for founders who are also balancing family and work. (confidence: 0.9)
- Venture has never been less collaborative; there is less information sharing and more competition among firms, which can hurt founders who rely on network effects. (confidence: 0.8)
- We should be optimistic about the future of diversity in venture because more funds are being raised by diverse managers and there is increasing awareness of the importance of diverse perspectives. (confidence: 0.8)
