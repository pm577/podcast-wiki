# Leo Polovets

**Key Beliefs:**
- Technical due diligence at seed stage is irrelevant because the product and team change so much. (confidence: 0.9)
- Investing is a game of access, not picking; the best deals are often not available to everyone. (confidence: 0.9)
- A contrarian mindset does not always lead to optimal returns; being too contrarian can lead to missing out on consensus winners. (confidence: 0.9)
- Founders should focus on building a product that a small number of people love rather than a large number like. (confidence: 0.9)
- The best founders are often those who have a deep understanding of the problem they are solving, not necessarily those with the most experience. (confidence: 0.9)
