# Jonathan Ross

**Key Beliefs:**
- Inference will become the dominant compute workload, and companies should optimize for inference efficiency rather than just training. (confidence: 0.9)
- Deepseek's innovation came from a combination of Mixture of Experts (MoE) and multi-head latent attention, which reduced compute requirements by an order of magnitude while maintaining performance. (confidence: 0.8)
- NVIDIA's dominance in training may not translate to inference, as specialized architectures like Groq's LPU can offer significant advantages. (confidence: 0.8)
- Foundation models will become commoditized, and the real value will be in applications and inference infrastructure. (confidence: 0.8)
- The US government and companies like OpenAI should respond by focusing on building better infrastructure and not overreacting with export controls, as innovation can happen anywhere. (confidence: 0.7)
