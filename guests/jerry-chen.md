# Jerry Chen

**Role:** Partner at Greylock Partners

**Key Beliefs:**
- The two fundamentals to assessing startup risk are market risk and execution risk. Market risk is about whether the market exists and is growing; execution risk is about whether the team can build the product and get customers. (confidence: 0.9)
- VCs get in trouble when they move outside their strike zone, meaning they invest in areas where they don't have deep domain expertise or network. (confidence: 0.9)
- Good investors have to be optimistic because if you're not optimistic, you won't see the potential in early-stage companies that look like nothing at the beginning. (confidence: 0.85)
- For enterprise SaaS, the key is to find a wedge that is a must-have, not a nice-to-have, and then expand from there. (confidence: 0.85)
- In AI/ML, the most important thing is to have a unique data advantage because algorithms are commoditized. (confidence: 0.8)
