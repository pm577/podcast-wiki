# Christian Hecker

**Role:** Founder and CEO at Trade Republic

**Key Beliefs:**
- When you sell a large portion of your company early, you lose control and alignment; Christian Hecker sold 75% of Trade Republic for €600K and later realized it was a mistake because it limited his ability to raise future capital and maintain ownership. (confidence: 0.9)
- Founders should focus on building a product that is 10x better than incumbents, not just incrementally better; Trade Republic succeeded by making investing simple, mobile-first, and commission-free, which disrupted traditional brokers. (confidence: 0.9)
- Building a strong culture and team is essential for scaling; Christian emphasized hiring people who are aligned with the mission and have a high bar for execution. (confidence: 0.9)
- Raising capital from top-tier investors like Sequoia and Accel requires a clear vision and the ability to articulate how your company will dominate a large market; Christian emphasized that investors bet on the founder's conviction and the size of the opportunity. (confidence: 0.85)
- Timing is critical in fundraising; Christian raised money when the market was hot and when Trade Republic had strong momentum, which allowed him to command a higher valuation. (confidence: 0.85)
