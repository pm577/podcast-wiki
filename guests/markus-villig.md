# Markus Villig

**Role:** Founder and CEO at Bolt

**Key Beliefs:**
- We started Bolt with a $5,000 loan from my parents, and every VC turned us down. We had to prove the model ourselves before anyone would invest. (confidence: 0.9)
- The key to competing with Uber was focusing on cities where Uber was weak and offering a better local experience, not trying to outspend them. (confidence: 0.9)
- We built a two-sided marketplace by first solving the supply side: we recruited drivers aggressively, even if it meant initially having more drivers than riders. (confidence: 0.9)
- We raised money from Sequoia and D1 only after we had strong unit economics and a clear path to profitability. They invest in businesses that are already working. (confidence: 0.9)
- Our micromobility business (scooters and bikes) is profitable in many cities because we own the hardware and optimize operations, unlike some competitors who rely on third-party hardware. (confidence: 0.8)
