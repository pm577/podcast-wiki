# Brian Norgard

**Role:** Former Chief Product Officer at Tinder

**Key Beliefs:**
- Products fail because they don't solve a real problem or they solve a problem that doesn't exist. (confidence: 0.9)
- Marketplaces need to focus on liquidity and reducing friction for both supply and demand sides simultaneously. (confidence: 0.9)
- Founders should prioritize speed of iteration over perfection; shipping quickly and learning from user feedback is more valuable than getting it right the first time. (confidence: 0.9)
- The most successful products have a clear 'aha moment' that users experience quickly, often within the first session. (confidence: 0.85)
- The best founders assess risk by asking 'What's the worst that can happen?' and then realizing it's usually not that bad, so they take the risk. (confidence: 0.8)
