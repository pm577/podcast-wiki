# Adam D'Augelli

**Role:** Partner at True Ventures

**Key Beliefs:**
- You cannot grow ownership in your best companies over time because later rounds dilute you and you can't keep investing pro rata if the company is doing well and raising at higher valuations. (confidence: 0.9)
- You should think about portfolio construction in terms of power law outcomes: a few investments will drive the majority of returns. (confidence: 0.9)
- The best venture investments come from being early and having a high ownership percentage that you can maintain through later rounds. (confidence: 0.85)
- To maintain ownership in your best companies, you need to reserve capital for follow-on investments and be willing to invest in later rounds at higher valuations. (confidence: 0.85)
- To make space for serendipity, you need to have a systematic process for meeting new founders and companies outside your existing network. (confidence: 0.8)
