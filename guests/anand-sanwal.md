# Anand Sanwal

**Key Beliefs:**
- The most dangerous myth some investors have promoted is that you need to be a domain expert to start a company in that space. Actually, being an outsider can be an advantage because you bring fresh perspective and are not constrained by industry dogma. (confidence: 0.9)
- Most B2B content is crap because it's too promotional and doesn't provide real value. To make it successful, focus on data-driven insights and original research that helps your audience do their job better. (confidence: 0.9)
- When scaling teams, hire for culture add rather than culture fit. You want people who bring new perspectives and challenge the status quo, not just those who fit in. (confidence: 0.9)
- Predigree (pedigree) is overrated. Many of the best hires come from non-traditional backgrounds. Look for raw intelligence, hustle, and a willingness to learn rather than just Ivy League degrees. (confidence: 0.9)
- To build a great company culture, you need to be intentional about it from day one. Write down your values and use them in hiring and firing decisions. (confidence: 0.9)
