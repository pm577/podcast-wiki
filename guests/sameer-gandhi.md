# Sameer Gandhi

**Role:** Partner at Accel

**Key Beliefs:**
- When a founder has a term sheet, encourage them to shop it to other investors to create competition and improve terms. (confidence: 0.9)
- Speed of execution is critical; you need to move fast to capture market opportunity, but sometimes you need to go slow to go fast (e.g., invest in infrastructure upfront). (confidence: 0.9)
- Market sizing should be done bottom-up by identifying the specific customer pain point and the total addressable market that can be captured, not top-down with large numbers. (confidence: 0.85)
- Founders should focus on building a product that solves a real, urgent problem for a specific customer segment rather than trying to address a vague large market. (confidence: 0.85)
- Leading multiple internal rounds can be a signal of strong conviction and alignment, but it requires careful management of investor dynamics and dilution. (confidence: 0.8)
