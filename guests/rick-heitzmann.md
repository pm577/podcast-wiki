# Rick Heitzmann

**Role:** Founder and Partner at Firstmark Capital

**Key Beliefs:**
- Founders should focus on building a product that is 10x better than the competition, not just incrementally better, to achieve breakout success. (confidence: 0.9)
- VCs can prepare their portfolio companies for a downturn by encouraging them to raise more capital than they think they need and to focus on unit economics. (confidence: 0.9)
- The best founders have a deep understanding of their customer and a clear vision for the product, and they are able to articulate that vision in a compelling way. (confidence: 0.9)
- Pre-emptive rounds are becoming more common, where investors proactively offer terms to founders before they formally raise, often at higher valuations to secure the deal. (confidence: 0.8)
- Now is a good time to be contrarian and invest in consumer startups because many VCs are avoiding the space, creating less competition and better valuations. (confidence: 0.8)
