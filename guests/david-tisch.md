# David Tisch

**Key Beliefs:**
- Founders often say they want direct feedback but many cannot handle it; good investors calibrate feedback to the founder's receptivity. (confidence: 0.9)
- Seed investors should focus on the team and market more than the product, as products pivot frequently. (confidence: 0.9)
- Concentrated investing at seed stage works if you have high conviction and can add value beyond capital. (confidence: 0.8)
- Founders should seek investors who provide honest, constructive feedback and have a track record of helping companies through tough times. (confidence: 0.8)
- Consumer social is interesting again because of new platforms and changing user behaviors, especially among younger demographics. (confidence: 0.7)
