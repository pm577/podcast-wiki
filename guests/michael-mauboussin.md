# Michael Mauboussin

**Role:** Head of Consilient Research at Counterpoint Global

**Key Beliefs:**
- The single biggest mistake people make in their decision-making process is not distinguishing between good and bad outcomes and good and bad processes. (confidence: 0.9)
- To improve your process, you need to keep a decision journal and review your decisions systematically. (confidence: 0.9)
- You should focus on the process, not the outcome, because good processes lead to good outcomes over time. (confidence: 0.9)
- When evaluating a decision, ask yourself: 'What would I do if I had all the information?' to separate luck from skill. (confidence: 0.9)
- The best way to know if your process needs improvement is to track your decisions and compare your hit rate to your expected hit rate. (confidence: 0.9)
