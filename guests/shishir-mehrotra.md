## Key Beliefs

- **Reference checks are the #1 hiring signal** — They reveal how someone performed over years of real work, providing far more insight than 30 minutes of artificial interview scenarios. The reference check is more valuable than any interview signal.
- **Products grow through loops, not funnels** — Most products have a natural loop (e.g., share → create → share). Understanding and diagramming your ecosystem's loops is critical for growth. A "Black Loop" (share with team) and a "Blue Loop" (publish to the world) are two distinct growth engines.
- **Remove all friction from the share edge** — "No dollar signs in the share dial." The moment someone shares your product is the most important growth moment; charging for sharing kills viral growth. Coda's Maker Billing model (charging only for makers, not viewers or editors) was designed around this principle.
- **Golden rituals are named, known by every employee by their first Friday, and templated** — These three rules (from Bing Gordon) define a company's "golden rituals." Rituals are a mirror of culture, and culture is the product you build for your employees (Dharmesh Shah's framing).
- **Eigenquestions are the highest-leverage questions** — An eigenquestion is the question that, when answered, also answers the most subsequent questions. Instead of ranking decisions by importance, rank them by which one would eliminate the most other questions from your list (e.g., YouTube's "Do we value consistency or comprehensiveness?").
- **To create a ritual, use the Switch framework** — Direct the rider (teach it in onboarding), motivate the elephant (give it a name so people form identity around it), and shape the path (templatize it to make it easy to follow).
- **Evaluate talent on PSHE, not scope** — PSHE (Problem, Solution, How, Execution) is the framework for leveling product managers and other roles. Scope is an input, not an output — judge what people do with their scope, not how much scope they're given.
