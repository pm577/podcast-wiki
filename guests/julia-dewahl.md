# Julia DeWahl

**Role:** Angel Investor at Self-employed

**Key Beliefs:**
- The most important thing is to have a very clear understanding of who your customer is and what their pain points are, and then build a product that solves that pain point. (confidence: 0.9)
- The right way to structure customer discovery is to talk to customers early and often, and to be willing to pivot based on what you learn. (confidence: 0.9)
- You need to have a system for growth that is repeatable and scalable, and that starts with having a very clear understanding of your unit economics. (confidence: 0.85)
- You need to have a culture of transparency and open communication, especially when things are going wrong. (confidence: 0.85)
- One of the biggest lessons from Opendoor's scaling journey is that you need to hire people who are smarter than you and give them the autonomy to do their jobs. (confidence: 0.8)
