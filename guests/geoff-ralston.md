# Geoff Ralston

**Role:** President at Y Combinator

**Key Beliefs:**
- Founders should focus on building products that a small number of users love rather than trying to appeal to a broad audience initially. (confidence: 0.95)
- The single most important perspective an investor can provide a founder is that the company is not the founder's identity; it's a separate entity that can be iterated upon. (confidence: 0.9)
- Investors lose when they 'profile invest'—making decisions based on surface-level traits rather than deep understanding of the founder and product. (confidence: 0.9)
- Investors should provide honest, direct feedback to founders, even if it's uncomfortable, because it helps them improve faster. (confidence: 0.9)
- The best founders are relentlessly resourceful and have a clear vision, but are also willing to pivot based on user feedback. (confidence: 0.85)
