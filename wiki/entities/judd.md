# Judd Antin

Judd Antin built user research at Facebook and was longtime head of research at Airbnb. His direct reports have gone on to lead research at Figma, Notion, Slack, Robinhood, Duolingo, and more. He's become a prominent voice arguing that the user research discipline is at a reckoning point and must adapt to survive.

## Key Views

**The User Research Reckoning Is Here — Research Must Adapt or Die**
Antin sparked a firestorm with his article arguing user research is at a reckoning. His thesis: research teams have been hit disproportionately hard by layoffs because the discipline isn't driving enough value. "The research that is most interesting to researchers is often the least impactful for the business." He identifies a "middle range" of research — user-understanding questions that are interesting but not actionable — as the culprit. Researchers need to shift toward more macro (strategic, business-focused) and micro (usability, experiment analysis) work where impact is clearer.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/judd-antin.md]]*

**"User-Centered Performance" Is the Enemy of Real Impact**
Antin coined the term "user-centered performance" to describe the symbolic customer-obsession work teams do to signal how customer-focused they are without intending to change decisions. "Every time a PM comes to a researcher at the end of a product process and says 'Can you just run a quick user study to validate our assumptions,' that's user-centered performance. It's too late to matter." The cure: researchers should adopt a falsification mindset — "We don't validate, we falsify. We are looking to be wrong" — and refuse research requests that come too late to influence decisions.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/judd-antin.md]]*

**Researchers Must Be Profit-Focused, Not Just User-Focused**
Antin's most controversial point: researchers need to care about business outcomes and profitability, not just user needs. "There are a lot of people out there who think that's not cool or not research's job. I'm like, 'Well, what are we doing then, if we're not helping businesses succeed?'" Research that doesn't connect to business impact will always be the first to go in downturns. Researchers who understand the business model and can frame their insights in terms of revenue, retention, or growth will be indispensable.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/judd-antin.md]]*

**Macro, Middle-Range, Micro: The Three-Layer Research Framework**
Antin categorizes research into three types. Macro: big-picture, strategic, forward-looking (market analysis, innovation research). Middle-range: "blobular" user-understanding questions that are interesting but often not decisive. Micro: usability testing, A/B test analysis, pixel-perfect refinement. His argument is that research teams are over-invested in middle-range work and under-invested in macro and micro. The middle-range feels important but often fails to change decisions or drive business outcomes.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/judd-antin.md]]*

**Great Research Leaders Develop People — The Airbnb Diaspora Proves It**
Antin's network of former reports now leading research at the most important tech companies (Figma, Notion, Slack, Robinhood) demonstrates that the highest-leverage contribution of a research leader is developing people. He doesn't take credit for their success — "I'm just a privileged to work with all these amazing humans" — but the pattern is clear. Building a culture where researchers are challenged, supported, and given ownership of meaningful problems creates a talent pipeline that multiplies your impact far beyond your own tenure.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/judd-antin.md]]*
