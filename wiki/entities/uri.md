# Uri Levine

Uri Levine is the co-founder of Waze (acquired by Google for $1.3 billion) along with nine other companies, two of which he sold for over $1 billion. He's the author of "Fall in Love with the Problem, Not the Solution" and a renowned expert on startup crisis management.

## Key Views

**Two types of startup crisis: cash crisis and loss of product-market fit.** Uri distinguishes between a cash crisis (when your cash plan is jeopardized by losing a customer or investor) and loss of product-market fit (when everything you knew becomes irrelevant and you must return to square one). Founders must recognize which type they face because each demands a fundamentally different response.

**Never give up — the most important founder behavior.** Uri argues that persistence is the single most important trait of successful startup CEOs. Combined with making decisions with conviction, this combination separates successful founders from those who fail. If the team doesn't see conviction from the leader, they won't follow.

**It's always the founder's fault.** Uri believes founders cannot rely on anyone else — they must assume full responsibility for their company's destiny. This ownership mindset is what enables founders to control their own fate rather than becoming victims of circumstance. Blaming external factors is a trap that prevents effective action.

**Every founder will face multiple crises.** Rather than asking how to avoid crisis entirely (which is impossible), Uri advises founders to accept that building a startup is "a journey from one crisis to the next." The key is developing the tools and mindset to handle each crisis as it comes, rather than hoping to avoid them.

**Fall in love with the problem, not the solution.** Uri's core philosophy emphasizes that founders should become obsessed with deeply understanding the problem they're solving rather than falling in love with their initial solution. When product-market fit disappears, this mindset makes it easier to pivot and find a new path forward.
