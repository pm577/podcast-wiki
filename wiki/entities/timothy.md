# Timothy Davis

**Role:** Head of Performance Marketing at Shopify; former growth consultant for Pinterest, LinkedIn, Redfin, Eventbrite  
**Known for:** Deep expertise in paid growth and performance marketing at scale, building and scaling paid acquisition programs at major tech companies.

## Key Views

### 1. "Paid is for everyone" — but start with paid search

Timothy's hot take: paid growth is table stakes for virtually every company today. With Google's AI-powered results pushing organic listings far down the page and Meta becoming a "pay for play" platform, organic reach alone is insufficient. He recommends starting with Google Search because it is user-driven (the user types in a relevant keyword) rather than disruptive. This should be followed by Meta, then YouTube if video creative is available.

> "If you look at the way each platform is doing, Google — you have to scroll pretty far down to get to an organic listing. Meta — it's almost a pay for play now."

### 2. Run "signs of life" tests before scaling

Instead of committing large budgets to a new platform upfront, Timothy advocates running small tests to check for a "sign of life." Use your existing customer data to build 1% lookalike audiences on the platform. If there's a positive signal, then build a proper campaign around it. Never scale from a sign of life directly to full throttle — validate your creative, messaging, and targeting first.

> "There's no reason to say, 'All right, we have a sign of life. Let's now turn it up all the way.' Do we have the right creative? Do we have the right campaign? Do we have the right messaging?"

### 3. Focus on "signal not noise" in performance data

Timothy's hiring philosophy centers on separating signal from noise. Platforms provide an overwhelming amount of data — impressions, reach, frequency, CPMs, CPCs, conversion rates. The key is identifying which metric actually maps to the campaign goal. If the goal is conversions, focus on conversions and click-through rates, not impressions or vanity metrics.

> "Who cares about how many impressions you're getting? Who cares about the reach? Who cares about the frequency? If the goal of the campaign is to get the conversions, that's what we should be doing."

### 4. Multi-touch attribution with time decay is the right approach

Timothy advocates for multi-touch attribution with time decay — giving more credit to touchpoints closer to conversion. But he cautions that attribution is inherently biased and doesn't answer the question of incrementality. For larger spend (over $50K/month per platform), running GeoX or Conversion Lift tests to measure true incrementality is critical.

### 5. Start with an agency/consultant, build a milestone to hire in-house

For startups beginning their paid growth journey, Timothy recommends starting with an agency or consultant who can get things off the ground. Set a clear milestone (e.g., "$50K/month in spend") when you plan to hire someone in-house. A good partner will help you get there and willingly transition — a bad one will try to hold on to the account.

> "If your end goal is to have this performing at $100 million spend, there's no way one person can do that. So let's create milestones along the way."
