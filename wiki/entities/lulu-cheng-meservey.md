# Lulu Cheng Meservey

Lulu Cheng Meservey appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss lulu cheng meservey, communications leader (substack, activision), on cultural erogenous zones, concentric circles of influence, and strategic communication for underdogs.

## Key Views

**Cultural Erogenous Zones** — Don't try to change people's worldview; it's a huge lift. Instead, shape your message to fit what your audience already cares about. Create an 'API' or bridge from their existing passions to your product. If there's no natural fit, accept that those people aren't your audience and move on. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lulu-cheng-meservey.md]

**Concentric Circles Framework** — To spread a message effectively, start from your own desk and work outward in sequence: yourself to co-founders/employees to investors to power users to influencers to broader audience. Never skip a circle, because each inner circle validates the message for the next ring. Disgruntled inner-circle members can do outsized damage because their credibility is highest. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lulu-cheng-meservey.md]

**Pressure = Force / Surface Area** — In communications, pressure (impact) equals force (effort/time/money) divided by surface area (audience breadth). To maximize impact with limited resources, decrease your surface area: hyper-target a small group of diehards rather than broadcasting weak messages to millions. A tiny monopoly you dominate beats food coloring in the ocean. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lulu-cheng-meservey.md]

**Mistakes of Commission Beat Omission** — For underdog startups, the enemy is the status quo. You learn nothing from inaction. Make bold moves; when you commit mistakes you can see, observe, and adapt instantly. Playing it safe means letting your competitors win by default, like sitting on cash while the market grows around you. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lulu-cheng-meservey.md]

**Go Direct and Be a Person** — Underdogs can't rely on traditional media or institutional gatekeepers. Build your own audience from day one by speaking as a human (not a faceless corporation). Pick one channel that matches your CEO's natural communication style — long-form writing, Twitter, video, or podcasts — and invest everything there. Consistency and genuine value beat chasing viral one-offs every time. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lulu-cheng-meservey.md]
