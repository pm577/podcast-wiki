# Yuriy Timen

**Role:** Former Global Head of Marketing and Growth at Grammarly (8.5 years); growth advisor for Canva, Airtable, Whimsical, Hims, Otter, and a dozen other companies  
**Known for:** Transforming Grammarly into a household name, deep tactical expertise in growing subscription businesses through virality, SEO, and paid growth.

## Key Views

### 1. The only thing worse than a channel not working is prematurely concluding it doesn't work

Yuriy's most common observation when advising companies: they try a channel once, do it poorly, and declare it dead. "Oh, YouTube? We tried it. It doesn't work." Nine times out of ten, the execution was flawed — wrong creative, wrong targeting, wrong measurement. His advice: give every channel a real shot with proper setup before concluding it's ineffective.

> "The only thing that's worse than a channel or a tactic that you tried not working... is when you didn't give it the appropriate shot, and you prematurely and erroneously concluded that it doesn't work."

### 2. Virality is engineered, not accidental

Yuriy breaks down virality into a mechanistic framework: it's about building sharing into the core product experience. Grammarly's growth was driven by embedding a "share your score" mechanic that made every user a distribution channel. The key insight: think about virality at the product design stage, not as a growth tactic bolted on later.

### 3. SEO and content are long-term compound growth engines

At Grammarly, Yuriy invested heavily in SEO and content marketing as a compound growth engine. Unlike paid growth, which stops when you stop spending, SEO builds an asset that compounds over time. The tradeoff: it takes longer to see results, but the ROI grows exponentially.

### 4. Growth advisors should focus on the highest-leverage bets

As an advisor working with multiple companies, Yuriy's approach is to identify the 1-2 highest-leverage growth opportunities and direct all energy there, rather than spreading efforts across a dozen tactics. This requires deep understanding of each company's product, market, and existing growth loops.

### 5. Subscription growth requires optimizing the full funnel, not just acquisition

Yuriy emphasizes that subscription businesses must optimize activation, retention, and monetization alongside acquisition. A leaky bucket — high churn — negates any acquisition gains. The most successful subscription companies obsess over the moment of first value (the "aha moment") and build retention loops into the product.
