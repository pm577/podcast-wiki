# Cliff Weitzman

Founder & CEO of Speechify AI. Built a Voice AI agent to overcome his own dyslexia and ADHD in college. Speechify is now used by over 60 million people with over 1 million five-star ratings and 200+ software and AI engineers. Won the Apple Design Award.

## Key Views

### 1. Until You Hit $100K, Do Not Spend on Any Channel Other Than Meta

Cliff's hard-won growth lesson: for early-stage consumer AI companies, Meta (Facebook/Instagram) is the only channel worth investing in before reaching $100K in revenue. Every other channel — SEO, content, partnerships, paid search — is a distraction at the earliest stages. The playbook: find your audience on Meta, iterate on creative until unit economics work, then expand.^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2026-05-09-20vc-what-i-learned-from-100.md]

### 2. Hacking the System: Applying to 26 Universities

Cliff's resourcefulness was forged early: he applied to 26 universities to maximize his options, hacking the college admissions system. This same tenacity carried into building Speechify — he approached every constraint (dyslexia, ADHD, lack of funding) as a creative problem to solve rather than a barrier.^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2026-05-09-20vc-what-i-learned-from-100.md]

### 3. How We Will Spend More on Tokens Than Salaries

Cliff predicts that as AI becomes core to Speechify's product, the company's token costs (inference and API calls) will soon exceed its salary costs. This represents a fundamental shift in company economics — from people being the largest expense to compute being the largest expense. This has profound implications for how AI companies should think about pricing, margins, and scaling.^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2026-05-09-20vc-what-i-learned-from-100.md]
