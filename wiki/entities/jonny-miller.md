# Jonny Miller

Jonny Miller appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss jonny miller, founder of nervous systems mastery, on managing nerves, anxiety, burnout, and nervous system regulation.

## Key Views

**State over story** — The most effective way to calm down is not to reframe your thoughts (top-down) but to change your physiological state first (bottom-up). With four times more afferent neurons traveling from body to brain than vice versa, adjusting your breath or posture creates a cascading effect that shifts your thoughts and feelings automatically, rather than trying to talk yourself out of anxiety. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/jonny-miller.md]

**The feather-brick-dump truck phenomenon** — Early signs of burnout are subtle (the feather: waking up tired). If ignored, they escalate to disproportionate reactivity (the brick: losing your cool, snapping at people). Eventually they culminate in a full-blown health crisis or career implosion (the dump truck). Developing interoceptive awareness lets you catch the feather and adjust before the dump truck hits. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/jonny-miller.md]

**Emotional debt functions like technical debt** — Unresolved stress responses (incomplete mobilization cycles) accumulate as allostatic load in the body, creating nervous system fragility. Key warning signs: needing alcohol or other substances to downshift at the end of the day, being disproportionately reactive to minor triggers, and being unable to ever truly relax. Paying off emotional debt requires body-based practices (somatic experiencing, breathwork), not just talk therapy — the body needs to complete the stress cycle it was holding. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/jonny-miller.md]

**The APE practice for interoception** — Three dimensions to regularly scan: Awareness (narrow/focused vs. expansive/spacious), Posture (how you are holding your body), and Emotion (somatic sensations, mood, tension). This trains your interoceptive palate — your sixth sense for tracking your internal landscape — so you can catch dysregulation early and apply the right breathing intervention before it escalates. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/jonny-miller.md]

**Feeling the feels is a competitive advantage in business** — Damasio's landmark study of patient Elliot showed that without access to emotions, people cannot make even basic decisions. Leaders who numb or avoid uncomfortable feelings (anger, grief, conflict) make biased decisions to avoid those feelings. Those who welcome the full emotional spectrum make clearer decisions, and through emotional contagion, their regulated nervous system stabilizes the entire organization's culture. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/jonny-miller.md]
