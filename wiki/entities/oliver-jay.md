# Oliver Jay (OJ)

Former CRO @ Asana and one of the most successful sales leaders of the last decade. Spent 6 years at Asana as the company's first revenue leader, responsible for product-led and sales-led revenue, growing the team from under 20 to over 450. Previously spent 4 years at Dropbox as Head of APAC and LATAM, scaling the sales team from 0 to 50 while tripling ARR.

## Key Views

### 1. How to Create and Execute a World-Class Sales Playbook

A sales playbook should codify the specific motions that work for your ICP. It's not a theoretical document — it must be built from real calls, real objections, and real closes. The best playbooks are living documents that evolve as the team learns what actually wins deals.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2022-05-25-20-sales-how-to-create-and-ex.md]

### 2. Do Both PLG and Enterprise Sales at the Same Time

Companies should not choose between product-led growth and enterprise sales — the best companies run both simultaneously. PLG captures the bottom-up adoption, while enterprise sales closes the top-down contracts. The key is building a sales team that understands how to work with PLG motion rather than against it.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2022-05-25-20-sales-how-to-create-and-ex.md]

### 3. Three Non-Obvious Qualities the Best Sales Reps Have

Beyond the standard criteria (hustle, communication skills), the best reps have: (1) intellectual curiosity — they genuinely want to understand the customer's business; (2) pattern recognition — they can identify what's working across deals and adapt; (3) resilience without cynicism — they handle rejection without becoming jaded.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2022-05-25-20-sales-how-to-create-and-ex.md]

### 4. The Four Steps to Sales Team Onboarding

Effective onboarding follows four phases: (1) product and market immersion, (2) shadowing and call observation, (3) supervised execution with coaching, (4) independent ramp with accountability. Skipping any phase creates gaps that show up months later in underperformance.
