# Bob Baxley

Bob Baxley is a design leader who shaped products used by billions. Over 8 years at Apple he led design for the online store and the App Store. He was Director of Design at Pinterest, Head of Design at Yahoo, and most recently SVP of Product Design at ThoughtSpot. He also advised Disney on product design. His career spans over 35 years.

## Key Views

**Design Is Imagining and Building the Future** — "Design is trying to imagine the future you want to live in and then take the steps to make it real." This is a fundamentally optimistic and proactive definition. It means designers are responsible for not just making things look good, but for having a point of view about what should exist and driving toward it. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/bob-baxley.md]

**Design-Led Means DNA-Deep, Not Designer-Led** — "Saying a company is design-led does not mean it's designer-led. I've never seen somebody graft it on after the fact. It's there at the beginning in the root DNA or it doesn't exist." Apple was design-led because Jobs made design decisions from the founding moment — not because designers had veto power over engineers. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/bob-baxley.md]

**Wait as Long as Possible to Draw a Picture** — The biggest mistake in product design is prototyping too early. Premature visualization locks the team into a solution before they've deeply understood the problem. Baxley advocates for spending disproportionate time in abstract problem definition before any pixel is moved. Ideas that can't withstand verbal scrutiny won't be saved by a pretty mockup. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/bob-baxley.md]

**Designers Have a Moral Obligation** — "Almost everyone living in a modern economy now is going to have hundreds of interactions with a phone or with a computer. Unfortunately, a lot of those interactions are not going to be great. We have an obligation as product people to put that emotional energy back into people's lives." Bad design isn't just inefficient — it's emotionally costly at scale. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/bob-baxley.md]

**Steve Jobs on Building Apple Itself** — Jobs listed Apple itself as one of his proudest products, alongside Apple II, Mac, iPod, and iPhone. The longest-lasting creation isn't any device — it's the organization that keeps making great devices. Baxley contrasts this with Pixar: Jobs noted that tech products become doorstops in 3 years, but Pixar films are watched for 100 years. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/bob-baxley.md]
