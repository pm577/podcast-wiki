# Nikita Miller

Nikita Miller is SVP of Product at The Knot Worldwide, overseeing product for the company's suite of celebration-planning products. Previously, she was VP of Product at Dooly and spent over five years at Trello (Atlassian) as Head of Growth and Retention. She is also an angel investor and advisor to early-stage startups.

## Key Views

### Output Matters — Don't Swing Too Far on Outcomes
Nikita's counterintuitive take on the outcomes vs. output debate: "Many of the companies I've worked with or advised swung way too far on the outcomes train and forgot that output is an indicator of that." If a team has great processes (ideation, documentation, briefs) but isn't shipping things quickly, the outcomes won't materialize. The best teams balance rigorous discovery with consistent shipping velocity. You need both the thinking and the doing.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/nikita-miller.md]

### The Roles and Responsibilities Contract
Nikita's framework for building strong cross-functional teams: have the product, design, engineering, and data leaders each write down what they expect from each other, then align on a shared "contract." "I as a product leader write down what I think the expectations and roles and responsibilities of my engineering manager, my designer, my data scientist are. Then we look at it together and arrive at essentially a contract." This is time-intensive but prevents the friction and misalignment that slows teams down.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/nikita-miller.md]

### The Product Quaternity: Product, Design, Engineering, Data
Nikita advocates expanding the classic "product triad" (product, design, engineering) to include data science as a fourth core partner. "My mission is product, design, engineering, data." Data scientists should be embedded in the same cross-functional squad from ideation through analysis. They contribute to experiment briefs, help define success metrics upfront, and participate in the same product discovery process. This ensures decisions are grounded in evidence from the start.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/nikita-miller.md]

### Enterprise Growth Requires Thinking in Teams, Not Individuals
From her experience growing Trello's enterprise business, Nikita learned that consumer products need a fundamentally different approach when moving upmarket. "How do you create a shared perspective for everyone working on a project?" Enterprise growth requires shifting from single-player or small-team mode to supporting 5, 10, 20+ people collaborating. Features like team-level invitations, shared labels, and admin controls become critical. The hardest part is maintaining the simplicity that made the product great while adding enterprise capabilities.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/nikita-miller.md]

### Wedding Planning and Product Management Are Surprisingly Similar
Nikita draws a fascinating parallel between her two domains. "Folks planning their weddings — it's an emotional, high-stakes thing that you're hopefully going to do once. The pressure and expectations are really high, not unlike product managers." Both involve managing multiple stakeholders, coordinating vendors/teams, and operating on a defined timeline. The key difference: wedding couples have even higher emotional stakes — "for couples this is for many the most meaningful time of their lives, and you don't want to let them down because most aren't going to do it again."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/nikita-miller.md]
