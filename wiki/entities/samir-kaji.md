# Samir Kaji

Senior Managing Director at First Republic Bank, where he leads the technology banking team managing venture capital and startup company relationships. Over 18+ years in venture capital, has assisted or advised over 700 companies and 300 VC firms, completing tech financing transactions totaling over $4B in committed capital. Previously worked at Silicon Valley Bank. Writes the influential blog at pevcbanker.com.

## Key Views

### 1. The Micro VC Market Trajectory

Kaji provides deep analysis of the micro VC market, tracking where capital is flowing in the early-stage ecosystem. He explains the dynamics driving the rise of sub-$100M funds, the economics of managing smaller fund sizes, and why many micro VCs were outperforming larger funds on a multiple basis despite having less capital to deploy.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2015-12-02-20-vc-093-where-is-the-micro.md]

### 2. What It Takes to Raise a Venture Fund Today

Kaji analyzes the explosion of seed financing and why IRR no longer takes center stage for LPs evaluating fund managers. He breaks down what it actually takes to raise a venture fund — the track record requirements, the LP relationship building process, the marketing materials that matter, and how the bar has shifted between 2015 and 2017.
