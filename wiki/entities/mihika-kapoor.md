# Mihika Kapoor

Design-engineer-PM hybrid at Figma. Early PM on FigJam, now spearheading development of a new zero-to-one product. Previously founded Design Nation (nonprofit for design education) and led products at Meta focused on commerce and creators. Known as the go-to person at Figma for building new products from scratch.

## Key Views

### 1. Vision Is Everything — It Anchors Through Chaos

"Vision is everything. The reality of the product development cycle is that it's so messy, so chaotic. You're going to have extreme highs and extreme lows. In order to ensure those moments are learning opportunities rather than discouraging, it's so important to be anchored on that singular vision." The vision makes every step feel like forward progress, even when you pivot.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/mihika-kapoor.md]

### 2. Design and Prototype Before Getting a Green Light

"We lean heavily into designing and prototyping even before a project gets a green light. If you and your team do your job correctly, what does the world look like?" The most powerful way to sell an idea is to make it real enough for people to experience it — not through slides but through prototypes that convey the vision viscerally.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/mihika-kapoor.md]

### 3. Your Scope Is the World

"Nothing should ever be perceived as being out of bounds." The best PMs don't limit themselves by organizational boundaries. If something needs to happen to make the product successful, they own it regardless of who technically "should" do it. This ownership mentality is what separates great PMs from good ones.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/mihika-kapoor.md]

### 4. Maker Week (Internal Hackathons) Drive Innovation

Figma's "Maker Week" concept gives people breathing space "to see ahead into the horizon and be wildly ambitious." Many of Figma's most successful products and features began at hackathons. Creating space for unstructured exploration is how zero-to-one innovation happens inside companies.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/mihika-kapoor.md]

### 5. Lean Into Your Spikes, But Keep a Growth Mindset

Kapoor is a "left brain, right brain" person — CS major and visual arts minor. She found PM by straddling engineering and design. "Based on how you fell into PM, you might have different spikes and different strengths. Leaning into those is really important. But for the other things, it's also important to have a growth mindset and to constantly be conquering what comes next."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/mihika-kapoor.md]
