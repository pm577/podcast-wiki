# Karri Saarinen

Co-founder and CEO of Linear, the most beloved issue-tracking tool in software. Previously founding designer at Coinbase, principal designer at Airbnb, and co-founder of two prior startups. Known for building product with an extreme focus on craft, taste, and opinionated software design.

## Key Views

### 1. Design Matters More as Markets Get More Crowded

"The more it matters, the more the design matters." In new categories, first-movers don't need great design. But as hundreds of competitors emerge, the bar rises. Today, even early-stage startups need high-level design for people to pay attention. Design is not a luxury — it's table stakes that sets expectations and signals quality before a user even tries the product.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/karri-saarinen.md]

### 2. No PMs for Craft — Project Ownership Lives with Builders

Linear has only one head of product for ~50 people. Engineers and designers own the full scope of their projects — including thinking about direction, scope, and communication. This creates craft because the people building are the same people making decisions. "When you give people ownership of the project and the space to do that, they see opportunities to make things better" — like an engineer implementing dynamic safe zones in menus that no one asked for.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/karri-saarinen.md]

### 3. Ship Early Internally, Polish for Public

The Linear method: build a rough version and ship it to production internally within the first week. Let the team test it themselves. Then find 1-10 opt-in customers to co-develop. Only for the full public release do they obsess over polish. This avoids the perfection trap while still delivering a high-quality final product. "Craft is not about perfecting things very early — it's about pushing things out quickly, then fixing them quickly."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/karri-saarinen.md]

### 4. Build Opinionated Software

Productivity software should have strong defaults and opinions about how work should be done. Flexible tools create decision fatigue — teams spend time configuring instead of doing. Linear's design mantra: "Design something for someone." An opinionated solution is the most optimized solution for the problem, and when users agree, they feel the difference.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/karri-saarinen.md]

### 5. No A/B Tests, No Feature-Level Metrics Goals

Linear doesn't set quantitative goals for individual features. They don't run A/B tests. Instead, they use direct customer contact (shared Slack channels, weekly calls, everyone answers support) and the team's own taste and judgment. "The success of a feature is whether customers agree the problem is solved, not whether a metric went up." This requires deep customer empathy distributed across the entire team.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/karri-saarinen.md]

### 6. Build Brand Through Consistency, Not Campaigns

Brand is not a logo or color palette — it's built over time through every interaction: how you treat customers, how you write, how you build. At Airbnb, brand was "probably the most important thing" for Brian Chesky. For Linear, brand comes from consistent execution and authentic voice, not marketing campaigns.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/karri-saarinen.md]
