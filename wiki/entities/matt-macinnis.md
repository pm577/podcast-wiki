# Matt MacInnis

Matt MacInnis is Chief Product Officer (formerly longtime Chief Operating Officer) at Rippling, a unified workforce management platform valued at over $16 billion with 5,000+ employees. He previously spent seven years at Apple under Steve Jobs.

## Key Views

**Deliberately Understaff Every Project** — Overstaffing creates politics, people working on low-priority items, and organizational cruft. By deliberately understaffing, you force focus, eliminate waste, and maintain velocity. "If you overstaff, you get politics, you get people working on things that are further down the priority list than necessary. That is poison." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/matt-macinnis.md]

**Extraordinary Results Require Extraordinary Effort** — If you want to be in the 99th percentile in terms of outcomes, it's going to be really difficult and uncomfortable. If you ever find yourself in the comfort zone at work, you've made a mistake. This isn't about a single grand story — it's about a thousand little things (a thousand Jira tickets, not a thousand grand events) that separate great players and teams from good ones. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/matt-macinnis.md]

**Relentlessness Is Required** — In a competitive market, there can be no relaxation of the organization. As soon as you ship one version, you're immediately in the pit building the next one (the "death march" approach Matt learned at Apple). If you leave a crack for a competitor, they will fill it. It doesn't mean individuals can't take vacations — but the collective team must always be on the ball. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/matt-macinnis.md]

**Withholding Feedback Is Fundamentally Selfish** — The most selfish thing you can do is withhold feedback that would help someone improve, because you're optimizing for your own comfort. Teams will always optimize for local comfort over company outcomes. As a leader, your job is to preserve the founder's intensity through every concentric circle of management, where each layer risks an order-of-magnitude drop-off in energy. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/matt-macinnis.md]

**Escalation Is a Superpower** — Many teams and organizations avoid escalating issues because it's uncomfortable. But surfacing problems early and escalating them to the right level is one of the highest-leverage things a leader can do. The tendency to quietly solve problems at your level often means they don't get the visibility and resources they need. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/matt-macinnis.md]
