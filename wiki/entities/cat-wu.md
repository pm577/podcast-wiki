# Cat Wu

Cat Wu is Head of Product for Claude Code at Anthropic. She partners with Boris Cherny (engineering lead) to ship product at a pace "unlike anything Lenny has ever seen." She is responsible for the cross-functional execution, process, and go-to-market that turns Claude Code's technical vision into user value.

## Key Views

### The Right Amount of AGI-Pilled
Cat's central insight: "It is very hard to be the right amount of AGI-pilled. It's easy to build for the super AGI model. The hard thing is figuring out for the current model, how do you elicit the maximum capability?" The tension between building for the future vs. shipping value today is the core challenge of AI product management. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-04-23-cat-wu.md]

### Timelines Compressed from 6 Months to 1 Week
Under Cat's leadership, Claude Code timelines have gone from 6 months to 1 month to sometimes 1 week or even 1 day. The key: shipping almost all features in "research preview" with clear branding that this is an early experiment. This reduces commitment, accelerates feedback, and enables fast iteration. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-04-23-cat-wu.md]

### The PM's Job Is Creating the Framework, Not the Roadmap
Cat's philosophy: the PM's most important role is creating a framework that unblocks the team, not writing detailed roadmaps. At Claude Code, this means: (1) Set clear goals and user definitions to remove ambiguity; (2) Create repeatable processes (engineering → docs → marketing → launch in <24 hours); (3) Establish team principles so people can make decisions autonomously without feeling blocked on PM. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-04-23-cat-wu.md]

### Product Taste Is the Emerging Critical PM Skill
"As code becomes much cheaper to write, the thing that becomes more valuable is deciding what to write." Cat argues that the PM role is changing rapidly. The old skills — writing detailed specs, coordinating multi-quarter roadmaps with partner teams — are being replaced by product taste: the ability to quickly decide what's worth building and ruthlessly prioritize. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-04-23-cat-wu.md]

### Remove Every Single Barrier to Shipping
Anthropic's culture of speed isn't just about having great models — it's about intentional process minimization. Every process that exists must justify itself. Engineers can post features in an "evergreen launch" room, and marketing/docs/DevRel turn around the announcement the next day. The goal: "every single person on the team feels empowered to take their idea from just an idea to out in the world in less than a week." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-04-23-cat-wu.md]
