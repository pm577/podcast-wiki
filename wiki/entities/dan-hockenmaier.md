# Dan Hockenmaier

*Head of Strategy and Analytics at Faire. Former Director of Growth at Thumbtack and partner at Reforge. Co-creator of the Race Car Growth Framework and expert in marketplace dynamics.*

## Key Views

1. **A growth model is an analytical representation of how the business grows, built in a spreadsheet**: The act of building the model provides 50% of the value — it forces you to reconcile how all the pieces fit together. A spreadsheet is "very hard to fake"; you can talk about a business conceptually, but actually linking the assumptions forces a deep understanding.

2. **Marketplaces are gardens, SaaS businesses are construction projects**: For SaaS, growth is linear — build features, sell them, retain customers. For marketplaces, you're managing a complex ecosystem where actions have delayed, nonlinear effects. Change one incentive today and you might see chaos two months later. Tread lightly.

3. **Three core building blocks of a SaaS growth model**: Acquisition channels (traffic, conversion rates), retention (activation rates, monthly survival curves), and monetization (fees, subscriptions). For transactional businesses, add transactions per month and AOV. For marketplaces, add supply-side acquisition and the interaction between supply and demand.

4. **Nonlinear dynamics reveal the real growth levers**: Linking acquisition and monetization shows why payback period matters more than LTV:CAC. The speed at which you recoup acquisition costs determines reinvestment velocity. Viral loops create exponential growth when the coefficient is right. These connections are invisible in a linear model.

5. **The growth model is an opportunity assessment tool, not a forecast**: It's intentionally distinct from what finance builds. The output is highly variable because you're stress-testing assumptions. The value is in understanding trade-offs: "If we improve retention by 5% vs. acquisition cost by 10%, which has more impact?" The model answers that.
