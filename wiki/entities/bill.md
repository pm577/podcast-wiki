# Bill Carr

Bill Carr spent 15 years at Amazon, joining just five years after its founding. As VP of Digital Media, he launched Amazon Music, Prime Video, and Amazon Studios. He co-authored *Working Backwards* and now helps growth-stage companies implement Amazon's practices.

## Key Views

### Working Backwards — Start with the Customer, Then Build
The core Amazon practice: start by writing a fictional press release and FAQ for a new product — before any code is written. This forces clarity on what customers actually need. Jeff Bezos's mantra: "If we served customers well, sales, revenue, and free cash flow would follow." Every decision starts with "What's best for the customer?" and works backward from there. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/bill-carr.md]

### Single-Threaded Leaders
Amazon's approach to ownership: each major initiative gets a "single-threaded leader" whose sole focus is that initiative. No splitting attention across multiple projects. This ensures relentless progress and clear accountability. When a new priority emerges, either the leader gives up existing responsibilities or a new leader is hired. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/bill-carr.md]

### Input Metrics vs. Output Metrics
Bill distinguishes between output metrics (revenue, profit, customers — things you can't directly control) and input metrics (activities that drive those outputs). Successful teams obsess over input metrics. If you're hitting your input targets but outputs aren't moving, your theory of the business is wrong. If you're missing inputs, you're not executing. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/bill-carr.md]

### Disagree and Commit
One of Amazon's most powerful leadership principles: once a decision is made, even those who disagreed commit fully. The process is: debate openly and vigorously, escalate to the decision-maker if needed, then once decided, everyone gets behind it 100%. "Disagree and commit" prevents the organizational cancer of passive-aggressive non-support. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/bill-carr.md]

### The Bar Raiser Program
Amazon's hiring innovation: a "Bar Raiser" sits on every interview loop — someone from outside the hiring team who's been trained to evaluate candidates against Amazon's leadership principles. The Bar Raiser has veto power over any hire and doesn't report to the hiring manager. This prevents teams from lowering standards to fill headcount quickly. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/bill-carr.md]
