# Krithika Shankarraman

Krithika Shankarraman was the first marketing hire at both OpenAI and Stripe, led marketing at Retool, and was an early marketer at Dropbox. She currently serves as Executive in Residence at Thrive Capital. She's known for her "anti-playbook" approach to marketing — insisting that real marketing requires deep customer understanding, not copying what other companies do.

## Key Views

**There Is No Playbook — Marketing Requires Deep Customer Understanding**
Shankarraman's central message: "It seems like there's a playbook for everything, there is a framework for everything, but the reality is you have to spend the hours and the time to really understand your customer." She developed a four-step diagnostic approach that starts with understanding the customer's world before developing any marketing strategy. Her "anti-playbook" philosophy argues that copying competitors' tactics is the fastest path to mediocrity because you're solving for their customers, not yours.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/krithika-shankarraman.md]]**

**Vanity Metrics Are Bullshit — Focus on the Customer Experience**
Shankarraman is scathing about traditional marketing metrics: "A lot of marketing metrics tend to be vanity metrics about the number of clicks that you got, number of views, number of impressions. I think those are all bullshit numbers." Instead, she asks: "What is that experience that you want your customers to come away with when they interact with your brand?" In her view, brand is the sum of every interaction a customer has with your company — and measuring those interactions' quality matters far more than counting them.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/krithika-shankarraman.md]]**

**Create "Use Case Epiphany" — The Job of Marketing When Everyone Knows Your Name**
At OpenAI, ChatGPT was already the fastest-growing product in history. So what was marketing's job? "Everyone knew of ChatGPT, but when you clicked one zoom level further, the thing that came up was 'I don't know what to use it for.'" The work of marketing became creating use-case epiphany — helping people realize "I had no idea ChatGPT can do that." This is a critical insight for any product with broad awareness but shallow understanding of its full capabilities.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/krithika-shankarraman.md]]**

**B2B Companies Need Consumer-Grade Marketing Instincts**
Having worked across consumer (Dropbox) and B2B (Stripe, Retool, OpenAI) marketing, Shankarraman believes B2B companies can learn hugely from consumer marketing. B2B buyers are humans first — they respond to emotion, storytelling, and brand just as consumers do. The mistake B2B companies make is being too dry, too technical, and too focused on feature comparison. The most successful B2B brands (Stripe is the exemplar) make complex products feel simple, human, and even fun.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/krithika-shankarraman.md]]**

**The Earliest Marketer's Job Is to Listen — Not to Broadcast**
As the first marketer at both Stripe and OpenAI, Shankarraman's initial priority wasn't campaigns or content — it was listening. She spent months talking to users, understanding their language, their pain points, and how they described the product to each other. This listening phase informed every subsequent marketing decision, from positioning to channel selection to brand voice. "At Stripe, I was the only marketing person for three years. That forced me to be extremely disciplined about what mattered."
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/krithika-shankarraman.md]]**
