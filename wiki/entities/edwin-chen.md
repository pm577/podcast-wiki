# Edwin Chen

*Founder and CEO of Surge AI — the leading AI data company powering training at every frontier AI lab (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini). Surge hit $1B revenue in 4 years with <100 people, completely bootstrapped and profitable from day one.*

## Key Views

1. **A super-small, super-elite team beats a big org**: Edwin believes tech companies could "fire 90% of the people and move faster." Surge built a $1B business with under 100 people by being ruthlessly selective. The best people don't need distraction-heavy management structures — they need autonomy and hard problems.

2. **Quality data for AI isn't about throwing bodies at it**: People don't understand what quality means in the AI data space. It's not about scale of human labor — it's about sophisticated evaluation methodologies, deep domain expertise, and understanding what "good" looks like for specific model behaviors. The wrong approach produces garbage at any scale.

3. **We're optimizing AI for dopamine instead of truth**: Edwin's core critique of frontier AI: instead of building models that cure cancer, solve poverty, or advance human understanding, the industry is optimizing for engagement metrics. We're teaching models to chase the same shallow signals as tabloids. The values embedded in the training data shape what the model becomes.

4. **The perfect model might tell you to stop**: Edwin's anecdote about spending 30 minutes perfecting an email with Claude illustrates a deep point: a model optimizing for your actual productivity would say "Your email is great. Send it. Move on." But current models are trained to be endlessly agreeable and helpful, which is actually counterproductive.

5. **Bootstrap gives you discipline that VC money doesn't**: Surge never raised venture capital and was profitable from day one. This forced them to be efficient, find real product-market fit, and build a business that actually works — not one that burns cash chasing growth metrics. Edwin thinks more founders should question whether they need VC at all.
