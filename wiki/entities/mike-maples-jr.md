# Mike Maples Jr.

Legendary early-stage startup investor and co-founder of Floodgate, one of the pioneers of seed-stage investing. Early bets on Twitter, Lyft, Twitch, Okta, and Rappi. Author of *Pattern Breakers: Why Some Start-Ups Change the Future*. Eight-time Forbes Midas list member.

## Key Views

### 1. Breakthrough Startups Have Three Elements: Inflection + Insight + Founder-Future Fit

Maples analyzed thousands of startups and found breakthrough ideas share three components: **Inflections** (technology or market shifts that enable asymmetric warfare on the present), **Insights** (a "secret" the founder sees that others don't), and **Founder-Future Fit** (the founder is uniquely suited to the future they're trying to create). "Business is never a fair fight. What inflections let the founder do is wage asymmetric warfare on the present."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/mike-maples-jr.md]

### 2. 80% of the Best Returns Came from a Pivot

80% of Maples' biggest returning investments originated from companies that pivoted — Justin.tv became Twitch, the company that became Twitter started as Odeo. The best founders don't follow business plans; they stay "awake to the possibility that secrets are there." If you're living in the future and notice what's missing, your intuition about what to build is far more likely to be right.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/mike-maples-jr.md]

### 3. Startups Never Beat Incumbents by Executing Better

"The way startups win is because it proposes a radically different future, disorients the incumbent, and chaotically moves people to that different future." The rock is the inflection, the slingshot is the insight. "We're looking to create the conditions where we're going to get to play an unfair game by unfair rules that favor us."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/mike-maples-jr.md]

### 4. Founders Should Ignore "Best Practices"

The companies that broke through didn't follow conventional wisdom — no business model canvas, no formal customer development. Meanwhile, founders who "did all the right things" often failed. Maples' research led him to conclude that startup best practices are optimized for incremental progress, not breakthrough innovation. Pattern breakers break the pattern.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/mike-maples-jr.md]

### 5. Use Time Capsules to Understand Startup Origins

Maples keeps original pitch decks from companies like Airbnb (which he passed on) and Pinterest. He studies what they looked like at the time of decision, not after success. "It's easy to misremember how things really happened. Even the founders have a tendency to remember knowing more than they really did at the time." This discipline prevents hindsight bias in evaluating startup potential.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/mike-maples-jr.md]
