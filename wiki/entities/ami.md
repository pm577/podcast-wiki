# Ami Vora

**Role:** Chief Product Officer at Faire; former VP of Product at Meta (WhatsApp, Facebook, Instagram) — over 15 years  
**Known for:** Leading product at massive scale across Meta's family of apps, bringing authenticity and curiosity-driven leadership to product management.

## Key Views

### 1. "Fascinating, tell me more" — the power of curiosity over being right

Boz (Meta CTO) described Ami's signature move: in the most profound disagreement, she responds with genuine curiosity — "Fascinating, you have to tell me more about why you think that." Ami explains this was hard-won: she used to prioritize being right, but learned that sublimating ego and focusing on getting to the best outcome is more important. This posture transforms conflict into learning and makes people feel heard even in disagreement.

> "I really enjoy being right and then it turns out in the working world, that did not serve me so great. The hard part is sublimating your ego and saying it's more important to get to the outcome than to be right." — Ami Vora

### 2. Design for a feeling, not a feature set

Ami's framework for product teams: agree on the *feeling* you want users to experience, then let design and engineering decisions flow from that shared emotional target. Example: "If we all agree the feeling should be like sitting in Dolores Park with friends on a sunny Saturday, people will naturally build something that feels consistent." This creates more coherent products than specifying feature lists.

> "Think about what you want your users to feel when using the product... If we all agree that the feeling should be, I'm sitting in Dolores Park with my friends on a sunny Saturday, then people will just naturally build something that feels more consistent." — Ami Vora

### 3. Follow your "feeling at home" compass for career decisions

Ami rejects the spreadsheet approach to career decisions (weighing title, comp, future prospects). Instead, she advises "putting on the coat of the job" — imagining your actual day-to-day: who do you have lunch with, what problems do you solve, do you feel at home? Trust is the biggest unlock for her best work, and that comes from working with people who feel like friends.

> "The thing that has led me to the places where I do my best work is a feeling of being at home, which is all about trust with the people around me." — Ami Vora

### 4. Honesty about not having a plan is a superpower

Ami acknowledges she's never been a person with a 5-year plan. She used to feel held back by this, but now embraces it: the most important thing is to acknowledge it's true for her and follow what feels right. Her career path — growing through roles at Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, and then Faire — wasn't strategically mapped out but emerged from doing what felt aligned and authentic.

### 5. Building trust by being genuinely authentic at scale

Throughout her career at Meta, Ami built trust across massive organizations by being consistently authentic — sharing what she didn't know, admitting mistakes, and approaching every interaction with genuine curiosity. She demonstrates that vulnerability and authenticity scale, and that being known for intellectual honesty creates a foundation for high-trust, high-performance teams.
