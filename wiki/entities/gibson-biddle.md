# Gibson Biddle

Gibson Biddle is a former VP of Product at Netflix and Chegg who now spends his time teaching product strategy to leaders worldwide. He has directly taught more PMs than almost anyone else in the industry. Before Netflix, he worked at Electronic Arts and co-founded a joint venture between EA and Disney. He wrote one of the most popular Medium posts on product strategy ever published.

## Key Views

### Product Strategy Starts With a Clear Framework

Gibson is known for creating memorable, clear product strategy and prioritization frameworks. His approach demystifies the vagueness that surrounds strategy: define your target customer, the problem you solve, your competitive advantage, and your key metrics. Then and only then can you prioritize effectively. The framework is designed to be taught and applied — not kept as abstract theory.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gibson-biddle.md]

### The Power of Ending Direct Deposit

Gibson describes his career transition to teaching as "stopping direct deposit" — leaving a steady VP paycheck to pursue what he truly loves. After Netflix and Chegg, he realized his heart was in teaching product leaders, not in building one more product. He now does talks, workshops, and writing globally. His advice to others: identify the "coda" of your career — the thing you keep coming back to — and find a way to do it full-time.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gibson-biddle.md]

### Real-World Case Studies Beat Abstract Theory

Rather than teaching product strategy in the abstract, Gibson uses deep case studies from his time at Netflix. He walks through how they decided what to prioritize, how they balanced growth vs. engagement, and how they navigated the streaming transition. These real examples make the framework concrete and give PMs mental models they can apply to their own situations.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gibson-biddle.md]

### Failed Startups Are Part of the Journey

Gibson openly discusses his failed startups as essential to his development. Before Netflix, he had ventures that didn't work out. He normalizes failure as part of the learning process — "It's important to let folks know I had some failed startups." The key is extracting lessons from each failure and applying them to the next opportunity, rather than letting failure define you.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gibson-biddle.md]

### Great Product Leaders Are Teachers

Gibson's career arc — from marketing to product to teaching — reveals his core philosophy: the best product leaders are teachers at heart. Whether you're a VP of Product or a PM, your job is to help your team understand the customer, the strategy, and the trade-offs. Teaching is not a separate career; it's the essence of product leadership. He encourages PMs to cultivate their teaching skills early.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gibson-biddle.md]

## Notable Quotes
