# Max Levchin

One of the great founders and technologists of our time. Founder and CEO of Affirm ($18.7BN market cap in the buy-now-pay-later space); co-founder of PayPal (part of the original "PayPal Mafia"); co-founder and Chairman of Glow (data-driven fertility company). Highly successful angel investor with a portfolio including Yelp, Pinterest, and Evernote.

## Key Views

### 1. Grading Talent by Letter (A or B) Is Total BS

Levchin argues forcefully against the Silicon Valley practice of grading people as "A players" or "B players." He believes this framework is reductive and harmful — it ignores context, growth trajectory, and the specific needs of the organization at a given time. Instead, he advocates for hiring based on demonstrated curiosity, adaptability, and the ability to learn in ambiguity.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2025-02-05-20vc-affirm-max-levchin-on-wh.md]

### 2. How to Create a Culture of Post-Mortems and Writing

Levchin shares how Affirm built a culture where post-mortems are celebrated, not feared, and where writing is the primary mode of communication. He believes that studying failure — not success — is the most reliable way to improve, because success stories are often survivorship-biased while failures contain actionable lessons.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2025-02-05-20vc-affirm-max-levchin-on-wh.md]

### 3. Self-Awareness Is the Most Important Trait for Entrepreneurs

Levchin argues that self-awareness is the single most important trait for a great CEO. The ability to recognize your own blind spots, biases, and limitations determines how well you can compensate for them — either through personal development or by building a team that fills your gaps. He believes that many founders fail not because of market conditions but because they lack the self-awareness to know what they don't know.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2017-07-14-20vc-max-levchin-on-what-make.md]

### 4. "When There Is Doubt, There Is No Doubt"

Levchin shares his decision-making framework: when you feel doubt about a decision, that is itself the answer. True conviction is quiet and clear; doubt signals that you have not yet done the work to reach conviction or that the path is genuinely wrong. This principle guided his decisions at PayPal and Affirm alike.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2017-07-14-20vc-max-levchin-on-what-make.md]

### 5. Biggest Surprises Scaling to $18.7BN

Levchin discusses the unexpected challenges of scaling Affirm: the complexity of credit risk at massive scale, the regulatory navigation required in consumer finance, and the difficulty of maintaining startup culture through hypergrowth. He emphasizes that the hardest part of scaling is not technology but organizational design — how to structure teams that remain agile as the company grows.
