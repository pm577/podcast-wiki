# Todd Jackson

Todd Jackson is a Partner at First Round Capital. Before VC, he was product lead for Gmail, PM for Facebook's Newsfeed/photos/groups, Director of PM at Twitter, and VP of Product & Design at Dropbox. He's developed a comprehensive framework for finding product-market fit based on First Round's portfolio data.

## Key Views

**Product-market fit has four distinct levels: Nascent, Developing, Strong, and Extreme.** Todd's framework, based on First Round Capital's data across hundreds of portfolio companies, defines PMF as a progression rather than a binary state. Most importantly, roughly 60% of startups never get past Level 2 (Developing), making it the critical bottleneck that founders need to recognize and address.

**The four Ps to change when stuck: Persona, Problem, Promise, and Product.** When a startup is struggling to find product-market fit, Todd advises changing at least one of these four elements. He cites Lattice as an example — they kept the same persona but changed the problem, promise, and product. Vanta changed all four. The framework gives founders a systematic way to think about pivoting rather than relying on intuition alone.

**PMF is about three dimensions: Demand, Satisfaction, and Efficiency.** Todd's research identified consistent patterns across successful startups. But the crucial insight is that you don't pursue all three simultaneously from the beginning. Early-stage startups should focus on proving demand first, then satisfaction, then efficiency — in that order.

**PMF is underexplored and underexplained despite being the most important thing a startup does.** Todd points out that there's abundant tactical content on hiring, board meetings, and fundraising, but surprisingly little specific, actionable content on finding product-market fit. Most advice is vague ("you'll know it when you see it"), which is why Rahul Vohra's Superhuman PMF article (published on First Round Review) was so popular — it brought science to an area people thought was purely art.

**Treat PMF as a science, not just an art.** Todd's framework aims to demystify product-market fit by providing specific metrics, stages, and actions at each level. His three-month program for founders is designed to give them a structured, repeatable process rather than relying on intuition or luck. The key is knowing where you are on the PMF spectrum and having clear next steps for each level.
