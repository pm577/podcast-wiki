# Y Combinator (YC)

The world's most influential startup accelerator, founded by Paul Graham, Jessica Livingston, Robert Morris, and Trevor Blackwell. Known for standardizing early-stage funding with the SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity) and for backing companies including Airbnb, Stripe, DoorDash, and Coinbase.

## Key Innovations

### The SAFE

A two-page legal document published online for free, standardizing seed-stage startup funding. Before the SAFE, venture capital was fragmented — firms negotiating their own deals. The SAFE became the de facto standard for seed-stage funding, dramatically reducing transaction costs and friction. YC enforced it, becoming the institution that made it work.

### The AI-Native Thesis

YC is now teaching founders to build AI-native companies: closed-loop systems with agents embedded in every decision. Key examples: Salient (voice agents for loan servicing), Happy Robot (freight forwarding agents), Reductem (document processing).

### Unprecedented Growth

Historically only ~1% of YC companies grew 10% week-over-week. Now companies 3x in 3 months on average — never happened before. 6-person teams hitting $10M revenue.
