# Nicolas Dessaigne

Co-Founder and CEO of Algolia, the most reliable platform for building search into business applications. Algolia participated in Y Combinator's Winter 2014 batch and raised over $180M from Accel Partners, Point Nine Capital, and Storm Ventures. Dessaigne led Algolia from a pivot (originally an offline mobile search engine) to becoming one of the hottest SaaS companies serving enterprise customers worldwide.

## Key Views

### 1. Fundraising Is Also Business Development

Dessaigne argues that fundraising should not be viewed in isolation — it is a form of business development. The process of meeting investors, refining the pitch, and building relationships creates strategic value for the company beyond just the capital raised. He advocates treating every investor meeting as an opportunity to strengthen the company's position.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2016-04-22-20vc-fundraising-is-also-busi.md]

### 2. Series B Is the Hardest Raise — and Cash Should Be the Only Limit Post-Series B

Dessaigne shares his experience that Series B is genuinely the hardest round to raise, sitting between the narrative-driven seed/Series A and the metrics-driven growth stage. He argues that post-Series B, the only limiting factor for a company should be cash — not ambition, team, or market. He also discusses the strategic decision of when to move from Europe to the US to access deeper capital markets.
