# Miles Clements

Partner @ Accel, helping lead their $4BN growth fund. Led or invested in Atlassian, Cursor, Linear, and more.

## Key Views

### 1. Where is True Alpha in a World of AI

In an AI-driven world, the sources of alpha (excess returns) are shifting. Traditional pattern-matching (investing in founders who look like past winners) breaks down when the technology itself is reshaping every market. True alpha now comes from deep domain understanding of how AI changes competitive dynamics, not from generic founder pattern recognition.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2026-03-09-20vc-inside-accels-4bn-grow.md]

### 2. Why "Cursor is Dead" is Total BS

Despite predictions that Cursor (the AI coding IDE) would be killed by larger competitors like GitHub Copilot, Clements argues the narrative is wrong. Cursor built genuine product differentiation and a loyal user base. The company's growth and user engagement metrics contradicted the death predictions.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2026-03-09-20vc-inside-accels-4bn-grow.md]

### 3. What Missing Rippling and ElevenLabs Taught Us

Passing on Rippling and ElevenLabs were painful misses that taught Accel's growth team important lessons about the speed of AI adoption and the willingness of customers to adopt new platforms. The lesson: in AI, adoption curves are steeper than traditional SaaS, so hesitation costs more.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2026-03-09-20vc-inside-accels-4bn-grow.md]

### 4. Are $2BN-$10BN IPOs Dead

The IPO market has bifurcated — companies either go big ($10BN+) or stay private longer. The middle band ($2BN-$10BN) has less liquidity and fewer institutional buyers. This affects how growth investors think about entry price and exit strategy.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2026-03-09-20vc-inside-accels-4bn-grow.md]
