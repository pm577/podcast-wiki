# Lane Shackleton

Lane Shackleton appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss what sets great teams apart — product culture, two-way writeups, and learning by making.

## Key Views

**Oh Shit Moments as a Growth Metric** — Instead of asking "Are you growing?", ask "How many oh shit moments have you had?" where you felt underqualified or stretched — those are the moments that build a new foundation. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lane-shackleton.md]

**Systems, Not Goals** — Being obsessed with the system (e.g., a default-on cadence of talking to customers weekly) beats setting a goal like "talk to 10 customers this quarter," because goals come and go but systems compound into instincts. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lane-shackleton.md]

**Cathedrals, Not Bricks** — Everyone needs to see a different facet of the cathedral; a great PM doesn't just write a vision doc but pairs it with metrics, mocks, and billboards so every team member can connect their work to the broader arc. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lane-shackleton.md]

**Learn by Making, Not Talking** — When debating skippable ads at YouTube, instead of endless discussion they tested extremes (tiny skip button vs. giant skip button) within weeks — stop pontificating and go build something to learn. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lane-shackleton.md]

**Two-Way Writeups Beat One-Way Docs** — Traditional one-way writeups have problems (watching for read-avatars, mega comment threads on titles, missing quiet dissent); two-way writeups fix this with done-reading buttons, a Dory table for questions, and a sentiment/pulse column that surfaces silent disagreement. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lane-shackleton.md]
