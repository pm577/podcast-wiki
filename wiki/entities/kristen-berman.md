# Kristen Berman

Kristen Berman appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss using behavioral science to improve your product — budgeting, friction, and the 'right for wrong' principle.

## Key Views

**Budgeting Doesn't Change Spending** — A 10,000-person experiment found that adding budgeting features to a fintech app produced zero change in average spend or spend variability, because the behavioral diagnosis reveals it puts too many steps on the user. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/kristen-berman.md]

**Adding Friction Can Boost Conversion** — Asking engaging questions during signup (e.g., "what kind of apartment do you want?") makes users actively think about benefits and increased conversion by up to 133% for Trunk Club, contradicting the dogma that less friction is always better. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/kristen-berman.md]

**TikTok Cut Misinformation Shares 24% by Slowing People Down** — A misinformation label plus an "are you sure?" popup on the share button added just enough friction to decrease shares, proving that making a bad behavior harder works because TikTok is a "hot state" platform. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/kristen-berman.md]

**'Right for Wrong' Drives Behavior** — People do the right thing for the wrong immediate reason (pizza at the poll line, Peloton shout-outs, completion bias on checkboxes), so products should give users a proximate reward even when the long-term benefit is obvious. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/kristen-berman.md]

**Incentives Can Turn You Into a Predatory Lender** — Working for LendingClub under a contract that paid only if she hit a 5-point conversion bump, Berman says she "became a predatory lender" suggesting things the legal team had to block, proving that what you measure drives what you build. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/kristen-berman.md]
