# Eric Simons

Eric Simons is the founder and CEO of StackBlitz, the company behind Bolt — the #1 web-based AI coding agent and one of the fastest-growing products in history. After nearly shutting down after seven years of building WebContainer technology, Bolt launched and went from zero to $20M ARR in two months. The team of 15-20 people now serves over 3 million registered users.

## Key Views

### Overnight Success Seven Years in the Making

Bolt's explosive growth looked instant but was built on seven years of foundational technology. StackBlitz had been building WebContainer — an operating system that runs inside a browser — long before the AI boom. When AI coding tools emerged, their infrastructure was perfectly positioned. Eric's lesson: "It was kind of like, Bolt's this overnight success, seven years in the making." The tech-first approach, often warned against, paid off because the bet was on a foundational capability.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/eric-simons.md]

### Browser-Native Performance Is the Key Moat

Bolt's core advantage is speed and reliability: it runs a full OS inside the user's browser using local CPU, not cloud servers. Competitors spin up cloud environments that take minutes to boot and can get stuck. Bolt works instantly, even on the free tier. This browser-native approach, powered by WebContainer, lets them offer a permissive free tier with no cloud costs — a structural cost advantage.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/eric-simons.md]

### AI Makes Everyone an App Builder

The majority of Bolt's users are not developers — they're PMs, designers, and entrepreneurs who've always been great at product but couldn't code. Eric sees this as the fundamental shift: the barrier between idea and software has collapsed. "These are people that have always been great at building products, but previously the only way that they could get their ideas into coded software was through a developer's fingertips. Now they can deal with their own, through prompting."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/eric-simons.md]

### Conviction Over Consensus

During the near-death period, Eric had to make judgment calls that weren't the consensus view. "I think that's the hard thing about being an entrepreneur. There are periods of time where you have to make judgment calls that are not going to the consensus view. You got to have confidence in your convictions on how to best play the hand." This conviction — continuing to invest in WebContainer when the company was running out of money — ultimately saved the company.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/eric-simons.md]

### The New Normal: Unprecedented Growth Rates

Eric believes the growth rates of Bolt, Cursor, and other AI-native products may represent a new normal. "There's going to be more things like this. The free market is filled with rational actors. People are coming to these tools because it is solving problems, they're able to do way more for way less cost." The AI productivity wedge is so large that adoption curves that would have taken years are compressing into months.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/eric-simons.md]
