# Dhanji R. Prasanna

Dhanji R. Prasanna is the Chief Technology Officer at Block (formerly Square), where he oversees a team of over 3,500 engineers. Under his leadership, Block has become one of the most AI-native large enterprises in the world. He previously worked on Google Wave and Google Plus.

## Key Views

### AI-Native Enterprise Culture Requires Top-Down Permission

Dhanji argues the most important thing a leader can do to drive AI adoption is give explicit permission. At Block, he made it clear that using AI tools was not just allowed but expected. The cultural shift required leaders to model usage — if executives don't use AI, no one will. The boring but crucial change was removing friction: making tools available, providing training, and celebrating AI wins.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/dhanji-r-prasanna.md]

### Goose: The Open-Source Agent Saving 8-10 Hours Per Week

Block built and open-sourced Goose, an internal AI agent that employees use autonomously. Engineers report 8-10 hours saved per week, and Dhanji emphasizes this is the "worst it will ever be" — the value improves daily. Most remarkably, Goose sometimes anticipates what engineers want: it sees a Slack conversation about a feature idea and opens a PR for it hours later. Non-technical employees show the biggest productivity gains.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/dhanji-r-prasanna.md]

### Code Quality Does Not Equal Product Success

A counterintuitive lesson from Dhanji's career: "A lot of engineers think that code quality is important to building a successful product. The two have nothing to do with each other." He's seen beautiful codebases produce dead products and messy codebases produce market-dominating ones. Speed of iteration and user understanding matter far more than code craftsmanship.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/dhanji-r-prasanna.md]

### LLMs Should Never Be Idle

Dhanji believes LLMs should run 24/7 — not just during work hours. He envisions a world where AI agents work overnight and on weekends, building features in anticipation of what humans will want. The model is always-on autonomous agents vs. today's ask-and-respond paradigm. This shifts engineering from reactive task-execution to proactive value creation.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/dhanji-r-prasanna.md]

### The Value of AI Compounds Daily

Dhanji's key framing: "This is the worst it will ever be." AI tooling improves so fast that companies need to ride the wave continuously rather than making one-time investments. The teams that benefit most are not necessarily the most technical — they're the ones that embrace the tools to optimize their specific workflows. The winning approach is to start imperfectly and iterate as the technology improves.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/dhanji-r-prasanna.md]

## Notable Quotes

> "All these LLMs are sitting idle overnight and on weekends, while humans aren't there. There's no need for that. They should be working all the time. They should be trying to build in anticipation of what we want."

> "The value is changing every day, so you need to ride that wave along with it."
