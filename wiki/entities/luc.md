# Luc Levesque

Luc Levesque is the Chief Growth Officer at Shopify. Previously, he was recruited personally by Mark Zuckerberg to help grow Facebook Messenger, Instagram, and WhatsApp. Before that, he was VP of Growth and GM at Tripadvisor. He has also served as a growth advisor to Twitter, Pinterest, Patreon, Thumbtack, and Canva. He is known for his hiring playbook, his "signs of excellence" framework, and his relentless focus on impact.

## Key Views

### Impact Is the Only Metric That Matters

Luc's defining principle, learned from Mark Zuckerberg at Facebook: "What are the outcomes? What is the impact you're having?" Facebook's culture revolves entirely around this question — not how hard you worked or what activities you did. "I don't care how hard you've worked, I don't care what you're working on, what are the outcomes?" At his first executive review, three months in, Zuck's first question was "When are we going to start seeing results?" This laser focus on impact is what makes companies breakthrough.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/luc-levesque.md]

### The 10X Growth Advisor

"The right growth advisor can have literally company-changing impact. The right person at the right time can literally say a sentence that changes the trajectory of your company." Luc argues that just as there are 10X engineers, there are 10X growth people. He advises founders to wait until they have product-market fit before engaging growth advisors, because growing a product without PMF does more damage than good — users who try a bad product likely won't come back.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/luc-levesque.md]

### The Hiring Playbook: Signs of Excellence

Luc's hiring framework has three chapters: finding, assessing, and closing talent. Key insight: "The best predictor of future performance is past performance." He looks for "signs of excellence" — multiple instances of doing amazing things (professional or not). One powerful signal: when someone's former boss leaves the company and comes back to poach them. "They would never do that unless this person was really, really good." In closing, he recommends involving the candidate's family/spouse and being absolutely relentless (his own recruitment took 7 months).^[raw/transcripts/lenny/luc-levesque.md]

### Relentless Hiring: Involve the Family

From his own experience being recruited by Zuck: "Involving somebody's spouse and family is a really good idea because it's a very personal decision to change company. It involves more than just that person you're talking to, it involves the whole family." Zuck flew Luc and his wife Andrea down, had dinner with them and Priscilla, and had Andrea meet with Facebook executives. Toby Lutke did the same when recruiting Luc to Shopify.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/luc-levesque.md]

### Don't Focus on Growth Until You Have PMF

Luc's most repeated warning: don't hire a growth advisor or growth team until you have product-market fit. "If you start growing a product that doesn't have product-market fit, you're actually doing more damage than good because you have a product that is now being exposed through growth levers that is giving a bad experience." Wait until you have strong retention or a clear growth loop, then unleash growth.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/luc-levesque.md]
