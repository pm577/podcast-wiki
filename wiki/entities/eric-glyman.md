# Eric Glyman

Co-Founder and CEO of Paribus (YC S15), an app that scans your inbox for receipts and automatically saves you money when items you bought drop in price. Later co-founded Ramp, the corporate card and spend management platform now valued at over $40BN. Y Combinator alumnus.

## Key Views

### 1. Finding the "A-Ha" Consumer Fintech Idea

Eric identified a universal consumer pain point: stores guarantee lowest prices but rarely follow through unless you work for it. Paribus automated this process — scanning email receipts and automatically claiming price drop refunds. The insight was that consumers were leaving money on the table not because they didn't care, but because the manual process was too cumbersome.^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2015-10-02-ff-015-y-combinator-week-eri.md]

### 2. Doing Things Differently in YC

Eric's approach during Y Combinator (S15) differed from many peers in how he prioritized product development and customer acquisition. The Paribus experience laid the foundation for his later, much larger success with Ramp — showing how a YC batch company can serve as a proving ground for a founder's capabilities.^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2015-10-02-ff-015-y-combinator-week-eri.md]
