# David Placek

*Founder of Lexicon Branding, which pioneered the field of brand naming. Invented names including Pentium, BlackBerry, Swiffer, Sonos, Impossible Burger, Azure, Vercel, Windsurf, and CapCut. Over 4,000 naming projects completed.*

## Key Views

1. **You won't know a great name when you see it**: Clients almost always believe they'll recognize the perfect name on sight, but it almost never happens. Humans seek comfort in the familiar, but great names are uncomfortable — they break new ground. Harry Potter was rejected 16 times. The best names feel wrong at first because they don't match past patterns.

2. **A name should create asymmetric and cumulative advantage**: Your brand name will be used more often and for longer than anything else — design, messaging, and products will all change, but the name stays. A distinctive name creates cumulative advantage (stronger bonds over time) and asymmetric advantage (starting ahead before you even launch). Descriptive names like "Cloud Pro" miss both.

3. **"There is no power in comfort" — seek polarization and tension**: Placek looks for polarization within the team when presenting names. If everyone is comfortable, the name isn't bold enough. Sonos was initially rejected for being "not entertainment-like enough." Azure was called "a dumb idea." Great names create tension because they force people to think differently about what the company is.

4. **Focus on how you want to behave, not your positioning**: Most naming projects start with "tell us about your positioning, mission, and values." Placek starts with "talk about how you behave now and how you want to behave in the future." The behavioral lens produces more imaginative, distinctive names than the positioning lens.

5. **The best names are balanced — strong signal with smooth flow**: Noise in a name (the "Z" in Azure, the hard consonants in Pentium) creates signal in a crowded marketplace. But it must be balanced with smooth, flowing elements. Sonos works because it's a palindrome — flip it upside down and it means the same thing. Each letter has energy and feeling that contributes to the whole.
