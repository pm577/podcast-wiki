# Anton Osika

**Role:** Co-founder and CEO of Lovable — an AI tool that turns descriptions into working software products  
**Known for:** Building the fastest-growing startup in Europe ($10M ARR in 60 days with 15 people), pioneering AI-native software creation.

## Key Views

### 1. Lovable is your personal AI software engineer — the last piece of software

Anton's framing: Lovable is "the last piece of software" because it removes the bottleneck of finding and coordinating engineering talent. Users describe an idea and get a fully working product. The value prop is aimed at the millions of people who have ideas but can't find developers to build them. This democratization of software creation fundamentally changes who can be a builder.

> "Lovable is your personal AI software engineer. You describe an idea and then you get a fully working product. The reason is to enable those who have had such a hard time finding people who are good at creating software." — Anton Osika

### 2. $10M ARR in 60 days with 15 people — the new speed of AI-native startups

Lovable's growth trajectory is unprecedented: $4M ARR in 4 weeks, $10M in 2 months, with just 15 people. This compresses what used to take years into weeks. The key insight: AI-native companies can grow faster with fewer people because the AI is the product and the engine of growth simultaneously.

### 3. The bottleneck shifts from engineering to imagination

Echoing Amjad Masad's insight, Anton sees a world where the constraint is no longer "can we build it?" but "can we imagine it?" and "is it valuable?" Product thinking, user empathy, and vision become the scarce resources. Technical execution becomes a commodity that AI provides.

### 4. Small teams, AI leverage — the new unit of value creation

Lovable's 15-person team running a $10M+ ARR business demonstrates a new model: extreme AI leverage allows tiny teams to create massive value. This has profound implications for how startups are founded, funded, and built. The VC model of "hire fast, burn cash" may need to be rethought.

### 5. Product-first, not engineering-first

Despite being an AI coding tool, Anton emphasizes that Lovable's success comes from obsessive product focus — understanding who the user is, what they need, and what experience delights them. The AI is the mechanism, but product thinking is what guides it. This is a crucial distinction for anyone building in the AI era.
