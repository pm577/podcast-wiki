# Eoghan McCabe

Eoghan McCabe is the founder and CEO of Intercom, a customer service platform that successfully pivoted to become an AI-first company with its agent product, Fin. After stepping away due to health issues and a period of stagnation, Eoghan returned to lead a dramatic transformation that included a 40% employee turnover and the launch of what is now a $100M+ ARR AI business.

## Key Views

### AI Transformation Requires "Wartime" Urgency

When ChatGPT launched, Eoghan declared Intercom needed to become a "wartime company." He wrote new values designed to be "a sharp knife to cut out the parts of the company that just wouldn't be effective." The key insight: incremental change doesn't work when disruption is existential. He believes the way greatness is created is through a CEO willing to make brave hard decisions and own the results — what he calls "hardcore founder mode."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/eoghan-mccabe.md]

### Outcome-Based Pricing Aligns With Customer Value

Intercom's Fin charges 99 cents per resolved ticket — pure outcome-based pricing. This was a radical shift from Intercom's famously complex and hated pricing. Eoghan realized that fair, simple pricing builds trust and loyalty. They consciously gave away $50M in ARR by simplifying pricing. The lesson: charge for value delivered, not costs incurred. "The cost is our problem," he says, trusting that AI unit economics will improve over time.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/eoghan-mccabe.md]

### AI Can Be More "Personal" Than Humans

Eoghan wrestled with the irony that Intercom's mission was "make internet business personal" — yet they were building AI bots. His resolution: AI that provides instant, consistent, knowledgeable, and friendly service 24/7 is more personal than making customers wait days for a canned human response. He draws a parallel to Waymo — fewer crashes, no hygiene issues, no wrong turns. Sometimes AI is simply superior at delivering what we call "personal" service.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/eoghan-mccabe.md]

### Founders Often Betray Their Intuition Over Time

Eoghan is honest about the compromises he made as CEO — following "best practices," placating employees, and bringing investors along at the expense of his intuition. He believes most founders of once-great tech companies privately feel they've betrayed themselves in small ways over the years. Returning to Intercom meant being "very authoritarian, top-down, aggressive founder-first" — and he found it deeply cathartic.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/eoghan-mccabe.md]

### Desperation + Speed = Strategic Advantage

Intercom had a working Fin prototype just six weeks after GPT-3.5 launched. Eoghan credits their speed partly to desperation — the company was heading to zero net new ARR, so they had nothing to lose. He has empathy for companies that are doing well and struggle to pivot: "We were in a really tough spot, so we had no choice." The combination of urgency, a 30,000-customer base, and existing AI talent created a perfect launch pad.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/eoghan-mccabe.md]

## Notable Quotes
