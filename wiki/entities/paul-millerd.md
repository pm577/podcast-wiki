# Paul Millerd

Paul Millerd appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss paul millerd, author of the pathless path, on redefining success, money, belonging, and leaving the default path.

## Key Views

**The Default Path is an Unconscious Script** — The default path is the story in your head about what you 'should' do (go to college, get a good job, buy a house, get married). There is nothing inherently wrong with it, but most people never consciously opt into it — they just accept it. The real work is to 'fire the manager in your head' and examine the implicit contracts you have created about how and when you are supposed to work. Opting out often triggers insecurities in others because your very existence outside the frame challenges their conception of how the world should work. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/paul-millerd.md]

**You Can Design Around Liking Work** — The hidden assumption most people carry for the first 30+ years of their lives is that work sucks and you have to tolerate it, trading your time for money. Paul's big revelation was that you can design your life around actually enjoying the work itself — protecting what you do and don't do, saying no to traditional publishing deals that feel wrong, and treating the creative process as its own reward. 'Oh, you can design around liking work' was a genuinely paradigm-shifting insight for him. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/paul-millerd.md]

**A Sabbatical is a Near-Guaranteed Catalyst** — A three-month sabbatical out of roughly 500 months of adult working life has a ~99.9% approval rating — almost everyone who takes one gets something valuable from it. It takes six to eight weeks just to unwind from the default path, and companies are far more open to granting sabbaticals than people assume. Even a smaller experiment works: take three hours during a workday, sneak out, do something from your childhood (play basketball, paint, play an instrument), and pay attention to what emotions surface about your relationship with work. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/paul-millerd.md]

**Follow Energy, Not Plans** — The most reliable discovery tool on the pathless path is to track what energizes you versus what drains you after every activity. After calls, writing sessions, or meetings, ask: did that give me energy or sap it? Lenny discovered writing energized him while advising calls drained him. Paul discovered podcast conversations energize him and built his life around that signal. This energy-tracking practice is more trustworthy than strategic planning for finding the work you want to keep doing. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/paul-millerd.md]

**Coming Alive Over Getting Ahead** — Paul's core mantra. He rejected a traditional book deal from Penguin because the people talked down to him and told him he would probably fail — choosing intrinsic aliveness over extrinsic prestige. The pathless path is ultimately about feeling alive and connected to what you are doing rather than chasing achievement, status, or money as primary drivers. The extrinsic outcomes don't deliver lasting satisfaction, but extended states of feeling alive and connected to your work is everything. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/paul-millerd.md]
