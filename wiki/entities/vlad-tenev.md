# Vlad Tenev

Co-Founder and CEO of Robinhood, the commission-free stock trading and investing app. Under Tenev's leadership, Robinhood went from a startup to one of the defining stories on Wall Street of the last decade. In the 18 months between episodes, Robinhood increased net revenue by 58% to nearly $3B, turning a $500M loss into a $1.1B profit. Market cap surged from ~$20BN to north of $80BN. Robinhood has nine lines of business generating over $100M in revenue each. Previously founded two finance companies in NYC.

## Key Views

### 1. Founder Mode and Surviving the GameStop Saga

Tenev discusses "founder mode" — the need for founders to stay deeply engaged in product and strategy even as the company scales. He shares the single hardest element of the GameStop saga: the pressure of public scrutiny, regulatory inquiries, and the existential risk of losing access to clearinghouse capital. His key lesson: transparency with users and regulators, even when painful, is the only sustainable strategy for surviving a scandal.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2024-11-01-20vc-robinhoods-vlad-tenev-o.md]

### 2. Tokenization Is the Biggest Innovation in Finance

Tenev argues that tokenization — putting real-world assets like SpaceX and OpenAI shares on blockchain rails — is the biggest innovation in finance since the ETF. He explains why crypto will become Robinhood's biggest revenue line, and why stablecoins will ultimately replace traditional banking rails for settlement and payments.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2025-07-14-20vc-vlad-tenev-on-robinhood.md]

### 3. Building Nine $100M+ Revenue Lines

Tenev describes how Robinhood systematically built nine distinct revenue lines each exceeding $100M annually. The strategy: identify adjacent financial services where Robinhood's existing user base and platform can be leveraged with zero marginal distribution cost — from options trading and crypto to cash management and IPO access.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2025-07-14-20vc-vlad-tenev-on-robinhood.md]

### 4. Lessons from Raising $5BN

Tenev shares lessons from Robinhood's extraordinary capital-raising journey — over $5BN raised from Sequoia, a16z, DST, Ribbit, and Index. He emphasizes that raising large amounts of capital creates its own set of challenges: managing multiple investor constituencies, maintaining founder control, and the psychological weight of having so many stakeholders watching every move.
