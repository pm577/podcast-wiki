# Daniel Lereya

*Chief Product and Technology Officer at monday.com. Joined at ~40 employees and helped scale through IPO. Led the platform transformation that accelerated feature development from 4 months to 1 day per column type.*

## Key Views

1. **Your competition's speed is a gift, not a threat**: When a competitor shipped 30 new column types (each taking monday.com 4 months), Daniel saw it as "the biggest favor they could ask for." Instead of making excuses, they set an ambitious goal of 25 columns in one month. After a hackathon and infrastructure rethink, they achieved it. Competitive pressure exposes the limits of your current thinking.

2. **Ambitious goals force smarter work, not harder work**: If you set a goal to reduce from 4 months to 3 months, people just work longer hours. But a goal of 1 day forces people to rethink architecture, process, and approach entirely. monday.com redesigned their column infrastructure, defined shared capabilities, and built a platform. True speed is about doing things right, not skipping steps.

3. **Impact orientation transforms PMs from feature-builders to problem-solvers**: A great PM is relentless until they validate that impact is in place. Sometimes the biggest impact isn't a new feature — it's making current value more accessible, improving GTM motion, or teaching customers how to use what already exists. The team succeeds or fails together based on customer value delivered.

4. **Radical transparency creates partnership, not demoralization**: Before going public, monday.com shared every bit of information with employees. Instead of demoralizing people, it gave them a sense of deep partnership. They wanted "everyone's brains in the challenge, not just one centralized brain and a lot of working hands."

5. **Hackathons can compress months of work into days**: The breakthrough on columns came from a hackathon where each developer took one column and implemented it in one day. The key was building shared infrastructure first (filtering, sorting, Excel export capabilities) so each new column only required the unique product logic. Infrastructure investment enables exponential delivery speed.
