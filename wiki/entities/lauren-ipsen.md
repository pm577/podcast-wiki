# Lauren Ipsen

Lauren Ipsen appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss lessons from one of the world's top executive recruiters.

## Key Views

**Don't Chase Logos, Chase Fit** — Founders get distracted by big-name executives from Google/YouTube, but those people are often far from the tactical work. The right hire is someone close to the work who can operate as both PM and leader for where the company is today, not the most famous name in the market. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lauren-ipsen.md]

**'Best Talent' Is Not the Same as 'Best for This Role Today'** — Early in her career she chased "whales of executives" to prove herself, but that's not who actually moves the needle. The critical question is who is best for this specific role at this specific time — those are two very different things. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lauren-ipsen.md]

**Always Keep a Pulse on the Market, Even When You're Not Hiring** — Regardless of whether you're searching, both candidates and companies should always know what "good looks like" — which companies are thriving and which individuals are building great things — so you're never caught flat-footed. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lauren-ipsen.md]

**Play the Long Game: Court Candidates for Months Before They Join** — Her VP of engineering placement involved a seven-month courtship where the candidate was brought in for advising and collaborative conversations, building trust outside a formal interview process until "magic can happen." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lauren-ipsen.md]

**Transactional Recruiting Destroys Trust Permanently** — Recruiters who forget candidates are human beings, ignore what they've said (e.g., pushing crypto roles after being told "never crypto"), and treat them as numbers lose trust that can never be regained. Building relationship-first is the only sustainable approach. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lauren-ipsen.md]
