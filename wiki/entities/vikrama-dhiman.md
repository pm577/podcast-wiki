# Vikrama Dhiman

Vikrama Dhiman appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss head of product at gojek (overseeing product management, design, program management, and research across indonesia, singapore, and india), on the three w's pm skill framework, career growth, and the operating model that unlocks senior levels.

## Key Views

**The Three W's Framework for PM Career Growth** — Vikrama's framework has three pillars: what you produce (outputs to outcomes to direction), what you bring to the table (artifacts demonstrating craft across data, design, technology, and strategy), and what's your operating model (communication, collaboration, and community skills). Strong PMs excel at two of the three; those who rise rapidly perform well on all three axes simultaneously. The operating model is the highest-leverage lever for moving from mid-senior to senior levels. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/vikrama-dhiman.md]

**Focus on Outputs Early in Your Career, and Never Abandon Them** — Early PMs should prioritize tangible, shippable outputs (PRDs, briefs, analyses, experiments) over abstract strategy or impact. Even at senior levels, you must retain your IC roots and continue producing artifacts. Vikrama warns that many PMs prematurely obsess over outcomes and strategy, skipping the foundational craft of output. Volunteering to unblock leaders by drafting documents builds credibility and accelerates learning. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/vikrama-dhiman.md]

**'What You Bring to the Table' Means Proving Your Impact Through High-Quality Artifacts** — You must demonstrate your contribution on four craft pillars — data/metrics, design/research, technology, and strategy — through tangible documents like PRDs, product notes, strategy docs, and design briefs. When PMs complain their career isn't growing despite working on impactful products, Vikrama asks to see their last PRD or strategy doc. Weak artifacts are almost always the hidden blocker. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/vikrama-dhiman.md]

**Three Tenets of an Effective Operating Model: Raise Issues Without Being Difficult, Bring Up Important Topics Without Seeking Credit, Get Decisions Made Without Making All Decisions Yourself** — These three principles define how a PM works with stakeholders and are the highest-leverage skill for senior-level jumps. The 'mini CEO' folklore misleads PMs into being obstacles rather than enablers. Successful PMs bring emotional conversations to a logical space, push back constructively, and focus on unblocking the team rather than seeking credit. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/vikrama-dhiman.md]

**Three Mindset Shifts That Block Career Growth: Focusing on What You Can't Control, a Slowing Rate of Change, and the Limiting Stories You Tell Yourself** — As PMs become mid-senior, they shift from 'how am I learning?' to 'why isn't the org doing this for me?', their rate of skill acquisition plateaus, and their self-narratives become anti-signals that excuse blind spots. To counter this, Vikrama advises: (a) return focus to the three W's, (b) benchmark yourself externally to keep your rate of change high, and (c) replace limiting self-stories with enabling ones like 'I am a learner.' ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/vikrama-dhiman.md]
