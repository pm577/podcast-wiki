# Seth Godin

Seth Godin is a legendary marketer, teacher, entrepreneur, and author of more than 20 bestselling books including *Purple Cow*, *Permission Marketing*, *Linchpin*, and *This is Strategy*. He started his career as a product manager at Spinnaker Software, founded Yoyodyne and Squidoo, created the altMBA program, and has been blogging daily for over 10,000 consecutive days. His work has shaped how millions think about marketing, branding, and standing out.

## Key Views

### Strategy Is About Making Choices — Customers, Competition, Validation, Distribution
Seth's framework for strategy centers on four critical choices that most product people gloss over. **Choose your customers** — if your motto is "you can pick anyone and we're anyone," you won't succeed. Pick your smallest viable audience. **Choose your competition** — if you're competing against Walmart, don't be surprised they keep lowering prices. **Choose your source of validation** — are you trying to please your boss or your customers? **Choose your distribution** — distribution determines so much of what your product needs to be. These choices define your future.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/seth-godin.md]

### Good Taste Is Knowing What Others Want Just Before They Do
Seth defines good taste as "knowing what other people want just before they do." It's not about personal preference — it's about bringing something to the world that the world didn't expect but is glad to see. Standards mean relentlessly improving the spec in service of the people you're working with and for. Quality is not luxury and not perfection — quality means meeting spec. And if the spec isn't good enough, make a better spec.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/seth-godin.md]

### AI Is Becoming Like Electricity — Not a Differentiator
Seth predicts AI will very soon stop being a feature, just like electricity stopped being a feature. "What AI companies and all companies need to do is say, what's in this for the user? What promise do I want to make — a difficult promise, a remarkable promise — and then how do I keep it?" Making absurd promises might work at the VC level, but it doesn't work with consumers because you can only break that promise once. He contrasts Claude (which has a brand — kindness and humility) with ChatGPT (which regularly over-promises and under-delivers).^[raw/transcripts/lenny/seth-godin.md]

### Tension Is at the Heart of Every Great Strategy
Seth distinguishes tension from stress. Stress is "I hate my job but I need this job" — two competing desires that paralyze. Tension is different — it's the creative force at the heart of every art form and innovation. When you launch a product, the customer imagines their life if it were true. If they fall in love with that possibility, there's tension: "Did you tell the truth? Is it going to work for them?" Great strategies embrace this tension rather than avoiding it.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/seth-godin.md]

### Build Network Effects Into Your Product From Day One
"If you don't build the network effect into what you are making, you are almost certainly going to fail." The critical question: "Will this work better for my users if they tell other people about it?" If the answer is no, why would they tell anyone? If the answer is yes, your marketing problems are mostly solved. Most founders wait to add network effects later, but they should be baked into the product design from the very beginning.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/seth-godin.md]
