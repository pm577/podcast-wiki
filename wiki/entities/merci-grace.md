# Merci Grace

Merci Grace has been a founder, game designer, Head of Growth/Product at Slack, an investor/VC, and a founder again. She is also a co-founder of Women in Product. Her career has spanned game design, venture capital, and building growth teams at one of the most successful B2B SaaS companies in history.

## Key Views

**Great Founders Are Great Storytellers** — After seeing hundreds of pitches as a VC, Merci observed that the best founders are exceptional at storytelling — owning the room, crafting a pithy narrative, and starting in the middle of the action. The fundamentals of their businesses might not look better on paper, but the way they present makes all the difference. Every pitch should start in the middle of the action, like a thriller — hook the audience immediately. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/merci-grace.md]

**VC Deals Are More Subjective Than They Seem** — Despite how it appears in the press ("this deal happened because of market shifts"), most deals happen or don't happen due to interpersonal dynamics, personality clashes, or old grudges. It's highly subjective and personal. The founder's ability to be seen as capable and trustworthy is often more important than the business fundamentals. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/merci-grace.md]

**Product-Led Growth at Slack** — Slack pioneered the modern concept of product-led growth, and Merci was instrumental in building Slack's growth systems. The key insight was making the product so good that it sold itself — users would invite their team members organically. The growth loops were embedded in the product experience itself. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/merci-grace.md]

**Common Mistakes in Going Product-Led** — Companies trying to go product-led often make the mistake of treating it as a marketing campaign rather than a product philosophy. True PLG requires the product itself to deliver enough value that users become advocates. It's not about adding a "try it free" button — it's about building a product so good that growth is a natural byproduct. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/merci-grace.md]

**Follow Your Curiosity** — Merci's career arc (founder → game designer → PM → VC → founder again) wasn't planned — she was just following her curiosity and love of understanding how people think and make decisions. The nonlinear path gave her unique perspective that she wouldn't have gotten from a traditional product career. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/merci-grace.md]
