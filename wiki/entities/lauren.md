# Lauren Ipsen

Lauren Ipsen is one of the most respected executive recruiters in tech, having placed over 90 senior product leaders at companies including Twitter, Reddit, Opendoor, Postmates, and many others. She started at Daversa Partners before becoming the first recruiting hire at a startup, and now works at General Catalyst specializing in consumer and crypto investments for their portfolio companies.

## Key Views

### Don't Hire the Biggest Name — Hire for This Stage

Lauren's #1 mistake she sees founders make: chasing big-name executives from Google, YouTube, etc., without assessing whether they're right for the current stage. "Those individuals are often pretty far from the work and have a great team of executors that they've put into place that are actually the ones that are in the weeds." She recommends looking for someone closer to the work who can operate like a PM some days and build from a leadership perspective other days.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lauren-ipsen.md]

### Always Keep a Pulse on the Market

Regardless of whether you're hiring, Lauren advises staying connected to the talent market. "You never want to put yourself in a position where you have no idea what good looks like." She encourages founders to chat with great candidates even before having a specific need — use them as advisors, get them involved in some capacity. This reduces time-to-hire dramatically when the need becomes urgent.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lauren-ipsen.md]

### Define Success Before You Start the Search

"Whenever you're starting a search without a clear mandate, it's kind of doomed from inception." Lauren pushes founders to get incredibly granular: What will this person do in their first 90 days? What does success look like in one year? Two years? Do you want them to grow into a CPO role? The most common failure mode is hiring a "head of product" because the board said you need one, without clarity on the actual outcomes needed.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lauren-ipsen.md]

### The Three Archetypes of Product Leaders

Lauren categorizes product leaders into three buckets: (1) platform/infrastructure leaders, (2) core product/consumer product leaders, and (3) specialists (growth, monetization, UX-focused). Founders who try to hire one "unicorn" to do all three usually fail. "The facts of the matter is, they're going to have to bring in some key lieutenants to help them." Be clear about which bucket is most urgent and hire for that specifically.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lauren-ipsen.md]

### Titles Are Stage-Dependent, Not Status

Early-stage companies should use "Head of Product" rather than VP or CPO titles. Most startups are "almost allergic to C titles" until Series C or D. "If you're coming into a company and they say this is the head of product, the VP of product, or the CPO, that all means the same thing dependent on the stage." Don't over-promise titles — the talent you need now may not be the same person you need in 2.5 years.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lauren-ipsen.md]
