# Diana Hu

General Partner at Y Combinator. Co-presented the Stanford AI Frontier lecture on building AI-native companies. Focused on how AI is transforming company structure and enabling unprecedented growth (companies hitting 8-figure revenue within a year).

## Key Views

### 1. Closed-Loop vs Open-Loop Organizations

Pre-AI companies run as open loops — lossy information in people's heads, side conversations, unwritten meeting notes, vibes. Error accumulates. AI-native companies embed agents into every decision with read access to every artifact (GitHub, Discord, meetings). Tight feedback loop → self-healing system.

^[raw/transcripts/stanford-ai-frontier/garry-tan-diana-hu-yc-stanford-ai-frontier.md]

### 2. The Three Roles of an Agentic Organization

1. **IC** — everyone ships (even non-technical people build with AI tools)
2. **DRI** — Directly Responsible Individual owns every outcome, orchestrates ICs
3. **AI Founder** — lives at the edge, tries all tools, brings innovations in

^[raw/transcripts/stanford-ai-frontier/garry-tan-diana-hu-yc-stanford-ai-frontier.md]

### 3. Revenue per Employee: 10x Improvement

One employee can generate $1-2M in revenue (vs public SaaS companies under $100K/employee). YC's engineering team cut sprint time in half with 10x output.

^[raw/transcripts/stanford-ai-frontier/garry-tan-diana-hu-yc-stanford-ai-frontier.md]

### 4. Unprecedented YC Growth

Historically only top 1% (Airbnb) grew 10% week-over-week. Now companies 3x in 3 months on average. Massive white space: 50% AI penetration in software, but back office, finance, academia, cybersecurity, customer service are wide open.

^[raw/transcripts/stanford-ai-frontier/garry-tan-diana-hu-yc-stanford-ai-frontier.md]
