# Nickey Skarstad

Nickey Skarstad is a Director of Product Management at Duolingo, where she leads a stealth 0-to-1 product. Previously, she was VP of Product at The Wing, Product Lead at Airbnb (where she helped launch and scale Experiences), Product Lead at Shopify, and Director of Product Management at Etsy (where she started as a forum moderator and worked her way up). She is known for her legendary reputation as a PM who everyone loves but gets shit done.

## Key Views

### Obsession with Product Quality Is the #1 Differentiator

Nickey's defining lesson from Airbnb: "Airbnb does not ship product if it is not good. They are obsessed with the end consumer experience." This obsession trickles down into every review, every metric, every conversation. "If it shipped, it was really high quality. All the edge cases had been thought through." The key is picking quality metrics that balance or conflate with growth metrics — making quality the north star rather than just growth.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/nickey-skarstad.md]

### Use Quality Metrics as a Balancing Force

At Airbnb Experiences, the top-line goal was review rate and quality — not revenue or bookings. "If you are building early product, really think about how you can pick some good quality metrics that might actually balance or conflate with growth metrics, and use that as your north star." This ensured the whole team understood that the end consumer experience was paramount, and growth was important but secondary.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/nickey-skarstad.md]

### Dogfood Everything, All the Time

Nickey's approach to maintaining quality: dogfood your product relentlessly. Airbnb Experiences was "a really fun product to dogfood" — the team took experiences all over the world. "It really helped because you would know right away when you showed up to an event, if you were going to have a five-star experience or if it was not going to be great." Then they'd go back to the team: "We were just in San Francisco on an experience last night, and it did not go well. What do we do?"^[raw/transcripts/lenny/nickey-skarstad.md]

### Setting Vision → Translating to Goals → Executing

Nickey's systematic approach to strategy: set vision at the top, translate that into clear measurable goals (OKRs), and cascade those goals into actionable projects. She emphasizes designing product review meetings that keep the team aligned and focused on outcomes rather than outputs. Her substack (nickey.substack.com) goes deep into these rituals and frameworks.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/nickey-skarstad.md]

### From Forum Moderator to Director of Product

Nickey's career path is a testament to a non-traditional route into product. She started at Etsy as a forum moderator and seller education specialist, becoming "one of the internal voices that had a really good understanding of how their customers were actually using their product." The VP of Product nudged her to try PM. "I was very imposter syndrome-y about it," but the deep customer understanding she'd built became her superpower as a PM.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/nickey-skarstad.md]
