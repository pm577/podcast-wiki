# Christina Wodtke

Christina Wodtke is an author, Stanford University professor, and speaker who teaches strategies for building high-performing teams. She wrote *Radical Focus*, considered one of the definitive books on OKRs. She was a product leader at LinkedIn, MySpace, Zynga, and Yahoo, and a three-time founder.

## Key Views

### The Atomic Unit of an OKR
Christina breaks down OKRs to their fundamental structure: an **Objective** (inspiring, qualitative, time-bound) and 3-5 **Key Results** (measurable, quantitative outcomes, not tasks). A common mistake: writing KRs that are just to-do lists. A KR must be a measurable outcome — "Ship new onboarding flow" is a task; "Increase D7 retention by 15%" is a key result. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/christina-wodtke.md]

### Why OKRs Fail — The Most Common Root Causes
(1) **No commitment** — leaders set OKRs but don't actually resource them; (2) **Too many OKRs** — teams try to do everything and accomplish nothing; (3) **KRs as tasks** — measuring output instead of outcomes; (4) **No mid-quarter check-ins** — OKRs are set and forgotten until the quarter ends; (5) **Lack of alignment** — team OKRs don't connect to company objectives. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/christina-wodtke.md]

### The Power of Friday Celebrations
"People do not value celebrations enough." Christina has seen CEOs say, "We started Friday celebrations and oh my God, things are already changing." The simple act of gathering and sharing "the most awesome thing that happened this week" creates a sense of belonging and momentum. It's a low-effort, high-impact practice that builds team culture. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/christina-wodtke.md]

### OKRs, Mission, and Strategy Form a Hierarchy
Christina's framework: **Mission** (why we exist) → **Vision** (where we're going) → **Strategy** (how we'll get there) → **OKRs** (what we'll achieve this quarter) → **Roadmap** (what we'll build). Most teams skip the strategy step and jump directly from mission to OKRs, leading to misalignment. Strategy connects long-term aspiration to quarterly execution. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/christina-wodtke.md]

### Storytelling Is a Strategic Skill for PMs
Beyond OKRs, Christina emphasizes storytelling as a critical PM skill. The best PMs don't just communicate data — they tell a compelling story about why a problem matters, what the vision is, and why their proposed solution is the right path. Logic convinces the mind; story convinces the heart. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/christina-wodtke.md]
