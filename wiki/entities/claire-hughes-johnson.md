# Claire Hughes Johnson

*Former COO of Stripe (scaled from 160 to 7,000+ people). Previously 10 years at Google (from 1,800 to 60,000). Author of Scaling People. Board member at HubSpot and The Atlantic.*

## Key Views

1. **Build company operating structures before you think you need them**: Product-market fit is table stakes — it's the product, not the company. Companies fail when they don't build the "house" (posts, beams, mechanicals) early enough. She convinced Stripe they needed to be twice their current headcount just to handle inbound demand. Structure enables velocity.

2. **Start with self-awareness before company-building**: The book Scaling People starts with personal operating principles because management starts with you. Her four principles: build self-awareness to build mutual awareness, say the thing you think you cannot say, distinguish leadership from management, and be an explorer not a lecturer.

3. **"Say the thing you think you cannot say" — with ownership**: To deliver hard feedback: ask a question, own the observation as your perception, and don't pass judgment. "I felt like you seemed nervous in that meeting — were you?" is exploring. "You botched that presentation" is attacking. The detoxified left-hand column builds trust instead of damaging it.

4. **Be a force for positive momentum**: The #1 career advice she gives is to be someone who creates progress, impact, and momentum. If you're unsure who the decision-maker is, assume it's you and act accordingly. Getting stuck asking "who decides?" slows everything down. Make a decision, follow process, and keep moving.

5. **Minimal hierarchy creates optionality and culture**: Stripe famously avoided inflated titles for years. Early titles signal hierarchy before you need it, and create problems when you need to layer later. A flat structure reinforces that expertise — not seniority — drives decisions. The person with the knowledge should be in the room regardless of title.
