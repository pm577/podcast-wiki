# Sherwin Wu

Head of Engineering for OpenAI's API and Developer Platform. Has a unique vantage point into how AI is transforming software engineering, given that OpenAI's own engineering team is one of the heaviest users of AI coding tools.

## Key Views

### 1. 100% of Code at OpenAI Is AI-Generated

At OpenAI, 95% of engineers use Codex daily, 100% of PRs are reviewed by Codex, and essentially all code is written by AI first. Engineers using Codex open 70% more PRs than those who don't, and the gap is widening. "It literally feels like we're wizards casting all these spells and these spells are kind of like going out and doing things for you."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-02-12-sherwin-wu-v2.md]

### 2. Engineers Are Becoming Tech Leads of AI Fleets

The role of the engineer is shifting from writing code to managing AI agents. "Engineers are becoming tech leads. They're managing fleets and fleets of agents." The skill that matters is no longer syntax or algorithmic mastery — it's the ability to review, guide, and orchestrate AI-generated code. A team at OpenAI is experimenting with maintaining a 100% Codex-written codebase with no human-written escape hatch.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-02-12-sherwin-wu-v2.md]

### 3. Build for Where the Models Are Going, Not Where They Are

The biggest mistake companies make is building scaffolding and tools optimized for today's models. "The models will eat your scaffolding for breakfast." Kevin Weil's maxim: "This is the worst the models will ever be." Any tooling that compensates for current model limitations will be obsolete as models improve. Build for the trajectory, not the snapshot.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-02-12-sherwin-wu-v2.md]

### 4. The One-Person Billion-Dollar Startup Creates a Golden Age of B2B SaaS

The second and third-order effects of AI are underappreciated. A one-person billion-dollar startup may require a hundred other small startups building bespoke software to support it. This proliferation of niche, AI-powered businesses could create a golden age for B2B SaaS as every niche gets its own dedicated tooling.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-02-12-sherwin-wu-v2.md]

### 5. Listening to Customers Is Often Wrong in AI

In traditional product development, customer feedback is sacred. But in AI, the field changes so fast that customers often optimize for current limitations rather than future capabilities. The models disrupt their own use cases. Product strategy should be driven by conviction about where the technology is heading, not by current customer requests.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-02-12-sherwin-wu-v2.md]
