# Petra Wille

Petra Wille appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss how to be the best coach to product people.

## Key Views

**The Five Ingredients of Product Coaching** — Petra's core coaching framework from Strong Product People: (1) a solid definition of what a good product person looks like in your context, (2) a clear idea of where each PM is today plus their "next bigger challenge," (3) a shared vision for their future, (4) a development plan they own, and (5) consistent follow-up. "Step number one is really having a solid definition of what a good product person looks like in your context… step number two is having a clear idea where every PM currently is in their current career… and then even more important is what I call the next bigger challenge." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/petra-wille.md]

**Why Many Product Leads Struggle to Coach** — A key coaching gap: many people leading product teams come from marketing, business, data, or engineering backgrounds and have never worked as a product manager themselves, so they lack empathy for the PM role. "They often come from a marketing background, or a business background, or from the data background… or the engineering background, but they often never have worked in a product management role. So they're missing a lot of basic product management practice and a lot of empathy for the struggle of the product people." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/petra-wille.md]

**Start with the Development Plan, Not the Perfect Compass** — Don't get paralyzed trying to create the ideal competency framework first — just start where the PM is, since they usually know what they want to improve. "I usually advise people to start with the development plan because even if you have never done the assessment and even if you don't have your compass, your definition of what makes a good product manager, you usually have an idea… of what they want to learn next or where they want to get better at." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/petra-wille.md]

**Consistency Beats Intensity in People Development** — Small, regular coaching investments (weekly nudges, monthly check-ins) are far more effective than big annual performance reviews for both the PM and the product lead. "All of us know consistency beats intensity. So, really, the smaller time investments on a weekly basis and that applies for the PM's time investment in learning new things and it applies to the product leads investment and helping their people to grow." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/petra-wille.md]

**The PMwheel: A Coaching Conversation Starter** — Petra developed the PMwheel — eight skill buckets (understand problems, find solutions, planning, get it done, listen & learn, team, personal growth, agile) — as a practical tool to initiate coaching conversations with PMs who aren't sure what to work on. "It is often that coaches come totally unprepared to the coaching… And then they're just like, 'Okay, they told me to show up. Petra, what should we do in these sessions?' And then that's why I created the PMwheel to have these first conversations." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/petra-wille.md]
