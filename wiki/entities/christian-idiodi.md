# Christian Idiodi

*Partner at Silicon Valley Product Group (SVPG), product leader, and builder of over 205 products. Known for his "reference customer" approach to achieving product-market fit.*

## Key Views

1. **PMs earn the right to decide through competence**: The reason "people hate product managers" is they haven't worked with truly competent ones. A great PM knows the customer, data, industry, and business better than anyone else. That expertise earns the trust that lets them make decisions. Without it, stakeholders will override you.

2. **The reference customer is the holy grail of product work**: Idiodi's single favorite discovery technique is finding reference customers — people who love your product enough to put their reputation on the line by telling others about it. For B2B, find 6-8; for B2C, 15-25. He's never had a product failure using this method.

3. **Don't launch without 25 five-star reviews**: For every app he's ever launched, Idiodi ensures there are 25 people ready to give 5-star reviews on day one. He doesn't launch and hope people like it — he works with customers until they're truly referenceable. Product-market fit is when your target customers will stake their reputation on you.

4. **Value risk is the most overlooked**: Of the four product risks (value, usability, feasibility, viability), value is the hardest and most overlooked. Teams often assume value because their roadmap was handed down. The core PM job is ensuring "will people buy/choose/use this?" — everything else follows.

5. **Immerse in the problem environment**: Idiodi advocates getting out of the building and staying with someone who has the problem until you solve it. He drove around with his team talking to construction site managers and mall operators when solving a hiring problem for Starbucks. The fastest path to a solution is total immersion in the user's world.
