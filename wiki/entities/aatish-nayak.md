# Aatish Nayak

Head of Product @ Harvey, overseeing product vision, strategy, design, analytics, marketing, and support. This is his third hypergrowth AI unicorn — previously held product leadership roles at Scale AI (from 40 to 800 people) and Shield AI (from 20 to 100 people).

## Key Views

### 1. Biggest Product Lessons from Scale AI

Scaling product from 40 to 800 people requires a fundamental shift in how you operate. Early-stage, you can be in every detail; at scale, you need systems, clear ownership, and a shared understanding of what "good" looks like. The biggest lesson: hire people who can operate independently on a clear north star, not people who need constant direction.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2025-04-11-20product-how-scale-ai-and-ha.md]

### 2. Why Product Managers Are Wrong: They Are Not the CEOs of the Product

PMs who think they're the "CEO of the product" create friction and bottlenecks. The real job is facilitation and discovery — helping the team make the right decisions, not making all decisions yourself. The best PMs are invisible; the team succeeds and the PM enabled it.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2025-04-11-20product-how-scale-ai-and-ha.md]

### 3. Why Market Selection is More Important Than Anything

Nayak emphasizes that choosing the right market is more important than any product decision. Harvey's success in legal AI comes from focusing on a specific, high-value vertical where AI can deliver clear ROI. Market selection compounds every product decision you make.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2025-04-11-20product-how-scale-ai-and-ha.md]

### 4. How to Do Pre and Post Mortems Effectively

Pre-mortems (imagining the project failed and working backward) are more useful than post-mortems because they identify risks before they materialize. Post-mortems should be blameless and focused on systemic improvements, not individual mistakes.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2025-04-11-20product-how-scale-ai-and-ha.md]

### 5. The Future of Product Management in a World of AI

AI will eliminate the tactical parts of product management (spec writing, data analysis, stakeholder updates) and elevate the strategic parts (user empathy, vision setting, market understanding). The PMs who survive are the ones who think deeply about humans, not the ones who write good PRDs.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2025-04-11-20product-how-scale-ai-and-ha.md]
