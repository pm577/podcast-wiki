# Andy Raskin

Andy Raskin helps CEOs and leadership teams align around a strategic narrative — a single story that powers success in sales, marketing, product, fundraising, and recruiting. His clients include Gong, Dropbox, Uber, Salesforce, Square, and IBM. He started his career as a coder and discovered the power of storytelling after a VC called his pitch "a 1 out of 10."

## Key Views

**Strategic Narrative Beats Arrogant Doctor Pitching** — The standard pitch structure ("you have a problem, here's my solution, it's better than alternatives") is less effective than a strategic narrative that starts with a shift in the world. The archetypal example is Salesforce: "Software is over. There's a new world called the cloud. We'll help you win in it." This defines a movement rather than comparing features. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/andy-raskin.md]

**Name the Old Game, Name the New Game** — The most critical element is giving the shift a concise name (e.g., "transactions → subscription economy," "opinions → reality"). Though it loses completeness, it creates a rallying cry. Naming the stakes — showing winners already playing the new game — makes the shift feel like life or death. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/andy-raskin.md]

**The Zuora Deck as Template** — Tien Tzuo's pitch (employee #11 at Salesforce) used the same structure: "In the old world, businesses operated on transactions. In this new world, it's the subscription economy. Winners like Airbnb and Box are already there." This became the most-shared sales deck on the internet and helped Gong and others find their own version. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/andy-raskin.md]

**Write, Publish, and the Power of Platforms** — Raskin's career-defining post "The Greatest Sales Deck I've Ever Seen" got 2M views and global inquiries — but it was his 30th or 40th piece. Writing on Medium and LinkedIn bypassed editorial gatekeepers. The magic came from compounding: each post built on the last, with occasional spikes followed by steady growth. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/andy-raskin.md]

**Resist the Category Trap** — Many founders default to creating a category or comparing features. The strategic narrative approach reframes from "here's why we're better" to "here's where the world is going, and we'll help you get there." This shift moved Gong from being seen as a sales-ops tool to a sales-leadership platform. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/andy-raskin.md]
