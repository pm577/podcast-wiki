# Brian Chesky

**Role:** Co-founder and CEO of Airbnb, which grew from an apartment in San Francisco to an $80B+ global community with 4M+ hosts and 1.5B+ guest arrivals across 220 countries  
**Known for:** Radically restructuring Airbnb's product management function, the "founder mode" philosophy of staying deeply involved in details, and pioneering a functional org model that merges product, design, and marketing.

## Key Views

### 1. Leaders must be in the details — micromanagement vs. involvement

Brian distinguishes between "micromanagement" (telling people exactly what to do) and being "in the details" (understanding deeply what's happening so you can evaluate quality). He argues that too many founders apologize for how they want to run their company, finding a midpoint that makes everyone miserable. What people really want is clarity and direction. After Airbnb hit a growth plateau, Brian got deeply involved in every product detail — and the company's performance dramatically improved.

> "Way too many founders apologize for how they want to run the company. They find some midpoint between how they want to run a company and how the people they lead want to run the company. That's a good way to make everyone miserable." — Brian Chesky

### 2. The elimination of traditional product management at Airbnb

Brian's controversial restructuring: Airbnb eliminated the traditional PM function and split responsibilities between product marketing (inbound + outbound merged) and program management (offloaded coordination work). The remaining senior people (called "product marketers") manage by influence, not authority. The core insight: "You can't build a product unless you know how to talk about the product." PMs must own both the building and the marketing of what they build — otherwise, products can be well-engineered but fail because nobody knows about them or knows how to use them.

> "You can't build a product unless you know how to talk about the product. You can't be an expert in making the product unless you're also an expert in the market of it." — Brian Chesky

### 3. The arc from startup to bureaucracy — and how to prevent it

Brian describes a predictable death spiral: as companies grow, teams build dependencies, which creates delays, which leads teams to build their own siloed capabilities (marketing, engineering, etc.), which creates divisions, which creates politics and advocacy, which creates bureaucracy, which kills accountability, which breeds complacency. His solution: a purely functional org model where design, engineering, and marketing report up separate chains but are tightly integrated at the product level. A simple heuristic for org health: "How close is engineering and marketing?"

> "The health of an organization … one simple heuristic is how close is engineering and marketing?" — Brian Chesky

### 4. Growth through product excellence, not paid channels

Brian shifted Airbnb's growth strategy away from traditional paid growth and performance marketing, betting instead that if they build the best possible product and tell people about it, growth will follow organically. This meant consolidating the product roadmap into a single company-wide plan, with Brian personally involved in every feature and design decision. The bet paid off: Airbnb emerged from the pandemic stronger than ever, with record revenue and profitability, despite cutting the growth marketing budget.

### 5. Design must be embedded, not a service organization

Brian's critique of Silicon Valley: most companies treat design as a service organization — "catching things before it goes out the door" — rather than an integral part of the development process. Many heads of design are actually "design administrators." This frustrates designers and produces worse products. At Airbnb, design is fully embedded in the product development process, with Brian (a designer by training) setting the quality bar personally. This is why the 5,000 designers at Figma Config cheered when they thought Brian had eliminated PMs — they were cheering for a model where design is elevated.
