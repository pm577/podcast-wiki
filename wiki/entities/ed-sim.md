# Ed Sim

Founder and Managing Partner at Boldstart Ventures, one of the best seed-round investors in venture capital. Focuses on developer, infrastructure, and SaaS at pre-seed and seed stages. Backed category-defining companies including Snyk, BigID, Kustomer, Front, and Superhuman. Over nearly two decades in early-stage SaaS, his portfolio companies have been acquired by Google, LinkedIn, and Salesforce.

## Key Views

### 1. The Three Types of Seed Round

Sim identifies three distinct types of seed rounds in today's market, arguing that seed has never been more competitive nor pricing higher. He explains how too much cash too soon can harm companies, and that boards at pre-seed can actually be helpful rather than burdensome when set up correctly.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2023-10-27-20vc-the-three-types-of-seed.md]

### 2. The Repeatable SaaS Playbook

Drawing on 19+ years in early-stage SaaS, Sim outlines the repeatable playbook for building successful enterprise SaaS companies. He shares frameworks from his investments in category leaders like LivePerson (on Nasdaq) and GoToMeeting (acquired by Citrix), emphasizing that the fundamentals of enterprise sales, product focus, and capital efficiency remain timeless.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2015-11-30-20-vc-092-the-repeatable-play.md]

### 3. M&A Market Dynamics and Growth Investing Mistakes

Sim analyzes how the Figma/Adobe deal collapse affected the M&A market in 2024. He identifies the three biggest mistakes made in growth investing and outlines the three requirements companies need to go public. He argues that the bar for IPOs remains high and that many private companies need to adjust expectations.
