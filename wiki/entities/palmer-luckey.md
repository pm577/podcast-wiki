# Palmer Luckey

Founder of Anduril Industries, a defense technology company fusing AI with hardware to transform US and allied military capabilities. Raised over $385M from Founders Fund, a16z, Elad Gil, Spark Capital, Lux Capital, and General Catalyst. Previously founded Oculus VR (creator of the Oculus Rift) and sold it to Facebook for $2.3BN.

## Key Views

### 1. "I Am Here To Build a $50BN Company"

Luckey explicitly states his ambition to build Anduril into a $50BN company. He argues that defense technology is one of the few sectors where massive, concentrated value creation is still possible because the US government has been systematically under-investing in modern technology. His thesis: if you can build superior defense products faster and cheaper than traditional defense contractors, the market is essentially unlimited.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2020-09-28-20vc-anduril-founder-palmer.md]

### 2. His Relationship With Money Pre- and Post-Oculus

Luckey discusses how his relationship with money changed after the $2.3BN Oculus exit. He admits that having financial freedom changed his risk calculus — he no longer worries about personal financial failure — but also notes that it made him more, not less, ambitious because he can now take the long view on company building without being distracted by short-term financial pressures.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2020-09-28-20vc-anduril-founder-palmer.md]

### 3. The US DOD Needs To Be More Like China

Luckey makes a provocative argument that the US Department of Defense needs to adopt a more China-like approach to technology adoption: faster procurement cycles, willingness to buy from non-traditional vendors, and greater tolerance for failure. He argues that the current system of multi-year RFPs and incumbency protection is a national security risk.
