# Dharmesh Shah

Dharmesh Shah is co-founder and CTO of HubSpot ($30B market cap). He's the keeper of HubSpot's Culture Code and one of the most first-principles thinkers in tech. A self-described introvert who dislikes management, he has never had a direct report in HubSpot's 18+ year history.

## Key Views

**Zig When Everyone Zags** — Conventional startup wisdom says focus on one thing and be world-class at it. HubSpot's early "zig" was doing exactly the opposite: building a platform (CRM, marketing, sales, service) from day one. This asymmetry — betting against conventional wisdom — created a wedge that competitors couldn't easily copy because it went against everything they believed about focus. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/dharmesh-shah.md]

**Lean Into Your Superpowers, Ignore Your Weaknesses** — Dharmesh knew he would be "passively okay" at management with training, but didn't want to spend years becoming mediocre at something. Instead of fixing his weakness, he designed HubSpot so he never had to manage anyone. The lesson: companies should be designed so founders can operate from their strengths, not forced into roles that drain them. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/dharmesh-shah.md]

**Culture Is a Product — Define It Explicitly** — HubSpot's Culture Code is one of the most shared documents in tech history. Dharmesh treated company culture like a product: define the values explicitly, test them against decisions, iterate based on feedback, and make them visible to everyone. Culture isn't what you say — it's who you hire, who you fire, and what you celebrate. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/dharmesh-shah.md]

**Laughs Per Minute as a Presentation Metric** — Dharmesh built custom software to measure LPM (laughs per minute) in his talks. Comedy and keynotes share the same structure: setup, tension, payoff. By treating presentations as a craft with measurable outcomes, he transformed a weakness (public speaking anxiety) into a strength. The lesson: apply the same systematic improvement to any skill. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/dharmesh-shah.md]

**Fighting Entropy: The Second Law of Thermodynamics in Business** — Companies naturally trend toward disorder. The default state of any organization is chaos. Leadership is the constant application of energy to fight entropy — clear communication, aligned incentives, simple processes. The moment you stop applying that energy, disorder returns. Accept that maintenance is not a sign of failure — it's the job. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/dharmesh-shah.md]
