# Nick Turley

Head of ChatGPT at OpenAI — the product lead for the fastest-growing product in history, reaching 700M+ weekly active users (~10% of world population). Joined OpenAI when it was primarily a research lab. Part of the original hackathon team that built ChatGPT. Previously at Dropbox and Instacart.

## Key Views

### 1. Chat Was the Simplest Way to Ship — It Almost Wasn't Called ChatGPT

"Chat was the simplest way to ship at that time." The original name was going to be "Chat with GPT-3.5" because the team didn't think it would be a successful product. The hackathon codebase became the foundation of the most impactful consumer product in history. "I'm baffled by how much it took off, even more baffled by how many people have copied."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/nick-turley.md]

### 2. Ship First, Polish Later — You Won't Know What to Polish Until After

"You won't know what to polish until after you ship. My dream is that we ship daily." In AI products, the feedback loop from real users is the only reliable guide to what matters. Perfectionism before shipping is wasted because you don't know what users will actually care about. "Is it maximally accelerated?" is the question Turley asks to set the pace.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/nick-turley.md]

### 3. ChatGPT Is MS-DOS — The Windows Version Hasn't Been Built Yet

"ChatGPT feels a little bit like MS-DOS. We haven't built Windows yet, and it will be obvious once we do." The current chat interface is the primitive first layer. The full vision — the intuitive, graphical, agentic future — is still to come. This frames ChatGPT not as a destination but as a foundation for something much bigger.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/nick-turley.md]

### 4. The Best Advice: When Offered a Rocket Ship, Don't Ask Which Seat

Turley joined OpenAI when it was a research lab with no clear product function. His first task was "fix the blinds." His advice: "When someone offers you a rocket ship, don't ask which seat." The willingness to join before the role is defined and do whatever is needed is how you end up in consequential positions.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/nick-turley.md]

### 5. With Scale Comes Responsibility — 10% of the World

ChatGPT's reach (700M weekly active users, ~10% of the world) fundamentally changes the product calculus. Every decision has massive second and third-order effects. "With scale comes responsibility" isn't a platitude — it shapes how they think about safety, reliability, and which features to prioritize.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/nick-turley.md]
