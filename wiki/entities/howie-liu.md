# Howie Liu

Howie Liu appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss howie liu, co-founder and ceo of airtable, on restructuring for ai, the ic ceo trend, and fast/slow thinking org models.

## Key Views

**The IC CEO and Tasting the Soup** — Howie argues CEOs must become individual contributors again in the AI era, getting hands-on with products daily. "You can't just look at screenshots or a pre-recorded video of a new product feature. AI is something you have to play with." He cut his one-on-one roster to create urgency-driven meetings and prides himself on being Airtable's most expensive inference-cost user. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/howie-liu.md]

**Fast-Thinking vs. Slow-Thinking Org Structure** — Inspired by Kahneman's Thinking Fast and Slow, Howie reorganized Airtable's EPD into a "fast thinking" AI platform group that ships jaw-dropping capabilities weekly, and a "slow thinking" group that handles deliberate infrastructure work like HyperDB. They complement each other: fast thinking creates top-of-funnel excitement, slow thinking turns seeds into durable growth. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/howie-liu.md]

**The Clean-Slate AI-Native Question** — Howie's guiding test: "If you were literally founding a new company from scratch with the same mission, how would you execute using a fully AI native approach?" Companies must avoid bolting on AI features and honestly assess whether existing assets are an advantage or a liability. If you can't leverage them better than starting fresh, you should sell. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/howie-liu.md]

**Airtable's Lego Kit Advantage Over Vibe Coding** — Howie argues that Airtable's pre-built no-code components (real-time collaborative interfaces, view types, automations) give it a structural edge over pure vibe-coding platforms. An agent can assemble these reliable building blocks rather than generating buggy code from scratch. Non-technical users can inspect and participate through the GUI, unlike opaque code from v0 or Lovable. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/howie-liu.md]

**Collapse Role Silos — Everyone Needs T-Shaped Skills** — Howie argues PMs, engineers, and designers all need to be "minimally good" at the other two functions. A PM must become a "hybrid PM prototyper" with design sensibilities; designers must understand technical constraints; sales AEs must be fluent enough to demo without relying on SEs. "The baseline is increasing of how much you need to understand." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/howie-liu.md]


### 1. "Vibes Before Evals" — Start with Open-Ended Exploration, Not Formal Testing — Howie argues that for completely novel AI product experiences, teams should begin with open-ended "vibes-based" testing — throwing diverse prompts at the system and seeing what works — rather than defining formal evals upfront. Evals are useful only after you've converged on the basic form factor and identified the cluster of use cases that actually matter. Premature evals constrain creativity and box you in before you understand what the product could be. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2025-08-31-howie-liu.md]

### 2. Aggressive AI Compute as a Strategic Lever — Howie takes pride in being Airtable's #1 most expensive inference-cost user globally, running costly LLM map-reduce operations on thousands of sales call transcripts to extract product insights. He argues that spending hundreds of dollars on inference to mine strategic intelligence is trivial compared to paying a consulting firm millions for the same quality of work. Leaders should aggressively throw compute cycles at very high value problems rather than penny-pinch on inference costs. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2025-08-31-howie-liu.md]

### 3. Inspect the Chain of Thought — Extract Reasoning, Not Just Recommendations, from Advisors — Howie's meta-learning from scaling Airtable is that the default playbook operators give you (hire execs, create fiefdoms, industrialize each swim lane) is trained on a specific corpus (e.g., ServiceNow, Oracle). Rather than blindly following advice, ask for the rationale — the chain of thought behind it — because the reasoning is more informative than the recommendation itself. Adapt the why to your own context. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2025-08-31-howie-liu.md]

### 4. AI Products Under-Merchandise Their Capabilities — Rich Visual UX Beats the Blank Chat Box — Howie argues that most AI products have very poor visual affordances for representing what the underlying model can do, defaulting to a blank chat box. Product designers should use the full canvas of the web interface — colors, visual states, interactive elements — to expose and differentiate AI capabilities. Airtable deliberately shows different states and uses color to help users understand what the agent can do. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2025-08-31-howie-liu.md]

### 5. Airtable's Hybrid Agent Architecture: Lego Pieces Plus Custom Code Gen — Howie describes a new product direction where Airtable's conversational agent Omni can both assemble existing no-code building blocks AND generate custom code for final-mile bespoke functionality. This gives the reliability of pre-built components plus the flexibility of vibe coding, with the GUI still available for non-technical users to inspect and participate. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2025-08-31-howie-liu.md]
