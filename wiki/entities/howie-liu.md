# Howie Liu

Howie Liu appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss howie liu, co-founder and ceo of airtable, on restructuring for ai, the ic ceo trend, and fast/slow thinking org models.

## Key Views

**The IC CEO and Tasting the Soup** — Howie argues CEOs must become individual contributors again in the AI era, getting hands-on with products daily. "You can't just look at screenshots or a pre-recorded video of a new product feature. AI is something you have to play with." He cut his one-on-one roster to create urgency-driven meetings and prides himself on being Airtable's most expensive inference-cost user. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/howie-liu.md]

**Fast-Thinking vs. Slow-Thinking Org Structure** — Inspired by Kahneman's Thinking Fast and Slow, Howie reorganized Airtable's EPD into a "fast thinking" AI platform group that ships jaw-dropping capabilities weekly, and a "slow thinking" group that handles deliberate infrastructure work like HyperDB. They complement each other: fast thinking creates top-of-funnel excitement, slow thinking turns seeds into durable growth. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/howie-liu.md]

**The Clean-Slate AI-Native Question** — Howie's guiding test: "If you were literally founding a new company from scratch with the same mission, how would you execute using a fully AI native approach?" Companies must avoid bolting on AI features and honestly assess whether existing assets are an advantage or a liability. If you can't leverage them better than starting fresh, you should sell. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/howie-liu.md]

**Airtable's Lego Kit Advantage Over Vibe Coding** — Howie argues that Airtable's pre-built no-code components (real-time collaborative interfaces, view types, automations) give it a structural edge over pure vibe-coding platforms. An agent can assemble these reliable building blocks rather than generating buggy code from scratch. Non-technical users can inspect and participate through the GUI, unlike opaque code from v0 or Lovable. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/howie-liu.md]

**Collapse Role Silos — Everyone Needs T-Shaped Skills** — Howie argues PMs, engineers, and designers all need to be "minimally good" at the other two functions. A PM must become a "hybrid PM prototyper" with design sensibilities; designers must understand technical constraints; sales AEs must be fluent enough to demo without relying on SEs. "The baseline is increasing of how much you need to understand." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/howie-liu.md]
