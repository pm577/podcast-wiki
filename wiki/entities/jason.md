# Jason Shah

Jason Shah has led product teams at Amazon, Airbnb, Microsoft, and Yammer, and most recently led product at Alchemy, a leading Web3 infrastructure company. He's a product leader who has navigated multiple boom-and-bust cycles, from Web3 winter to Airbnb's COVID collapse. His writing on being a PM in Web3 was among the most popular guest posts on Lenny's Newsletter.

## Key Views

**Replace "Pushback" with "Shift Direction" — Words Shape Mindset**
Shah argues that the word "pushback" frames disagreement as a negative, oppositional act. "It starts from a place of I need to disagree, I need to say no. It's a very negative mindset." Instead, he reframes the goal as shifting direction — understanding what the other person's actual goal is and finding alignment. This linguistic shift changes the entire posture of the conversation from adversarial to collaborative, making it easier to influence decisions without creating conflict.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/jason-shah.md]]*

**The Only Way to Maintain Morale During a Downturn Is to Make Progress**
When crypto winter hit and Web3 prices collapsed, Shah's approach at Alchemy was simple: focus on tangible progress. "No speech, no sort of extrinsic motivators really works. I think people get really excited when they see progress." He draws parallels to Airbnb's 85-90% revenue drop during COVID — the teams that stayed motivated were the ones that kept shipping and focused on their customers. Progress is the only reliable antidote to external uncertainty.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/jason-shah.md]]*

**Web3 Is Strongest When Prices Are Lowest — Build Through the Winter**
Shah advises that the strongest teams in Web3 are formed during the downturns. He points to record Ethereum transactions, new Layer-2 technologies, and increasing developer platform usage at Alchemy during the bear market as evidence that building during the quiet times positions you to capture the next wave. "A lot of new technologies don't move up in a straight line." The teams that keep their heads down and build when everyone else is panicking are the ones that emerge as category leaders.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/jason-shah.md]]*

**Lead Through Uncertainty by Being Clear Even If You Might Be Wrong**
Shah cites Kevin Systrom's early Instagram mantra: "We may not be right, but at least we are clear." When the future is ambiguous — as it always is in crypto, and as it was during COVID at Airbnb — having a clear point of view that the team can rally around is more important than being perfectly accurate. Clarity enables execution, and execution reveals what's actually true. Teams waste more time debating uncertainty than they do recovering from wrong-but-clear decisions.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/jason-shah.md]]*

**Career Growth Is About Finding Important Problems and Solving Them Uniquely**
Shah frames his career as a series of choices driven by two factors: "solving important problems in a unique way." The "important problems" part ensures his time is well spent. The "unique way" comes from being the youngest child who "has to be special." This combination — seeking high-impact challenges and bringing a differentiated approach — has led him through Yammer, Microsoft, Amazon, Airbnb, and Alchemy. His advice to PMs: don't optimize for title or company brand; optimize for the intersection of importance and your unique abilities.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/jason-shah.md]]*
