# Paul Buchheit

Partner at Y Combinator. 23rd employee at Google where he created Gmail (version 1.0), developed the original prototype for Google AdSense, and coined Google's former motto, "Don't Be Evil." Founder of FriendFeed (acquired by Facebook in 2009) where he created the "like" button as we know it today.

## Key Views

### 1. Creating Gmail Version 1.0 from Scratch

As the 23rd employee at Google, Paul built Gmail entirely from the ground up — a product that fundamentally redefined email and web applications. His approach was to start with a personal need (better email) and build a product that solved it at a level of quality that didn't exist in the market. Gmail's 1GB of free storage was seen as absurd at the time, but it changed the industry overnight.^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2017-11-01-20vc-y-combinators-paul-buch.md]

### 2. Leadership Lessons from Working with Zuckerberg and Larry Page

Having worked closely with two of the most iconic tech founders — Larry Page at Google and Mark Zuckerberg at Facebook (post-FriendFeed acquisition) — Paul observed what separates truly great leaders. The most important lesson: the best founders maintain a relentless focus on user value above all else. They ask "what does the user need?" not "what does the competitor do?"^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2017-11-01-20vc-y-combinators-paul-buch.md]

### 3. The "Don't Be Evil" Philosophy

Paul coined Google's famous motto, reflecting his belief that technology companies should prioritize doing right by users over short-term profit. This philosophy influenced everything from Gmail's privacy-first ad targeting to Google's early product decisions.^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2017-11-01-20vc-y-combinators-paul-buch.md]
