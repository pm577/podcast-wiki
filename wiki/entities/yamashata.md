# Yuhki Yamashita

**Role:** Chief Product Officer at Figma; former Head of Design at Uber (New Mobility); PM at Google and Microsoft  
**Known for:** Building Figma's product development philosophy, scaling a product-led growth business, creating one of the most influential products in the design industry.

## Key Views

### 1. Equip your customers to champion a new way of working

Figma's success came not just from building a great tool, but from championing a new way of working — collaborative, browser-based design that challenged the status quo. Yuhki's insight: controversy and resistance (e.g., early Figma users saying "if this is the future of design, I'm quitting") is actually a signal that you're part of a revolution. Equip your customers with a narrative they can champion.

> "It's not just that they're championing for a tool, they're also championing for a new way of working."

### 2. Product-led growth requires deep investment in the product itself

Figma's growth philosophy centers on making the product so good that it sells itself. This means obsessive attention to onboarding, activation, and the "aha moment." The PLG flywheel: great product → word of mouth → more users → better product. Yuhki emphasizes that PLG is not a marketing tactic; it's a product philosophy.

### 3. The CPO must bridge design, engineering, and business

Yuhki's unique background — PM, designer, and engineer — informs his view that the best product leaders sit at the intersection of all three disciplines. His career path from Microsoft (PM) to Google to Uber (Head of Design) to Figma (CPO) reflects a deliberate accumulation of cross-functional perspective.

### 4. Hire for taste and judgment, not just execution

At Figma, hiring emphasizes taste, design judgment, and product thinking over pure execution velocity. Yuhki believes that great products come from people who care deeply about quality and have strong opinions about what "good" looks like.

### 5. A strong product architecture enables sustainable growth

Like many great product leaders, Yuhki emphasizes that the underlying architecture of the product determines how fast you can move, how many features you can ship without breaking things, and how cohesive the user experience remains at scale.
