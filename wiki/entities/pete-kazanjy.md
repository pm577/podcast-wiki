# Pete Kazanjy

Pete Kazanjy appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss pete kazanjy, author of founding sales, founder of modern sales pros and atrium, on founder-led selling and b2b sales.

## Key Views

**Founder-Led Sales Is Non-Negotiable** — Pete's core thesis: founders must do their own sales first. It's easier for a founder to become 'minimally viable good' at selling than for any third party to become as expert in the product/market. The founder keeps the critical customer feedback loop intact — losing it to a hired salesperson early is like 'playing a game of telephone' with market understanding. Don't outsource the first couple dozen customers. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/pete-kazanjy.md]

**Sales Motion as Software — Iterate Like Code** — Pete treats the sales pitch as source code: when a prospect asks a question you can't answer well, 'make a slide for it' — update the collateral. Each interaction is an iteration. After dozens of these updates, you have a repeatable sales motion. The leading indicators tell you if it's working: are you getting second dates? Are you getting to commercial conversations? Monitor these the same way you'd monitor a product funnel. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/pete-kazanjy.md]

**The Sales While Loop — When to Hire** — Don't hire a VP of sales first. Founders should close 20-30 deals themselves (15-25% win rate across 50-100 at-bats). Then hire 2 early-stage AEs (not a VP), package your sales motion into collateral and scripts, and teach them. Once they're successful, scale to 4, then 8. Early-stage sales requires 'grimier, grittier' sellers who can operate without polished infrastructure — deputies from later-stage orgs are ideal. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/pete-kazanjy.md]

**Modern Sales Is Analytical — The Moneyball Shift** — Pete argues sales has undergone a Moneyball-style transformation: CRM, email, calendar, and Zoom data now make selling as measurable as product analytics. Salespeople are 'microeconomic agents bringing supply to demand.' The skill is learnable — 'there's a lot of people out there who would love to tell you a story that it's magical, and it's really not.' ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/pete-kazanjy.md]

**Rapid Rapport and the Mindset Shift** — Sales requires meeting multiple new humans daily — a radical shift from the maker schedule. Pete's advice: practice rapid rapport on bartenders, baristas, flight attendants. Get comfortable asking uncomfortable questions, then asking for money and shutting up. The core mindset: you're a consultant with a predilection for your solution. If someone isn't ICP, don't sell to them — you're not 'selling ice to an Eskimo.' Recommended reading: 'The Goal' (Goldratt) for systems thinking, 'The Score Takes Care of Itself' (Bill Walsh) for leading-indicator focus. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/pete-kazanjy.md]
