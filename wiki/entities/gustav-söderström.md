# Gustav Söderström

Gustav Söderström is Co-President and Chief Product and Technology Officer at Spotify, responsible for Spotify's global product and technology strategy. He joined Spotify in 2009 to lead mobile and eventually took on all of product development and technology. He is one of the longest-tenured senior leaders at Spotify, having helped take the company from a Swedish startup to a global public company.

## Key Views

### Curation → Recommendation → Generation

Gustav's framework for the internet's evolution: first came curation (users digitized and organized content — Facebook, Spotify). Then came recommendation (algorithms replaced user curation). Now we're entering generation (AI creates content). Each shift required a complete rethink of user experience and business models. He believes the transition to generative AI will be as transformative as the shift from curation to recommendation, and most products aren't ready for it.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gustav-söderström.md]

### Every Six to Twelve Months, It's a New Company

Gustav describes his 14+ years at Spotify as working at a new company every 6-12 months. Despite having the same title and reporting to the same CEO, the job transformed as Spotify grew: from a Swedish/Nordic challenge → European challenge → US market entry → public company. His lesson: in fast-growing companies, your job inevitably changes. The people who thrive are those who embrace constant reinvention rather than protecting their original role.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gustav-söderström.md]

### Product Strategy Requires Big Bets

Gustav is known for Spotify's "big bets" approach — making concentrated bets on product directions rather than spreading resources thinly. This requires conviction and the willingness to be wrong publicly. He emphasizes that big bets aren't reckless: they're calculated, informed by deep user understanding, and backed by the team's conviction. The real risk is making no bets at all.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gustav-söderström.md]

### Empathy Through Creation

Gustav started an internal podcast to build culture and help employees understand Spotify's strategy. The experience taught him invaluable lessons about being a creator — from navigating music rights to understanding production challenges. He believes leaders should create something their company makes to truly empathize with their users and creators. "If you want to understand podcasters, make a podcast."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gustav-söderström.md]

### Moving Beyond Squads

Spotify famously popularized the "squad" model, but Gustav reveals they've evolved beyond it. As the company grew, the pure squad model had limitations — coordination overhead, lack of strategic alignment, and difficulty making cross-cutting changes. Spotify's current structure is more nuanced, reflecting that organizational models must evolve as companies scale. The lesson: no organizational model is permanent.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gustav-söderström.md]
