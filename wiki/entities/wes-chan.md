# Wes Chan

Co-Founder and Managing Partner @ FPV Ventures, a $450M early-stage fund. Investor in five $10B+ "decacorns," most notably Canva where he is a board member and led the Series A and C rounds. Also wrote early checks into Plaid, Flexport, Gusto, Lucid, and RobinHood. Previously Managing Director @ Felicis Ventures and founded GV's seed investing program.

## Key Views

### 1. Market Always Wins Over the Founder

No matter how talented the founder, if the market is small or dying, the company will fail. Wes prioritizes market size and timing over team quality — a great founder in a massive market will always outperform a mediocre founder in a small one. He explicitly does not do market sizing as a top-down exercise; instead he looks for markets that are clearly large based on observable demand.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2022-08-22-20vc-why-market-always-wins-o.md]

### 2. Why I Do Not Do Market Sizing

Traditional market sizing (TAM/SAM/SOM) is often fabricated to fit a narrative. Wes believes the best markets are obvious — you can feel the pull of demand without needing a spreadsheet. If a market needs creative math to look big, it's probably not big enough.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2022-08-22-20vc-why-market-always-wins-o.md]

### 3. The Best Founders Have 100 Year Plans

The truly exceptional founders think in decades, not quarters. They have a long-term vision for what they're building and are playing a game that compounds over time. This long-term thinking is what allows them to make short-term sacrifices that most founders won't.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2022-08-22-20vc-why-market-always-wins-o.md]

### 4. Not the Best Time to be Investing, But the Best Time to Have a Fund

Market dislocations create the best vintage years for fund returns. When everyone else is scared to deploy capital, the best investors are finding the best deals at the best prices. Having a fund ready to deploy during downturns is the single biggest driver of long-term returns.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2022-08-22-20vc-why-market-always-wins-o.md]
