# Micha Kauffman

Founder and CEO of Fiverr, the leading online marketplace for freelance services. Fiverr went public in 2019 with a $650M market cap, peaked at over $8BN, and as of the episode sits at $1.121BN on estimated $430M revenues. Prior to Fiverr, founded and led several startups over 30 years.

## Key Views

### 1. "If You're Not Adapting to AI, F* You. You're Done."

Kauffman is blunt about the AI disruption facing marketplaces and knowledge work. Companies that don't fundamentally rethink their value proposition in an AI world will be obsoleted. Fiverr itself is adapting by helping freelancers use AI tools to deliver more value.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2025-06-09-20vc-fiverr-ceo-if-youre-n.md]

### 2. "Time to Copy" is the Most Important Metric in Startups Today

In the AI era, the speed at which competitors can copy your product is the most critical competitive metric. If your moat can be replicated in weeks, not years, your business is fragile. The only durable moats are network effects, brand trust, and proprietary data — not features.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2025-06-09-20vc-fiverr-ceo-if-youre-n.md]

### 3. Why 99% of AI Companies Today Will Die

Most AI startups are building thin wrappers on top of foundation models with zero defensibility. They will be crushed when the models improve or the platforms integrate similar features. The 1% that survive will have real distribution, unique data, or deep workflow integration.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2025-06-09-20vc-fiverr-ceo-if-youre-n.md]

### 4. Why Governments Will Take Control of AI

As AI becomes central to economic and national security, governments will inevitably regulate and control it. Companies building in AI need to factor in regulatory risk and compliance as a core strategic consideration, not an afterthought.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2025-06-09-20vc-fiverr-ceo-if-youre-n.md]
