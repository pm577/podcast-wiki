# Claire Butler

*Figma's first GTM hire (10th employee). Led Figma's go-to-market from stealth through monetization over 8+ years. Built the bottom-up community-led motion that powered Figma's growth.*

## Key Views

1. **Win ICs first, let them champion internally**: Figma's GTM motion centered on individual contributors (designers) who loved the tool so much they became internal champions. They'd spread Figma within their companies, put it on their credit cards, and eventually pull in sales for org-wide deployment. No sales team for the first 3 years — all self-serve.

2. **You can't optimize your way to product-market fit**: In early stages, metrics are meaningless because numbers are too small. Signal matters more — like a designer physically pulling the laptop from Dylan Field's hands because they wanted to play with it. One anecdote of genuine delight is worth more than a 5% conversion improvement.

3. **Launch without your key differentiator if there's enough magic**: Figma launched without multiplayer (their core differentiator) because the team needed momentum. The test: were designers excited when they saw the tool? Once people were literally grabbing the laptop, that emotional response was enough signal to launch even without the headline feature.

4. **Bottoms-up means inbound leads, not outbound spend**: Figma never spent programmatically on paid marketing. All sales leads came through a form on the website from users who were already using the free or pro tier and wanted help getting their whole company on board. The sales conversation became "unblock" rather than "sell."

5. **Quality obsession with the core tool drives everything**: For a tool designers use 8 hours a day, saving one click is huge. The editor — the core craft — is where love starts. Collaboration is the differentiator people talk about, but nobody wants to "learn about collaboration." They want the tool to work. Excellence in the core experience is non-negotiable.
