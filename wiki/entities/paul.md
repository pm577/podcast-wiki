# Paul Millerd

Paul Millerd is the author of *The Pathless Path: Imagining a New Story for Work and Life*, a self-published book that has sold over 40,000 copies. He spent years in strategy consulting before walking away in 2017 to explore a different relationship with work. His book has struck a chord within the tech community, where many are questioning the default path of continuous employment and corporate ascent.

## Key Views

### The Default Path vs. The Pathless Path

Paul defines the default path as "the story in your head about what you should do" — go to college, get good grades, get a good job, work continuously for all of adulthood. The pathless path is a shift away from this script: "a shift away from not knowing what you're doing as a problem to be solved toward an embrace of discomfort and uncertainty." The key is moving from operating from scarcity to operating from abundance.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/paul-millerd.md]

### You Can Design Around Liking Work

Paul's biggest personal realization: "You can design around liking work." He spent 32 years assuming work was supposed to suck and you just tolerated it. After leaving his job, he discovered he genuinely loved writing and creating — not for fame or money, but because the process itself was joyful. "The goal is not to find a job, make money, build a business, or achieve any other metric. It's to actively and consciously search for the work that you want to keep doing."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/paul-millerd.md]

### The Three-Hour Sabbatical (and the Real One)

For people who can't take months off, Paul recommends a "three-hour sabbatical" — block off a workday afternoon, sneak out, and do something from your childhood. Pay attention to how you feel. "Do you feel bad for sneaking out of work? Where does that bad feeling come from? What does that mean about your definition of work?" For those who can, a three-month sabbatical (out of ~500 working months in adulthood) has a 99.9% approval rating. It takes 6-8 weeks just to unwind.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/paul-millerd.md]

### Money Fears Are Scripts, Not Truths

The hidden assumption most people hold is that if they leave the default path, they'll run out of money. Paul pushes back by exploring the actual costs of living and the many ways to make work sustainable. "A lot of people are looking for meaning through work in a job-shaped container. And I think that's just really hard to find. You can find it for short bursts, but maybe after seven or 10 years, it's just not doing it anymore."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/paul-millerd.md]

### Sabbaticals Are More Attainable Than You Think

Paul has talked to 500+ people about their relationship with work. His consistent finding: companies are desperate to keep people and much more open to sabbaticals than most assume. "Three months in a total of 500 months — people accomplish much more impressive things than that, like saving for their dream home." He argues that creating space for exploration is more important than ownership, marriage, or any other milestone.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/paul-millerd.md]
