# Donna Lichaw

*Executive coach, keynote speaker, and bestselling author of The Leader's Journey. Helps founders, CEOs, and executive teams level up leadership skills. Former product manager and designer. Clients include Google, Disney, Twitter, Microsoft, and Adobe.*

## Key Views

1. **Great leaders start with themselves, not their audience**: Conventional wisdom says "it's not about you, it's about your team." Donna flips this: leadership starts with the inner circle (you), extends to one-on-one relationships, then to teams, and finally to the business. You can't lead others effectively until you can lead yourself.

2. **The most powerful stories are the ones we tell ourselves**: Our brains don't distinguish between true stories and the narratives we construct. The stories we tell ourselves — "I'll never succeed," "she'll never go for it" — shape our reality. Leverage this: choose to be the hero of your own story rather than the victim.

3. **Pull your superpowers from your past stories**: When superheroes discover their powers, they initially wreak havoc. It's uncomfortable. To identify your real strengths, examine your past and present stories. What patterns emerge? What came naturally? Then transpose those superpowers to your future challenges.

4. **Storytelling alone won't fix broken relationships**: A team in conflict doesn't need better presentation skills — they need to connect as humans first. No one wants to hear your stories if they feel unheard or dismissed. Effective leadership requires building genuine connection before any narrative technique can work.

5. **Imposter syndrome can be reframed as a signal of growth**: Instead of fighting imposter feelings, recognize them as evidence that you're stretching beyond your comfort zone. The discomfort means you're in a learning/growing phase. Use it as fuel rather than letting it paralyze you.
