# Teresa Torres

Teresa Torres appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss teresa torres, author of continuous discovery habits, on product discovery, opportunity solution trees, and coaching product teams.

## Key Views

**The Opportunity Solution Tree** — A visual framework that connects an outcome at the root to the opportunity space (unmet needs, pain points, desires) and then to possible solutions and assumption tests. The hardest part is staying in the problem space: Torres says 98% of people who write opportunities write them as solutions, because our brains are wired to jump to answers. By decomposing the opportunity space using an experience map (e.g., for Netflix: trigger to deciding what to watch to viewing experience to post-viewing), teams get a big-picture view of where to play while also finding small, actionable opportunities they can actually address. As you move vertically down the tree, opportunities get smaller, enabling a continuous cadence of discovery alongside delivery. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/teresa-torres.md]

**Interview by collecting stories, not facts** — Most product teams ask direct, out-of-context questions like 'What do you like to watch on Netflix?' which yield unreliable, surface-level answers. Instead, Torres teaches asking one opening question — 'Tell me about the last time you...' — and then simply helping the person walk through their timeline: 'What happened next? Set the scene for me.' Grounding the conversation in a specific past instance reveals needs the customer isn't even aware of (because 'we are so used to everything being mediocre'), and the interview should feel like having a beer with a buddy. The sign of a good interview is the customer saying, 'Wow, when can we do this again?' ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/teresa-torres.md]

**Automate recruiting to make interviews a recurring meeting** — Inspired by nudge theory, Torres argues you should design the choice architecture so that interviewing every week is easier than not doing it. The simplest method: embed an opt-in in your product — instead of an NPS survey, ask 'Do you have 20 minutes to talk to us?' and let scheduling software put the interview on your calendar automatically. For B2B buyers and decision-makers, have sales, account management, or support teams recruit during their existing calls by defining a weekly trigger (e.g., 'someone experiencing this pain point'). The goal is to wake up on Monday morning with an interview already scheduled, with no effort from the product team. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/teresa-torres.md]

**The product trio (PM, designer, engineer) should decide together, not the PM as CEO** — Torres rejects the 'PM is the decider' model as a symptom of toxic business culture that reinforces silos and office politics. She advocates for a well-functioning trio that works from shared understanding, where disagreements signal that the team hasn't found the best option yet — so they keep searching until they do. She references the marshmallow tower experiment, where kindergartners outperform MBA students because they just start doing instead of negotiating power and roles. Torres estimates that only about 2% of the industry has experienced a truly well-functioning trio, but she has seen it work repeatedly. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/teresa-torres.md]

**Discovery and delivery are concurrent, not sequential — everything is a bet** — The most common mistake teams make is treating discovery as a phase that must finish before delivery can start. Torres argues everything in a backlog is a bet; discovery just helps you make a better one. Never say 'Let us stop making bets until we discover' — that kills any appetite for discovery. Instead, keep delivering while starting discovery in parallel, and your bets will improve over time. Assumption testing is not separate from delivery — it is the start of delivery. Small data is valid because product teams are in the business of changing behavior (not seeking proof) and have strong feedback loops: make a small bet, ship it, and get larger-scale data over time. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/teresa-torres.md]
