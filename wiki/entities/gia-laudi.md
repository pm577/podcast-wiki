# Gia Laudi (Georgiana Laudi)

Gia Laudi is the co-founder and CEO of Forget The Funnel, a consulting agency that helps SaaS companies unlock and accelerate growth through customer-led growth. She previously led marketing at Unbounce and has worked with dozens of B2B SaaS companies to double and triple conversion rates through customer-centric approaches.

## Key Views

### Funnels Are Bad: The Case for Customer-Led Growth

Gia argues that traditional funnel models (including pirate metrics and MQLs/SQLs) put the business at the center instead of the customer. They group all customers into the same buckets, ignore post-acquisition retention and expansion, and "feel gross" — treating customers as objects to push through a pipeline. For recurring revenue businesses, growth doesn't end at acquisition; the model must account for the entire customer lifecycle.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gia-laudi.md]

### Map the Customer's Journey, Not the Funnel

Gia's alternative: instead of measuring a funnel, map the actual journey your most important customers take through your product. Identify the moments that matter — where they get value, where they get stuck, where they invite others. Then set goals and execute on improving those specific moments. This approach naturally surfaces the highest-impact opportunities, which are often not where the traditional funnel says they should be.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gia-laudi.md]

### Identify Your Most Important Customers First

Before optimizing anything, Gia advises identifying which customers matter most to your business — not just who pays the most, but who stays the longest, refers the most, and provides the best feedback. Then deeply understand their journey. Most growth efforts fail because they optimize for the average customer when they should be optimizing for the ideal customer.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gia-laudi.md]

### The Unlock Is Often in Post-Acquisition

Gia's biggest growth unlocks for clients typically come not from acquisition channels but from retention, expansion, and activation improvements. Most companies over-invest in top-of-funnel and under-invest in the experience after signup. She frequently finds 2-3x improvements in conversion simply by removing friction from onboarding and making the "aha moment" more immediate.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gia-laudi.md]

### Customer-Led Growth Requires Organizational Empathy

Customer-led growth isn't just a strategy — it's a mindset shift that requires the entire organization to empathize with customers. Gia believes growth teams should spend time in customer support, watch session replays, and actually use their own product as a new user would. This organizational empathy is what separates companies that optimize metrics from companies that genuinely improve customer experiences.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gia-laudi.md]

## Notable Quotes

> "The problem with funnels and pirate metrics... is that nobody knows what those mean. It puts every customer in the same sort of buckets."

> "Recurring revenue businesses, you cannot think about marketing and growth and the business overall as ending at acquisition, otherwise you're not in business anymore."
