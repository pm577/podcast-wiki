# Garrett Lord

Garrett Lord is the co-founder and CEO of Handshake, a career network for college students and new grads that has over 1,500 colleges, 20 million students/alumni, and 1 million companies. The company recently discovered that its proprietary network of students is extremely valuable to AI labs for creating high-quality training data, launching a new business that hit $50M ARR in just four months.

## Key Views

### The Only Moat in Human Data Is Access to an Audience

Handshake's AI data business succeeded because they already had what no one else could replicate: millions of highly educated students, including tens of thousands of PhDs and master's candidates. "The only moat in human data is access to an audience." While competitors could try to recruit experts, Handshake already had a deeply engaged audience with zero customer acquisition cost for this new line of business.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/garrett-lord.md]

### Post-Training Is Where All the Gains Are Now

Garrett explains that frontier AI labs have hit diminishing returns on pre-training (sucking up all internet data). The massive gains now come from post-training — augmenting and improving data across specific domains like coding, mathematics, law, and finance. This involves running experiments like a scientific process: hypothesize, collect small data samples, test, and iterate. Handshake's Data Assist business provides this critical post-training data.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/garrett-lord.md]

### AI-Native Young People Have a Massive Advantage

Counter to fears about AI replacing jobs, Garrett argues that being AI-native gives young people a huge advantage. "You used to put 'Google Search' on a skill on your resume because you grew up with Google. Being AI native, young people are at a huge advantage." Employers report that AI enables human beings to be even more productive — it augments rather than replaces early-career talent.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/garrett-lord.md]

### Unlimited Demand Creates a Unique Business Moment

Garrett describes the AI data market as having "unlimited demand" — something he's never experienced before. The strategic question shifts from "how do we find customers?" to "how do we ensure we have no regrets in 3-6 months?" His advice: "Get on the plane to go talk to a customer, make the late night push, check the data six times over again." The scarcity is supply-side, which is a CEO's dream scenario.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/garrett-lord.md]

### Disrupt Yourself Before Someone Else Does

Handshake is the rare example of a company successfully disrupting itself. Their decade-old recruiting business is profitable and growing, but their new AI data business will exceed it in revenue within two years. Garrett didn't wait for competitors to eat into their business — they proactively found the adjacent opportunity by understanding what their proprietary asset (student audience) was actually worth in a new market.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/garrett-lord.md]

## Notable Quotes
