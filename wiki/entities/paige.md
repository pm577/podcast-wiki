# Paige Costello

Paige Costello is the Head of Core Product at Asana, leading teams responsible for the core product experience across desktop, web, and mobile. She previously served as Director of Product at Intercom and Group Product Manager at Intuit. Known as a remarkable coach and mentor, she is deeply respected for her systematic approach to product development and her ability to build trust with skeptics.

## Key Views

### Bring the Insight to Earn Trust

Paige's advice for earning trust when you're the youngest/least experienced person in the room: "Bring the insight. Know thy customer. Know thy market. Know thy competitors. Know thy numbers. Know thy product." Preparation and depth of understanding trump seniority every time. She also recommends challenging scarcity mindset by asking "How might the opposite be true?" — which instantly opens new possibilities.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/paige-costello.md]

### The Double Diamond Process at Asana

Asana uses the Double Diamond framework — going broad then narrow, twice. First: pick the customer and problem (broad→narrow). Second: explore solutions (broad→narrow). Key review inflection points are: kickoff, customer/direction selection, design concept review, product spec, full experience review, and launch. "It forces people to get out of their opinion-driven lens because so often, we need to be curious quantitatively and qualitatively about what we're doing."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/paige-costello.md]

### Rolling 12-Month Planning at Six-Month Cadence

Asana shifted from annual planning to planning every six months for a rolling 12 months. Higher confidence in the immediate half, lower in the following half. "The more you try to do things in a joined up way where you have a single target customer with sales and marketing, the more it's important to reflect on it frequently and to be able to pivot quickly." This reduces thrash and prevents teams from sticking to outdated roadmaps.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/paige-costello.md]

### Pillar → Area → Team: Nested Strategy and Metrics

As Asana scaled, they introduced an intermediate "area" layer between pillars and teams. Each area has a specific target customer and problem space, with its own metrics nested within pillar-level and R&D-level metrics. "We've had to reorganize to create more agency and accountability close to teams that are focused on specific target customers and problems." This prevents feature-based roadmaps and keeps the focus on outcomes.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/paige-costello.md]

### Lead by Example as a Coach

Paige believes the most effective way to coach PMs is leading by example — demonstrating the behavior, the curiosity, and the rigor you want to see. She emphasizes that product thinking must always "start with enough customer insight such that we can creatively do what they're trying to do" — a principle she models in every review and planning cycle.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/paige-costello.md]
