# Julie Zhuo

Julie Zhuo appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss the making of a manager, ai, and the future of product design.

## Key Views

**Management is about outcomes, not just people** — The core skills of defining a goal, understanding your resources' strengths, and designing the right process apply whether your resources are people or AI models. "Management is just about... having an outcome... you have a north star, you have a vision, and you're just trying to figure out how to use the resources that you have to get that thing done. Used to be people, but now it's basically models." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/julie-zhuo.md]

**Diagnose with data, treat with design** — Data tells you what's happening and where the problems are, but creative design still determines the solution. "Data is not a tool that's going to tell you what you should build... what you really want is you want to diagnose with data and treat with design." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/julie-zhuo.md]

**Be sturdy while flexible — the willow tree** — In an era of accelerating change, managers must be both resilient and adaptable. "Today management is really about this idea of be sturdy while being flexible. So I think about this metaphor a lot of the willow tree. It can survive a lot of storms, disasters, et cetera, but it's also very flexible." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/julie-zhuo.md]

**Dissolve traditional role boundaries — everyone is a builder** — AI empowers individuals to work across multiple disciplines, making rigid role distinctions obsolete. "We need to dissolve the boundaries of these traditional roles... call ourselves builders. I'd love for us to get to the world where that's the title." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/julie-zhuo.md]

**Feedback is a daily practice, not a biannual ritual** — The best tool for getting better as a team is continuous feedback, and giving it vulnerably builds trust. "Feedback... ideally, should be a daily practice. Because the thing that matters for us in the long run as a team is how quickly are we getting better?" ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/julie-zhuo.md]
