# Dalton Caldwell

*Managing Director and Group Partner at Y Combinator (10+ years, 21 batches). Worked with Instacart, Retool, Brex, DoorDash, Webflow, Replit, Amplitude, and 20+ other unicorns in their earliest days. Former founder of imeem and App.net.*

## Key Views

1. **"Just don't die" is the most powerful startup advice**: The single most impactful thing Dalton tells founders is "don't give up." Like a basketball coach in the huddle saying "we need to get the ball and win," this sounds simple — but the constant affirmation to keep going, keep doing high-quality reps, is what separates successful founders. Most unicorns should have rationally shut down 3-4 times.

2. **A good pivot gets warmer, not colder**: The most successful pivots build on what the founders are genuinely expert at, not what seems most exciting. Brex pivoted from a VR headset company to a fintech because the founders had fintech experience. Retool pivoted from a P2P Venmo competitor to internal tools because they'd built dozens of dashboards for their own startup.

3. **The worst companies in the batch often become the best**: In Dalton's Winter-17 batch, the two companies that seemed objectively worst (Vyond/Brex and Cashew/Retool) became the most successful. When everyone else is struggling and the founders themselves look despondent, that's often where the breakthrough pivot is brewing — not a signal to quit.

4. **"Are you still having fun?" is the key signal to keep going vs. quit**: If founders love their customers, love working with each other, and love the problem — even when everything is going badly — that's a signal to keep going (like Airbnb early days). If they dread it, hate their co-founder, and don't care about the problem, the opportunity cost of continuing is too high.

5. **Tar pit ideas seem unsolved but never work**: Some ideas (like certain social networks or payments startups) have been tried since the '90s and get endless positive feedback but never break through. Founders confuse "lots of people agree this is a problem" with "this is a solvable problem." Dalton shares 20+ categories of ideas YC is actually looking to fund to reset founders' information diet.
