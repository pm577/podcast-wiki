# David Singleton

David Singleton is Chief Technology Officer at Stripe, overseeing engineering and design. A former VP of Engineering at Google (10+ years), he has been Stripe's CTO for over five years, shaping one of the most admired engineering cultures in tech.

## Key Views

### Co-Create Products with Early Users
Stripe's product development approach: find the right set of early users and co-create the product with them. For Stripe Billing, they partnered with Figma and Slack — existing users pushing the boundaries of subscriptions. They had shared Slack channels, showed product regularly, and only went broader when the alpha group was "super, super happy." Every Stripe engineer exercises many of the attributes you'd find in PMs at other companies. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/david-singleton.md]

### Engineers as Product People
Stripe held off on hiring its first PM for many years because engineers were expected to develop product instincts. Every engineer building product at Stripe works directly with users, forms hypotheses, and validates solutions. This creates a culture of ownership where engineers don't just implement specs — they understand the customer problem deeply. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/david-singleton.md]

### Friction Logging — A Stripe Internal Practice
Teams at Stripe practice "friction logging" — systematically documenting every point of friction in their tools, processes, and workflows. These logs are reviewed regularly and used to drive continuous improvement. The practice ensures that the team's internal efficiency improves at the same rate as the product. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/david-singleton.md]

### Be Meticulous in Your Craft
One of Stripe's operating principles operationalized through "engineer occasions" — structured opportunities for engineers to practice and demonstrate craftsmanship. David believes that attention to detail in code, design, and user experience is what separates good companies from great ones. Excellence is not a one-time achievement but an ongoing discipline. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/david-singleton.md]

### Walking the Store
Stripe's practice of "walking the store" — regularly experiencing the product as a customer would — keeps leadership connected to user friction. Leaders at all levels are expected to use Stripe's products regularly, identify pain points, and escalate them. This prevents the disconnect between what leadership thinks the product is like and what users actually experience. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/david-singleton.md]
