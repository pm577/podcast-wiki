# Noam Lovinsky

Noam Lovinsky appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss noam lovinsky, product leader (youtube, thumbtack), on organizational advocacy, channel risk, growth masks problems, and career stretching.

## Key Views

**Advocate for the Organization, Not Just Your Team** — Noam convinced YouTube leadership to reassign his own 50-person engineering team to higher-priority projects just months after joining, effectively negotiating himself out of a role. This radical honesty demonstrated organizational thinking, and leadership rewarded it by putting him in charge of the creator focus area. In healthy cultures, doing what's best for the business — even at your own expense — builds trust and opens better opportunities. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/noam-lovinsky.md]

**Don't Be Afraid to Layer Yourself for Growth** — At YouTube, Noam was struggling reporting directly to the CEO and proactively asked to report to Hunter Walk instead, an atypical demotion in reporting structure. This allowed him to learn faster, get better support, and ultimately grow into leading the entire viewer PM team. Restructuring yourself for better mentorship and impact, even if it looks like a step back, can accelerate your career. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/noam-lovinsky.md]

**One-Channel Growth Is a Sword That Cuts Both Ways** — Thumbtack's heavy reliance on SEO led to negative year-over-year growth after Google algorithm changes. Noam's hard-learned lesson: one-channel growth is always a no-no. Companies must diversify growth channels (paid, referrals, organic) before one dries up, and leaders should maintain paranoia about over-concentration in any single channel. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/noam-lovinsky.md]

**Growth Masks All Problems** — During Thumbtack's triple-digit growth phase, severe supply-demand liquidity issues were ignored. It took growth going negative to force honest prioritization and rebuild the core product (shifting to instant quotes, changing the monetization model). Going through a downturn often builds a healthier, more sustainable business by surfacing the real issues that hypergrowth hides. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/noam-lovinsky.md]

**Put Yourself in Positions That Stretch You, but Keep One Foot on Solid Ground** — Prioritize situations that force growth and learning, even when painful. But ensure you have 1-2 foundational strengths you can lean on while stretching into new areas. If everything is net new, the pain becomes overwhelming. The cave you fear contains the treasure you seek — but you need a base camp to return to. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/noam-lovinsky.md]
