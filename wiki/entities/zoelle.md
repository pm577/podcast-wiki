# Zoelle Egner

**Role:** Early employee (#11) at Airtable where she built and led marketing and customer success; later Head of Marketing & Growth at Block Party  
**Known for:** Airtable's unconventional growth strategy, early-stage startup marketing tactics, VaccinateCA nonprofit work.

## Key Views

### 1. Invest in quality and craft even when you're small

Zoelle's counterintuitive advice for early-stage startups: don't ship things that look sloppy just because you're small. Sample content matters — don't use "Jane Doe" 12 times in your productivity app. Use references your target audience will recognize and appreciate. Small investments in quality signal that you built the product with the customer in mind, which builds trust and brand personality.

> "Take the time to not have it be like Jane Doe 12 times in the name list. Have it be references to your industry so that people are like, 'Oh, hey. That's a joke about Steve Jobs. This person is thinking about me.'"

### 2. When you're small, you need everyone rooting for you

Brand personality and trust are survival tools for early-stage startups. Every touchpoint — from email copy to sample content to customer support interactions — should make people want to root for you. This "rooting factor" is how small companies win against incumbents who have more resources but less personality.

> "When you are small, you need everyone rooting for you that you can possibly get."

### 3. Unconventional marketing can outperform conventional channels

Airtable's growth strategy included tactics like billboards, PR launches, and word-of-mouth programs that seemed unconventional for a B2B SaaS product. Zoelle's lesson: don't default to the standard SaaS playbook (content marketing, paid search, etc.). The best growth channels are often the ones your competitors aren't using.

### 4. PR and launches are underutilized by startups

Many startups dismiss PR as something for enterprise companies. Zoelle argues the opposite: a well-timed product launch or PR story can be one of the highest-ROI activities for an early-stage company, creating a surge of awareness that no amount of paid spend can replicate. The key is having a real story to tell, not just a press release.

### 5. Customer success is a growth function

At Airtable, Zoelle treated customer success as a growth driver, not just a support cost. Happy customers generate referrals, case studies, and word-of-mouth — all of which are higher-LTV acquisition channels than paid marketing. Investing in customer success early pays compounding returns.
