# Aaron Levie

Co-Founder and CEO of Box, the cloud content management platform. Box does over $1BN in revenue with a market cap of $3.85BN, having raised over $560M from DFJ, Andreessen Horowitz, Coatue, and others. Levie founded Box from his dorm room at the University of Southern California and has led the company to ~1,900 employees. One of the OG founders of the SaaS generation.

## Key Views

### 1. Founders Cannot Hedge Their Bets

Levie argues that one of the biggest mistakes founders make is trying to hedge their bets. When a founder is not fully committed, the company suffers from lack of conviction, slow decision-making, and diluted culture. He distinguishes between two categories of wrong decisions: those made with conviction (fixable) and those made from hedging (usually fatal).

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2022-02-11-20vc-boxs-aaron-levie-on-why.md]

### 2. The Biggest Danger of Being Over-Funded

Levie discusses the dangers of raising too much capital as a startup. Over-funding can mask underlying product-market fit issues, lead to bloated teams, and create a culture where the urgency to find product-market fit is replaced by the comfort of a large bank account. He believes the most dangerous thing for a startup is having enough cash to avoid making hard decisions.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2022-02-11-20vc-boxs-aaron-levie-on-why.md]

### 3. Predictions for the AI Wave: Foundation Models Will Commoditise

Levie predicts that foundation models will ultimately become commoditized, and the value will accrue at the application layer. He discusses how the business model of SaaS is changing forever due to AI — startups vs. incumbents, app layer vs. infrastructure — arguing that we are in a transformative window where incumbents who move fast can retain their edge against AI-native startups.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2024-05-22-20vc-boxs-aaron-levie-on-pre.md]

### 4. What It Takes to Succeed in the AI Transformation

Levie outlines the key success factors for the current AI wave: speed of adaptation, willingness to cannibalize existing revenue, deep enterprise relationships, and the ability to integrate AI into existing workflows rather than expecting users to adopt entirely new behaviors.
