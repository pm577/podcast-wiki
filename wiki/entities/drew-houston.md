# Drew Houston

*Co-founder and CEO of Dropbox (700M+ registered users, $9B+ valuation). Built one of the most iconic viral growth machines. Navigated competition from Apple, Microsoft, and Google. Reinvented Dropbox for the future of work.*

## Key Views

1. **Viral growth came from focusing on one customer: himself**: Dropbox started from Drew's personal frustration with file syncing. He didn't start with a growth strategy — he started by scratching his own itch. The famous demo video was a byproduct of wanting to explain a technical product simply, not a calculated marketing move.

2. **When incumbents attack, the narrative flips overnight**: After years of explosive growth, Apple, Microsoft, and Google all launched competing products. Google Photos offered free unlimited storage — "nuking" Dropbox's business model. The hardest part wasn't the competitive pressure — it was that employees stopped wanting to wear the company T-shirt, and the narrative completely flipped against them.

3. **Killing beloved products (Mailbox, Carousel) was the right painful call**: Dropbox had to kill Mailbox and Carousel to go all-in on productivity. It was the right move but felt terrible at the time. The lesson: when facing existential threats, you must make painful portfolio cuts that feel like admitting defeat but are actually strategic focus.

4. **The founder's irrational persistence is what separates survivors from quitters**: Dropbox should have died multiple times. The early YC demo was just a video — no product existed. The competitors had infinite resources. The only thing that kept it going was Drew's willingness to keep going when rational analysis said quit. "Just don't die" is the most powerful strategy.

5. **Reinvention requires accepting you're no longer the underdog**: Dropbox's third era required letting go of the startup identity and embracing being an incumbent. The shift from "cool startup fighting giants" to "mature company finding its next act" was psychologically the hardest transition — but necessary for survival.
