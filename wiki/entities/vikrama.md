# Vikrama Dhiman

**Role:** Head of Product at Gojek (overseeing PM, design, program management, and research across Indonesia, Singapore, and India)  
**Known for:** Building strong product talent in Asia, creating the "Three W's" framework for PM career growth.

## Key Views

### 1. The Three W's framework for PM career growth

Vikrama's career growth framework for product managers comprises three dimensions: **What you produce** (outputs → outcomes → impact), **What you bring to the table** (your unique skills, domain expertise, and perspective), and **What's your operating model** (how you work with teams, navigate ambiguity, and drive alignment). Strong PMs excel at two of three; those with rapid growth perform well on all three.

> "The really strong product managers who were also growing in their careers did some things differently. Based on that, I created a career growth framework for product managers which comprises of three things: what you produce, what you bring to the table, and what's your operating model."

### 2. Early career: focus on outputs, not just outcomes

Early in your PM career, focus on producing tangible artifacts — PRDs, product strategy docs, analysis, launched features. As you grow, shift to owning outcomes and goals. Later, move to impact — the broader business results. Vikrama emphasizes that even a "cool product" won't guarantee growth if you're not producing visible, high-quality outputs.

> "Can you show me your last PRD? Can you show me the last product note that you sent? Can you show me the product strategy doc? You must have that impact through the artifacts that you work on."

### 3. Three internal narratives that impede growth

Vikrama has identified three "stories" people tell themselves that block career growth: (1) how you view change (resisting vs. embracing it), (2) whether you focus on things you can control vs. things you can't, and (3) how you see yourself (fixed vs. growth mindset). Correcting these internal narratives puts you back on a growth path.

> "How you view change, whether you are focusing on things you control, and third is how you see yourself. The moment you are able to correct those stories, you may be back on the growth path again."

### 4. Great PMs are good at at least two of the three W's

The framework isn't about being perfect at everything — it's about having sufficient strength in at least two dimensions while not being weak in the third. This creates a realistic development path for PMs at any stage.

### 5. Operating model determines your ceiling

The "operating model" dimension — how you collaborate, communicate, build consensus, and navigate organizational complexity — often becomes the ceiling for senior PMs. Technical skills and output may be strong, but without a mature operating model, career progression stalls.
