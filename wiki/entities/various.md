# Various (Year-End Review)

**Role:** Year-end compilation episode featuring clips from Lenny's most popular guests of 2022  
**Known for:** Curated highlights from top episodes featuring April Dunford, Crystal Widjaja, Julie Zhuo, Shishir Mehrotra, Kristen Berman, Elena Verna, and more.

## Key Views

### 1. Positioning starts with understanding what you must beat (April Dunford)

April Dunford's five-step positioning framework: (1) Identify competitive alternatives — including the status quo (40% of B2B deals are lost to "no decision"). (2) Determine what makes you different — capabilities the alternatives lack. (3) Translate features into value — "so what?" for each one. (4) Define your best-fit customer — who cares most about that value. (5) Choose your market category — the context that makes your value obvious to those customers.

> "In B2B we lose about 40% of our deals to 'No decision,' which actually means we lost to the spreadsheet, we lost to pen and paper, we lost to interns."

### 2. Instrumentation is about insights, not measurements (Crystal Widjaja)

Most analytics efforts fail because teams treat data gathering as entertainment — collecting interesting observations without acting on them. Crystal distinguishes between a measurement (an observed fact) and an insight (contextualized understanding that changes action). The key to instrumentation is instrumenting properties into events so you can segment behavior, form hypotheses, test them, and get causal understanding.

> "A measurement would be an observation... But it's not an insight because it doesn't have context, it doesn't give you information that lets you act on it."

### 3. Imposter syndrome can be overcome by focusing on impact (Julie Zhuo)

Julie shares that rising through the ranks quickly at Facebook came from focusing on delivering real impact rather than worrying about whether she "deserved" to be there. Her trajectory from designer to VP of design demonstrates that consistent output and taking on increasing responsibility naturally leads to career growth.

### 4. Understand your competitive alternatives beyond direct competition (April Dunford)

A key insight from the compilation: people mess up positioning by thinking only of direct competitors. In reality, you have two sets: status quo (what they're doing now, even if flawed) and the shortlist alternatives. Winning means beating both.

### 5. Data without action is entertainment (Crystal Widjaja)

The framing that resonates most: "Real news is information that changes what you do in the real world. If you don't change what you're doing, what you are doing is just getting entertainment." This applies to everything from analytics dashboards to competitive research.
