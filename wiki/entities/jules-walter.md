# Jules Walter

Jules Walter appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss jules walter, product leader at youtube and slack, on mentorship, pm skills, and building black pms network.

## Key Views

**IQ vs. EQ Skills Progression** — Jules argues PM careers follow a clear arc: early on, focus on IQ skills (execution, product sense, strategy, interviewing). Later, shift to EQ skills (communication, leadership, management, self-awareness). The EQ skills are harder to learn because they're personal — what you need to work on is specific to you and requires honest feedback to discover blind spots. His own pattern: under stress he withdraws, which others read as disengagement. A mentor helped him see this and change it. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/jules-walter.md]

**Mentorship-Driven Learning Loop** — Jules's repeatable approach: (1) define a concrete outcome (e.g. 'drive activation by X% in 6 months'), (2) identify the best people who've solved that problem (e.g. Bangaly Kaba from Facebook growth), (3) ask good questions and learn frameworks, (4) apply with data and research, (5) return to the mentor to iterate. He credits this loop with his transition from knowing nothing about growth to shipping double-digit activation improvements within six months at Slack. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/jules-walter.md]

**Interviewing Is an Undervalued PM Skill** — Jules emphasizes that interviewing is a learnable skill that most people neglect. His advice: do dozens of mock interviews (not just read or practice in your head) with people who are good at interviewing. He himself failed Google interviews multiple times over a decade before succeeding. The barrier is higher for underrepresented folks who face added psychological stress — practice until even your worst performance is good enough. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/jules-walter.md]

**Outcome-Driven Skill Building** — Rather than abstract learning, Jules sets a forcing-function outcome ('my ass is on the line') and works backwards: what questions do I need to answer? Who knows the answers? Read just enough to ask good questions, then go talk to experts. This approach made him learn growth in months, then monetization, then how to build and structure a growth org — each time finding new mentors for the new domain. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/jules-walter.md]

**Community as Infrastructure for Diversity** — Jules co-founded CodePath (training 5,000+ CS students/year at underrepresented universities) and Black PMs Network (started with 15 people in a room, now 1,000+ members). Both grew from his own need — he wished these communities existed when he was earlier in his career. The Black PMs Network now hosts conferences and helps members find community, mentorship, and career growth. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/jules-walter.md]
