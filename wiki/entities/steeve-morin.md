# Steeve Morin

Founder and CEO of ZML, a next-generation inference engine enabling peak performance on a wide range of chips. Previously VP Engineering at Zenly for 7 years, leading engineering to millions of users and an acquisition by Snap. Deep expertise in AI infrastructure, inference optimization, and the economics of compute.

## Key Views

### 1. Google Will Win the AI Arms Race, Not OpenAI

Morin makes a bold prediction that Google, not OpenAI, will ultimately win the AI arms race. His reasoning: Google has the trifecta of product, data, and compute — the three things needed to win. Google's vertically integrated infrastructure (TPUs, data centers, massive user data) gives it structural advantages that pure-play AI companies cannot replicate.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2025-02-24-20vc-why-google-will-win-the.md]

### 2. NVIDIA vs AMD: A Nuanced View

Morin provides a detailed comparison of NVIDIA versus AMD in the AI chip landscape. He explains that while NVIDIA currently dominates training workloads due to CUDA's software moat, AMD has real opportunities in the inference market where raw hardware performance matters more than the software ecosystem.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2025-02-24-20vc-why-google-will-win-the.md]

### 3. The Future of Inference vs Training Economics

Morin breaks down how AI infrastructure needs are diverging between training and inference. Training requires massive parallel compute with long runtimes, while inference demands low latency, high throughput, and cost efficiency. He argues that inference optimizations will become the primary battleground as AI models mature and deploy into production at scale.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2025-02-24-20vc-why-google-will-win-the.md]

### 4. You Must Have Product, Data, and Compute

Morin's core thesis for winning in AI: companies need all three of product, data, and compute. Missing any one of these pillars creates an exploitable weakness. He identifies challenges and innovations in AI hardware — from chip design to cooling — that will determine who captures value in the next wave of AI infrastructure.
