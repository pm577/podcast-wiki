# Sanchan Saxena

VP of Product at Coinbase. Previously Head of Product for all of Airbnb, Head of Product for Instagram Ads, and Director of Product at Yahoo. Known as one of the hardest-working and most beloved product leaders in Silicon Valley, having learned from CEOs including Brian Chesky, Brian Armstrong, Mark Zuckerberg, and Kevin Systrom.

## Key Views

### 1. Stay Close to the Customer — Deliver on Weekends

Before becoming CPO at Uber (referenced in the episode metadata), Saxena would deliver food on weekends to stay connected to the customer experience. The insight: no amount of data analysis or user research replaces the visceral understanding you get from doing the job yourself. Leaders who stay in touch with the frontline make better product decisions.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/sanchan-saxena.md]

### 2. First PM Hire Should Be When the CEO Can't Keep Up

The right time to hire your first PM is when the CEO/founder can no longer personally manage the product backlog, prioritization, and cross-functional coordination. Too early and the PM has nothing to own; too late and organizational chaos sets in. The first PM should take over the operational aspects of product management so the founder can focus on vision.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/sanchan-saxena.md]

### 3. A/B Test When You Have Users; Use Conviction When You Don't

A common mistake: A/B testing too early, before you have enough traffic for statistical significance, or A/B testing vision-level decisions that shouldn't be optimized. Saxena's guideline: use A/B tests for optimization decisions once you have user volume; use conviction and qualitative feedback for new product directions and 0-to-1 decisions.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/sanchan-saxena.md]

### 4. How to Hire Amazing PMs

Saxena looks for: (1) **Curiosity** — genuine interest in understanding users and problems, (2) **Conviction** — willingness to form and defend a point of view, (3) **Communication** — ability to make complex things simple, (4) **Collaboration** — earns trust from engineers and designers. He recommends behavioral questions that probe how candidates have handled ambiguity, disagreement, and failure.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/sanchan-saxena.md]

### 5. Navigating COVID at Airbnb — Leadership Under Extreme Uncertainty

When COVID hit and travel completely stopped, Saxena was at the center of Airbnb's response. The lesson: in moments of existential crisis, focus on what you can control (costs, communication, customer refunds) while preparing for recovery. Airbnb's eventual IPO success was built on the decisions made during those darkest months — protecting the host community was the right long-term bet.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/sanchan-saxena.md]
