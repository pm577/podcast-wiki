# Camille Ricketts

Camille Ricketts was the first marketing hire at Notion and longtime head of marketing, playing a pivotal role in Notion's rise to a $10B business. She previously launched the First Round Review at First Round Capital and worked in communications at early Tesla alongside Elon Musk.

## Key Views

### Content-Market Fit — The Overlooked Parallel to Product-Market Fit
Before you can build a community, you need content-market fit. Even though content feels adjacent to the product, you must identify your audience, understand their deepest needs, and create content that addresses their anxieties and ambitions. The content itself must achieve product-market fit with its own target audience. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/camille-ricketts.md]

### Community-Led Growth as a Real Strategy
Notion's early growth was fueled by community. Rather than spending heavily on ads, Notion invested in superusers who created templates, tutorials, and evangelized the product. The key: when to invest in community. It works when your product has a passionate user base that naturally wants to share. It's not a quick fix — it's a long-term investment in relationships. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/camille-ricketts.md]

### Content as a Product, Not Just Marketing
Camille treats content as a product in itself — with its own roadmap, metrics, and iteration cycle. At First Round Review, she built a media property that became the gold standard for startup content. The insight: great content doesn't just acquire users; it builds a brand that compounds over time. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/camille-ricketts.md]

### Why Notion's Template Ecosystem Worked
Notion's template gallery was more than a feature — it was a growth loop. Users created templates to share, which brought new users into the product, who then created more templates. This combination of user-generated content and product usage created a self-reinforcing growth engine that didn't require paid acquisition. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/camille-ricketts.md]

### Marketing Lessons from Working with Elon Musk
From her time at Tesla, Camille learned the power of mission-driven storytelling. Tesla didn't sell cars — they sold the mission of accelerating sustainable energy. This principle translates to any company: the most effective marketing connects product features to a larger purpose that resonates emotionally with the audience. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/camille-ricketts.md]
