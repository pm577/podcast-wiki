# Paige Costello

Paige Costello appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss paige costello, head of core product at asana, on trust-building, the double diamond product process, and coaching pms.

## Key Views

**Bring the Insight — Trust When You're the Youngest in the Room** — Paige's advice for winning over skeptics when you're less experienced: 'Know thy customer. Know thy market. Know thy competitors. Know thy numbers. Know thy product.' Bring unique insight to every meeting — not by pretending to have more experience, but by being the person who has watched customers use the product and has a point of view. Curiosity + insight wins trust faster than authority. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/paige-costello.md]

**The Trust Equation** — Paige cites the trust framework from Anne Raimondi (First Round): Trust = (Credibility + Reliability + Authenticity) / Perception of Self-Interest. Credibility comes from bringing insight. Reliability is your say-do ratio. Authenticity is vulnerability. And lowering perceived self-interest — proving you're not in it for a hidden agenda — amplifies all three. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/paige-costello.md]

**Double Diamond Product Process at Asana** — Asana uses the Double Diamond framework (broaden then narrow) across four phases: kickoff (who's the customer?), customer/direction selection (which problem?), design concept review (which solution?), then spec and launch. This forces teams out of opinion-driven thinking into systematic, curiosity-driven decision-making. Each artifact maps to a diamond point — ensuring quality of thinking before building. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/paige-costello.md]

**Six-Month Rolling Plans With Pivot Permission** — Asana plans every six months for a rolling 12-month horizon (higher confidence in the near half, lower in the far half). This admits strategy will evolve and reduces thrash. Teams don't commit to specific dates or quarters — they align on success metrics and let teams ship iteratively. The key insight: if a roadmap item feels like it must be shipped regardless of learning, you'll make bad scope decisions. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/paige-costello.md]

**The Three Es: Experience, Exposure, Education** — Paige coaches PMs using this framework. Early careers over-index on education (reading, courses). The real growth comes from experience (doing the work) and exposure (watching great practitioners model behavior). Leading by example — letting PMs see you handle a tough meeting or ask a hard question — is more effective than telling them what to do. Repetition through observation internalizes skills. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/paige-costello.md]
