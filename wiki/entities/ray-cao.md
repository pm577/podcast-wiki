# Ray Cao

Ray Cao appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss global head of monetization product strategy & operations at tiktok, on tiktok's culture, global prioritization, context-not-control principle, and succeeding as an advertiser on the platform.

## Key Views

**Context, No Control — TikTok's Core Operating Principle** — Give people full visibility into the business problem and let them think like business owners rather than cogs in a machine. This breaks down silos because everyone can see the full picture and connect the dots themselves. It's like building with Lego — if you don't see the full picture, you can't make the pieces fit together. Leadership provides the context; teams own the execution. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/ray-cao.md]

**Always Day One — Hungrier at Mile 20 Than Mile 1** — TikTok maintains a startup mentality even at massive scale. The motto: "How can I run my second half of my marathon faster than the first half?" The company stays paranoid and hungry by keeping an open culture where anyone can ping the co-founder directly and engineering, product, and sales teams collaborate intensely rather than operating in fiefdoms. Growth is not a goal — it's a constant state of mind. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/ray-cao.md]

**Global Products Can't Be Built from a Single Market's Point of View** — TikTok launches products first in non-US markets — shopping launched in South Asia, gaming services for EU advertisers. You must send troops into local markets, hire local talent, and understand local culture. The algorithm can only do so much; humans must fine-tune it with local cultural understanding. What works in one market fails in another because behavior is deeply rooted in culture, not just demographics. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/ray-cao.md]

**Test 10+ Creatives Per Week and Embrace TikTok's Content Graph** — TikTok's advertising ecosystem runs on a content graph (different from Meta's people graph or Google's intent graph). The formula: test at least 10 different ad creatives per week, use a broad targeting approach (avoid narrow remarketing), and give it a minimum of one month to understand the platform's behavior. TikTok is a digital version of word-of-mouth — discovery is the core of advertising, not search or social connections. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/ray-cao.md]

**Hire for Curiosity Above All, and Never Compromise Standards** — Look for people who are naturally curious about new things and don't get stuck in their own domain. Pair that with discipline and the ability to prioritize. Ray's biggest mistake was compromising hiring standards to meet aggressive growth targets — wrong people slow you down more than vacancies. A smaller team of curious, disciplined people outperforms a larger team of mediocre hires every time. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/ray-cao.md]
