# Claire Vo

Claire Vo is CPO of LaunchDarkly and founder of ChatPRD. She was previously CPO of Color and Optimizely. She is known for her "clock speed" philosophy — she's hired to remind companies they can operate like a startup, not teach them to operate like a big company.

## Key Views

### Clock Speed One Click Faster
Claire's core management philosophy: "I communicate to my leaders that my expectation is they bring in the clock speed one click faster. If you think something needs to be done this year, it needs to be done this half." This incremental acceleration compounds into dramatically faster organizational velocity over time. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/claire-vo.md]

### Hired to Remind You of Startup Speed, Not Big Company Process
"People often think I get hired into later-stage companies because I'm supposed to teach them how to operate like a big company. In fact, I'm hired to remind them they can operate like a startup." Claire's contrarian positioning: as companies scale, the natural tendency is to add process. Her job is to preserve the speed, ownership, and autonomy that made the company successful in the first place. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/claire-vo.md]

### ChatPRD — AI for Product Managers
Claire founded ChatPRD, the most popular PM-specific AI product in the world. It uses AI to help PMs write product requirements documents faster. The insight: PMs spend too much time on documentation and not enough on thinking. AI can handle the structure while humans focus on strategy and judgment. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/claire-vo.md]

### Engineering-Product Integration Is the Future
Claire sees a trend of combining engineering and product roles. As AI makes coding more accessible, the boundaries between PM, engineer, and designer are blurring. The most effective teams will have fewer handoffs and more people who can operate across the stack — writing specs, building prototypes, and shipping features. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/claire-vo.md]

### Experimentation as a Competitive Advantage
Drawing from her experience at Optimizely and LaunchDarkly, Claire emphasizes that experimentation isn't just a tool — it's a cultural value. Companies that embed experimentation into their DNA make better decisions faster. The goal is not to run more experiments but to learn more per unit of time. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/claire-vo.md]
