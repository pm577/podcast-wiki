# Dhanji R. Prasanna

Dhanji R. Prasanna is Chief Technology Officer at Block (formerly Square), where he manages more than 4,000 engineers. Under his leadership, Block has become one of the most AI-native large enterprises in the world, building internal AI agents and tools that are reshaping how the company operates.

## Key Views

### AI Agents Should Work While Humans Sleep
"My philosophy: all these LLMs are sitting idle overnight and on weekends, while humans aren't there. There's no need for that. They should be working all the time. They should be trying to build in anticipation of what we want." Dhanji envisions a world where AI agents run 24/7, proactively building features, fixing bugs, and suggesting improvements without waiting for human instruction. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/dhanji-r-prasanna.md]

### Goose — The AI Agent That Proactively Ships PRs
Block's internal AI agent, Goose, watches Slack conversations and emails. When engineers discuss a feature idea, Goose has already tried to build it and opened a PR hours later. This "chatter-driven development" represents the bleeding edge of AI proactivity — not just responding to prompts but anticipating needs based on ambient signals. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/dhanji-r-prasanna.md]

### Non-Technical Users See the Biggest Gains from AI
Counterintuitively, it's not the engineers who benefit most from AI tools — it's the non-technical people. "The people that are able to embrace it to optimize for their particular workday and their particular set of tasks are really showing the most impact." Non-engineers using AI agents to build and automate are seeing outsized productivity gains. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/dhanji-r-prasanna.md]

### Code Quality and Product Success Are Uncorrelated
"A lot of engineers think that code quality is important to building a successful product. The two have nothing to do with each other." Dhanji's most counterintuitive lesson: beautiful code doesn't make a successful product. Speed of iteration, user empathy, and solving real problems matter far more than code cleanliness. This insight drives Block's willingness to ship fast and iterate. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/dhanji-r-prasanna.md]

### 8-10 Hours Per Week Saved — And This Is the Worst It Will Ever Be
Block's AI-forward engineering teams report 8-10 hours saved per week. Dhanji's key framing: "This is the worst it will ever be. This is now the baseline. The truth is the value is changing every day, so you need to ride that wave along with it." The productivity gains from AI will only increase — companies that don't adapt today are falling behind the accelerating baseline. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/dhanji-r-prasanna.md]
