# Geoff Ralston

President of Y Combinator, the world's leading startup accelerator (portfolio includes Stripe, Airbnb, Dropbox, Coinbase, Instacart, DoorDash, Flexport). Former Chief Product Officer at Yahoo!. Built RocketMail at Four11 (became Yahoo! Mail in 1997). CEO of Lala (acquired by Apple in 2009). Worked alongside Paul Graham at YC.

## Key Views

### 1. The Single Most Important Perspective an Investor Can Provide a Founder

Geoff emphasized that the most valuable thing an investor can give a founder is perspective — especially the ability to see around corners and anticipate what comes next. Having worked alongside Paul Graham through YC's most formative years, he observed that the best investors don't just write checks; they help founders think more clearly about their trajectory.^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2019-05-27-20vc-y-combinators-new-presi.md]

### 2. The Danger of "Profile Investing"

Geoff warned against "profile investing" — making decisions based on superficial founder characteristics rather than genuine business fundamentals. The best founders come from unexpected backgrounds, and the most successful YC companies often defied initial assumptions about what a winning founder looks like.^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2019-05-27-20vc-y-combinators-new-presi.md]

### 3. Lessons from Building Yahoo! Mail

Having built RocketMail (which became Yahoo! Mail) from scratch, Geoff understands both the technical and business challenges of scaling consumer internet products from zero to millions of users. This experience, combined with his time as Chief Product Officer at Yahoo!, shaped his approach to product strategy and company building.^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2019-05-27-20vc-y-combinators-new-presi.md]
