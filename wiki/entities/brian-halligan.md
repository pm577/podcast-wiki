# Brian Halligan

Brian Halligan appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss why it's never been easier to start a company, and never been harder to scale one.

## Key Views

**Perpetual Constructive Dissatisfaction** — The best CEOs live in a state of perpetual dissatisfaction in a positive way. 'They don't really take stock of what they've done and feel it, they're always a little bit dissatisfied with where they are, and very focused on the end state.' This generation of CEOs is surprisingly humble compared to the previous one. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-02-15-brian-halligan.md]

**CEO's Real Job is Hiring** — CEOs at scaling companies spend ~50% of their time recruiting and interviewing. 'CEOs and everyone dramatically overrate their ability to interview, and overrate their gut feeling, and underrate a really high quality blind reference.' Hire slow and fire fast — most companies do the opposite. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-02-15-brian-halligan.md]

**Kids Table vs. Adults Table** — CEOs of companies under ~100 employees (kids table) focus on product/market fit. CEOs over 100 employees (adults table) obsess over their exec team, org design, and building the next level of leadership. The transition requires a complete mindset shift. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-02-15-brian-halligan.md]

**Spiky Over Safe** — When hiring, choose the candidate with passionate fans and vocal detractors (spiky profile, some 4/4 and some 2/4) over the one everyone rates 3/4 (safe but bland). HubSpot's hit rate improved dramatically after making this shift. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-02-15-brian-halligan.md]

**Impedance Mismatch** — Hiring big-company executives (Google, Microsoft, Salesforce) into a 50-person startup almost always fails. HubSpot had 100% attrition on big-company hires. The operating models and expectations are fundamentally incompatible. 'Avoid the big company hire.' ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-02-15-brian-halligan.md]
