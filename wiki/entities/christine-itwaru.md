# Christine Itwaru

*Principal Strategist at Pendo and longtime product operations leader. Former product manager who helped pioneer the product ops discipline.*

## Key Views

1. **Product ops frees PMs to focus on customers**: The core PM responsibility — spending time with customers, understanding their pain — should never be delegated. Product ops takes everything else off their plate: tooling, stakeholder alignment, feedback synthesis, content strategy, and process management. It lets PMs do what only they can do.

2. **The summer of 2019 birthed product ops as a discipline**: Growth during the pandemic, the rise of product-led growth, and increasing cross-functional complexity created a perfect storm. CPOs went from "deliver features" to "drive business metrics," and needed structure. Product ops emerged as the natural evolution — just as marketing ops and sales ops did before.

3. **Product ops should automate itself out of a job**: The goal is to stand up systems and processes, then get out of the way. Itwaru deliberately built her team to automate and delegate repetitive work so the humans could focus on strategic value — retention, growth, voice of customer, and in-app experience improvement.

4. **Voice of customer management bridges revenue and product**: Product ops aggregates feedback from sales, support, and product — then educates the revenue team that "not everything requires a product change." Sometimes a knowledge base update or enablement session solves the problem faster than a feature. This alignment prevents the org from pulling in different directions.

5. **Buy-in starts at the top**: The most successful product ops implementations have CEO or CPO sponsorship. The winning argument: "Do you want your PMs constantly fielding questions from the revenue team, or spending time with customers?" When leaders see product ops as enabling quality time rather than adding bureaucracy, resistance dissolves.
