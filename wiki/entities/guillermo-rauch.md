# Guillermo Rauch

Guillermo Rauch is the founder and CEO of Vercel, creators of v0 (one of the most popular AI app building tools), and the mind behind foundational JavaScript frameworks including Next.js and Socket.IO. Vercel's technologies power some of the fastest websites on the internet, including Claude, Grok, and Midjourney.

## Key Views

### AI Will Become Synonymous With Software

Guillermo's key prediction: "We need to stop talking about AI at some point. I just see a future where AI becomes synonymous with software. We build software and we use software to build software." This means AI will become an invisible, embedded layer — not a separate category. Companies that treat AI as a distinct feature set will be outcompeted by those that weave it into the fabric of everything they build.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/guillermo-rauch.md]

### v0's Mission: 100 Million Builders

v0 isn't just a coding tool — it's a TAM expansion play. Guillermo's goal is to help 100 million people participate in building software, not just the ~30 million professional developers today. PMs, designers, and entrepreneurs who could previously only express ideas through developers can now ship directly. Vercel's business model: every new builder creates more demand for hosting, scaling, and deployment — Vercel's core business.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/guillermo-rauch.md]

### Taste Is a Skill, Not a Gift

"Taste, sometimes I think we think of as this inaccessible thing that, 'Oh, that person was born with taste.' I see it as a skill that can be developed." Guillermo's operating principle: increase exposure hours. Quantify how much time you spend watching how people use your products, studying great design, and using other products critically. Taste develops through deliberate observation, not innate talent.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/guillermo-rauch.md]

### Specialization Tasks Are Going Away

Guillermo believes many programming specializations — particularly translation tasks (converting designs to code, writing boilerplate) — are being eliminated by AI. However, understanding how things work under the hood remains critical because "you're going to be able to influence the model and make it follow your intention a lot better." Deep knowledge becomes leverage rather than a commodity. The best engineers will be those who understand systems deeply enough to direct AI effectively.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/guillermo-rauch.md]

### Everyone Should Be Full-Stack With AI

Guillermo envisions a world where designers ship production-ready products and PMs prototype and deploy directly. "We shouldn't put limits on ourselves and what we can build, and what we can ship, and what we can dream about making possible on these web surfaces." The collapsing of roles (designer, developer, PM) into "builder" is the most significant change AI brings to product development.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/guillermo-rauch.md]

## Notable Quotes

> "v0 is like a super genius five-year-old PhD with ADHD. It knows everything about everything, but it has these sparks of brilliance."

> "We need to stop talking about AI at some point. I just see a future where AI becomes synonymous with software."
