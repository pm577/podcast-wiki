# Maya Prohovnik

Maya Prohovnik is Spotify's Head of Product for Podcasting. She was employee #1 at Anchor, which was acquired by Spotify in 2019 and now powers over 75% of all new podcasts created globally. She has launched four of her own podcasts and is a strong advocate for dogfooding.

## Key Views

**Obsession with Reducing Friction** — Anchor's superpower was relentlessly reducing friction in podcast creation. The most extreme example: college interns manually created Apple Podcast accounts and submitted hundreds of thousands of podcasts by hand, making the process feel "magical and automatic" to users. This manual workaround gave Anchor massive hosting market share over competitors who had commoditized features. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/maya-prohovnik.md]

**Dogfood Everything** — Maya encourages everyone on her team to create their own podcasts. She has four herself. Dogfooding creates empathy for users, surfaces pain points that data can't capture, and builds a deep understanding of the product experience. It's the difference between knowing about a problem and feeling it. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/maya-prohovnik.md]

**Operating Like a Startup Inside Spotify** — After acquisition, Maya worked to preserve Anchor's startup culture: fast iterations, direct customer contact, and autonomy for small teams. The key is maintaining the sense of ownership and mission even as you integrate with a larger organization's processes and resources. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/maya-prohovnik.md]

**Gut vs. Data** — Finding the balance between intuition and data-driven decisions is critical. Data tells you what happened; gut/intuition — built from deep product experience and dogfooding — tells you what to do next. The best PMs develop both muscles and know when to lean on each. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/maya-prohovnik.md]

**Productivity in Senior Leadership** — As a senior leader at a large company, staying productive and organized requires ruthless prioritization, strong delegation, and maintaining direct customer connection despite organizational distance from end users. Her approach includes managing energy as much as time. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/maya-prohovnik.md]
