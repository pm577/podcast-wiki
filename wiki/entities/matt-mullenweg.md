# Matt Mullenweg

Matt Mullenweg is the co-creator of WordPress, which powers 40% of websites on the internet. He is CEO of Automattic, valued at over $7 billion, which owns WordPress.com, Tumblr, WooCommerce, Gravatar, and Pocket Casts.

## Key Views

**Open Source Must Be Defended** — "If you're really open and open source, sometimes you have to stand up to the bullies and you have to fight to protect your open source ideals." Matt's recent legal battle with WP Engine (owned by private equity firm Silver Lake) stemmed from trademark violations and brand dilution. The narrative that he's the "bad guy" is a result of "a lie gets around the world seven times before the truth has time to get out of bed." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/matt-mullenweg.md]

**"Fake Open Source" vs. Real Open Source** — Matt distinguishes between truly open source projects (like WordPress under GPL) and "fake open source" like Meta's Llama, which has usage restrictions that violate core open source principles. The difference matters because real open source creates ecosystems, community governance, and lasting value — not just corporate-controlled access. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/matt-mullenweg.md]

**20+ Years of Good Sentiment Can Burn in Days** — After two decades as a beloved figure in open source, the WP Engine controversy shifted public perception dramatically. Matt's response: "Previously, 1% of the world thought I was terrible and now I feel like it's up to four or 5%." The experience illustrates how quickly reputation can change when you take a principled but unpopular stand. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/matt-mullenweg.md]

**AI and Open Source Code** — Matt discusses how AI models are trained on open source code and what that means for the future. His perspective: open source code has been used to train AI, which in turn can create more code and more value for the ecosystem. The relationship between open source and AI is complex but potentially symbiotic. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/matt-mullenweg.md]

**Acquisition Philosophy at Automattic** — Matt's approach to acquisitions focuses on acquiring products that fit within the broader WordPress ecosystem and can benefit from being part of a larger open source platform. Examples include Tumblr, WooCommerce, and Pocket Casts — each integrated to extend the reach and capability of the Automattic/WordPress ecosystem. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/matt-mullenweg.md]
