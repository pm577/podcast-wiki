# Ian McAllister

Ian McAllister is the Senior Director of Product for Vehicles at Uber, focused on electrification and autonomous vehicle platforms. He spent 12 years at Amazon where he created Amazon Smile and led Alexa International expansion, and also worked at Airbnb leading customer support technology. He is the author of the legendary "What Separates a Top 1% PM from a Top 10% PM" post, one of the most widely read product management articles of all time.

## Key Views

### Communicate, Prioritize, Execute: The Core PM Trinity

Ian distills the PM craft into three foundational skills. **Communicate**: "Answer first and then explain." He developed a "Book of Kim" collecting communication best practices (avoid weasel words, own your problems). **Prioritize**: "A PM with a great innate ability to prioritize is going to generate 5X the impact of someone without that skill." **Execute**: "You're the one beating the drum and driving everyone forward." Focus on these three in year one; other skills can come later.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/ian-mcallister.md]

### Driven by Impact, Not Promotion

Ian's #1 guiding principle: "If you simply wake up every day trying to have the biggest impact you can, that's a really good guiding light." His early success at Amazon came not from being smarter or more technical, but from being "super hungry for impact" — obsessively driving a key metric up and to the right. "Do that instead of focusing on how to get promoted and having that guide your day."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/ian-mcallister.md]

### The Power of Writing to Sharpen Thinking

Ian credits his influential writing (the "Top 1% PM" post and "Working Backwards" process) with transforming his career. "I used to look for questions in the Goldilocks zone — not too high-level, not too specific." The practice of writing for an external audience forced him to organize his thinking compactly. "You've got to be a clear thinker to be a clear communicator." The resulting connections led directly to his opportunities at Airbnb and beyond.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/ian-mcallister.md]

### Trust Is the Currency of Product Leaders

Ian added "Earn trust with others" as a critical skill he missed in his original post. "Trust is the currency of a product manager and a product leader, especially if you're going to grow in your career." He also emphasizes "pushing back effectively" as an art correlated with growth — "if you say yes to everything, you're going to go nowhere."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/ian-mcallister.md]

### Working Backwards from the Customer

Ian is known for codifying Amazon's "Working Backwards" process. The approach starts with the customer and works backwards to the technology — writing the press release and FAQ before building anything. This forces prioritization by impact and clarity of thinking. He encourages PMs to adopt this discipline: define the customer problem first, then invent the solution.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/ian-mcallister.md]
