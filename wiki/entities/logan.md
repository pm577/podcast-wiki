# Logan Kilpatrick

Logan Kilpatrick is the Head of Developer Relations at OpenAI, where he supports the millions of developers building on OpenAI's APIs and ChatGPT. Before OpenAI, he was a machine learning engineer at Apple and advised NASA on open source policy. He provides rare insight into how OpenAI operates internally and where the biggest AI opportunities lie.

## Key Views

### High Agency and Urgency: OpenAI's Hiring Superpowers

"If I was hiring five people today, those are the top two characteristics I would look for: high agency and work with urgency." High-agency people hear about a customer challenge and start pushing on a solution without waiting for consensus. "People just go and do it and solve the problem, and I love that. You can take on the world if you have people who have high agency."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/logan-kilpatrick.md]

### The OpenAI Board Weekend: Culture as a Strength

Logan's view on the dramatic November 2023 board weekend where Sam Altman was fired and rehired: "The thing that surprised me most was how quickly everybody got back to business. I was walking into the office expecting something weird to be going on, and it was like people laser-focused and back to work." He believes the stakes were actually lower now than they will be in 5-10 years, making it the right time for the trial by fire.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/logan-kilpatrick.md]

### Avoid Competing on General-Purpose AI

Logan's advice for startups: don't try to build a general-purpose assistant that competes with ChatGPT unless you are "so radically different" that users will switch. Instead, go vertical: "We're not going to launch an AI sales agent. That's just not what we're building towards." Harvey (legal AI) is the model — deeply vertical, domain-specific, fine-tuned for a particular profession. "You shouldn't be surprised when we end up launching some general purpose agent product."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/logan-kilpatrick.md]

### 2024 Is the Year of New AI UX Paradigms

Logan is most excited about non-chat interfaces for AI, like TL Draw's infinite canvas. "Chat is the predominant way that folks are using AI today, but it actually makes a lot more sense for us as humans to see stuff in a visual format." He believes 2024 will be the year people push boundaries on UX paradigms around AI — multimodal, visual, spatial interfaces that go beyond text conversations.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/logan-kilpatrick.md]

### AI's Biggest Efficiency Gains Are in Engineering

Logan's view on where AI provides the most leverage today: software engineering. "I am guessing on the order of at least a 50% improvement, especially for lower-hanging fruit tasks." He sees GPTs (custom versions of ChatGPT) as the next wave for companies to encode their specific domain knowledge and voice, making AI tools more useful for internal business processes.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/logan-kilpatrick.md]
