# Jules Walter

Jules Walter is a product leader at YouTube, formerly Slack's first growth PM and leader of monetization and mobile teams. He's also co-founder of the Black Product Managers Network and CodePath.org — nonprofits increasing diversity in tech. His writing on building product sense is among the most shared content on Lenny's Newsletter.

## Key Views

**Give Feedback a Positive Reception — Even When It Stings**
Walter's approach to feedback is counterintuitive: thank the person immediately, even if your heart is melting. "Inside my heart might be melting — 'I thought I got better at this' — but externally, I'm like 'Hey, thank you, this is super helpful.'" By signaling that feedback is welcome, you train your colleagues to keep giving it to you — which is the only way to get better. The key is meaning the gratitude, not just performing it.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/jules-walter.md]]*

**Build Product Sense by Getting Deep Into One Area First**
Walter advises PMs to develop a reputation for excellence in a specific domain before broadening. His journey: started as Slack's first growth PM, mastered monetization, then mobile, then moved to YouTube to lead a major new product launch (Primetime Channels). Depth in one area creates credibility, which creates opportunities, which creates breadth. PMs who try to be generalists too early often struggle to gain traction or influence.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/jules-walter.md]]**

**Mentorship Is a Two-Way Street — Come Prepared**
Walter shares his framework for effective mentorship: mentees should come with specific questions and a clear sense of what they want, not a vague "can you mentor me?" He advocates for finding mentors who are one or two levels ahead — close enough to your current situation to remember what it's like, but far enough ahead to offer genuine guidance. The best mentor relationships are reciprocal: the mentor also learns from the fresh perspective and energy of the mentee.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/jules-walter.md]]**

**The First Growth PM Sets the Template — Be System-Builder First, Hacker Second**
As Slack's first growth PM, Walter's job was less about running experiments and more about building the growth infrastructure — defining metrics, setting up experimentation frameworks, and establishing cross-functional growth rhythms. He argues that the first growth PM at any company needs to be a system-builder who creates the conditions for growth, not just someone who runs tactical campaigns. "When I joined, revenue was around $50 million; when I left, it was 10x that."
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/jules-walter.md]]**

**Nonprofit Work Makes You a Better Product Leader**
Walter's work with CodePath.org and the Black Product Managers Network isn't separate from his PM career — it's integrated. Building programs to increase diversity in tech requires the same skills as building products: understanding user needs (students and professionals seeking opportunities), designing for scale, measuring impact, and iterating based on feedback. This parallel track has made him a more empathetic, systems-oriented, and mission-driven product leader.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/jules-walter.md]]**
