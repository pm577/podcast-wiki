# Marily Nika

Marily Nika is a computer scientist and AI Product Leader currently at Meta's Reality Labs, and previously at Google for 8 years working on Google Glass, computer vision, and speech recognition. She completed a PhD in Machine Learning in 2014 and teaches a popular Maven course on AI and Product Management.

## Key Views

**Avoid the Shiny Object Trap** — "Don't do AI for the sake of doing AI." Make sure there is a real problem and pain point that needs solving before layering AI on top. Identify the problem first, define the high-level solution, then figure out how to implement it with AI if appropriate. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/marily-nika.md]

**Every PM Will Be an AI PM** — All products will need personalized experiences, recommender systems, and intelligent features by default. In the future, every product manager will effectively be an AI product manager because AI will be baked into every product experience. PMs need to build AI literacy — understanding what models are, how they're trained, and what's possible. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/marily-nika.md]

**ChatGPT as a PM Productivity Tool** — Practical applications: (1) Refining mission statements — ChatGPT produces clearer, more cross-disciplinary messaging than most PMs can write alone. (2) Generating user segments with motivations and pain points that a human mind wouldn't naturally think of. (3) Getting ideas for AI-enhanced features. The key is using it as an enhancement tool after you've already done the thinking, not as a replacement. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/marily-nika.md]

**AI Enhances Rather Than Replaces** — Despite fears that AI will replace creative and knowledge work, Marilyn sees AI as enhancing human capabilities. Writing is not dying — tools like ChatGPT augment the process. Under-hyped areas include AI's ability to detect deception (lie detection for security/workplace) and other applications beyond large language models. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/marily-nika.md]

**Stay Informed Through Curated Newsletters** — To stay on top of the fast-moving AI space, subscribe to curated newsletters like The Download by MIT Technology Review. Everything technology-focused will increasingly include AI, so broad tech coverage is valuable. Being a continuous learner is essential for AI PMs. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/marily-nika.md]
