# Chad Peets

One of the greatest sales leaders and recruiters of the last 25 years. Previously Managing Director at Sutter Hill Ventures (2018–2023) where he worked with the world's best CEOs and CROs. Known as the "sales saviour" at Snowflake where he advised the CEO on building their go-to-market machine. Currently partners with founders at high-growth companies like Harvey and Factory. Sits on boards including Lacework, Luminary Cloud, Clumio, and Sigma Computing.

## Key Views

### 1. You Need a CRO Before Product-Market Fit

Peets argues strongly that founders must hire a CRO pre-product, not post-product-market-fit. The CRO should be involved in shaping the go-to-market strategy from the earliest stages, not brought in after the product is built. He believes this is one of the most underrated hiring decisions a founder can make.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2024-08-23-20sales-how-snowflake-built-a.md]

### 2. Remote Sales Teams Do Not Work

Peets takes a hardline stance against remote work in sales. He argues that remote sales reps do not care about their development, that in-office presence is essential for culture, mentorship, and performance, and that the best sales organizations are built around physical proximity. He calls remote work "BS" for sales teams.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2025-11-07-20sales-why-you-need-a-cro-pr.md]

### 3. The Best Sales People Are Unconventional

Peets believes that the best salespeople are "f**** in the head" — meaning they have a unique, sometimes obsessive drive that doesn't fit normal corporate profiles. He argues that hiring panels are BS for evaluating sales talent because they filter out the exact personality traits that make great closers. He advises evaluating sales talent through green and red flags rather than conventional interview processes.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2026-01-09-20sales-why-the-best-sales-pe.md]

### 4. The $100M CRO Bubble and AI's Impact on Sales

In his most recent episode, Peets discusses how AI companies like Anthropic are causing a compensation crisis in sales (the "$100M CRO bubble"). He warns against hiring from Salesforce or ServiceNow because those reps are "order takers" rather than builders. He advocates for rethinking how to hire, train, and forecast in a world where AI is transforming the sales function.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2026-05-23-20sales-the-100m-cro-bubble.md]

### 5. Most Sales Reps Do Not Perform

Drawing on his experience scaling Snowflake, Peets estimates that most sales reps simply do not perform to expectation. The key is not to try and fix underperformers but to build a hiring machine that identifies the right profile from day one and a culture that makes top performers thrive.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2024-08-23-20sales-how-snowflake-built-a.md]
