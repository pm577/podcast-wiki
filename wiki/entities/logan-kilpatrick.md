# Logan Kilpatrick

Logan Kilpatrick appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss inside openai — developer relations, hiring philosophy, prompt engineering, and the future of ai interfaces.

## Key Views

**Hire for High Agency and Urgency** — Logan identifies high agency and working with urgency as the top two hiring traits at OpenAI. High-agency people see a problem and go solve it without needing consensus from 50 people or waiting for top-down direction — they hear customer challenges and immediately push on solutions. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/logan-kilpatrick.md]

**Context Is All You Need** — Prompt engineering is fundamentally about providing context. The model is like a human-level intelligence with literally no context about you — it has never met you, doesn't know your goals. Logan's mantra: "Context is all you need. Context is the only thing that matters." Adding context (links, background, examples) transforms generic outputs into tailored, high-quality responses. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/logan-kilpatrick.md]

**Build Vertical, Not Horizontal Against OpenAI** — Startups should build vertical-specific applications (like Harvey for legal), not general-purpose assistants that compete directly with ChatGPT. OpenAI focuses on general reasoning, coding, and writing capabilities, not verticalized products like an AI sales agent. Domain-specific solutions leveraging OpenAI's models can stay on the cutting edge without competing head-on. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/logan-kilpatrick.md]

**Plan for Normalization, Not Magic** — Each new model (GPT-5, etc.) will be a more effective tool, but the same fundamental problems remain — people quickly normalize advances. The real edge is planning for a world where people become used to these tools quickly, not expecting them to change everything overnight. Hype fades; grounded application persists. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/logan-kilpatrick.md]

**New Interfaces Beyond Chat Are the Next Frontier** — Chat is the predominant AI interface today, but products that move beyond it (infinite canvas experiences, multimodal interaction, agents that work asynchronously) will have a significant advantage. 2024 is the year of multimodal AI and pushing boundaries of UX paradigms — consumers will reward interfaces that don't just replicate a chatbot. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/logan-kilpatrick.md]
