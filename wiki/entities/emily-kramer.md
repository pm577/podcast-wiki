# Emily Kramer

Emily Kramer led and built marketing teams at Asana, Carta, Ticketfly, and Astro (acquired by Slack). She is now the co-founder of MKT1, where she helps founders and marketers build effective marketing functions. She also writes the popular MKT1 newsletter on B2B marketing.

## Key Views

### Marketing = Fuel + Engine

Emily distills all of marketing into two components: fuel (content, words, design, value-creating assets) and engine (distribution channels, tracking, operations). Every company needs both, but the challenge determines which to prioritize. If you have great content but no distribution, work on the engine. If you have distribution channels but nothing compelling to say, work on fuel. This framework simplifies what is often an overwhelming function.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/emily-kramer.md]

### The Biggest Marketing Hiring Mistake

Founders commonly hire marketers who match their company's stage instead of their business model. A PLG company and an enterprise sales company need completely different marketing archetypes. The most common mistake: hiring a brand marketer when you need performance marketing, or vice versa. Emily advises founders to first determine their primary growth motion, then hire the marketer that fits that motion.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/emily-kramer.md]

### PMs and Marketers Should Think Alike

Emily describes herself as "a marketer that thinks like a product manager." The best marketers use the same skills as great PMs — user research, iterative testing, data-driven decisions. She recommends PMs understand marketing basics and marketers understand product thinking, because the line between the two is blurring in modern SaaS. The most effective teams have marketers who can participate in product discussions and PMs who understand go-to-market.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/emily-kramer.md]

### Your First Marketing Hire Should Match Your Risk Profile

The archetype of the marketer you hire first depends on your company's risk. If your biggest risk is awareness (nobody knows you), hire a demand generation marketer. If your risk is positioning (people don't understand what you do), hire a product marketer. If your risk is retention (users churn quickly), hire a lifecycle marketer. Emily has a detailed framework matching archetypes to business models and stages.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/emily-kramer.md]

### Red Flags That Marketing Isn't Working

Emily identifies clear red flags: marketing can't articulate the specific customer problem, they measure outputs instead of outcomes (e.g., "we published 5 blog posts" vs. "signups from content"), or they're disconnected from the product team. The most important metric for any marketing team: do they understand who the customer is and what drives them to buy? If not, nothing else will fix the problem.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/emily-kramer.md]

## Notable Quotes
