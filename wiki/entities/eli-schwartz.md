# Eli Schwartz

Eli Schwartz is a leading SEO consultant and author of Product-Led SEO. He has worked with industry giants including Zapier, Tinder, Coinbase, Quora, LinkedIn, and WordPress to build and execute SEO strategies. He was previously Director of SEO at SurveyMonkey.

## Key Views

**SEO Is a Product Question, Not a Marketing Tactic** — Product managers should be the ones driving SEO strategy because it's fundamentally a product question: "How do we position this to the user who is on their own self-discovery journey?" If you can't answer what someone would search for, don't do SEO. It's about understanding the user's intent before they even know your product exists. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/eli-schwartz.md]

**The AI Overviews Apocalypse Has Been Overstated** — Eli initially thought AI Overviews would be an SEO apocalypse (whoever won the long-form content got the first click — now that's gone). But the reality is more nuanced: AI answers create new opportunities for being cited as the source. The brands that win are the ones positioned as authoritative sources that AI systems trust — not the ones gaming keywords. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/eli-schwartz.md]

**Product-Led SEO: Build What People Search For** — The most effective SEO strategy is to build features and content that directly answer what people are searching for, not to optimize existing content for keywords. Zapier's entire business is product-led SEO: every integration page ranks for "[app] + [app] + connect" searches. Product decisions become SEO assets. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/eli-schwartz.md]

**Most Companies Skip Step One** — The first step everyone misses: validate that people actually search for what you plan to write about. Most SEO efforts fail because companies create content for topics nobody searches for, or worse, topics that get searched but signal the wrong intent (someone searching "what is CRM" is not buying a CRM). ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/eli-schwartz.md]

**SEO Is Not a Dark Art — It's Simple But Hard** — The fundamentals haven't changed: find the right keywords, create content that serves searcher intent, build authority through genuine value. What's changed is that AI makes it harder to game the system and easier to identify the truly useful content. Companies that treat SEO as a product function rather than a dark art will win in both AI and traditional search. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/eli-schwartz.md]
