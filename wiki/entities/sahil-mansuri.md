# Sahil Mansuri

CEO and founder of Bravado, the world's largest online sales community (300,000+ salespeople). Previously a longtime salesperson himself. Known for deep expertise in sales strategy, compensation design, and go-to-market execution in turbulent markets.

## Key Views

### 1. Plan Conservatively with Milestones That Unlock Growth

"When you have limited visibility, set up a really conservative plan and then have short-term milestones that unlock the ability to lean into growth." In downturns, companies oscillate between panic and overconfidence. The right approach: start conservative, define clear triggers (e.g., "if we hit X in Q1, we can invest Y in Q2"), and use short feedback loops to adjust. This avoids the "we're screwed, everything's better, we're screwed" whiplash.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/sahil-mansuri.md]

### 2. Double Down on Retention and Existing Customers

In a recession, the highest-leverage activity is retaining and expanding your existing customer base. New acquisition is expensive and uncertain. Focus on: reducing churn, identifying expansion opportunities in your current accounts, and ensuring your best customers become advocates. A retained dollar is worth more than a new dollar in uncertain times.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/sahil-mansuri.md]

### 3. Sales Comp Plans Must Be Recession-Proofed

Traditional sales comp plans designed for growth break in a downturn. Mansuri advises: decouple compensation from factors the salesperson can't control (like market conditions), add "retention accelerators" that reward keeping customers, and ensure the plan doesn't incentivize behavior that harms long-term relationships. The best salespeople stay motivated when the comp plan is fair, even in a tough market.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/sahil-mansuri.md]

### 4. Forecast Honestly — Don't Let Hope Drive Numbers

Bad forecasting is the root of most sales org dysfunction in downturns. Mansuri advocates: build bottoms-up forecasts based on actual pipeline, not top-down targets. If the pipeline doesn't support the number, the number is wrong. Honest forecasting allows the company to make real operational decisions rather than operating on hope.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/sahil-mansuri.md]

### 5. Sales Skills Are for Everyone

"You don't have to be in sales to benefit from sales skills." Every role — product, engineering, design — involves persuasion, negotiation, and closing. Understanding sales principles (qualifying, discovery, objection handling, closing) makes you better at getting buy-in for your ideas, negotiating resources for your team, and advancing your career.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/sahil-mansuri.md]
