# Chris Hutchins

*Former Head of New Product Strategy at Wealthfront, now full-time creator of All the Hacks podcast.*

## Key Views

1. **Podcasting persistence is the #1 differentiator**: Of ~4M podcasts, only ~150K have published 10+ episodes in the last 10 days. Simply committing to 10 weekly episodes puts you in the top ~4%. Most people quit before they've given it a real chance — consistency alone separates you from the vast majority.

2. **Internal buy-in is as important as customer insights**: When driving big bets inside a company, Hutchins learned that customer research alone isn't enough. You must create a compelling vision and constantly restate your mission — just like a founder at all-hands. Your ability to persuade internally and build influence is as important as identifying user needs.

3. **State your intent to neutralize skepticism**: When pushing contrarian ideas, preface by stating "all I care about is that the company is successful." This disarms people who might attribute your advocacy to ego or self-interest. It's a relational tactic Andy Rachleff taught him that makes radical ideas more palatable.

4. **Slugging average over batting average**: Inspired by Andy Rachleff, Hutchins advocates for swings at big-impact projects where 1 in 10 hits a home run. Iterative improvements on current features are safe, but truly outsized impact requires bets that will most likely fail — and that's okay if the wins are massive.

5. **The "pretend it exists" customer test**: Before building Wealthfront's Autopilot, Hutchins used clickable prototypes and pretended the product already existed in interviews. When customers said "Can I go get my husband? I need to show him this" — or tried to sign up on the spot — he knew they had something real, even if eventual adoption didn't match the hype.
