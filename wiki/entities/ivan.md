# Ivan Zhao

Ivan Zhao is the co-founder and CEO of Notion, one of the most beloved productivity tools in the world. He grew up in Urumqi, China, moved to Canada at 16, and learned English watching SpongeBob SquarePants. After winning a programming competition in Beijing, he eventually built Notion over several "lost years" that nearly killed the company multiple times. Deeply philosophical about computing, he is driven by the vision that "everybody can create their software."

## Key Views

### Sugar-Coated Broccoli: Hide Your Vision Inside What People Actually Want

Ivan's defining product insight: most people don't care about creating software — they care about getting their job done. Notion's early years failed because they built a developer tool for non-developers. Their breakthrough came from hiding their true vision (everyone can create software) inside a familiar form factor (productivity software). "People don't want to eat the broccoli but people like sugar, so give them the sugar then hide your broccoli inside of it."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/ivan-zhao.md]

### The Lost Years: Four Years Before Finding PMF

Notion's first 3-4 years were "lost years" — building the wrong product on the wrong technical foundation, nearly running out of money multiple times. The company rebooted from 5 people down to 2 (Ivan and co-founder Simon), moved to Japan to reset, and rebuilt from scratch. Ivan's advice: "Don't be afraid to reset. You can create progress through better abstractions, and that thing compounds faster and can catch up to all the things you built much quicker than you ever thought."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/ivan-zhao.md]

### The Smalltalk Vision: Computing Without Separation

Ivan is deeply influenced by 1960s-70s computing pioneers like Douglas Engelbart, Alan Kay, and Ted Nelson. Their vision: "There shouldn't be a separation between builders and users. It's the same medium." He argues that Steve Jobs saw the GUI but missed the deeper power of Smalltalk's malleable environment. Notion aims to fulfill that original promise — a tool where everything is customizable and composable.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/ivan-zhao.md]

### Balance Between Your Values and Market Reality

Building a product business requires balancing two energies: building for your values (what you want the world to have) and building for the market (what users will pay for). "Too much of yourself, then there's no users, you're just doing a research project. Too much for business, you're building a commodity." The sweet spot is where your authentic vision happens to be useful for others.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/ivan-zhao.md]

### Daily Reset: Sleep as a Strategic Tool

When things get hard, Ivan's simple strategy: "Whenever I feel down, I just go to sleep and next day I'm just reset." This personal practice extends to the company level — Notion reset its codebase, team structure, and strategy multiple times. "High is never too high, low is never too low for us... It's like a daily personal physical reset. You can reset your code base. You can reset your mental model."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/ivan-zhao.md]
