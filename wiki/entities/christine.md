# Christine Itwaru

Christine Itwaru is a long-time product operations leader at Pendo, a role she transitioned into from product management. She now serves as Principal Strategist. She is one of the leading voices on the emerging discipline of product operations.

## Key Views

### What Product Ops Actually Does
Product Ops serves as the connective tissue between product management, engineering, data, customer success, and leadership. Day-to-day work includes: managing tooling and data infrastructure, creating feedback loops from customer support and sales, running product reviews, and ensuring the product team has the information and processes needed to make good decisions. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/christine-itwaru.md]

### The Line Between Product Ops and Product Management
The distinction: PMs own the "what" and "why" — strategy, discovery, prioritization. Product Ops owns the "how" — processes, tools, data infrastructure, and operational excellence. PMs are strategists; Product Ops are operators who make the strategy executable. The best arrangement has Product Ops as a force multiplier for PMs. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/christine-itwaru.md]

### When Your Company Needs Product Ops
Signs you should invest in Product Ops: (1) PMs spend more time on process than on strategy; (2) Customer feedback is scattered across emails, Slack, and support tickets with no system; (3) Product data is unreliable or hard to access; (4) Product reviews are chaotic or inconsistent; (5) PMs are drowning in meetings and can't think deeply. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/christine-itwaru.md]

### The Ops Role as a Career Path
Christine argues that ops roles are not "a sign of inefficiency" — they're a sign of maturity. Just as companies hire finance ops, HR ops, and sales ops as they scale, product ops becomes necessary when the product organization grows beyond a certain size. It's a legitimate career path, not a consolation prize for failed PMs. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/christine-itwaru.md]

### Never Give Up Spending Time with Customers
Christine's core advice to PMs: "I would not ever give up spending time with customers and watching their pain." That's how she fell in love with product — watching an internal customer "fighting with the keyboard, fighting with the mouse." Direct customer observation is irreplaceable, even — especially — when you have a Product Ops team to handle the operational work. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/christine-itwaru.md]
