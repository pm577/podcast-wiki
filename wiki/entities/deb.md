# Deb Liu

Deb Liu is CEO of Ancestry and former longtime VP of Product at Facebook, where she created Facebook Marketplace, developed the first mobile ad product for apps, and built Facebook's gaming platform. She also served as Director at eBay and PayPal and is on the board of Intuit.

## Key Views

### PM Your Career Like a Product
"Some of the best PMs I have ever worked with are terrible PMs for their career. They just drift from job to job." Deb's framework: treat your career like a product. Write a "spec" for your career — what does success look like? How will you get there? What metrics will you track? Apply the same strategic thinking to your career that you apply to your product. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/deb-liu.md]

### Succeeding as an Introvert in an Extrovert's Workplace
"The workplace really favors people who can speak up. It looks like self-promotion." Deb reframes this: instead of self-promotion, think of it as "educating about all the great work your team has been doing." You're not boasting — you're helping people see why your team deserves more resources. Introverts can be just as effective by focusing on the team's success rather than their own. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/deb-liu.md]

### Building Zero-to-One at Facebook
Deb led the creation of Facebook Marketplace from scratch. Her approach: start with a deep understanding of user behavior (people were already buying/selling in Facebook Groups), build the simplest version that delivers value, and iterate relentlessly based on real usage. Zero-to-one is about removing barriers between users and value, not adding features. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/deb-liu.md]

### The First Mobile Ad Product
Deb developed Facebook's first mobile ad product for apps. The insight: mobile was growing explosively but no one had figured out how to monetize it effectively. By building for the platform's future (mobile) rather than its present (desktop), she positioned Facebook to capture the mobile advertising revolution. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/deb-liu.md]

### Leadership Through Service
Deb's leadership philosophy: the best leaders serve their teams, not the other way around. Her job as CEO of Ancestry is to remove obstacles, provide resources, and create an environment where her team can do their best work. This service mindset builds trust and loyalty that command-and-control leadership never achieves. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/deb-liu.md]
