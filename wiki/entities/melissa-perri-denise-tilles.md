# Denise Tilles

Denise Tilles is a product leader, coach, and consultant specializing in product vision, strategy, and execution. She works with Melissa Perri at Produx Labs and co-authored *Product Operations: How Successful Companies Build Better Products at Scale*.

## Key Views

**Product Ops Unlocks PM Strategic Time** — Product operations creates shared systems and infrastructure (data, tools, processes) that free PMs from operational overhead. Without product ops, PMs spend 70% of their time on non-strategic work. With product ops, they can concentrate on strategy and customer discovery the majority of the time. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/melissa-perri-denise-tilles.md]

**Product Ops Is Not Project Management** — The role differs fundamentally from project management: product ops focuses on scaling the product management function itself — improving tooling, data accessibility, processes, and cross-team coordination. It's the system that enables PMs to be more effective, not a replacement for PMs. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/melissa-perri-denise-tilles.md]

**The Role Is Growing Rapidly** — The product ops role has gone from almost non-existent to something like half of scaling tech companies having at least one product ops person. Companies like OpenAI, Uber, Stripe, and Ramp all have product ops functions. It's one of the most significant changes happening in product management. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/melissa-perri-denise-tilles.md]

**First Product Ops Hire** — The right first product ops hire is someone who can build systems and processes, understand data and tooling, and work cross-functionally without needing to be a product manager themselves. They should complement PMs, not compete with them. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/melissa-perri-denise-tilles.md]
