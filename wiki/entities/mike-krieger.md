# Mike Krieger

**Role:** Chief Product Officer at Anthropic (makers of Claude); co-founder of Instagram; co-founder of Artifact; board member; product visionary  
**Known for:** Co-founding and scaling Instagram to a billion users; pioneering product thinking at Anthropic in the age of AI-generated code; insights on how product development changes when 90%+ of code is written by AI.

## Key Views

### 1. 90%+ AI-Generated Code Reshapes the Entire Bottleneck

At Anthropic, over 90% of code is now written by AI, with Claude Code itself being 95%+ Claude-generated. This has completely upended the traditional product development bottleneck. The constraint is no longer engineering time — it's now **upstream (strategy/alignment)** and **downstream (merge queue/deployment)**. Product managers and designers can now prototype functional demos directly using Claude, making ideas tangible much earlier. But the merge queue became a crisis: Anthropic had to completely re-architect it because so many more pull requests were being submitted. The lesson: when you dramatically accelerate one part of the system, you discover new bottlenecks you never anticipated.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/mike-krieger.md]

> "We really rapidly became bottlenecked on other things — our merge queue, decision making, alignment. The upstream bottleneck is how do I provide minimum viable strategy to let people feel empowered to go build at the edge of model capabilities." — Mike Krieger

### 2. Models Now Have Genuinely Independent Opinions

Mike's biggest mindset shift since joining Anthropic: models have crossed a **creativity threshold** where they can offer genuinely novel perspectives, not just anodyne feedback. He shared his product strategy with Opus 4 and it came back with a genuinely new angle he hadn't considered, which he immediately incorporated. This is fundamentally different from earlier models that would politely agree or offer obvious suggestions. The ability to "roast" a strategy or provide a creative counterpoint makes Claude a legitimate **thought partner**, not just a tool. Mike now uses Claude as his go-to product strategy collaborator.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/mike-krieger.md]

> "I had this notion: 'Are they able to have an independent opinion?' It's actually really flipped for me. Opus 4 came back and I was like, 'That is a new angle that I hadn't been looking at before.'" — Mike Krieger

### 3. The PM's Job: Strategy, Comprehension, and Human Empathy

Even as AI becomes more capable, Mike identifies three areas where product people create disproportionate value: **(1) Strategy** — figuring out where to play and how to win remains a deeply human skill; **(2) Comprehensibility** — the gap between what AI models can do and how people actually use them is enormous ("huge overhang"), and bridging that gap is a product challenge; **(3) Human empathy and psychology** — his background in human-computer interaction taught him that understanding human needs doesn't get automated away. The best PMs at Anthropic are the ones who embed with research teams and feed product insight back into model training, rather than just designing UX wrappers.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/mike-krieger.md]

> "Making this all comprehensible — the delta between what the models can do and how they're being used on a daily basis is huge. That's the overhang. That's where a very strong necessary role for product remains." — Mike Krieger

### 4. How to Prompt: Push Beyond Politeness

Mike's behind-the-scenes prompting advice: our natural instinct is to be polite with AI, but you get much better results by doing the opposite. Instead of "What could be better?" he asks Claude to "Roast me. Be brutal. Tell me what's wrong with this strategy." This forces the model to be more critical and surface genuine flaws. He also recommends using the **prompt improver** tool in Anthropic's Console — Claude itself is often better at writing prompts for Claude than humans are, naturally inserting XML tags and structural elements that improve performance. The meta-insight: the AI itself is your best prompt engineer.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/mike-krieger.md]

### 5. Nurture Curiosity and Independent Thinking for the AI Era

Asked what skills he's encouraging his kids to develop, Mike focuses on the **process of inquiry** rather than AI proficiency. When his 6-year-old has a question, their family ritual is not "ask Claude" but "how would we find out?" — preserving the scientific process of hypothesis, experiment, and discovery. His proudest moment: his daughter insisted "I know I'm right" even when asked to verify with Claude, demonstrating independent conviction. The skill of asking good questions, systematic inquiry, and independent thought will remain essential even as AI handles more of the answer-finding. This mirrors advice from other tech leaders like Shopify's Tobi Lütke, who gave the same answer about prioritizing curiosity.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/mike-krieger.md]
