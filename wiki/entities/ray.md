# Ray Cao

Ray Cao is the Global Head of Monetization Product Strategy & Operations at TikTok, where he has spent over four years. Previously, he spent six years at Google helping scale Google Shopping globally. He offers a rare inside look at TikTok's culture, operating principles, and go-to-market strategy.

## Key Views

### Context, No Control

TikTok's #1 operating principle: give people full context and then let them act without specific instructions. "We're always encouraging people to see themselves as a business owner rather than a piece of machine that keeps the machine running." This requires hiring people who are curious, disciplined, and good at prioritization. The approach breaks down human-made silos that slow organizations down.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/ray-cao.md]

### Always Day One: Run the Second Half Faster

TikTok maintains a startup mentality despite its massive scale. Ray's framing: "How can I run my second half of my marathon faster than the first half?" This manifests in flat communication (anyone can ping the co-founder), tight engineering-product-sales collaboration, and a refusal to fall into the trap of thinking "we're successful so we don't need to worry about our existence anymore."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/ray-cao.md]

### Globalization Requires Local Immersion, Not Just Algorithms

While TikTok's algorithm does heavy lifting, true globalization requires sending "troops into the market, hiring local talent, understanding the culture." Ray's framework: technology is a tool that must be fine-tuned by local experts. For example, content trends differ wildly by market — Japan leans into food and 3C products, while the US started with lip-syncing and expanded to shopping.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/ray-cao.md]

### Engineering, Product, and Sales as One Unit

TikTok's "secret sauce" is how closely engineering, product, and sales teams collaborate. They hold 180-person quarterly doc-reading meetings where IC-level engineers and product managers join sales leaders. PMs and engineers go on client immersion trips to "feel the heat" — directly experiencing customer pain. "A lot of the company, if you want to meet the engineering leaders, it's literally once a year maybe. For us, it's always on."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/ray-cao.md]

### Curious, Disciplined, Prioritizing: The Hiring Triangle

Ray looks for three qualities in every hire: natural curiosity (willingness to learn beyond their domain), discipline (following the "reactive doing" collaboration guidance), and prioritization ability. "I don't believe one thing is everybody can do everything. You have to prioritize properly so that you're able to push the right agenda."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/ray-cao.md]
