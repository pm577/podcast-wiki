# Tristan Handy

Founder and CEO @ dbt Labs, the data transformation tool that enables data analysts and engineers to transform, test and document data in the cloud data warehouse. Raised over $400M from Sequoia, Altimeter, Coatue, ICONIQ and GV, with a $4.2BN valuation at the latest round. Previously VP Marketing @ RJ Metrics.

## Key Views

### 1. The CEO Should Make As Few Decisions As Possible

Handy's core leadership philosophy: the CEO's job is not to make all the decisions but to create a system where the best decisions get made without them. If you're the bottleneck for every important call, you cap the organization's velocity. The goal is to build a team that can operate independently within clear boundaries.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2022-10-14-20vc-why-the-ceo-should-make.md]

### 2. The Trade-Off Between Freedom and Raising Big From VCs

Raising large amounts of venture capital gives you resources but constrains your freedom. The more money you take, the more you're playing a game that requires venture-scale returns. Handy advocates being intentional about this trade-off — knowing what you're giving up when you take that big check.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2022-10-14-20vc-why-the-ceo-should-make.md]

### 3. Our Jobs Are Not As Hard As We Think

A humility-based leadership perspective: most knowledge work jobs are not as difficult as we tell ourselves. This realization should make us less precious about our own contributions and more open to delegating. It also changes how you assess talent — look for people who get things done without drama, not people who need constant heroics.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2022-10-14-20vc-why-the-ceo-should-make.md]

### 4. How to Assess Talent and Potential

Handy looks for signals of potential rather than just pedigree. The best hires are people who have demonstrated the ability to learn fast, operate in ambiguity, and care deeply about the mission. Experience in a specific role matters less than the capacity to grow into the next role.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2022-10-14-20vc-why-the-ceo-should-make.md]
