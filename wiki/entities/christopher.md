# Christopher Miller

Christopher Miller is VP of Product for Growth and AI at HubSpot. Starting as an IC PM, he created HubSpot's early growth team and shifted the company toward one of the most successful product-led growth (PLG) businesses in history.

## Key Views

### Radical Accountability — Every Problem Is Our Problem
HubSpot's early growth team operated with a "radically accountable" mindset. When they saw that self-service subscription revenue was underinvested, they approached the owning team, asked to take it over, "and immediately blew it up." This attitude of saying "every problem is our problem" helped them find opportunities the business wasn't explicitly asking them to solve. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/christopher-miller.md]

### Grow Hungry — Let Your Success Feed You
"When you do that, we look hungry, so let's keep feeding us." Chris's team built a reputation for delivering results, which earned them more resources and scope. The virtuous cycle of growth teams: find an overlooked opportunity, deliver outsized results, get more responsibility, repeat. This is the flywheel that built HubSpot's PLG engine. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/christopher-miller.md]

### Scrape Your Knees as an Early PM
Chris's advice to early-career PMs: take risks and "scrape your knees." The best learning comes from failures, not successes. Early in your career, you should optimize for learning velocity, not career safety. Take on projects that stretch you, even if they might fail. Those scars will make you a better product leader. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/christopher-miller.md]

### Finding Mentors Through Action, Not Asking
Rather than asking someone to be your mentor, Chris suggests demonstrating competence and curiosity first. Work alongside potential mentors, show initiative, ask thoughtful questions about their work. The best mentorship relationships emerge naturally from working together, not from formal "will you be my mentor?" requests. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/christopher-miller.md]

### PLG Requires Content, Sales, and Product Working Together
HubSpot's PLG success came from integrating content marketing, sales, and product into a unified growth system. Content drives awareness and education, product delivers value and virality, sales captures enterprise opportunities. Each channel amplifies the others. Treating them as separate functions with separate metrics is the fastest way to undermine PLG. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/christopher-miller.md]
