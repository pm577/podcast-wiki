# Julian Shapiro

Founder of Demand Curve (YC startup training companies on growth and marketing), former VP of Marketing at Webflow, part-time columnist at TechCrunch, creator of a widely-used JavaScript animation library, and full-time investor. Known as a polymath across writing, growth, community building, and fitness.

## Key Views

### 1. The Creativity Faucet: Good Ideas Come After Bad Ideas Are Exhausted

"Why do good ideas arrive after the bad ideas are empty? Because when you've gone through a bunch of bad ideas, your brain starts reflexively identifying what elements are causing the badness." Most creators resist their bad ideas and stop too early. The creative process requires pushing through the bad ideas to reach the good ones. If you scribble a few thoughts, don't like them, and walk away, you never finished the creative process.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/julian-shapiro.md]

### 2. "Mind Followers" vs "Labor Followers" on Social Media

There are two types of followers: those who follow you for the quality of your mind (original thoughts, insights, takes) and those who follow you for your labor (curation, threads, memes). Mind followers have higher affinity, loyalty, and are far more likely to act on your recommendations. Shapiro intentionally posts less frequently, focusing on original blog-post-quality content rather than "fortune cookie threads" that attract low-quality followers.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/julian-shapiro.md]

### 3. Product-Led Acquisition Framework

Shapiro developed a framework for growth called product-led acquisition — embedding growth mechanisms directly into the product experience rather than relying on traditional marketing. The key insight: your product itself should be the primary acquisition channel, with built-in shareability, virality, and referral mechanics that feel native to the user experience.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/julian-shapiro.md]

### 4. Writing: Novelty Is Everything

Shapiro emphasizes that the most important quality in writing (and content creation generally) is novelty — saying something the reader hasn't heard before. Most business advice is recycled. The highest-leverage writing comes from first-principles thinking and personal experience that yields genuinely new insights. This is why he shifted from high-volume tweeting to occasional, deeply thoughtful posts.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/julian-shapiro.md]

### 5. The Best Time to Be Junior Is Now (Contrarian)

Contrary to the narrative that AI is eliminating junior roles, Shapiro argues that AI tools lower the barrier to building so dramatically that juniors can now create real products independently. "When else in history could you get out of school and just build a startup on your own?" The key is learning to use AI as a force multiplier rather than waiting for traditional career progression.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/julian-shapiro.md]
