# Michael Truell

Co-founder and CEO of Anysphere, the company behind Cursor — the fastest-growing AI code editor, reaching $300M ARR within two years of launch. Studied computer science and math at MIT, did AI research at MIT and Google. One of the most consequential builders in the AI-assisted programming space.

## Key Views

### 1. The Goal Is a New Kind of Programming "After Code"

Cursor's vision is not a better text editor — it's inventing a new type of programming where you describe intent to the computer in the most concise way possible. Engineering will shift toward being a "logic designer" who specifies how software should work and look. This is distinct from both the "text editing forever" view and the "talk to a chatbot" view — it's a higher-level, more productive paradigm.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/michael-truell.md]

### 2. Every Magic Moment in Cursor Involves a Custom Model

The most counterintuitive lesson: Anysphere didn't expect to do their own model development, but "at this point, every magic moment in Cursor involves a custom model in some way." Building a great AI product means not just wrapping existing APIs but deeply integrating model development into the product experience. This is a strategic moat.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/michael-truell.md]

### 3. They Hired Too Slow Initially

"Many people you hear hire too fast. I think we actually hired too slow to begin with." The conventional wisdom about cautious hiring doesn't always apply when you're in a hyper-growth market. Speed of team building was a bottleneck that cost them time in the early innings.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/michael-truell.md]

### 4. Exponential Growth Feels Slow at First

Cursor went from $0 to $100M ARR in 20 months, then to $300M in 2 years. But exponential growth "to begin with feels fairly slow when the numbers are really low." The consistent exponential, not an inflection point, characterized their trajectory. The lesson: don't be discouraged when early numbers are small — the compounding is happening.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/michael-truell.md]

### 5. Deep Domain History Informs Product Vision

Truell has been working on AI for 10 years and is a student of tech and business history. This combination — deep technical understanding of the trajectory plus historical perspective on platform shifts — informs Cursor's conviction about where programming is heading. Breakthrough products require understanding not just the technology but the historical patterns of how computing paradigms shift.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/michael-truell.md]
