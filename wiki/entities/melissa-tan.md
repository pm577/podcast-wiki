# Melissa Tan

Melissa Tan is a growth expert and advisor who has worked with Dropbox (first growth PM, long-time head of growth for B2B), Webflow (head of growth), Canva, Grammarly, Miro, and Ro. She helps companies find product-market fit and scale growth through go-to-market strategy, team building, and growth systems.

## Key Views

**First Principles Thinking in Growth** — At Dropbox, they hired incredibly smart people who had never done sales and had them do sales. The advantage: they came in with no preconceptions and innovated new go-to-market motions. Those people then moved into other functions with deep context on the user, spreading growth thinking across the company. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/melissa-tan.md]

**Building High-Performing Teams** — The most common ingredients of a high-performing growth team: clear outcome-focused goals, a culture of experimentation, strong cross-functional collaboration, and people who are genuinely curious about user behavior. Leaders that people will follow from company to company create environments where team members feel developed and empowered. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/melissa-tan.md]

**Go-to-Market Strategy: Product-Led vs. Sales-Led** — Early-stage companies need to think carefully about their GTM motion: should they be product-led, sales-led, or hybrid? The decision impacts everything from pricing and packaging to team structure and metrics. Most mistakes come from choosing a GTM model because it's trendy rather than because it fits the product and market. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/melissa-tan.md]

**First Growth Hire Advice** — The first growth hire should be someone who can think across the entire funnel (acquisition, activation, monetization, retention) and has both analytical and creative skills. They need to be comfortable with ambiguity and able to build growth processes from scratch. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/melissa-tan.md]

**Interviewing PMs for Growth** — Melissa shares her actual interview process for growth PMs: focus on how they think about prioritization, experimentation design, and what they've learned from both successes and failures. The best growth PMs have strong opinions loosely held and a demonstrated ability to move metrics through a combination of analytical rigor and creative problem-solving. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/melissa-tan.md]
