# Matt Mochary

Matt Mochary is a full-time executive coach who has worked with Naval Ravikant, the CEOs of OpenAI, Coinbase, Reddit, Rippling, Notion, and partners at Sequoia, Y Combinator, Benchmark, and other top VC firms. He is author of *The Great CEO Within* and creator of the widely-used Mochary Method Curriculum.

## Key Views

**Fear Is the Biggest Barrier for Even the Most Successful Founders** — Most founders and leaders feel fear to some degree, and when they do, it grips their mind and prevents them from doing what is difficult but necessary. The bar is fear — how strongly do people feel it? The few who don't feel fear at all are the exception. The majority need coaching to recognize when they're operating from fear versus clear-minded decision-making. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/matt-mochary.md]

**How to Conduct a Layoff with Grace and Dignity** — The biggest marker between a botched and successful layoff: did the person hear it from their manager in a one-on-one? If they heard it from an email, a group chat, or alongside other people, that's when people get angry and go public. Being one-on-one, human, and allowing the person to express their emotions is the most important factor. The Mochary Method emphasizes treating people with respect even in the hardest moments. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/matt-mochary.md]

**Fears Point in the Opposite Direction of Where You Should Go** — Anger and fear often signal the exact opposite of the right action. When you feel strong emotional resistance to something, that's often a sign it's the thing you most need to do. Coaching involves helping leaders identify when fear is running the show and then moving toward the discomfort rather than away from it. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/matt-mochary.md]

**Practical Tools Over Theory** — The Mochary Method Curriculum document is a compilation of incredibly practical, tactical, and useful templates and processes for leaders — covering everything from one-on-one meeting structures to decision-making frameworks to goal-setting. Matt's approach is relentlessly practical, drawing from real experience coaching the world's top founders. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/matt-mochary.md]

**Building Trust Through Radical Honesty** — The foundation of effective coaching and leadership is creating an environment where hard truths can be said without fear of retaliation. Leaders who create psychological safety and are open to receiving difficult feedback build the strongest, highest-performing teams. Withholding feedback because it's uncomfortable is a form of selfishness. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/matt-mochary.md]
