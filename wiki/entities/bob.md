# Bob Moesta

Bob Moesta is the co-creator of the Jobs To Be Done (JTBD) framework and a close collaborator of the late Clayton Christensen. He is the CEO and founder of The Re-Wired Group, has started nine different companies, and has helped launch over 3,500 products. His new book, *Job Moves: 9 Steps to Finding the Right Job*, applies JTBD thinking to career decisions, which he believes will be his most impactful contribution yet.

## Key Views

### Employees Hire Companies More Than Companies Hire Employees
Bob's foundational insight: the moment you stop making progress in your career is the moment you start looking for another job. "Employees hire companies more than companies hire employees." Most people attribute job changes to luck, but luck is when opportunity meets preparedness. The key is understanding the pushes (13 types of dissatisfiers) and pulls (14 types of attractors) that drive job moves.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/bob-moesta-20.md]

### The Four Quests That Drive Job Changes
Through 1,000+ interviews, Bob identified four distinct quests people are on when they change jobs. **Get Out** — your energy is being sucked, you just need to escape and breathe. **Take the Next Step** — you want to build new skills and capabilities. **Regain Control** — you like the work but there's too much of it, home life is falling apart. **Realign** — you've been stretched into areas you're not good at and need to get back to what you do best. Each quest requires a different search and decision strategy.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/bob-moesta-20.md]

### Energy Drivers vs. Energy Drains
Bob's core framework for finding the right job: identify the moments in your career that gave you energy (energy drivers) and the moments that sucked the life out of you (energy drains). Most people spend 95% of their time on drains for 5% joy. The goal is to get to a 50/50 ratio where you don't even feel like you're working. "If you can pull that ratio to 40% energy drive or 50-50, you don't even know you're working anymore."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/bob-moesta-20.md]

### Job Features vs. Job Experiences
Bob draws a direct parallel to product: there's a difference between product features and product experiences. Similarly, job features (salary, title) are different from job experiences. Money is often a surrogate for respect, or a signal of falling behind, or a way to keep score. "Everybody wants more money, but the question is why do you want more money?" Unpacking the real meaning behind what you think you want is the path to finding the right job.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/bob-moesta-20.md]

### The Jobcation — A Prescription for Recovery
For people leaving intense startup environments, Bob recommends a "jobcation" — taking a job you can do "with one hand tied behind my back" to rest and recover. "When you're in a startup, it changes who you are and the moment you get out of that environment, you need to take the time to reset your mind and your body." The moment you are comfortable doing nothing, you know who you are again and can figure out what comes next.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/bob-moesta-20.md]
