# Shweta Shrivastava

Shweta Shrivastava is a Senior Product Leader at Waymo, where she builds software for fully autonomous vehicles. Previously she was CPO of Nauto, Head of Product at AWS for database and analytics services, and held product roles at Cisco and Amazon. She brings a unique perspective on building AI-powered physical products at scale.

## Key Views

**The MVP bar for safety is fundamentally different in autonomous driving.** Unlike SaaS products where you can ship an MVP and iterate, Waymo's MVP bar for safety is extremely high from the start because lives are at stake. This changes the entire product development philosophy — you can't "cut corners on safety" and the concept of minimum viable has a dramatically different meaning when the product operates in the physical world.

**Autonomous vehicles must feel human-like to build trust, but in a good way.** Waymo trains its deep learning models on human driving data while discarding bad driving behaviors. The goal is for the car to feel natural and predictable — not robotic. Subtle behaviors like inching into a lane to signal intent, slowing down on steep San Francisco hills, and never exceeding the speed limit all contribute to building rider trust. Within five minutes, riders report the experience feeling "as if this is how it was always meant to be."

**Challenging your own assumptions is the most important PM skill.** Shweta emphasizes that if there's no conflict or contention in your product discussions, you're probably not listening hard enough. Senior PMs and leaders must proactively question their own assumptions rather than waiting for others to challenge them. This is especially critical at Waymo where the technology is unprecedented and established patterns don't exist.

**Autonomous driving is a highly interactive social system, not just a technology problem.** Driving involves social norms, gestures, eye contact, and unspoken rules that vary by city and culture. Waymo's system must understand pedestrian intent (body orientation, which way they're looking), road user gestures, and local social norms (e.g., when pedestrians cross without a walk sign). The sophistication of human driving is vastly underestimated.

**PMs in autonomous driving need deep technical engagement and mission-driven tenacity.** Unlike traditional software PM roles, Waymo PMs must go deep technically, be comfortable with extreme uncertainty and ambiguity, and play a "long game" that requires patience. The mission of saving 1.35 million lives lost annually to traffic accidents drives the team through the challenges of building what Shweta calls "the most game-changing product anybody would ever work on."
