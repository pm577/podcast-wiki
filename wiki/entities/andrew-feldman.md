# Andrew Feldman

Co-founder and CEO of Cerebras Systems. Led Cerebras through the largest semiconductor IPO in history ($70BN market cap). Cerebras has a massive multi-year $20B compute agreement with OpenAI.

## Key Views

### 1. We Are Not in an Infrastructure Bubble — It's Just the Start

Andrew argues that the AI infrastructure buildout is not a speculative bubble but the early stages of a fundamental technological shift. The demand for compute driven by AI models and agents will continue to grow exponentially, and the current investment levels are rational given the opportunity ahead.^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2026-05-26-20vc-cerebras-ceo-on-the-futu.md]

### 2. Sam Altman's Superpower Is Forecasting Capex

Andrew identified Sam Altman's unique ability to forecast capital expenditure needs as his key superpower. OpenAI's massive compute commitments, including the $20B agreement with Cerebras, reflect a strategic understanding that compute will be the scarce resource in the AI race — and locking it in early creates a durable moat.^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2026-05-26-20vc-cerebras-ceo-on-the-futu.md]

### 3. Anthropic Did Not Get a Good Deal with Elon for Compute

Andrew offered a critical perspective on Anthropic's compute deal with Elon Musk, suggesting the terms were unfavorable compared to what was available in the market. This reflects his broader view that compute procurement strategy will be a key competitive differentiator for AI labs.^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2026-05-26-20vc-cerebras-ceo-on-the-futu.md]

### 4. Should US Companies Sell to China?

Andrew weighed in on the debate around selling US AI hardware to China, reflecting the geopolitical tensions in the semiconductor industry. His perspective is grounded in the reality that Cerebras operates at the intersection of cutting-edge technology and national security considerations.^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2026-05-26-20vc-cerebras-ceo-on-the-futu.md]

### 5. Most Layoffs Are AI-Washed

Andrew argued that many companies are using "AI" as a justification for layoffs when the real reasons are unrelated to AI. The trend of announcing AI-driven restructurings masks more mundane cost-cutting and strategic pivots that would have happened anyway.^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2026-05-26-20vc-cerebras-ceo-on-the-futu.md]
