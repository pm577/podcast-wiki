# Carilu Dietrich

Carilu Dietrich is a former CMO who led Atlassian through its IPO. She now advises CEOs and CMOs of hypergrowth B2B companies including Segment, Miro, 1Password, Bill.com, Productboard, Sprout Social, and Weights & Biases.

## Key Views

### Hypergrowth Requires Organic, Inbound, and Viral — You Can't Pay for It
To achieve hypergrowth, you must have organic, inbound, and viral word-of-mouth growth channels. You literally can't pay enough to grow at those rates and have a viable company. The core requirement is an amazing product that people love to use — like ChatGPT, the most hypergrowth product in history. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/carilu-dietrich.md]

### Ride the Lightning — Hire 2x and 3x Leaders
Hypergrowth companies compress 5-10 years of growth into 1-2 years. They go from 10 to 50 to 100 to 200 employees in rapid succession. The key to surviving this: keep hiring leaders who have seen the next stage of growth. Hire people who've already done what you're about to attempt — before you need them. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/carilu-dietrich.md]

### Product and Marketing Must Be a Single Function
Carilu argues that in hypergrowth B2B companies, product and marketing cannot be siloed. CMOs who treat marketing as a separate function from product fail. The modern CMO must be data-driven, understand the product intimately, and participate in the product iteration cycle — not just broadcast messages about features they didn't help create. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/carilu-dietrich.md]

### Brand Is an Ongoing Investment, Not a Campaign
Traditional marketers treat brand as a campaign with a start and end date. In hypergrowth, brand must be treated as a continuous investment with measurable returns. Every interaction — from product UX to support to content — builds or erodes brand. The CMO must think in systems, not campaigns. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/carilu-dietrich.md]

### Don't Mistake Activity for Progress
In hypergrowth, the noise level is immense. Carilu emphasizes that activity (more campaigns, more channels, more content) doesn't equal progress. The discipline of saying "no" to good ideas to focus on great ones is what separates hypergrowth companies from those that plateau. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/carilu-dietrich.md]
