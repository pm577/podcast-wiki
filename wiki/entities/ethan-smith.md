# Ethan Smith

**Role:** CEO of Graphite (SEO growth agency)  
**Known for:** 18+ years in SEO, pioneering Answer Engine Optimization (AEO), and being Lenny's go-to SEO expert.

## Key Views

### 1. AEO = Optimizing for LLM citations (RAG), not training data

Ethan clarifies that Answer Engine Optimization is about influencing how LLMs cite your product when they answer questions — primarily through the **RAG (retrieval-augmented generation)** layer, not the core training data. The LLM performs a search, retrieves a set of citations, and summarizes them. If you don't show up in the search results, the LLM can't cite you. This means AEO is fundamentally an SEO-adjacent discipline with additional levers. Everything that works in SEO works in AEO — but there are *additional* things beyond SEO that also work.

> "In order to win something like 'what's the best website builder?' at Google, they would win if their blue link showed up first. But that's not the case in the LLM. The LLM is summarizing many citations, and so you need to get mentioned as many times as possible." — Ethan Smith

### 2. LLM traffic converts 6x better than Google traffic

Through his work with Webflow, Ethan found that visitors coming from LLM citations convert at **6x the rate** of Google search traffic. He attributes this to the conversational nature of LLM interactions: by the time a user clicks through, they've already narrowed their intent through multiple follow-up questions and are highly primed. For B2B SaaS companies specifically, this means AEO investment can produce dramatically higher-quality leads than traditional SEO — making it a high-ROI channel even for startups that can't yet compete on domain authority.

> "There's so much intent that you've built through multiple follow-ups. You've really narrowed in on what you want, so when you're going somewhere, it's probably highly qualified." — Ethan Smith

### 3. Early-stage companies can win quickly at AEO — no domain authority needed

Ethan's key distinction from traditional SEO: you don't need to build domain authority over years to show up in AEO. **A single Reddit thread, YouTube video, or blog mention can put you in LLM citations tomorrow.** Early-stage companies can punch above their weight by getting mentioned across citation sources. The head (high-volume queries) is harder because you need mentions from many different authorities. But the **tail is larger** — people ask much longer, more specific questions in chat (average 25 words vs. 6 words in search), creating an explosion of long-tail queries that never existed in traditional search. A startup with a very specific product can dominate these niche questions immediately.

> "Early-stage companies can win, they can win quickly. And they can win quickly and anyone can win quickly, by getting mentioned as many times as possible by the citations." — Ethan Smith

### 4. Three AEO levers: SEO content, YouTube, and authentic Reddit

Ethan's proven playbook for winning at AEO breaks down into three primary channels:
- **Onsite SEO:** Traditional landing pages optimized for topics (not individual keywords), answering every follow-up question comprehensively. The more questions you answer, the more likely you are to get cited.
- **YouTube/Vimeo videos:** Surprisingly uncontested for B2B topics — there aren't many videos about "AI-powered payment processing APIs," making it a blue-ocean opportunity for high-LTV keywords.
- **Reddit optimization:** The most powerful but hardest to fake. The right approach: create a real account, state who you are and where you work, and give genuinely useful answers. Five high-quality comments beat 10,000 spammy ones. Reddit's community policing protects LLMs from spam — ChatGPT's search algorithm specifically trusts Reddit as a citation source.

> "The Reddit strategy is the obvious strategy, which is just to be an actual user of Reddit. Make an account, say who you are, say where you work, and give a useful answer." — Ethan Smith

### 5. Experiment and track — don't trust AEO best practices blindly

Ethan notes that the majority of AEO "best practices" online are unvalidated because people don't run controlled experiments. His methodology: use **answer tracking tools** (similar to keyword tracking but measuring share of voice across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini) to establish a baseline. Run experiments with test and control groups. The output is a **share of voice** metric — what percentage of query runs does your brand appear in? — and an average rank. His advice: pick the cheapest tracker that works (they're all similar), design experiments rigorously, and don't assume anything you read online is correct without testing it yourself. This echoes the broader SEO truth that most "best practices" are recycled without evidence.

> "Design experiment and see if it worked. Maybe half the stuff I said works, maybe half it doesn't. Do your own experiment." — Ethan Smith
