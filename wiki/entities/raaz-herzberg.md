# Raaz Herzberg

**Role:** CMO and VP of Product Strategy at Wiz — the cloud security company that became the fastest-growing startup in history, hitting $100M ARR in 18 months and $500M+ ARR by year five  
**Known for:** Being one of the first 10 employees at Wiz; helping build the product strategy and go-to-market motion that created the fastest-growing enterprise startup ever; cybersecurity product leadership.

## Key Views

### 1. Find the Unconventional Go-to-Market Angle

Wiz succeeded not by competing head-on with incumbents but by finding an unconventional GTM angle. Instead of targeting security teams directly, they went through cloud infrastructure teams (devops, platform engineering) who had the pain point of visibility. This side door into organizations was faster and faced less competitive resistance. The lesson: if you're entering a crowded market, don't use the same playbook as incumbents. Find the buyer or entry point your competitors are ignoring.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/raaz-herzberg.md]

> "We didn't try to beat the incumbents at their own game. We found a different entry point — the teams that were actually feeling the pain of cloud complexity." — Raaz Herzberg

### 2. Speed of Execution is the Ultimate Moat

Wiz's growth was built on extreme speed. They shipped faster, iterated faster, and closed deals faster than competitors. Raaz emphasizes that in enterprise software, speed is a feature — the company that can solve a customer's problem fastest wins, even if their product is less mature. Wiz's culture of urgency and rapid iteration meant they could out-execute much larger, well-funded competitors who were slowed by process and bureaucracy.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/raaz-herzberg.md]

> "In security, the threat landscape changes every day. The company that can respond fastest wins, period. Speed isn't just a nice-to-have — it's existential." — Raaz Herzberg

### 3. Product-Led Growth in Enterprise is Real

Wiz pioneered a product-led approach in enterprise security — usually a top-down, sales-driven category. They made the product easy to try, quick to deploy, and immediately valuable so individual engineers and teams could adopt it without a lengthy procurement process. This bottom-up adoption created internal champions who then pulled in the broader organization. The key: make the first experience so good that users want to bring it to their boss, not the other way around.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/raaz-herzberg.md]

> "If you can make an engineer's life better in the first 10 minutes, they'll go tell their security team. That's how you crack enterprise — through the people doing the work." — Raaz Herzberg

### 4. Build for the Cloud-Native Reality

Wiz's core product insight: the world had moved to the cloud, but security tools were still built for on-premise data centers. Wiz was built cloud-native from day one — it understood containers, serverless, Kubernetes, and multi-cloud architectures natively. This architectural advantage meant they could provide visibility and security that legacy tools simply couldn't. The lesson for any product builder: when the infrastructure shifts, build for the new reality rather than trying to bolt onto the old one.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/raaz-herzberg.md]

> "Legacy security tools were built for a world that doesn't exist anymore. We built for the cloud from the ground up — that was our biggest strategic advantage." — Raaz Herzberg

### 5. Stay Independent and Think Long-Term

Despite massive acquisition offers (including Google's $23B offer that ultimately fell through), Wiz chose to stay independent. Raaz's perspective: independence allows you to make long-term bets that a larger parent company might not tolerate. It also keeps the team focused on the mission rather than integration. For founders, the lesson is to carefully evaluate whether an exit or independence gives you the best platform to execute your vision. Independence can be a competitive advantage if you have the discipline to think in decades, not quarters.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/raaz-herzberg.md]

> "Staying on an independent path, we all really believe that Wiz can become one of the biggest security companies in the world." — Raaz Herzberg
