# Will Larson

Will Larson appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss the engineering mindset — treating engineers as adults, good strategy is boring, and em/pm pairs.

## Key Views

**Treat Engineers as Adults, Not Children** — The ZIRP era forced leaders to coddle engineers for retention, which actually prevented them from being put into senior roles because leaders weren't comfortable holding them accountable; the current market shift finally lets us treat engineers as peers and give them real responsibility. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/will-larson.md]

**Reality Is Always Right, Your Model Is Always Wrong** — People who get too deep into systems thinking believe reality is wrong when it conflicts with their model, but reality is never wrong — the gap is where you learn, and you must eventually stop measuring and actually do the work. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/will-larson.md]

**Good Strategy Is Boring** — The most effective engineering strategies are mundane constraints like "we only use the tools we have today" (the standard kit), which focus energy on the company's real problems rather than letting engineers chase novel technologies — the goal of strategy is not to appease everyone. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/will-larson.md]

**Give EM/PM Pairs the Same Performance Rating** — At Carta, the engineering manager and product manager share the same performance review rating, which aligns their incentives and forces them to solve for the entire set of constraints together rather than being adversaries with conflicting goals. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/will-larson.md]

**Good Values Must Be Honest, Applicable, and Reversible** — Many company values fail because they aren't reversible (nobody would argue against "integrity" or "we care about customers"), aren't honestly reflective of actual decisions, or can't be applied to real trade-offs — a useful value has to be one you could imagine not having. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/will-larson.md]
