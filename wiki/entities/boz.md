# Andrew Bosworth (Boz)

Andrew "Boz" Bosworth is the CTO of Meta and head of Reality Labs (AR/VR). He was one of Facebook's first engineers (2006, employee ~10) and during his 18-year tenure created the Newsfeed (the first algorithmically ranked social feed), built the original Facebook mobile ads platform, and helped establish the engineering bootcamp. He later led the ads and business platform before creating Reality Labs in 2017.

## Key Views

**Communication Is the Job** — As a leader, your primary job is communication. Boz learned that the gap between what you think you've communicated and what people actually understand is enormous. Over-communicate, repeat the message in different formats, and constantly check for understanding. Almost every org failure traces back to a communication breakdown. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/boz.md]

**The Newsfeed Lesson: Data Beats Outrage** — When Newsfeed launched, people were publicly outraged — yet immediately doubled their usage. The lesson: don't confuse loud voices with representative feedback. Measure what people do, not what they say. The Newsfeed-ads combined flywheel created $1 trillion+ of value, but at launch it seemed like a disaster. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/boz.md]

**Early Facebook Was Glamourless Grinding** — "I didn't sleep for more than four hours at a time. I'd wake up every four hours and check the report and see if anyone was attacking the site." The romanticized startup story hides the reality of constant firefighting, sleep deprivation, and uncertainty. But that intensity was also what made the team care so deeply. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/boz.md]

**Ask Your Manager for Help More Often** — One of Boz's most counterintuitive career insights: your manager wants to help you, and asking for help is a sign of strength, not weakness. Managers have context, resources, and influence you don't. The people who succeed fastest are the ones who constantly pull on their manager's expertise and connections. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/boz.md]

**Meta's Turnaround: Focus and Execution** — Meta's pivot from "metaverse bet everything" to a more balanced approach (AI leadership, ads recovery, Reality Labs with clearer milestones) came from re-establishing focus. The company's biggest challenge was doing too many things. The turnaround came from ruthlessly prioritizing — kill the bottom 50% of projects and double down on the top 20%. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/boz.md]
