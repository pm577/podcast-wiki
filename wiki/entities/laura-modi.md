# Laura Modi

Founder and CEO of Bobbie, the only female-founded and mom-led organic infant formula company in the US. Previously spent 5.5 years at Airbnb as Director of Hospitality, leading the global host community. Known for building a mission-driven CPG brand in a highly regulated industry dominated by a decades-old duopoly.

## Key Views

### 1. Culture Is Not a Perk — It's the Engine

At Airbnb, "energy was the currency." Modi carries this into Bobbie by prioritizing personal connections even in a remote-first environment. Her approach: build a culture where people genuinely care about each other, with regular personal and professional check-ins across the org. "If you can get people excited behind a vision and storytelling, it carries through cross-functional work."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/laura-modi.md]

### 2. Sometimes You Need to Slow Growth to Protect the Business

At Bobbie, the head of growth identified that inventory was being depleted faster than it could be replenished — with only six days of stock for existing customers. The counterintuitive decision: turn off the site and stop growing. "We need to turn off our site and stop growing the business" to protect commitments already made. Preserving trust with existing customers matters more than short-term growth.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/laura-modi.md]

### 3. Storytelling Is the Most Powerful Leadership Tool

At Airbnb, even the smallest features were launched with powerful storytelling. "We were changing lives" was the frame. This makes people wake up and say, "Fuck, yeah, I want to work on this." The lesson: storytelling isn't just marketing — it's how you get internal teams to bring their full energy to a project. This has to be felt all the way down the org.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/laura-modi.md]

### 4. The Most Innovative Ideas Come from Outsiders

Modi had no experience in CPG or infant formula before starting Bobbie. "One of the last remaining industries in the CPG space that has seen any disruption or change in probably 40 years" was exactly why she could innovate — she wasn't bound by industry conventions. Her lack of experience was an asset, not a liability.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/laura-modi.md]

### 5. Big Leaps Require Major Risk — But Conviction, Not Luck, Drives Them

"Taking a big leap without first taking a major risk" is Modi's career pattern: leaving Google for a struggling early Airbnb, leaving Airbnb to start a powdered milk company. But it's not blind luck — it's intentional: researching the marketplace, understanding the business, determining financial runway, and feeling deep conviction. "I put a lot of intention into researching the marketplace, understanding the business, determining how much of a risk I'm really able to take."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/laura-modi.md]
