# Ebi Atawodi

*Director of Product Management for Creator Experience at YouTube. Former Head of Product at Uber and Director of Product (Payments and EMEA) at Netflix. Known for her framework on crafting compelling product visions.*

## Key Views

1. **A great vision has four elements**: It must be (1) lofty — almost scary in an exciting way, (2) realistic and attainable — within reach for the team, (3) devoid of today's technological limitations — time-travel to the future and describe what you see, and (4) grounded in a clear, potent user problem. A city without parking lots was Uber's vision; that's a picture you can see.

2. **Vision is the picture, mission is the purpose**: Vision is fast-forwarding to the future and describing what you see (a city without parking, a world where you don't need a car). Mission is the purpose behind it (make transportation as reliable as running water). Confusing them leads to visions that are too abstract or missions that aren't inspiring enough.

3. **"I do not believe in being liked — I believe in being loved"**: Ebi defines love as extending yourself for the spiritual growth of another. This means having hard conversations raw, knowing that the person knows you genuinely care. Being liked is comfortable; being loved means you occasionally deliver uncomfortable feedback for their growth.

4. **Product visions should exist at every level**: Even a junior PM needs a micro-vision for their area. If a pilot says "I don't know where we're going but I'm a good pilot," you'd get off the plane. The same applies to product — your team needs to know the destination even if the full company vision is set at the exec level.

5. **Use the mission to challenge leadership**: At Uber, Ebi saw the mission "reliable transportation everywhere for everyone" used to challenge the CEO when strategic decisions deviated from it. A well-crafted mission becomes a tool for the whole organization to hold itself accountable — not just a marketing slogan.
