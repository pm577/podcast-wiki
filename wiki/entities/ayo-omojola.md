# Ayo Omojola

Ayo Omojola co-created and scaled Square's Cash Card and the Cash App. He's currently Chief Product Officer at Carbon Health, one of the fastest-growing health tech companies. Previously he was a founder (Mailform), an angel investor in Mercury Bank, Fair, and Modern Treasury, and sits on the board of Pinwheel.

## Key Views

**Differentiate in a Way That Matters** — Being different is easy. Being better is easy. The hard part is being both different and better in a way that matters to the end user. Cash App's winning differentiator: "Send me a dollar I can use now." No other app could do same-day cash access. That concrete, user-visible difference drove adoption. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/ayo-omojola.md]

**Build a Startup Within a Startup** — Ayo's approach to building Cash Card within Square was to treat it as an independent startup with complete ownership, while leveraging Square's brand and resources. This requires clear boundaries, a separate P&L mindset, and a leader who can navigate internal politics while moving fast. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/ayo-omojola.md]

**First-Principles Team Building** — When hiring, start from: "What does success look like for this role in 6 months?" then work backwards to the skills, experience, and temperament needed. Avoid "clone hiring" (hiring people like yourself). Look for pattern breaks — candidates who solved similar problems in different contexts bring fresh thinking. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/ayo-omojola.md]

**Deep Problems Beat Broad Ones** — Go deep on one problem rather than shallow on many. Cash App focused relentlessly on peer-to-peer payments before expanding into banking, investing, and Bitcoin. The depth-first approach builds a moat: once you own a core user behavior deeply, adjacent expansions become natural. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/ayo-omojola.md]

**Fintech and Healthtech Share a Core Truth** — Both are regulated, trust-intensive industries where the product is a promise. In both, the winning companies are the ones that make complex compliance feel invisible while delivering a consumer-grade experience. The principles for building in fintech (speed, simplicity, transparency) apply directly to healthtech. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/ayo-omojola.md]
