# Nikita Miller

Nikita Miller appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss nikita miller on driving alignment within teams, work-life optimization, and the changing pm landscape.

## Key Views

**Balance Outcomes with Output — Velocity Is a Leading Indicator** — Nikita warns that the industry swung too far on the "outcomes, outcomes, outcomes" train, forgetting that output is an indicator of progress. Teams can have perfect product briefs, design briefs, and experiment briefs, but if they aren't shipping many things to market quickly enough, the outcomes don't matter. Execution velocity and shipping cadence are the real proof points. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/nikita-miller.md]

**The Roles & Responsibilities Contract for Cross-Functional Teams** — Her core framework for team alignment: have product managers, designers, engineering managers, and data scientists each independently write down what they believe their own role is and what they expect of their counterparts. Then the group reviews together and arrives at a shared "contract." This exercise is especially critical because the PM role is notoriously ill-defined and varies widely across companies. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/nikita-miller.md]

**Reframe Work-Life Balance as 'What Are You Optimizing For?'** — Nikita rejects the concept of balance in favor of optimization — asking "what are you optimizing for right now?" at different time horizons (day, quarter, year, life stage). You can't have it all at the same time, but you can intentionally choose what to prioritize, whether it's a sprint at work or handling family drop-offs for a week. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/nikita-miller.md]

**Embed Data as the Fourth Pillar of the Product Team** — She advocates making data science/analytics a dedicated fourth member of the product triad (product, design, engineering, data — "the chair"). Data scientists become far more effective when focused on a specific product area rather than fielding tickets from a central team, and embedding them removes blockers that slow down decision-making and experimentation. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/nikita-miller.md]

**The Single Most Powerful Question: 'What Are You Optimizing For?'** — Nikita asks this question constantly — to her team during quarterly planning, to her husband, even to her five-year-old. It cuts through complexity, clarifies trade-offs, and forces explicit prioritization. If the answer to "what are we optimizing for" isn't clear, then no trade-off decision can be correctly made. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/nikita-miller.md]
