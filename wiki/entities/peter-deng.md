# Peter Deng

Peter Deng is one of the most impactful yet under-the-radar product leaders in tech. He was VP of Product at OpenAI (ChatGPT, ChatGPT Enterprise), first Head of Product at Instagram, Head of Rider Product at Uber, 4th PM at Facebook (Newsfeed, Messenger, photos, groups), and CPO at Airtable. He also led product management at Oculus and is now a General Partner at Felicis.

## Key Views

**The 6-Month Hiring Test** — "In 6 months, if I'm telling you what to do, I've hired the wrong person." This shifts the relationship from task execution to strategic calibration. The goal isn't "did you hit this OKR?" — it's "are we calibrating enough so that you're the one telling me what needs to be done?" Hire for agency and taste, then get out of the way. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/peter-deng.md]

**Sometimes Your Product Doesn't Actually Matter** — At Uber, Peter learned that price and ETA were the product — not the app features. Bugs and UI polish had less impact than getting the core promise right. This reframe changes where you invest engineering resources: the thing customers actually care about might not be the thing your product team is optimizing. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/peter-deng.md]

**Plan Chess Moves in Advance, Then Build Systems for Speed** — Across Facebook, Instagram, Uber, and OpenAI, the consistent lesson: think before you act, plan the sequence of moves, but then build systems that let you execute sustainably fast. Sustainable speed comes from infrastructure and discipline, not heroics. This is how you go from idea to billions. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/peter-deng.md]

**Education Will Be Transformed by AI** — Peter's 9-year-old son built a custom GPT that generates sentences containing every letter of the alphabet. His brain is already rewiring. The generation growing up with AI will think differently — not just use AI as a tool, but think in terms of what they can create with it. This has profound implications for how we educate and hire. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/peter-deng.md]

**Five Types of Product Managers** — Peter's taxonomy: (1) The Strategist — sets vision and direction, (2) The Builder — ships fast and iterates, (3) The Analyst — data-driven and rigorous, (4) The Advocate — deeply understands users, (5) The Leader — aligns the org. Great PMs can flex across multiple, but most have a dominant type. Knowing your type helps you play to your strengths and build complementary teams. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/peter-deng.md]
