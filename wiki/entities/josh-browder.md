# Josh Browder

Founder of Browder Capital, an emerging manager who turned his $100K Thiel Fellowship Grant into a $10M angel portfolio. First check investor into unicorns including Micro1, Owner.com, and Yuzu Health. Founder & CEO of DoNotPay. Known for his unconventional "one-man accelerator" model where portfolio companies live at his Four Seasons residence until they raise their seed round.

## Key Views

### 1. Turning $100K into $10M: The Thiel Fellowship Edge

Josh received a Thiel Fellowship grant of $100K — and turned it into a $10M angel portfolio. His approach: invest early in founders who are building in spaces he understands, provide hands-on operational support, and leverage his network to help portfolio companies raise follow-on capital. The Thiel Fellowship gave him the capital and credibility to start investing before traditional angel investors typically begin.^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2026-05-18-20vc-turning-peter-thiels-1.md]

### 2. The One-Man Accelerator at the Four Seasons

Josh's unique model: portfolio companies move into his Four Seasons residence where he trains them on company building. They are only allowed to leave when they raise their seed round. This immersive approach provides intense, around-the-clock mentorship that he believes is more effective than traditional accelerator programs.^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2026-05-18-20vc-turning-peter-thiels-1.md]

### 3. Why VCs Can Be Sharks (and What Founders Need to Know)

Josh is candid about the dynamics between founders and VCs, warning that many investors prioritize their own economics over founder success. His advice: founders should understand term sheet mechanics, negotiate ownership carefully, and be skeptical of investors who promise more than they can deliver.^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2026-05-18-20vc-turning-peter-thiels-1.md]

### 4. Why Stocks and Cash Are BS — Invest in Land

Josh holds an unconventional view on asset allocation: he argues that stocks and cash are poor stores of value compared to land. His investment philosophy extends beyond startup investing into real assets, reflecting a broader skepticism about traditional financial markets.^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2026-05-18-20vc-turning-peter-thiels-1.md]
