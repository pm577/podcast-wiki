# Shiv Rao

CEO and Co-Founder of Abridge, a leader in generative AI for healthcare. Practicing cardiologist. Scaled the company to $5.3BN valuation and 450 employees following a $300M funding round with investors including Jensen Huang, Henry Kravis, USV, Bessemer Venture Partners, and Elad Gil. Partnered with major health systems like Emory and Yale.

## Key Views

### 1. You Just Have to Survive Long Enough to Not Die

Shiv's core founder philosophy: in healthcare AI, the path to success is measured in years, not months. Abridge spent five years in a "desert" before finding product-market fit. The key insight is that many healthcare startups fail not because the product is wrong, but because they run out of runway before the market catches up to their vision.^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2026-05-16-20vc-lessons-from-jensen-huan.md]

### 2. How USV Liking Music Made Them $1BN

A fascinating story about USV's investment in Abridge: the partner who led the deal was initially interested because of a shared music connection with Shiv, not because of a deep healthcare thesis. This serendipitous connection led to one of USV's best healthcare investments, demonstrating that pattern-matching and personal resonance can be as important as domain expertise in early-stage investing.^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2026-05-16-20vc-lessons-from-jensen-huan.md]

### 3. The 5-Year Desert to Product-Market Fit

Abridge's journey to PMF took five grueling years. Shiv's framework for navigating this: maintain enough financial discipline to survive, iterate relentlessly based on clinician feedback, and resist the temptation to pivot too early. The breakthrough came when the technology (generative AI) finally caught up to the vision of what ambient clinical documentation could be.^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2026-05-16-20vc-lessons-from-jensen-huan.md]

### 4. Lessons from Jensen Huang on "Founder Mode"

Shiv shared what he learned from Jensen Huang about founder-led companies. Jensen's "founder mode" — staying deeply involved in product details, maintaining direct communication lines, and refusing to delegate strategic understanding — is a model Shiv applies at Abridge. The lesson: founders who stay close to the product and customer create better outcomes than those who "professionalize" management too early.^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2026-05-16-20vc-lessons-from-jensen-huan.md]

### 5. How to Know If OpenAI or Anthropic Will Kill Your Company

Shiv's framework for assessing existential threat from AI labs: the closer your product is to a general capability (transcription, summarization), the more vulnerable you are. Abridge survives because they focused on the clinical workflow integration and regulatory compliance layer — things AI labs are unlikely to prioritize. The moat is depth in a specific vertical, not breadth of AI capability.^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2026-05-16-20vc-lessons-from-jensen-huan.md]
