# Madhavan Ramanujam

Madhavan Ramanujam is the world's foremost expert on pricing and monetization strategy. He was managing partner at Simon-Kucher (working with 250+ companies including 30 unicorns), is author of *Monetizing Innovation* and *Scaling Innovation*, and now invests full-time with his own fund (49 Palms VC) focusing on early-stage AI companies.

## Key Views

**Profitable Growth Requires Dual Engines** — The core thesis of *Scaling Innovation*: founders must master both market share (acquisition) and wallet share (monetization + retention). Companies that focus on a single engine fall into traps — the disruptor lands but can't expand, the moneymaker nickel-and-dimes customers, the community builder misses the frontier. The goal is to be a "profitable growth architect" operating all three archetypes simultaneously. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/madhavan-ramanujam.md]

**20-80 Axiom — The Easiest Features Drive Most Willingness to Pay** — 20% of what you build drives 80% of willingness to pay, yet ironically that 20% is often the easiest to build. Founders give this away almost for free, then chase building the 80% that only drives 20% willingness to pay. The real MVP should be the "Most Valuable Product," not the Minimum Viable Product. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/madhavan-ramanujam.md]

**AI Pricing Is Fundamentally Different** — AI founders must tackle monetization from day one because: (1) there are cost dynamics to navigate (compute costs), and (2) AI products bring unprecedented value and attribution. Agentic AI taps into labor budgets which are 10X software budgets — using old SaaS playbooks means under-monetizing. AI enables solving the attribution problem for the first time, making outcome-based pricing viable. The pricing *model* matters more than the price point. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/madhavan-ramanujam.md]

**Outcome-Based Pricing Is the Destination** — A two-by-two matrix (attribution vs. autonomy) defines four pricing archetypes. The golden quadrant is outcome-based pricing (high attribution, high autonomy), where only ~5% of companies sit today. Founders should pick their right archetype now but have a roadmap to get closer to outcome-based — by building attribution mechanisms (dashboards, value audits) and more autonomous/agentic products. Examples: Intercom's Fin ($0.99/resolved ticket) vs. traditional per-seat pricing. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/madhavan-ramanujam.md]

**Mastering Negotiations with Options and Tapered Concessions** — Three pillars: (1) Gives-and-gets — always ask for something in exchange for a concession (e.g., a value audit). (2) Value selling — create needs rather than discover them, build affirmation loops, and co-create ROI models with customers from day one. (3) Show up with options — a good/better/best pricing structure shifts conversation from price to value. Taper concessions (15%, then 5%, then 2%) to signal the negotiation is ending rather than escalating discounts. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/madhavan-ramanujam.md]
