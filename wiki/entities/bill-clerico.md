# Bill Clerico

Co-Founder & CEO @ WePay, the complete payments solution for platforms. Raised close to $75M from Max Levchin, Dave McClure, August Capital and others. Also a part-time partner @ Y Combinator and angel investor. Previously worked at Jefferies in investment banking.

## Key Views

### 1. We Are Still in the Very Early Days for Fintech

Clerico argued (in 2017) that fintech had barely scratched the surface. The opportunity was not just in displacing traditional banks but in enabling entirely new commerce models through platforms. Every platform would eventually need payments built in, and WePay was positioned to serve that need.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2017-05-05-20vc-why-we-are-still-in-the.md]

### 2. Your Company Must Have an Opinionated Culture

A company without strong opinions is a company without a point of view. Clerico believes the best companies are unapologetically opinionated about how they operate, who they hire, and what they build. An opinionated culture attracts the right people and repels the wrong ones naturally.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2017-05-05-20vc-why-we-are-still-in-the.md]

### 3. The CEO Should Not Be the Sole Point of Contact With the Board

This is a common founder mistake — becoming the bottleneck between the board and the rest of the company. Clerico advocates for building direct relationships between board members and other executives (CFO, CTO, etc.) so the company can operate effectively even when the CEO is unavailable.
