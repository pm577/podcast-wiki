# Jason Lemkin

Founder of SaaStr (the world's largest SaaS community) and the SaaStr Fund. One of the OG SaaS investors of the last decade with a portfolio including Algolia, Talkdesk, Pipedrive, Salesloft, and RevenueCat — all of his first five investments became unicorns. Previously co-founded EchoSign, acquired by Adobe for $100M.

## Key Views

### 1. Seed Investing Is Systemically Broken

Across multiple episodes (2023–2025), Lemkin argues that seed-stage investing has become dysfunctional: pricing is worse than ever despite more funding available, seed funds can no longer invest in hot startups, and the extended time companies stay private distorts incentives. He warns that "seed is for suckers" in the current environment, comparing inflated seed rounds against the backdrop of a16z's mega-fund strategy.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2025-04-17-20vc-why-seed-is-for-suckers.md]

### 2. SaaS Growth Rate Benchmarks: Good vs Great

Lemkin provides concrete SaaS benchmarks: what constitutes an average, good, and great growth rate and churn profile. He argues that founders are too often blinded by raw top-line growth without understanding unit economics, and that the last vintage of private equity will underperform because of inflated pricing during the boom.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2024-05-27-20vc-why-seed-is-systemically.md]

### 3. VCs Do Not Add Value — The Best Ones Package Startups

Lemkin is brutally honest about the VC industry: most VCs do not add value to portfolio companies. The best VCs differentiate by knowing how to "package" startups for the next round — positioning narratives, building FOMO among follow-on investors, and strategically managing LP relationships. He believes founders increasingly do not care about their VCs.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2017-03-20-20vc-saastrs-jason-lemkin-on.md]

### 4. Mega Funds Will Return and Markups Corrupted VC

Lemkin predicted that mega funds would come back (eventually) and argued that artificial markups have corrupted the VC asset class. He is particularly critical of RIFs (reductions in force) as an embarrassment to SaaS founders, arguing that most cuts reflect poor planning rather than genuine market shifts.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2023-08-23-20vc-new-format-mega-funds-w.md]

### 5. IPO Market Predictions and VC Funding Cycles

Across his 2023 and 2024 prediction episodes, Lemkin forecast that the IPO window would eventually explode open, that Stripe and Databricks would go public, and that early-stage venture markets would face a shakeout where many funds would die. He also noted that Zoom made everyone a worse investor by normalizing easy, high-conviction remote meetings.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2024-01-04-20vc-predictions-for-2024-wh.md]
