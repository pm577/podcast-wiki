# Keith Rabois

Keith Rabois appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss hard truths about building in the ai era.

## Key Views

**The Team You Build is the Company You Build** — Vinod Khosla's adage: 'If you have the right people, everything else will be easy. If you have the wrong people, everything else is going to be difficult.' PayPal's 25-year success tail comes from Peter Thiel and Max Levchin marshaling an incredible density of talent. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-04-12-keith-rabois.md]

**PMs Don't Exist in the Future** — The traditional PM role makes no sense in an AI-native world. 'The skill is more like being a CEO now, which is what are we building and why?' The number one consumer of tokens in top organizations is the CMO, not engineers. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-04-12-keith-rabois.md]

**High Performance ≠ Psychological Safety** — 'High performance machines don't have psychological safety. They're about winning.' Criticize in public to calibrate standards. 'Really talented people — when things are going well, they're not happy. Morale goes down when people are skating.' ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-04-12-keith-rabois.md]

**Hiring is a Muscle** — Identifying talent requires practice, learning from misses, and developing techniques. Rabois learned to steal talent from within PayPal before he could assess strangers from the outside. 'If a founder shows the ability early to assess talent ruthlessly and accurately, here's how you can go very far with no other abilities whatsoever.' ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-04-12-keith-rabois.md]

**iPad-First Operating Model** — Rabois hasn't touched a computer since 2010 (adopted from Jack Dorsey at Square). Everything done from phone, watch, or iPad — a decade ahead of the trend toward mobile-first/AI-assisted engineering. 'There's no reason to use a more powerful, heavier weight, less flexible machine.' ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-04-12-keith-rabois.md]
