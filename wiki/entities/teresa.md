# Teresa Torres

Teresa Torres is an internationally acclaimed author, speaker, and coach who teaches a structured system for continuous product discovery. She's the author of "Continuous Discovery Habits," the most recommended book in Lenny's newsletter community, and has taught over 11,000 students through Product Talk Academy.

## Key Views

**The Opportunity Solution Tree provides scaffolding for outcome-focused teams.** Many product teams are asked to shift from output focus (build these features) to outcome focus (achieve these results), but aren't given tools to manage this complex transition. The Opportunity Solution Tree is a simple visual framework that starts with a desired outcome at the root, branches into the opportunity space (unmet needs, pain points, desires), then branches into solutions and assumption tests. It adds structure to an otherwise unstructured, messy problem.

**98% of people write opportunities as solutions.** This is the most common mistake Teresa sees when teaching the Opportunity Solution Tree. Teams struggle to distinguish between an opportunity (an unmet need or desire) and a solution (a feature or product). Learning to articulate opportunities in terms of customer needs rather than feature ideas is a fundamental skill that most product teams haven't developed.

**Continuous discovery means talking to customers every week, not just before a big release.** Teresa advocates for making customer touchpoints a regular, ongoing habit rather than a discrete research phase. This doesn't mean formal user testing every week — it means having a system of ongoing engagement that keeps the team continuously connected to customer problems and needs throughout the development process.

**The shift from output to outcome requires fundamental rewiring.** Most product managers learned their job by being told "build these features" — a structured, execution-oriented role. Asking them to shift to identifying outcomes and discovering opportunities is asking them to do a fundamentally different type of work. Teams need scaffolding and practice to make this transition successfully.

**MVPs in discovery aren't about building less — they're about learning faster.** Teresa emphasizes that the goal of discovery is not to ship faster but to learn what's worth building. Teams should focus on testing their riskiest assumptions first, whether through prototypes, experiments, or conversations, before committing to full-scale development.
