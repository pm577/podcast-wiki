# Gaurav Misra

Gaurav Misra is the co-founder and CEO of Captions, an AI-powered video creation tool with over 10 million users. He was an early employee at Snap, where he led the design engineering team, and previously worked at Microsoft. Captions has raised over $100M and is at the cutting edge of consumer AI applications.

## Key Views

### Every Engineer Ships a Marketable Product Every Week

Gaurav's engineering goal at Captions: every engineer should ship a marketable product or feature every single week. This aggressive cadence forces the team to make small, safe bets rather than big, risky ones. It also means the team gets constant feedback from the market — if something doesn't work, they know within days, not months. This philosophy stands in stark contrast to big company release cycles.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gaurav-misra.md]

### The Secret Roadmap

Captions maintains two roadmaps: a public one (what users are asking for, which competitors can also see) and a "secret roadmap" (their true competitive bets). Gaurav's insight: "If a user is asking us for it, they're asking everybody for it. It's not going to be a game changer in terms of winning against your competition." The real innovation happens in the secret roadmap — bets that users haven't thought to ask for.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gaurav-misra.md]

### Take On Technical Debt Intentionally

Gaurav believes startups should actively take on technical debt to move faster. "I actually think as a startup your job is to take on technical debt because that is how you operate faster than a bigger company. Bigger companies don't take on technical debt, they pay it usually right away." The key is being intentional about it — know what you're borrowing against and have a plan to eventually pay it back.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gaurav-misra.md]

### The Current Moment Is Unprecedented for Builders

Gaurav describes the current AI era as a once-in-a-career opportunity: "There's rarely a time like this where so much is possible. Everything you try just works. There's so many possibilities. There's not enough people in the world to work on them." He cautions that this won't last forever, but right now the constraint is not technology — it's finding enough builders to execute on all the viable ideas.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gaurav-misra.md]

### Attention, Not Building, Is the Hardest Problem

While AI makes building dramatically easier, Gaurav warns that getting and keeping user attention is harder than ever. "The tech is now there to build all these things that have not been possible before. The thing that is increasingly difficult is getting anyone to pay attention and stick with your thing." His Snap experience taught him that consumer products live or die on engagement loops, not feature sets.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gaurav-misra.md]
