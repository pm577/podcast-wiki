# Sachin Monga

Head of Product at Substack. Previously co-founded Cocoon (acquired by Substack), and spent over 7 years at Facebook working on video/camera products, the developer platform, and ads growth. Known for building the Substack recommendations feature, a legendary growth mechanism.

## Key Views

### 1. Direct Subscriptions Rewire the Internet for Quality

"The economic model for supporting great writing on the internet has been generally pretty terrible for the entirety of the internet's history." Substack's insight: if you rewire the internet around paid subscriptions between readers and writers, you unlock a clearly better user experience that doesn't depend on advertising's attention-maximization incentives. Writers can make a fortune "just doing great work" with a relatively small, highly engaged audience.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/sachin-monga.md]

### 2. The Recommendations Feature — A Legendary Growth Loop

Substack's recommendations feature is one of the most effective growth mechanisms in the creator economy. It creates a virtuous cycle: writers recommend other writers to their subscribers; those writers gain new subscribers and then recommend others in turn. The feature solved both distribution (helping writers grow their audience) and discovery (helping readers find great content) in a single system.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/sachin-monga.md]

### 3. Ads as a Business Model Constrains Product Design

At Facebook, Monga saw firsthand that when advertising is the business model, you need to accumulate time spent and attention to convert into sellable eyeballs. This constrains product design in fundamental ways. Cocoon and Substack both explored what a better solution looks like when the user, not the advertiser, is the customer. The subscription model enables product decisions that prioritize user experience over engagement metrics.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/sachin-monga.md]

### 4. Talent Density Enables Lean Teams

Monga emphasizes the importance of hiring high-caliber, multi-disciplinary people who can operate with autonomy. Substack's lean team approach means everyone has outsized impact. The philosophy: a small team of exceptional people will outperform a large team of good people, especially when building a platform that needs to be opinionated and focused.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/sachin-monga.md]

### 5. The Golden Era of Writing Is Just Beginning

"We're just starting into this golden era of what it might mean to be a writer on the internet." Writers who might not even consider themselves writers — people with deep expertise in a niche — can now make a living doing high-quality work without needing millions of viewers. This represents a structural shift in who gets to be a professional writer, enabled by the direct subscription model.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/sachin-monga.md]
