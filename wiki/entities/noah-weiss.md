# Noah Weiss

Noah Weiss appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss the 10 traits of great pms, ai, and slack's approach to product.

## Key Views

**The U-Curve of Founder Involvement** — When working with product-minded founder-CEOs, involve them heavily at the start (strategic buy-in, principles, goals) and at the very end (quality check in working software), but give the team space to explore in the middle. "What I think is important there is that hopefully you're refining in code and you're not still at static design mocks, because using the software is so different than looking at what the software will visually appear." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/noah-weiss.md]

**Complaint Storms as Product Discovery** — Slack's most powerful discovery tool is running structured sessions where internal teams surface every customer complaint they've heard, then cluster and prioritize them. This led directly to fixing day-one workspace setup as Slack's biggest self-service growth lever. "We did this thing called Complaint Storms ... we just wrote every single complaint we've ever heard from customers." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/noah-weiss.md]

**The Mini-CEO Myth is Dangerous** — The most dangerous piece of advice in product management is telling PMs to act like mini-CEOs. The real job is facilitating the pace and quality of decision-making, not making all decisions yourself. "I think that is how you end up having PMs who try to act like dictators instead of leaders and facilitators." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/noah-weiss.md]

**Impact Solves All PM Issues** — If a team is consistently building things people love and moving business metrics, everything else is secondary. The path to career growth as a PM isn't visibility or politics — it's relentlessly driving impact. "If a team is consistently building things people love and changing the direction of the business, everything else is just an input." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/noah-weiss.md]

**Data Fluency + Product Taste = Power Combo** — Great PMs need both deep command of customer insights (qualitative and quantitative) and cultivated product taste — the intuition for what people will love before testing. "I actually think taste plus fluency in data, that combination, is a pretty powerful combo." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/noah-weiss.md]
