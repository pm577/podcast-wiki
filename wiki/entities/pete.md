# Pete Kazanjy

Pete Kazanjy is the author of *Founding Sales: The Early Stage Go-to-Market Handbook* — considered the definitive resource on startup sales. He founded Modern Sales Pros (the nation's largest sales operations community with 30,000+ members) and is the CEO/Founder of Atrium, a data-driven sales management platform. He learned sales the hard way as a non-sales-background founder, and codified everything into the book he wishes he had.

## Key Views

### Founder-Led Sales Is Non-Negotiable

Pete argues founders must do sales themselves for the first several dozen customers. Reason one: the product is probably going to suck at first, and you need direct customer feedback to iterate. Reason two: you can't outsource learning your market's language. "Startups versus incumbents is a race between can innovation get to distribution before distribution can get to innovation." The founder is the cheapest R&D engine for discovering the sales playbook.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/pete-kazanjy.md]

### The Repeatable Sales While Loop

Don't hire your first salesperson until you have a repeatable "while loop" — reliably turning 15-25% of first meetings into customers after 50-100 at-bats. "If it runs on your local, now it's time to see if it reproduces over here." Start by getting 2 salespeople successful before scaling to 4, then 8. Jumping stages (hiring a VP of Sales before you've figured out the playbook) is the most common fatal mistake.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/pete-kazanjy.md]

### Sales Motion Is Software — Iterate Constantly

Pete frames a sales motion like software: every objection you encounter is a bug. "I should make a slide that handles that objection so I can show it to them visually." Update your collateral, scripts, and talk-tracks after every 10-prospect cohort. "You're just going to do that dozens and dozens of times before you have a repeatable sales motion." Track leading indicators (second dates, third dates) not just lagging ones (closed deals).^[raw/transcripts/lenny/pete-kazanjy.md]

### Modern Sales Is Moneyball for Go-to-Market

"Modern sales is a more thoughtful, operational, rigorous, analytical bent." Just as product teams evolved from gut-feel to data-driven (Mixpanel, Amplitude), sales is now measurable. CRMs plus digital activity (email, calendar, Zoom) generate data you can use to measure, manage, and improve sales behavior. "The pre-Moneyball moment in sports to the post-Moneyball moment — we're now just flipping over there."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/pete-kazanjy.md]

### PLG Needs Sales — It's a Question of When, Not If

Almost every B2B company eventually builds a sales team. "Never mistake your lead gen for your business." PLG is great for landing and permeating an organization, but $50K-$250K contracts require human conversations. Even developer tools like Datadog and Snowflake have massive sales orgs. The key is layering sales onto self-serve, starting with founder-led, then transitioning to a scalable team.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/pete-kazanjy.md]
