# Richard Rumelt

Legendary strategy scholar and author of *Good Strategy/Bad Strategy* and *The Crux*. Professor emeritus at UCLA Anderson School of Management, formerly on faculty at Harvard Business School. Consultant to Microsoft, Apple, Intel, and US Army Special Operations Command. Widely considered one of the foremost authorities on business strategy.

## Key Views

### 1. Strategy Is a Design for Overcoming a High-Stakes Challenge

"Don't call it strategy, call it an action agenda." Strategy comes from the Greek *strategos* — the Athenian generals elected to deal with the issues of the day (invasion, plague, funding). It's not a vision statement or a set of goals. It's a coherent set of actions designed to address a specific challenge. If there's no challenge, there's no strategy — just ambition.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/richard-rumelt.md]

### 2. The Kernel of Good Strategy: Diagnosis + Guiding Policy + Coherent Action

Rumelt's famous kernel: **Diagnosis** (what's the real nature of the challenge?), **Guiding Policy** (what's our overall approach to dealing with it?), and **Coherent Actions** (what specific, coordinated steps will we take?). Most bad strategy skips the diagnosis and jumps to vision or goals. Without a shared understanding of the problem, the actions won't cohere.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/richard-rumelt.md]

### 3. Begin by Identifying the One or Two Key Challenges

"Begin to try to identify the one or two key challenges that can actually be addressed and what are we going to do about it?" Strategy is about focus — choosing a small number of decisive challenges to tackle. Attempting to address everything is a recipe for mediocrity. The crux of the strategy is finding the challenge whose resolution unlocks progress on many other fronts.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/richard-rumelt.md]

### 4. Bad Strategy Is Prolific: Fluff, Failure to Face the Problem, and Mistaking Goals for Strategy

Rumelt identifies four hallmarks of bad strategy: (1) **Fluff** — words that sound strategic but are meaningless, (2) **Failure to face the problem** — not acknowledging the real challenge, (3) **Mistaking goals for strategy** — "we will grow 20%" is a goal, not a strategy, (4) **Bad strategic objectives** — a list of things to do that don't cohere. Most strategy documents fail on multiple counts.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/richard-rumelt.md]

### 5. Organizational Politics Often Block Good Strategy

The biggest enemy of good strategy isn't lack of analytical rigor — it's organizational dynamics. People avoid making hard choices because they don't want to disappoint stakeholders, lose resources, or admit that some initiatives are lower priority. Good strategy requires the courage to say no — to choose what you will *not* do as much as what you will do.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/richard-rumelt.md]
