# Melissa Perri

Melissa Perri is a legend in the product management community. She is author of *Escaping the Build Trap* and *Product Operations*, CEO/founder of The Product Institute, and has worked with hundreds of companies (including Fortune 50) on product management transformation. She has trained over 5,000 PMs and taught at Harvard Business School.

## Key Views

**Most Product Org Issues Are Strategic, Not Training** — "I've met a lot of organizations that think most of their issues are in the training of their people. 99% of the time I see that it's actually in the way that they're setting their goals and deploying their strategy." Training people without giving them context on what to work toward is ineffective. The fix is holistic: fix goal-setting and strategy deployment first. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/melissa-perri.md]

**Don't Use SAFe (Scaled Agile Framework)** — "Every single person I have talked to who likes SAFe, found success with SAFe, they ended up ripping it up and making it into something else." The product owner role emerged from Scrum, not from product management — it was designed to help developers prioritize, not to drive product strategy. Companies scale better by focusing on agile principles (moving fast, delivering customer value) rather than rigid frameworks. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/melissa-perri.md]

**Escaping the Build Trap** — Many product teams are stuck in a "build trap" where they measure success by output (features shipped) rather than outcomes (customer value delivered). Feature factories produce lots of work that doesn't move the needle. The escape requires shifting from a project-based mindset to a product-based mindset focused on continuous value delivery. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/melissa-perri.md]

**Product Operations as a Strategic Enabler** — Product ops is an emerging function that creates shared systems and infrastructure so PMs can focus on strategic work. Instead of hiring 10,000 PMs who do operational work off the side of their desk, build a product ops team that handles data, tools, and processes. This lets PMs concentrate on strategy 70% of their time instead of 30%. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/melissa-perri.md]

**The Product Owner Role Is Growing — But Not Where You Think** — The product owner role is the third-fastest growing role in tech, but it's concentrated in large non-tech enterprises adopting SAFe/Scrum. This is a different world from Silicon Valley product management. For PO's looking to level up: develop skills in value and viability, not just backlog management. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/melissa-perri.md]
