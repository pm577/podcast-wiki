# Brendan Foody

Brendan Foody appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss brendan foody, ceo and co-founder of mercor, on why expert-written ai evals are creating the fastest-growing companies in history.

## Key Views

**Evals Are the New PRDs (and the New Marketing)** — Brendan defines the era: "If the model is the product, then the eval is the product requirement document." Evals serve dual purpose: they guide researchers on what to build and demonstrate capability to customers as sales collateral. Labs use them as reward signals in RL environments, making evals the primary bottleneck to improving models. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/brendan-foody.md]

**The Shift from Crowdsourcing to Expert Sourcing** — Brendan describes a fundamental market transition: moving from low-skilled crowd workers ($30/hr average) to elite professionals ($95/hr median, up to $500/hr) who create rubrics for high-value capabilities. "The market is bound by the amount of things where humans can do something that models can't." Mercor grew from $1M to $400M run rate in 16 months by tapping this need. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/brendan-foody.md]

**AGI Is a Longer Road — Evals Are Evergreen** — Brendan pushes back on superintelligence-in-three-years claims: "It's a longer road... that long road is paved with all of the evals that help to make those capabilities possible." He argues post-training data (evals, RL) will drive progress far more than 10x more pre-training data. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/brendan-foody.md]

**Pursue Elastic-Demand Industries** — Brendan advises focusing on domains where increased productivity expands demand rather than shrinking it. "In software development, I think we can ship 100 times more features... There's just unlimited demand." By contrast, accounting has finite demand. The winning mindset is abundance and leaning into doing so much more, not fighting displacement. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/brendan-foody.md]

**Three Cultural Pillars: Can-Do, High Standards, Intensity** — Brendan credits Mercor's explosive growth to: (1) setting ridiculously ambitious goals that the company's trajectory forms around (they projected $50M run rate from $1.5M and hit it within weeks); (2) a super high hiring bar — first 10 hires included ex-Scale head of growth and multiple former founders; (3) output-oriented intensity where bought-in people burn the midnight oil. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/brendan-foody.md]
