# Geoff Charles

*VP of Product at Ramp — the fastest-growing SaaS startup in history. Helped scale Ramp to $100M+ ARR in 2 years with under 50 total R&D staff. Built products that compete with Amex, Expensify, and Bill.com in 3-month cycles with tiny teams.*

## Key Views

1. **Velocity is everything — it's how you design the entire product development process**: At Ramp, velocity isn't just a value, it's the operating system. It determines how teams are organized, incentivized, hired, and promoted. High velocity attracts top talent and de-risks decisions — if the cost of a decision is low because you ship fast, you simplify everything.

2. **Small teams can build competitors to public companies in 3 months**: Geoff's team (3 engineers, 1 designer, 1 PM) built a Bill.com competitor in 3 months that now moves billions of dollars annually. The recipe: tiny teams, extreme clarity on the goal, and a bias for shipping over perfecting.

3. **Velocity is a talent magnet**: When Geoff asks candidates why they want to join Ramp, the most common answer is "because you're actually building and shipping things." Talented people want to work where they can see their work in the world quickly. Velocity creates a positive selection loop for the best builders.

4. **Iteration velocity drives impact over time**: Some teams optimize for getting the first version perfect. Ramp optimizes for getting something out fast and iterating. Teams with high velocity reach the right solution faster through many cycles of real-world feedback than teams that spend months planning the perfect first version.

5. **Don't confuse velocity with burnout**: Fast shipping at Ramp doesn't mean long hours — it means focus, clear priorities, and eliminating process overhead. Small teams with clear mandates and minimal bureaucracy naturally move faster than large teams with extensive coordination requirements.
