# Geoff Charles

Geoff Charles appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss geoff charles, vp of product at ramp, on velocity as a design principle, first-principles thinking, and a+ talent as the foundation of speed.

## Key Views

**Velocity as the Primary Operating Principle** — Geoff frames velocity not as a side effect but as the conscious design principle behind everything at Ramp. They operationalize it through small, single-threaded teams (3 engineers, 1 PM, 1 designer) given tight timelines and shielded from organizational chaos. A 3-person team built a Bill.com competitor in 3 months that now moves billions of dollars annually. The mantra: 'Any second you spend planning is a second you don't spend doing.' ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/geoff-charles.md]

**Context Over Control** — Instead of prescribing solutions, Geoff aligns teams on goals, hypotheses, and data upstream. Leaders should 'publish the API by which they interact with me' — teams own their strategy and roadmap as a contract. When things went wrong, it was because he was being prescriptive about solutions without aligning on the 'why.' ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/geoff-charles.md]

**First-Principles Thinking Over Pattern Matching** — Ramp actively discourages 'I've seen this before' reasoning. Support reports into product because every support ticket is a failure of the product. This drove an extremely low contact rate — 400K+ users with fewer than 30 support agents. Geoff had to abandon his prior playbook when he realized he was facing an A+ engineering team, not a B+ one. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/geoff-charles.md]

**A+ Talent as the True Foundation of Velocity** — Geoff is candid: behind Ramp's velocity is far more the quality of engineering and design talent than the culture. The CTO spent his entire first year purely focused on hiring the best engineers and designers. PMs should look for engineers who care about business context, are proactive in customer-facing channels, and set the pace. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/geoff-charles.md]

**Radical Process Minimalism** — No status meetings (async only), no bug backlog (fix everything immediately), no OKRs for product teams (strategy + financial plan + roadmap instead), moved from quarterly planning to biannual one-pagers. Geoff's personal system: write down every action item from every meeting, group by importance rather than source. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/geoff-charles.md]
