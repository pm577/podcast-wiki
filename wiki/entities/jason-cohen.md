# Jason Cohen

Jason Cohen appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss 5 questions to ask when your product stops growing.

## Key Views

**5-Question Growth Diagnosis (Ordered)** — When growth stalls, ask in sequence: (1) Are customers leaving? (logo churn first), (2) Is pricing wrong? (too low, not too high), (3) Is NRR healthy? (expansion revenue masking churn), (4) Are marketing channels saturated?, (5) Do you even need to grow? 'If you don't fix the first problem, it doesn't matter if you fix the ones below.' ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-01-25-jason-cohen.md]

**Prices Are Almost Always Too Low** — 'Your prices are way too low because you just guessed and you haven't changed them.' Raising prices often doesn't change signup rates. Enterprise buyers seeing $2/month or even $100/month think 'that can't be good enough.' Low prices signal low quality. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-01-25-jason-cohen.md]

**If They Churn, Something Is Terribly Wrong** — 'Think about the gauntlet they went through to get to the product. They found you, didn't bounce, got past pricing, bought it — clearly they wanted it to work — and then they said 'No, bye.' What? On an emotional level, you have to go, 'Wait, that's terrible.'' Logo churn is the most urgent signal. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-01-25-jason-cohen.md]

**Quality Over Quantity in Content** — Wrote 150-200 posts he's proud of over 18 years, some years only 1-2 posts. 'I will only put out stuff if it's the best that I can do. It's up to the reader to decide if it's good.' Consistency matters less than quality — the bar of 'evergreen depth' justifies irregular publishing. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-01-25-jason-cohen.md]

**Growth Deceleration is Normal** — Growth doesn't usually crash — it decelerates. 'It's not sneaky, but it is gradual. You feel more like you're running through mud.' Mechanical reasons (scale, market saturation) are as likely as strategic failures. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-01-25-jason-cohen.md]
