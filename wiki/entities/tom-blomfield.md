# Tom Conrad

Tom Conrad is the CEO of Zero Longevity Science and a seasoned product leader who was an engineer at Apple, CTO of Pandora (growing it from zero to 80M users), VP of Product at Snap, CPO of Quibi, and on the board of Sonos. He's unique in having been part of both billion-dollar successes (Pandora) and infamous failures (Pets.com, Quibi).

## Key Views

**Not everyone should be a founder.** Tom believes the tech industry would be better off with fewer founders. Many talented, creative people who can raise a seed round would be far more effective joining an existing team with a business model that's mathematically proven to win. The acclaim, financial reward, and impact people seek can all be achieved in collaboration with others — "you don't have to be the person that raises the seed round."

**Understand the math formula of the business model before joining a company.** Tom emphasizes that you need to evaluate whether the business has a "mathematical formula that's going to win on any metric." Before committing to a role or company, understand the unit economics, the growth mechanics, and whether the fundamentals actually work. This is the single most important filter for choosing where to work.

**Product shortcuts often become permanent features.** Tom shares the story of spring-loaded folders at Apple — a feature he built as a summer intern where he added a blinking animation as a placeholder. That placeholder survived 30+ years, three complete rewrites of the Finder, and remains in macOS and iOS today. The lesson: the design shortcuts you think you'll fix later may become permanent parts of your product.

**Learning from failure requires being part of famous failures too.** Having been at Pets.com (from IPO to bankruptcy in 19 months) and Quibi ($2B raised, dead in under a year), Tom has unique perspective on why products fail. His ability to analyze what went wrong — and separate the signal from the noise — makes him a remarkable source of wisdom on how to avoid common product pitfalls.

**Burnout and health are inextricably linked to product leadership.** Tom's career journey spans both incredible highs and crushing lows, and he's unusually candid about the physical and mental toll of building products at the highest level. His experience informs a more holistic view of what sustainable product leadership looks like.
