# Casey Winters

Casey Winters has worked with and advised more consumer companies on their product and growth strategy than anyone in the world. His portfolio includes Pinterest, Airbnb, Reddit, Canva, Hipcamp, Thumbtack, Tinder, and Eventbrite. He is one of the most sought-after growth advisors in tech.

## Key Views

### Brick Strategies Unlock Fire Strategies
Casey's core growth philosophy: non-scalable "brick strategies" (manual, hacky growth tactics) exist only to unlock "fire strategies" (scalable growth engines). You start by doing things that don't scale — personally recruiting users, manually creating content — to learn what works, then build automated systems around those insights. The brick work reveals the path to the fire. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/casey-winters.md]

### The Spectrum of Product People and How to Level Up
Casey categorizes PMs on a spectrum from "execution-focused" to "strategy-focused." The best PMs can operate across the spectrum, but most excel at one end. To level up, PMs need to actively seek opportunities that force them to develop their weaker side — execution PMs need strategy exposure and vice versa. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/casey-winters.md]

### Content Loops as a Growth Engine
Casey highlights content loops (user-generated content that drives acquisition) as one of the most powerful and underutilized growth mechanisms. Notion's template sharing, Canva's designs, and Reddit's posts are all examples. The key insight: if you can embed sharing into the core product experience rather than as an afterthought, you create a self-reinforcing growth loop. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/casey-winters.md]

### Making Tradeoffs as a Product Leader
Product leaders must constantly justify "non-sexy" product investments (infrastructure, reliability, tooling). Casey's framework: connect every investment to a measurable business outcome. If you can't articulate how a feature improves retention, acquisition, or revenue, it doesn't get prioritized. This discipline prevents feature bloat and keeps teams focused. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/casey-winters.md]

### When to Focus on Growth vs. Product
Not every company needs a growth team. Casey advises that growth should only be a focus after: (1) You've achieved clear product-market fit; (2) Your core product experience is solid; (3) You have data to measure the impact of growth experiments. Premature growth focus leads to optimizing a leaky bucket. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/casey-winters.md]
