# Beezer Clarkson

Managing Director at Sapphire Partners (Sapphire Ventures), where she leads investments in venture funds domestically and internationally. She invested in top-tier firms including USV and Point Nine. Previously managed day-to-day operations at DFJ's Global Network ($7B under management across 16 funds) and spent time at Omidyar Network, Hewlett Packard, and Morgan Stanley. Founded OpenLP.com to foster transparency in the LP-GP relationship.

## Key Views

### 1. What LPs Look For in VC Funds

Across four episodes spanning 2016–2023, Clarkson provides a consistent LP perspective on evaluating venture funds. She emphasizes that LPs care about alignment of incentives, transparency, and consistent communication. Her platform OpenLP.com was built to demystify the LP-GP relationship and open up access to information that had traditionally been opaque.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2016-06-13-20vc-what-lps-want-in-vcs-wi.md]

### 2. The Rise of First-Time Funds

In 2017, Clarkson noted the explosion of first-time funds being raised while only a few mega funds dominated the headlines. She analyzed how this shift was changing the venture landscape, with more emerging managers competing for LP dollars while the denominator effect made fundraising harder for everyone.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2017-05-01-20vc-explosion-of-first-time.md]

### 3. Record Fundraising and War Chests

In her October 2016 appearance, Clarkson observed that more capital had been raised in that year than in the previous 15 years combined. She discussed the implications of VC war chests being built, the decline of first-time funds despite the total dollars in market, and what this meant for LP portfolio construction.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2016-10-24-20vc-more-raised-this-year.md]

### 4. The Tougher Fundraising Environment of 2023

By 2023, the environment had shifted dramatically. Clarkson discussed whether LPs were "open for business" and what it actually takes to raise a fund in the post-ZIRP era. She explained how LP incentive mechanisms need to change, which fund types would be hit hardest, and how what LPs want to see in fund investments has fundamentally changed.
