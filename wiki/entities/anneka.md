# Anneka Gupta

**Role:** Chief Product Officer at Rubrik (cybersecurity); former President and Head of Product at LiveRamp (11 years); Stanford GSB lecturer  
**Known for:** Strategic thinking frameworks, navigating difficult colleagues, managing energy over time, building product careers.

## Key Views

### 1. "Being strategic" = compelling why + willingness to champion hard things

Anneka's precise definition: being strategic means two things — (1) coming up with and articulating a compelling, simple "why" behind decisions and direction, and (2) championing and being a change agent for things that are hard but best for the long-term interest. You need both. Without the compelling why, you're just stubborn. Without the willingness to do hard things, you're all talk.

> "When people say 'I want someone that's strategic,' they want someone who can articulate a compelling and simple why, AND someone who will champion hard things that are best for the long-term interest." — Anneka Gupta

### 2. Navigating difficult colleagues — separate person from behavior

Anneka's framework for dealing with difficult people: separate the person from the behavior. Understand what's driving the behavior (fear, misaligned incentives, lack of information) and address the root cause rather than labeling the person. She advocates for direct, kind, specific feedback and creating environments where hard truths can be shared without blame.

### 3. Manage your energy, not just your time

A key distinction Anneka makes: time management is about efficiency; energy management is about sustainability. She advises identifying what tasks and interactions give you energy vs. drain it, and structuring your day accordingly. This is especially critical for product leaders who have high-cognitive-load roles.

### 4. Humor and gratitude as leadership tools

Anneka deliberately brings humor and gratitude into every situation, especially hard ones. She argues that levity creates psychological safety, makes tough feedback more receivable, and builds the kind of team culture where people want to do their best work. It's not about being unserious — it's about being human.

### 5. Breaking into product management — tactical advice

Anneka shares a practical path for aspiring PMs: develop deep domain expertise in one area (don't try to be a generalist too early), learn to communicate the "why" behind decisions, and build a track record of shipping. At LiveRamp, she valued demonstrated product sense over PMP credentials.
