# Naomi Ionita

Naomi Ionita is a Partner at Menlo Ventures, focusing on early-stage SaaS from seed to Series B. She started her career in engineering before falling in love with product at Evernote, where she was there from 10 to 100 million users and helped pioneer the growth product function (before "PLG" was a term). She was also an early contributor to Reforge and VP of Growth at Invoice2go.

## Key Views

### Don't Set and Forget Pricing

Naomi's #1 mistake companies make: "Do not set it and forget it. I see companies labor over designs and features and build this perfect product, and then pricing's sort of plucked out of thin air, and then they don't revisit it." Her advice: treat pricing like your product roadmap — every 6-12 months, there's probably something meaningful to launch, and that's an opportunity to revisit your monetization strategy. Evernote went many years before overhauling pricing, which was a significant missed opportunity.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/naomi-ionita.md]

### Don't Wait Too Long to Monetize

Naomi sees startups waiting way too long to make the shift from building a product to building a business. "The true signal of product-market fit is ultimately having people open up their wallets and pay you." By not monetizing, you're inadvertently cheapening your product, missing critical feedback loops, and setting yourself up for backlash when you eventually start charging. Freemium is fine — but decide where the paywall goes early.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/naomi-ionita.md]

### Evernote's Missed Opportunity: Single-Player to Multiplayer

Naomi's reflection on why Evernote didn't become Notion: "Evernote was philosophically antisocial. It was meant to be your second brain, your personal tool. I always used to say you can't retrofit collaboration. You have to be collaboration-first." The bridge from single-player to multiplayer is the chasm that capped Evernote's potential — it affects acquisition, retention, and monetization. Companies that get this right become default platforms.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/naomi-ionita.md]

### The Modern Growth Stack

Naomi developed the "Modern Growth Stack" concept — a framework for all the third-party tools and platforms that growth teams can leverage instead of building everything in-house. Just as the modern data stack (Snowflake, dbt, Fivetran) transformed data infrastructure, the modern growth stack (tools for experimentation, onboarding, analytics, personalization) is transforming how products grow. Her advice: know what to buy vs. build in your growth infrastructure.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/naomi-ionita.md]

### Underpricing Is Leaving Money on the Table

Beyond setting the base price too low, Naomi warns against "not offering different plans to cater to different segments." Multi-plan pricing captures value from different customer segments that derive different levels of value from your product. The right pricing metric (value metric) is pound-for-pound the most important pricing decision — get it right and everything else follows.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/naomi-ionita.md]
