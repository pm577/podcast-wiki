# Julie Zhuo

Julie Zhuo was longtime Head of Design at Facebook (used by over 3 billion people) and author of the bestselling *The Making of a Manager*. She founded Sundial, an AI product analytics company used by OpenAI, Gamma, and Character.AI. Her newsletter The Looking Glass inspired Lenny's own newsletter.

## Key Views

**Diagnose with Data, Treat with Design**
Zhuo's simple heuristic for when to use data versus intuition: "You want to diagnose with data and treat with design." Data tells you what's happening and where problems are — it's the diagnostic tool. But data doesn't tell you what to build; that requires design thinking, creativity, and intuition. She believes many fast-growing companies get by on "good instincts and good vibes" but eventually things stop growing. The companies that build data capabilities early have a massive advantage when that day comes.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/julie-zhuo-20.md]]*

**Management in the AI Era: Be Sturdy While Being Flexible**
Zhuo uses a willow tree metaphor for modern management: it survives storms because it's both sturdy and flexible. "Today management is really about this idea of be sturdy while being flexible." The rate of change is accelerating, and a manager's job has always been to manage change — but now the pace is unprecedented. Leaders need to hold firm on vision and values while being radically adaptable on methods and org structure.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/julie-zhuo-20.md]]**

**The Future Is "Builder" — Not PM, Not Engineer, Not Designer**
Zhuo predicts that AI will dissolve traditional role boundaries. "It used to be 'I don't have the skills to do 10 different jobs,' but now with AI it allows me to do many of those jobs myself." She envisions a world where everyone's title becomes "builder" — someone who can design, code, test, and ship with AI as their multiplier. Companies that resist this blurring of roles will be outmaneuvered by those that embrace it.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/julie-zhuo-20.md]]**

**The Manager's Job: Assemble the Avengers, Don't Direct an Army**
Zhuo reframes management from orchestrating people to orchestrating resources — which now include both humans and AI models. "Used to be people, but now it's basically models and different models have different strengths. You have to assemble the Avengers so that you can use the right tools for the right purposes." This fundamentally changes how managers think about team composition, resourcing, and capability-building.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/julie-zhuo-20.md]]**

**The Most Valuable Skills Are the Ones AI Can't Easily Replicate**
While AI gets better at coding and analysis, Zhuo emphasizes that creative thinking, aesthetic judgment, and human empathy become more valuable. "It's actually really, really hard to teach the model how to be aesthetic or really good visual design or how to be extremely creative in the way they write." Product leaders who double down on these human-advantage skills — taste, vision, emotional intelligence — will thrive as AI commoditizes technical execution.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/julie-zhuo-20.md]]**
