# Upasna Gautam

Upasna Gautam appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss product manager at cnn digital, leading the team responsible for cnn's content management system for journalists; board member of the news product alliance, on product management in a breaking news environment, stakeholder collaboration, and building for journalists.

## Key Views

**Product Managers in News Must Develop Equanimity to Thrive in Chaos, Not Just Ambiguity** — Upasna emphasizes that equanimity — mental calmness and composure under stress — is the most critical skill for product management in a breaking news environment. The ability to pause before reacting cuts through chaos and provides an outsized advantage in stakeholder management, team morale, and translating user feedback into product decisions. In news, the chaos is not hypothetical — it happens every day. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/upasna-gautam.md]

**Four Structured Touchpoints for Stakeholder Intake from Journalists** — Upasna implemented a system of weekly demo days, collaborative working sessions (rebranded from 'user testing' to be more collaborative), breaking news dress rehearsals, and open office hours. These create consistent channels for feedback, product evangelism, and relationship-building between the product team and editorial staff. Journalists are not traditional users — their feedback comes in the language of news, not product requirements. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/upasna-gautam.md]

**Breaking News Dress Rehearsals Stress-Test the Platform Under Simulated Crisis Conditions** — The team scripts a full breaking news scenario — from the initial news-gathering email through to publish — and runs it in minutes while engineers and support staff observe. This allows them to gather high-density feedback about whether the platform can serve breaking news needs, because if it can't handle that, it's useless. The dress rehearsal reveals failure modes that months of normal testing would never surface. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/upasna-gautam.md]

**Engineers Should Be Embedded in Product Discovery as Partners, Not Treated as Resources** — Upasna brought her tech lead and engineers into user testing sessions, design jams, and editorial conversations. When engineers understand user needs and the 'why' at the same depth as PMs, they define the technical 'how' with far greater clarity and precision. The most efficient software development happens when engineers have context, not just tickets. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/upasna-gautam.md]

**Build Buffers and Backup Plans Into Every Timeline Because Breaking News Will Always Disrupt Plans** — At CNN, user research sessions and onboarding cycles routinely get derailed by breaking news. The team builds in extra time — from days to months — as a systematic buffer. They also maintain layers of infrastructure fallback, which proved critical when their in-progress new platform served as the emergency fallback during the 2020 election. In news, your backup plan needs a backup plan. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/upasna-gautam.md]
