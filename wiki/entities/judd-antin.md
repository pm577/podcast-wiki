# Judd Antin

Judd Antin appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss the ux research reckoning is here — falsification, profit-focus, and the five-tool researcher.

## Key Views

**User-Centered Performance** — Judd coined this term to describe work that signals customer obsession without intending to change any decisions, like a PM asking for validation research at the end of a product cycle simply to check a box. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/judd-antin.md]

**Macro, Middle-Range, and Micro Research Framework** — He argues the industry does far too much "middle range" research (interesting but unimpactful), and should shift toward macro (strategic/business-focused) and micro (rapid usability) work that actually drives business outcomes. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/judd-antin.md]

**We Don't Validate, We Falsify** — Judd's mantra is that researchers should seek to be wrong, not to validate assumptions; most PMs and designers avoid being wrong and treat research as confirmation, which is the core of user-centered performance. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/judd-antin.md]

**Researchers Must Embrace Profit-Focus** — His most controversial take: researchers need to read quarterly reports, understand OKRs and the conversion funnel, and explicitly find the overlap between user needs and business goals — not just advocate for users. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/judd-antin.md]

**The Five-Tool Swiss Army Knife Researcher** — Great researchers master formative/generative research, evaluative usability, rigorous survey design, applied statistics, and SQL or prompt engineering, rather than relying on a single method. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/judd-antin.md]
