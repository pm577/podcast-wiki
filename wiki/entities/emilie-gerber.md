# Emilie Gerber

Emilie Gerber is the founder and CEO of Six Eastern, a top PR agency that has worked with over 100 tech companies from stealth startups to publicly traded companies. Before starting her own firm, she led PR for business development at Uber and worked on product communications at Box.

## Key Views

### PR's Second-Order Effects Matter More Than Direct Signups

Emilie argues that for B2B companies, PR rarely drives direct signups — but it creates massive second-order value. Press coverage validates the company for potential enterprise buyers, helps sales teams close deals (they include articles in outreach), attracts top talent, and signals legitimacy to partners and investors. For Lenny's Podcast specifically, she notes that coverage in CNBC and Fast Company helped validate it for sponsors even if it didn't directly drive subscribers.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/emilie-gerber.md]

### Pattern Matching Beats Complex Narrative

Startups often want to craft elaborate problem statements and trend stories in their pitches. Emilie's advice: be straightforward and pattern-match. If your pitch sounds like what reporters have seen work before, they'll engage. Overcomplicating the narrative is the most common mistake — the clearest, simplest articulation of what you do wins.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/emilie-gerber.md]

### Warm Introductions to Reporters Are Overrated

Counter to conventional wisdom, Emilie says warm introductions aren't that important for press outreach. A well-crafted cold pitch to the right reporter at the right time can be just as effective. What matters more is understanding what each reporter covers, personalizing the pitch, and having a genuinely newsworthy angle. She also recommends targeting niche newsletters and podcasts that may have more engaged audiences than traditional media.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/emilie-gerber.md]

### Perplexity's PR-First Growth Model

Emilie highlights Perplexity as a case study of PR-driven growth done right. With zero paid marketing and no marketing team, Perplexity used press coverage as its primary growth engine. The key: the product is instantly understandable ("Google alternative"), the barrier to trying is zero, and the value is immediately apparent after one query. PR works best when the product has a simple, graspable value proposition.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/emilie-gerber.md]

### VC and Investor PR: The 100-Character Pitch

The most practical PR tip Emilie shares: when your recruiter or sales rep reaches out on LinkedIn with only 100 characters, linking to a press article creates instant credibility. A CNBC or TechCrunch mention in a cold message transforms it from spam to a legitimate opportunity. This is PR's hidden ROI — it powers every other outbound channel.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/emilie-gerber.md]

## Notable Quotes

> "You can get so in the weeds with your own messaging that you want to set up this massive problem statement, you want to make it a huge trend story, but if you're very straightforward and you're pattern matching, it's generally actually going to work."

> "Third-party validation... that credibility goes a really long way."
