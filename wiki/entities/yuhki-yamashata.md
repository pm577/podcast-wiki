# Yuhki Yamashita

Yuhki Yamashita appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss an inside look at how figma builds product — yuhki yamashita (cpo of figma).

## Key Views

**Storytelling and Memification as the PM Superpower** — Yuhki argues that being a great PM is being a great storyteller. He introduces 'memification' — the art of distilling data or research insights so memorably that senior leaders spontaneously cite them in meetings. A story is only as good as the action it drives. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/yuhki-yamashata.md]

**PMs Own the Why, Not the What** — At Figma, Yuhki holds PMs uniquely responsible for the 'why' behind a product. He learned this at YouTube/Google: when teams are too big for one person to specify everything, the only way to scale is to ensure everyone deeply understands the problem being solved, so they make great autonomous decisions. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/yuhki-yamashata.md]

**Radical Customer Proximity Through 'Concerning Tweets'** — Dylan Field reads more customer feedback than anyone at Figma, even a decade in. To avoid chaos, Yuhki and the CTO created a private 'Concerning Tweets' channel where Dylan drops tweets (sometimes with zero likes) that contain 'an essence of truth' — treated as canaries in the coal mine, not statistical proof. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/yuhki-yamashata.md]

**Using Your Own Product Is the Secret to Quality** — Yuhki believes the biggest driver of Figma's quality is everyone using Figma internally. He switched the company from memo culture to deck culture just so PMs would spend more hours in the product. When employees personally experience pain points, they feel accountable and fix them, creating a virtuous cycle of improvement. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/yuhki-yamashata.md]

**The Three Pillars of a Good OKR: Legibility, Actionability, Authenticity** — After years of experimentation (deprecating OKRs, trying 'headlines,' reintroducing them), Yuhki distilled what matters: (1) Legibility — people understand the metric; (2) Actionability — it inspires different behavior; (3) Authenticity — it honestly reflects daily work. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/yuhki-yamashata.md]
