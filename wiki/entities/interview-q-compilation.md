# Interview Q Compilation

A curated compilation episode featuring the best interview questions from over 100 podcast guests, with 17 of Lenny's favorite questions. Each question reveals different dimensions of a candidate's thinking, self-awareness, and problem-solving approach.

## Key Views

### 1. "To What Do You Attribute Your Success?" — Eeke De Miliano

The twist: you can't say "luck." This tests self-awareness and curiosity. How deeply has the candidate reflected on their own trajectory? What patterns do they identify in their success? The question separates those who passively experience their career from those who actively shape it.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/interview-q-compilation.md]

### 2. "What's the Hardest Thing You've Ever Done?" — Geoff Charles (Ramp)

Reveals what "hard" means to the candidate, why it was hard, how they overcame it, how they worked with others, and how much agency they demonstrated. Working at high-intensity companies requires people who understand difficulty as something to be engaged, not avoided.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/interview-q-compilation.md]

### 3. The Teleportation Device (Eigenquestion) — Shishir Mehrotra (Coda)

A made-up scenario: scientists invented a teleportation device but will only answer two questions. What do you ask? This tests the ability to identify the **Eigenquestions** — the few questions whose answers unlock everything else. Great candidates quickly find the two dimensions that matter most (e.g., safety + cost model) and form a strategy from them. The question works for any role and children often outperform adults at it.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/interview-q-compilation.md]

### 4. "Describe a Controversial Product Decision" — Yuhki Yamashita (Figma)

Tests whether the candidate can represent both sides of a conflict in an even-keeled way, understand why the conflict existed, and articulate their role without defensiveness. Also tests: does hearing this problem make you want to work on it? Great PMs make even boring problems sound existential.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/interview-q-compilation.md]

### 5. "What Work Are You Most Proud Of?" — Katie Dill (Stripe), Karri Saarinen (Linear), Camille Hearst (Spotify)

The most common favorite question across guests. Reveals taste, judgment, motivation, and what the candidate considers good work. The "why" behind their pride tells you more than the work itself. A positive framing that lets candidates shine.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/interview-q-compilation.md]

### 6. "What Would You Do on Your First 30 Days?" — Jackie Bavaro (Asana/Google)

Tests onboarding philosophy, prioritization, and how the candidate learns. Great answers show a balance of listening, building relationships, and finding quick wins without being presumptuous.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/interview-q-compilation.md]

### 7. Tell a Story from a Non-Obvious Experience — Jason Fried (Basecamp)

Looking for people who have done interesting things outside the standard tech career path. Unusual experiences predict unusual thinking and resilience.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/interview-q-compilation.md]

### 8. "What Would You Remove From the Product?" — Various

Reveals whether the candidate understands the product's core value proposition. Removing features shows more strategic thinking than adding them — it requires understanding what matters most.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/interview-q-compilation.md]
