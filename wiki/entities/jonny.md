# Jonny Miller

Jonny Miller is the founder of Nervous System Mastery, a course that has helped hundreds of tech leaders cultivate calm, upgrade resilience, and reduce anxiety. A former tech founder who went through burnout and a personal tragedy, he's become a leading voice on the intersection of physiology, breathwork, and high performance.

## Key Views

**Work with the Body First (Bottom-Up), Not the Mind (Top-Down)**
Miller's core insight is that most people try to calm down by reframing their thoughts — a "top-down" approach that has limited effectiveness. Instead, he advocates a "bottom-up" approach: change your physiology first through breath, and the thoughts will follow. "There are four times more afferent neurons going from the body to the brain as from the brain to the body." By using breath to shift your physiological state, you create a cascade effect that changes your thoughts and feelings effortlessly.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/jonny-miller.md]]*

**The Exhale/Inhale Ratio Is a Lever on Your Nervous System**
Miller shares a practical, science-backed finding: when the exhale is twice as long as the inhale, it has a calming effect. When the inhale is longer or more intense, it has an activating effect. This simple ratio gives anyone a direct lever on their nervous system. Before a high-stakes presentation, 15 minutes of extended-exhale breathing can shift you from terrified to calm — Miller used this technique before his TEDx talk with dramatic results.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/jonny-miller.md]]*

**The Feather, Brick, Dump Truck of Burnout — Catch It Early**
Miller describes burnout through a vivid metaphor: first comes the feather (waking up tired), then the brick (losing your cool in an argument), then the dump truck (a full-blown health crisis or getting fired). Most people ignore the feather, then rationalize the brick, and only act when the dump truck hits. The key skill is learning to notice and respond to the earliest signals — before the cost becomes catastrophic. This requires tuning into your body rather than overriding it.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/jonny-miller.md]]*

**Use the APE Framework: Awareness, Posture, Emotions**
Miller's APE framework is a quick check-in tool: scan your Awareness (where is your attention?), your Posture (how are you holding your body?), and your Emotions (what are you feeling right now?). This 10-second practice creates a regular circuit-break from autopilot and trains the habit of body-awareness. It's especially useful in high-stress moments when the instinct is to power through without checking in.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/jonny-miller.md]]*

**Emotional Debt Compounds — Release It Regularly**
Miller explains that unprocessed emotions accumulate like financial debt, with interest. Each time we suppress a feeling rather than fully experiencing it, we add to the load our nervous system carries. Over years, this creates a baseline of tension and reactivity that degrades performance, relationships, and health. Regular practices like breathwork, somatic experiencing, and simply allowing yourself to fully feel emotions act as "emotional debt payments" that keep the nervous system in good standing.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/jonny-miller.md]]*
