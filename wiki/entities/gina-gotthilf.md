# Gina Gotthilf

*Co-founder and COO of Latitud, supporting tech startups in Latin America. Former growth and marketing leader at Duolingo (helped scale from 3M to 200M+ users). Former growth at Tumblr (Latin America). Worked on the Mike Bloomberg presidential campaign.*

## Key Views

1. **Brand voice and personality are massively underrated growth levers**: Duolingo's distinctive, quirky, funny voice wasn't a nice-to-have — it was a core growth driver. The unexpected, self-deprecating tone makes people feel something (giggle, surprise, delight). That emotional connection drives sharing and retention more than any optimization of a conversion funnel.

2. **Communication isn't about what you say — it's about what they receive**: The real skill is conveying a message so the listener understands it, remembers it, and feels something. Most companies optimize for sending information, not for reception. Duolingo optimized every touchpoint for how it would land, not just what it said.

3. **Duolingo's subscription success was rare for consumer businesses**: Most consumer apps fail at subscriptions. Duolingo succeeded by making the paid tier genuinely valuable (ad-free, unlimited hearts, offline access) while keeping the free tier generous enough to build habit. The key was that users who loved the product wanted to remove friction, not unlock features.

4. **Organic growth channels are infinitely more valuable than paid**: Duolingo's scale (3M to 200M+ users) came primarily through non-paid channels — word-of-mouth, press coverage, the unique brand voice that got shared, and product features that naturally drove referrals. Paid channels can amplify, but they shouldn't be the primary growth engine.

5. **Embrace failure loudly — it's a differentiator**: Gina is unusually open about failed experiments. By sharing what didn't work, she builds trust and helps the whole community learn faster. Most companies hide failures, which means everyone repeats the same mistakes. Openness about failure is an underappreciated competitive advantage.
