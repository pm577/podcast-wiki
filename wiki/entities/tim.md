# Tim Holley

Tim Holley is VP of Product at Etsy, where he has been for over 10 years and has helped grow the marketplace from ~$500M to over $13B in GMV. He leads the Etsy buyer experience and has lived through the company's major cultural and growth transformations.

## Key Views

**A clear north star KPI is the most powerful cultural change tool.** When Etsy's new CEO Josh Silverman took over, the most impactful change was establishing Gross Merchandise Sales (GMS) as the single north star metric. This provided clarity for prioritization — if you couldn't articulate how a project would drive GMS within a quarter or 12 months, it was harder to justify. This replaced a consensus-based culture that was thoughtful but slow.

**Cultural transitions require existential soul-searching.** Etsy's shift from a "touchy-feely" consensus culture to a performance-driven one was deeply painful for employees. Many had their identity tied up in the company's mission. Tim notes that the mission of helping independent sellers survived the transition, but the way of working had to fundamentally change — from lengthy debates to faster, more decisive execution.

**Marketplaces must know they're competing for eyeballs, not just direct competitors.** Etsy doesn't have a one-to-one direct competitor, but its buyers shop across the entire internet and on the high street. Tim emphasizes the importance of an "outside-in perspective" — benchmarking against the broader consumer experience, not just direct marketplace competitors.

**Narrative + KPI is the most powerful leadership combination.** Tim credits Josh Silverman's ability to pair a clear narrative with a clear metric as the thing that drove Etsy's transformation. "The old adage of you need to say something three times before people understand it — I would wager you need to say it another three times before they internalize it." Repetition of both the "why" (narrative) and the "what" (KPI) creates organizational alignment.

**COVID was "Black Friday overnight" for Etsy.** When the CDC mandated face masks in April 2020, Etsy's traffic and sales instantly transformed into holiday-level volume. The company did something unprecedented — putting out a call to sellers to start making face masks. Sellers pivoted from wedding dresses to masks overnight, demonstrating the agility of Etsy's supply side and the power of its marketplace model.
