# Kayvon Beykpour

Kayvon Beykpour appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss product turnaround at twitter, sacred cows, and founder-led innovation.

## Key Views

**Sacred cows are your roadmap** — Kayvon's playbook for product transformation was to identify everything the company believed it couldn't change and start there. By flipping the cultural fear on its head, he turned Twitter's most entrenched taboos into a built-in innovation pipeline. "The sacred cows are their own roadmap. It's like a built-in free roadmap of like, all right, what are all the things that you think we're not allowed to change? Let's start there." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/kayvon-beykpour.md]

**Staff by obsession, not availability** — To drive ambitious bets, staff projects with people who are irrationally obsessed with the idea, not whoever happens to be available. Belief in the mission is the non-negotiable ingredient that wills a product into existence against all odds. "You need to staff projects with the team of people that are well equipped from a skillset standpoint, but more importantly have an obsession with the idea they want to pursue." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/kayvon-beykpour.md]

**Frameworks work until they become religion** — Jobs-to-be-Done, OKRs, and any framework become actively harmful when followed dogmatically. The real skill is knowing when the framework is leading to bad customer outcomes and having the judgment to override it. "Every framework at its limit is followed to such a religious extent it's just unhelpful. You need to have nuance in how you leverage these frameworks." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/kayvon-beykpour.md]

**Acquire founders, not just companies** — Twitter's most successful new products — Spaces, Communities, Community Notes — were all led by entrepreneurial founders brought in through small acquisitions. These founder-types brought urgency, ambition, and the willingness to challenge the system. "A lot of our most ambitious bets that were the riskiest and most misaligned with how the product worked... were driven by founders who we basically acquired and said, 'Here, you are going to run this.'" ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/kayvon-beykpour.md]

**Be a voracious user to build great products** — The single best way to sharpen product taste is to constantly try new things as a user — even the silly ones — and build muscle memory for what works and what doesn't. There's no substitute for first-hand product consumption. "The best cheat codes for getting better at building products is just being a voracious user of products. And just trying new things, feeling out what works well, what doesn't, what you like, what you don't like." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/kayvon-beykpour.md]
