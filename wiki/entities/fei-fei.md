# Dr. Fei-Fei Li

**Role:** Co-Director of Stanford's Human-Centered AI Institute (HAI); founder of World Labs; Professor at Stanford; former Chief AI Scientist at Google Cloud  
**Known for:** Creating ImageNet — the dataset that sparked the deep learning revolution and ended the AI winter; known as the "Godmother of AI"; pioneer of world models and spatial intelligence.

## Key Views

### 1. ImageNet and the "Golden Recipe" for Modern AI

Fei-Fei's breakthrough insight was that AI's missing ingredient was **big data**. While the field obsessed over better models, she realized that human learning is fundamentally a big-data process — and machines needed the same. ImageNet (15 million curated images across 22,000 categories) combined with neural networks and GPUs formed the "golden recipe" that launched modern AI. The 2012 AlexNet breakthrough (using ImageNet data with just 2 NVIDIA gaming GPUs) proved the recipe worked. This trio — massive data, neural networks, and GPU compute — remains the foundation of today's AI including ChatGPT.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/fei-fei.md]

### 2. AI Was a "Dirty Word" as Recently as 2016

A startling historical note: in 2015-2016, tech companies actively avoided calling themselves AI companies because "they were not sure if AI was a dirty word." The rebranding to "AI companies" only began around 2017. This context underscores how rapidly the field has evolved — from AI winter to the most talked-about technology in the world in less than a decade.

> "There's nothing artificial about AI. It's inspired by people. It's created by people, and most importantly, it impacts people." — Dr. Fei-Fei Li

### 3. AGI Is a Marketing Term, Not a Scientific One

Fei-Fei takes a measured stance on AGI: "I don't know if anyone has ever defined AGI. As a scientist, AI is my north star." She notes that today's AI still can't do what a toddler can (count chairs in a video of an office), let alone derive Newton's laws from observational data. We are far from achieving human-level general intelligence, and claims of imminent AGI are premature. The field needs more innovation, not just scaling of existing approaches.

### 4. World Models Are the Next Frontier — Beyond Language

Fei-Fei founded World Labs to build large world models (LWMs) — AI systems that understand **spatial intelligence** and 3D worlds, not just text. Unlike LLMs which process tokens, world models reason about physical space, enabling:
- **Robots** that can plan paths and tidy kitchens
- **Design and scientific discovery** (e.g., reasoning about 3D molecular structures like DNA)
- **Infinite explorable worlds** from a single prompt

Her thesis: "spatial intelligence" is the linchpin connecting visual intelligence, language, and embodied AI. The company's first model, "Marble," launched in late 2025.

### 5. Why "Bitter Lesson" Scaling Alone Won't Work for Robotics

Responding to Ben Horowitz's question, Fei-Fei explains that the recipe that worked for language models (more data + more compute + simpler models) faces fundamental challenges in robotics: **(1)** training data lacks 3D actions — we have web videos, not robot action logs; **(2)** robots are physical systems closer to self-driving cars than LLMs — requiring bodies, supply chains, and real-world safety; **(3)** self-driving cars took 20 years from first DARPA challenge to today's Waymo, and robots are more complex (3D manipulation vs. 2D navigation). "The goal of a self-driving car is not to touch anything. The goal of a robot is to touch things."
