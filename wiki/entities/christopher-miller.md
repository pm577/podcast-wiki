# Christopher Miller

*VP of Product for Growth and AI at HubSpot. Led HubSpot's transformation into one of the most successful product-led growth businesses in history over 7+ years.*

## Key Views

1. **Relentless curiosity is the #1 PM trait**: The best PMs have an insatiable desire to understand things and no fear of admitting what they don't know. They're uncompromising about getting answers. Miller looks for this above all else when hiring — it's more predictive of long-term success than any specific domain experience.

2. **In growth, 70-80% of experiments fail — and that's healthy**: If you're hitting >30-40% success rate on experiments, you're thinking too small. Resilience is essential because the constant failure is demotivating without it. The trap is "grasping for a win" by making smaller, safer bets that don't move the needle.

3. **Radical accountability means grabbing problems nobody asked you to solve**: HubSpot's early growth team picked up the self-service revenue stream that nobody else was working on, blew it up, and proved the PLG model. Miller's philosophy: "Every problem is our problem." When you solve things the business didn't explicitly ask you to, you look hungry, and leadership keeps feeding you.

4. **Pricing and packaging is everyone's business, even IC PMs**: Miller crashed an executive party at the Guinness storehouse, pitched a contrarian pricing vision to the COO, and got invited to the next exec meeting. PMs should proactively think above their pay grade — having a well-formed point of view on tough strategic questions opens doors.

5. **Coachability and creativity matter more than PLG experience**: Product-led growth is still a fledgling sub-discipline — it varies wildly between companies. The best growth PMs are ambivalent about solution complexity, valuing simple solutions that deliver outcomes. They're adaptable, coachable, and focused on the outcome, not the cleverness of the implementation.
