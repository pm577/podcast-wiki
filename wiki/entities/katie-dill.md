# Katie Dill

Katie Dill appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss building beautiful products with stripe's head of design.

## Key Views

**Beauty Enhances Functionality** — Beauty and functionality are not opposites; beauty actually enhances functionality by making things easier to use, more approachable, and more compelling. It also increases trust — when users see painstaking detail in a product, they feel assured the team cares about details they can't see. "Functionality is important. And actually beauty enhances functionality because it does make things easier to use, more approachable, more compelling to use." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/katie-dill.md]

**Quality Is Growth** — Quality improvements are not a trade-off against growth — they directly drive it. Improving usability and understandability leads people to use the product more and share it with others. "Quality is growth. And if you think about how you can make your product easier to use and more understandable, that will of course drive people to use it, and use more of it, and have a better experience with it that they'll want to talk about with others." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/katie-dill.md]

**Performance = Potential - Interference** — A clarifying leadership formula: a leader's job is to increase potential (through hiring and development) while actively decreasing interferences — process friction, misalignment, wasted work — that hold talented people back. "Performance equals potential minus interference. It's a good reminder that, as a leader… you can hire the best people in the world, but like a muscle atrophying underneath a cast, if there are interferences that are holding them back from doing great work, they're going to burn out." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/katie-dill.md]

**Come In Listening, Not Swinging** — When joining a new team, trust must precede change. Katie's biggest leadership lesson came from an intervention staged by her Airbnb design team — they told her she had not earned their trust yet. She shifted from imposing changes to listening first. "Instead of coming in swinging, come in listening, so that you can really set out to make change that actually has true, positive impact on the folks around you and that you bring along with you." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/katie-dill.md]

**Design Is Simply Intention** — Design demystified: at its core, design is intentionality about who something is for and how decisions affect them. Anyone in any function — engineering, product, operations — can bring that intentionality to their work. "Design is simply intention. You're bringing intentionality to the decisions that you make in thinking about who is this thing for… That literally could be anything from designing a doorknob to designing your org structure to designing your strategy." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/katie-dill.md]
