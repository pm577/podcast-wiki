# Alisa Cohn

Alisa Cohn is an executive coach named one of the top 50 coaches in the world by Thinkers50 and the #1 startup coach by Global Gurus. She has worked with C-suite executives at Venmo, Etsy, Wirecutter, DraftKings, Microsoft, Google, Pfizer, and The New York Times.

## Key Views

### Scripts for Difficult Conversations
Alisa provides specific language for tough conversations: "Hope for the future is so important. I know this is going to be challenging for you to hear — not going to promote you — but I want you to know, it's really important to me that you're able to succeed in your career here." Lead with care while being direct about the situation. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/alisa-cohn.md]

### Your Job as a Leader Is Not to Make Employees Happy
Many leaders try to be "the leader who everyone loves," but Alisa argues this is counterproductive. If you avoid giving difficult feedback because you don't want to upset someone, you're ultimately harming the company and the employee. The real job is driving towards results while supporting growth. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/alisa-cohn.md]

### Three Questions to End Every Meeting
Alisa's three closing questions: (1) What are the key decisions we've made? (2) What are the next steps and who owns them? (3) When will we follow up? These ensure alignment, accountability, and forward progress after every meeting. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/alisa-cohn.md]

### The Founder Prenup
A set of questions every co-founder should discuss: How do we handle disagreements? What happens if someone wants to leave? How do we split equity? How do we make decisions when we're deadlocked? Getting alignment upfront prevents catastrophic relationship breakdowns later. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/alisa-cohn.md]

### The Upside of Difficult Conversations
"On the other side of that upset can be a whole new possibility, a whole new revelation, and actually a lot of joy and freedom." Avoiding hard conversations — not having them — creates the biggest problems. The discomfort is temporary; the growth is lasting. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/alisa-cohn.md]
