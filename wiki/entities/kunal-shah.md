# Kunal Shah

Kunal Shah appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss building cred, the delta 4 framework, and entrepreneurship in india.

## Key Views

**Delta 4 Framework** — When a product's efficiency delta is ≥4 points better than the existing solution, it becomes irreversible: users tolerate its failures, and it generates word-of-mouth growth. "Every time you see that the product efficiency delta is greater than or equal to four, three things happen. It is irreversible. Second is that you have a very high tolerance for it to fail. And the third thing is what I call the UBP, 'Unique Brag-worthy Proposition.'" ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/kunal-shah.md]

**Wealth as Information Asymmetry** — Wealth comes from having an unfair information advantage, built through relentless curiosity: constantly collecting and connecting dots. "To me, wealth is nothing but information asymmetry. All the best companies in the world have unfair information asymmetry. And that comes from curiosity. Because you're constantly collecting dots, connecting dots." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/kunal-shah.md]

**The Concept of Time in India** — Because almost no Indian has ever been paid an hourly wage, the internal sense of time's value is fundamentally different, making it difficult to monetize time-saving products. "No Indian has ever been paid an hourly salary in their entire life... So when you're not paid an hourly salary, the concept of time is not the same." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/kunal-shah.md]

**Low-Trust Markets Create Super Apps** — In developing nations where institutions are weak, trust concentrates around a few established brands, which is why super apps that do hundreds of things thrive rather than narrow, focused products. "In a low trust country... there is concentration of trust. So you will see that super apps, superstars, super companies all exist in low trust markets." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/kunal-shah.md]

**Second-Order Thinking as the Ultimate Predictor** — The ability to trace the butterfly effect of an event through multiple layers of consequence is the single most powerful trait for predicting success, though the brain hates doing it. "Second order thinking is known to be the most powerful trait to predict the success of somebody... The brain absolutely hates second order thinking unless you've trained your brain with second order thinking all the time." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/kunal-shah.md]
