# Yuhki Yamashita (yuhki-yamashata)

**Role:** Chief Product Officer at Figma; former Head of Design at Uber (New Mobility); PM at Google and Microsoft  
**Known for:** Building Figma's product development philosophy, scaling a product-led growth business, creating one of the most influential products in the design industry.

## Key Views

### 1. Equip your customers to champion a new way of working

Figma's success came not just from building a great tool, but from championing a new way of working — collaborative, browser-based design that challenged the status quo. Yuhki's insight: controversy and resistance is actually a signal that you're part of a revolution. Equip your customers with a narrative they can champion — not just for a tool, but for a new way of working.

> "It's not just that they're championing for a tool, they're also championing for a new way of working."

### 2. Product-led growth requires deep investment in the product itself

Figma's growth philosophy centers on making the product so good that it sells itself. This means obsessive attention to onboarding, activation, and the "aha moment." The PLG flywheel: great product → word of mouth → more users → better product.

### 3. The best product leaders bridge design, engineering, and business

Yuhki's unique background — he's been a PM, a designer, and an engineer — allows him to operate at the intersection of all three disciplines. His career path deliberately accumulated cross-functional perspective, from Microsoft (Hotmail PM) to Google (YouTube) to Uber (Head of Design) to Figma (CPO).

### 4. Hire for taste and judgment, not just execution velocity

At Figma, hiring emphasizes taste, design judgment, and product thinking. Yuhki believes that great products come from people who care deeply about quality and have strong opinions about what "good" looks like — not just people who can ship fast.

### 5. Strong product architecture enables sustainable growth

The underlying architecture of the product determines how fast you can move and how cohesive the experience remains at scale. This architectural thinking — borrowed from his engineering background — is central to Figma's ability to innovate rapidly without breaking the product.
