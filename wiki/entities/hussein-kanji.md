# Hussein Kanji

Founder and Managing Partner of Hoxton Ventures, one of Europe's leading early-stage venture firms. Portfolio includes mega-wins Darktrace and Deliveroo. Previously at Accel Partners where he was a board observer at Playfish (acquired by Electronic Arts) and made seed investments in OpenGamma and Dapper (acquired by Yahoo). Started his career at Microsoft.

## Key Views

### 1. Starting a Venture Fund in Europe vs the US

Kanji shares his experience of raising Hoxton Ventures — his first fund took 39 months to close. He compares the European and US venture ecosystems, arguing that London was at a distinct disadvantage in 2015 but that the gap has been closing. He advises founders to fundraise for a set amount of time and not til they achieve a certain dollar amount.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2015-11-09-20-vc-086-starting-a-new-vent.md]

### 2. Large Seed Rounds Increase Success Rates

Ten years later, Kanji argues that large seed rounds actually increase the chances of success for startups, contrary to the prevailing wisdom that smaller rounds enforce discipline. He discusses when to sell in venture, why multi-stage firms do not do the necessary work at early stages, and why AI finally means London can compete head-to-head with the US for technical talent and company building.
