# Patrick Campbell

Patrick Campbell is the founder and CEO of ProfitWell, which he bootstrapped and sold for over $200 million. He is one of the most cited experts on SaaS pricing and retention, known for publishing industry benchmark data and contrarian takes on bootstrapping, team building, and monetization. He now advises startups and writes extensively on the intersection of product, pricing, and growth.

## Key Views

### Bootstrapping Can Be a Mistake (Even After a $200M Exit)

Patrick's hottest take: "Bootstrapping is for lifestyle businesses that want to cash flow. Funding is for companies trying to create a billion dollars in annual revenue." Despite his $200M exit, he believes ProfitWell could have been a billion-dollar company if they had taken venture money earlier. "We got hooked on the efficiency and that was great, but we could have moved even quicker." His advice: know your goals, match them to your funding model.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/patrick-campbell.md]

### Team Culture: Define Who You Are NOT For

Patrick argues most companies fail at team-building because they try to be everything to everyone. At ProfitWell, they explicitly defined who was NOT a fit. One key value: "the most charitable interpretation" of every conflict. "If you have trouble taking the most charitable interpretation, you can't be at your company, and you have to go." Values only matter if there's a clear trade-off.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/patrick-campbell.md]

### Pricing: Just Do Something Once a Quarter

Most companies neglect pricing entirely. Patrick's simple formula: focus on **revenue per customer** as a KPI, and do exactly one pricing action per quarter. "Pound for pound, the most powerful lever is the pricing metric/value metric — how you charge per user, per visit, per whatever." He recommends starting with a price increase if your NPS is above 20, and building a pricing committee to own it.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/patrick-campbell.md]

### Two Types of Retention: Strategic and Tactical

Product teams obsess over strategic retention (ICP, time-to-value, features) but ignore tactical retention — which accounts for 25-40% of churn. Tactical fixes include: payment failure recovery, cancellation flows, offboarding, and term optimization. His data from 2M cancellation flows shows you have 18-30 seconds when someone hits cancel. Ask two questions: "Why are you leaving?" (multiple choice) and "What did you like?" — the second triggers a nostalgia effect that slows the freight train.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/patrick-campbell.md]

### The Billion-Dollar Threshold: Know If You're VC-Scale

Patrick's heuristic: if you can't see a clear path to $1B in annual revenue, don't take VC money. "If you can take a step back and create a $10M business, that's amazing. You don't have to have the pressure to create a billion dollars in revenue." He recommends bootstrapping the first 18-24 months to validate the idea, then raising if the market is truly massive.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/patrick-campbell.md]
