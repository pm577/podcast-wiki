# Jeetu Patel

Jeetu Patel appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss transforming a 90,000-person company to be ai-first.

## Key Views

**Megatrend vs Hype Cycle** — 'You have to know the difference between a megatrend and a hype cycle. When there's a megatrend, don't fight it. AI is a megatrend, one of the most foundational movements in human history.' Cisco didn't hedge — they went all in, making clear what was up for debate and what wasn't. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-02-26-jeetu-patel.md]

**Stamina Trumps Intellect** — 'Stamina trumps intellect. It's very important to have smart people, but you can become smart if you have curiosity and hunger and staying power and persistence. You can't teach hunger.' The most important hiring filter isn't IQ but resilience and drive. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-02-26-jeetu-patel.md]

**Public Critique Builds Trust** — 'Every management book will tell you criticize in private. I fundamentally disagree. What you have to do is establish enough trust so the team is comfortable critiquing and debating in public.' High-trust teams can handle real-time debate without damage. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-02-26-jeetu-patel.md]

**All-In Over Hedging** — 'Large companies experiment a lot. What they don't do is when an experiment works, they go all in and double down. They try to keep hedging. We didn't hedge on AI.' The gap between exploration and commitment kills enterprise innovation. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-02-26-jeetu-patel.md]

**AI Adoption is the Real Bottleneck** — 'The capabilities overhang is real.' While science solves amazing problems, enterprises struggle with adoption. The hardest part isn't building AI — it's getting organizations to use it effectively. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-02-26-jeetu-patel.md]
