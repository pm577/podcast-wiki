# Joe Hudson

Joe Hudson is one of Silicon Valley's most sought-after executive coaches. Founder of Art of Accomplishment, he's worked with leaders from OpenAI, SpaceX, Apple, and other world-class companies. His approach combines spiritual, psychological, and neurological practices to help ambitious people create lives they enjoy.

## Key Views

**The Critical Voice in Your Head Is Always Wrong — Stop Trying to Silence It**
Hudson's most foundational insight: the repetitive critical voice in your head is always wrong. Not because there's no truth in what it says, but because its framing is never accurate. "If I was your boss sitting right next to you criticizing you every few minutes, no way you'd say 'I need you to be productive.'" Instead of trying to stop the voice (which doesn't work), he recommends changing your relationship to it — respond with "Oh, I see you're really scared. Don't worry, I'm right here with you."
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/joe-hudson.md]]*

**Your Relationship with Emotions Determines Your Success More Than Any Skill**
Hudson identifies two root causes of stuckness: a dysfunctional relationship with the critical inner voice, and a dysfunctional relationship with emotions. "Whatever emotion you're trying to avoid, you are inviting into your life in exactly the way you're trying to avoid it." People spend hundreds of millions arranging a life they think they'll enjoy, then discover it doesn't work because they're still avoiding their feelings. Falling in love with your emotions — fully feeling them — is the path to genuine freedom and effectiveness.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/joe-hudson.md]]*

**Adopt an Experimental Mindset — You Can't Fail at an Experiment**
Hudson's most practical framework: treat personal growth as a series of experiments rather than a project with right and wrong outcomes. "If you do it as an experimental thing, you can't lose — you're just learning about yourself." This bypasses the habenula (the brain part that causes you to give up after one failure) because experiments have no failure condition. Pick one new way to respond to your inner voice every day and see what happens. The goal isn't to fix yourself; it's to understand yourself.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/joe-hudson.md]]**

**Joy Is a Productivity Tool — 10% More Enjoyment Creates 10% More Efficiency**
Hudson challenges the Silicon Valley ethos of grind and sacrifice. "If you say 'I'm going to figure out how to enjoy what I do 10% more' and you succeed, you are 10% more efficient. Not only that, usually the quality is going to get a lot better too." This reframes enjoyment from a luxury or reward to a performance enhancer. The most productive state isn't driven discipline — it's genuine engagement and joy in the work.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/joe-hudson.md]]**

**If You Fully Understand the Problem, the Solution Is Obvious**
Hudson's meta-advice for anyone stuck: "If you understood the problem, there would be no question about the solution." Most people half-understand their issues and then search for solutions prematurely. The real work is going deeper — through experimentation and self-inquiry — until the problem is fully understood. At that point, the path forward reveals itself naturally. This applies to everything from career dissatisfaction to relationship challenges to creative blocks.
*Source: [[raw/transcripts/lenny/joe-hudson.md]]**
