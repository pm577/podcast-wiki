# Heidi Helfand

Heidi Helfand is the author of *Dynamic Reteaming*, a book on practical strategies for orchestrating successful team and organizational changes. With over 20 years in fast-growing SaaS companies, she now teaches workshops and consults on how to effectively reorganize teams. She was employee #10 at a startup that grew to 900+ people and has deeply studied how team structures evolve through hypergrowth.

## Key Views

### Embrace Change, Don't Fight It

Heidi challenges the conventional wisdom that stable teams are always better. In fast-growing (or shrinking) companies, team change is inevitable. "If you're in a fast-growing company or a shrinking company... don't look at trying to fight the natural evolution." Her research shows that trying to maintain static team structures in a dynamic company creates more problems than it solves — stagnation, knowledge silos, and missed opportunities.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/heidi-helfand.md]

### The Five Types of Reteaming

Heidi's framework identifies five distinct ways teams change: (1) One-for-One Swap (swapping members between teams), (2) Grow and Split (a team grows then splits into smaller units), (3) Isolation (pulling people out for a focused initiative), (4) Merging (combining two teams), and (5) Shrinking (reducing team size). Each type has different dynamics, risks, and best practices. Understanding which type of reteaming you're doing helps you execute it better.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/heidi-helfand.md]

### Transparency in Reorgs Is a Superpower

Heidi advocates for "whiteboard reteaming" — visualizing proposed org changes on whiteboards and letting affected teams provide input before finalizing. At Procore, she rolled out whiteboards showing the future team structure complete with names, team missions, and open slots. People identified design mistakes and surfaced unexpected interest in overlooked teams. The key: transparency builds trust and surfaces better solutions than secretive executive planning.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/heidi-helfand.md]

### The People Layer Is as Important as the Product Layer

Heidi emphasizes that company building (people structures) deserves as much attention as product building. "If we could just build the software, deliver to the customer, get the product market fit... If only it could be that easy. No, we have the people layer, so let's focus there too." Companies that invest in how they organize, reorg, and communicate change build cultures where people feel included in decisions about how the organization grows.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/heidi-helfand.md]

### Endings, Neutral Zone, and New Beginnings

Heidi applies William Bridges' transition framework to reorgs: every change involves an ending (loss of old structure), a neutral zone (uncertainty and liminality), and a new beginning. Leaders should paint a vision of the new reality's benefits while being honest about the discomfort of the neutral zone. Crucially, be clear about who the decision-maker is — sometimes people have a say, sometimes they don't — and communicate that upfront.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/heidi-helfand.md]

## Notable Quotes

> "Reteaming is hard. Reorgs are hard. You can't lump them all into one thing with oh, it's all great all the time. No, it's not."

> "If we could just build the software, deliver to the customer, get the product market fit, hey, have we delighted them or not? If only it could be that easy. No, we have the people layer, so let's focus there too."

> "It would be highly convenient if we could just focus on building products that people love... but the reality is we're humans together."
