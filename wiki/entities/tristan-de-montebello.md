# Tristan de Montebello

Tristan de Montebello appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss why most public speaking advice is wrong — and how to finally overcome anxiety.

## Key Views

**Speak conversationally, not "professionally"** — The single biggest mistake speakers make is switching into a fake public speaking voice. Tristan's key cue: just think out loud. "People tend to get into a public speaking voice… I click play, and suddenly I say, 'The important part about doing this,' and they enter into a different version of themselves, a professional version, whatever that would mean. It's so much more freeing, powerful, connecting, and effective to speak conversationally." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/tristan-de-montebello.md]

**Speaking is subconscious flow, not conscious control** — The root-cause insight: you speak best when you stop thinking about speaking. Focus on the message, not the mechanics. "The day I understood that speaking was a subconscious flow-oriented process and not a conscious process, completely changed the way I approached it. So instead of thinking tactics and frameworks and adding more to the outside of the things I need to think about, when I realized when I speak best, I'm actually not thinking about speaking." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/tristan-de-montebello.md]

**Stay in character — never leak your insecurities** — Your audience cannot see your inner doubt. Breaking character (apologizing, hedging, nervous laughter) creates the very impression you're trying to avoid. "The solution is very, very simple. The solution is that is just, don't share your insecurities. Put your best foot forward and stay in it the whole time. Stay in character from beginning all the way through past the ending." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/tristan-de-montebello.md]

**The Accordion Method: prepare by speaking, not writing** — Instead of writing a script and memorizing it, compress your talk iteratively (3 min → 2 min → 1 min → 30 sec) to find the essence, then expand back up. The result is internalized, not memorized. "Instead of preparing our talk by writing, we're going to prepare our talk by speaking, and we're going to do so in a very specific way where we're going down the accordion to create extreme clarity." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/tristan-de-montebello.md]

**The Bow and Arrow: one thing per talk** — Switch your mindset from "what do I want to say?" to "what do I want my audience to remember?" Audiences leave with at most one takeaway — choose it deliberately. "The mistake that most people do when they're preparing a talk, a presentation, an all hands a meeting, whatever it is we tend to focus more on what we want to say than what we want our audience to remember." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/tristan-de-montebello.md]

# Tristan de Montebello

Tristan de Montebello is the co-creator of Ultraspeaking, widely regarded as one of the most effective methods for rapidly improving public speaking and communication skills. In 2017, he became the fastest competitor to reach the finals of the World Championship of Public Speaking.

## Key Views

**Speaking is a meta skill, not a specialized skill.** Tristan argues that improving your speaking transforms every area of your life — work, relationships, family — just like fitness does. People underestimate how transformative becoming a better speaker can be because it affects everything, not just presentations and meetings.

**If you're not enjoying speaking, you're doing it wrong.** Tristan views enjoyment as a barometer for effective speaking. Since humans are naturally evolved to communicate, discomfort signals that you've adopted counterproductive habits like overthinking or trying to control every word. The goal is to feel like yourself, the way you do when talking to close friends or family.

**Speaking is a subconscious flow-state process, not a conscious one.** The breakthrough insight for Tristan was that the best speaking happens when you're not thinking about speaking at all. Instead of focusing on symptoms (filler words, racing speech), he advises addressing root causes — like discomfort with slowing down, pausing, or allowing emotion under pressure. Fix the root cause and the symptoms resolve naturally.

**Most public speaking advice focuses on the wrong things.** Traditional advice often adds more frameworks and tactics to consciously think about, which makes speaking worse. Tristan's approach is about "upgrading the software" by removing bad habits (bugs) and installing new ones, allowing speakers to stay in flow state without getting pulled out by self-consciousness.

**Practice must be enjoyable to be sustainable.** Just like a fitness journey, public speaking improvement requires consistent practice. If the practice itself isn't enjoyable, people will quit after two weeks. Tristan structures exercises as games that reward participants, creating positive reinforcement loops that keep people engaged in their development.
