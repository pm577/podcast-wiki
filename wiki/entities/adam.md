# Adam Grenier

Adam Grenier is a growth marketing veteran who served as Head of Growth Marketing and Innovation at Uber, VP of Product/Marketing at Lambda School, and VP of Marketing at MasterClass. He pioneered the "Growth CMO" role and advises companies on growth strategy, emerging acquisition channels, and marketing leadership.

## Key Views

### The Three-Ingredient Framework for Emerging Channels
Adam's framework for evaluating new acquisition channels has three components: (1) **Channel overlap** — does the channel's strengths align with customer needs and company goals? (2) **Channel DNA** — where is the channel in its trajectory, and what is its monetization strategy? Aligning with a channel's monetization goals gives you a competitive edge. (3) **Company DNA** — does your risk profile, team capacity, and existing channel mix support investing in something new? Most channels fail — ~5% success rate — but when they work, they're game-changing. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/adam-grenier.md]

### Assume You No Longer Have Product-Market Fit During Shifts
When market conditions change, Adam advises founders to "start by assuming you no longer have product-market fit, because you had product-market fit in a different market." The entire customer base changes, not just the next 10% of customers. Launching a new channel won't fix a fundamental product-market problem. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/adam-grenier.md]

### The Growth CMO Is a New Breed of Marketing Leader
Traditional CMOs struggle at product-driven companies because they treat brand as campaigns rather than continuous iteration. The Growth CMO is data-driven, iterative, and marries product and marketing at the hip. They measure everything (including brand), run experiments on the entire funnel (not just top-of-funnel), and treat marketing with the same agile mindset as product development. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/adam-grenier.md]

### Improv Skills Translate Directly to Business
Adam's improv background taught him two critical business skills: (1) **"Yes, and..."** — accepting ideas and building on them instead of denying them, essential for cross-functional collaboration; (2) **The gift of details** — using specific, concrete details to open up creative possibilities and sharpen positioning. These skills improve marketing creativity, team collaboration, and problem-solving. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/adam-grenier.md]

### Don't Let Emerging Channel Experiments Bleed Past a Quarter
New channels deserve experimentation but need hard boundaries. Adam recommends capping initial exploration to roughly one quarter (or less for low-investment channels). Most signal comes in a month or less of "fishing." If directionally things aren't improving after a quarter, put it back on ice. Run emerging channel tests as part of a structured backlog, not random one-off bets. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/adam-grenier.md]
