# Tomer Cohen

Tomer Cohen is the Chief Product Officer at LinkedIn, where he's pioneering a radical new approach to product development called the Full Stack Builder program. He's leading the transformation of how product teams operate in the age of AI.

## Key Views

**The Full Stack Builder model collapses process and organizational complexity.** Over time, product development at scale companies has become bloated with sub-steps — multiple information sources for research, layers of reviews (design, privacy, security), and micro-specializations within each function. Tomer's model aims to empower individual builders to take ideas from concept to launch, regardless of their role, by using AI to collapse the stack back to craftsmanship.

**70% of job skills will change by 2030.** Tomer cites LinkedIn's unique data showing that the skills required to do your current job will change dramatically within four years. This isn't about changing jobs — your existing job is transforming. The fastest growing jobs today grew by 70% from last year, and many didn't exist on any list a year ago. This pace of change demands a fundamental rethinking of how teams build products.

**Process and organizational complexity are the real enemies of speed.** Tomer explains that every sub-step of product development (research, reviews, specs) has a valid reason to exist, but collectively they create an overwhelming system. At LinkedIn, visualizing the full process + organizational complexity diagram leaves people "mind-blown" because they only see their small piece. AI offers an opportunity to collapse this complexity.

**AI tools alone aren't enough — you need incentives and cultural change.** Tomer warns that simply rolling out AI agents and expecting adoption doesn't work. Companies must build incentive programs, motivation systems, and visible examples of how to use these tools. Change management is a critical but often overlooked component of AI transformation in product organizations.

**Top talent continuously seeks to improve their craft.** Tomer observes that AI's impact amplifies existing talent differences — top performers use it to get even better. The key trait he emphasizes for builders is a continuous drive to improve. The full stack builder model creates a new career path where anyone from any function can become a builder, making product development more meritocratic.
