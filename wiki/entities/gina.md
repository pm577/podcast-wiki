# Gina Gotthilf

Gina Gotthilf is a co-founder and COO of Latitud, a startup platform supporting the next generation of iconic tech startups in Latin America. Previously, she led growth and marketing at Duolingo, helping scale the company from 3 million to over 200 million users primarily through organic and non-paid growth channels. She also led growth and community for Tumblr in Latin America and worked on the Mike Bloomberg presidential campaign.

## Key Views

### The A-Side and B-Side of Every Story
Gina's core philosophy: every person, company, and country has an A-side (highlights, successes) and a B-side (struggles, failures). We're encouraged to only share the A-side professionally, but this creates unrealistic expectations. "Between all of those highlights, there were so many B-moments that get shoved under the rug... It's easy to think that things aren't just not going to work out for us because we're in one of those B-moments." Her own B-sides include dropping out of college, getting fired, losing her visa multiple times, and not being paid for six months. The key is resilience and recognizing that B-moments are just moments.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gina-gotthilf.md]

### Communication Is About Reception, Not Transmission
Gina's most fundamental belief about growth and marketing: "Communication isn't about being able to convey a message, it's about being able to convey a message in a way that the listener receives it, understands it, and remembers it." This is why Duolingo invested so heavily in its unique, quirky brand voice — the unexpected humor makes people feel something. "There is always a quirk, like it's unexpected. The way we talk to you is a little bit funny. It doesn't take itself too seriously."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gina-gotthilf.md]

### Brand and PR Are Underappreciated by Startups
Gina argues that most startups dramatically underinvest in brand and PR relative to their potential impact. While growth teams obsess over conversion rate optimization and paid channels, the things that make people feel something about a product — its voice, its personality, its story — are what create lasting word of mouth. She saw this at Duolingo, where the brand's quirky personality became a competitive advantage that paid channels couldn't replicate.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gina-gotthilf.md]

### Latin America Is an Underestimated Market
With 660 million people and a $6 trillion economy, Latin America represents a massive opportunity that US investors often overlook. "In terms of percentage of GDP that tech occupies compared to other markets, it's very promising. We're at one third of India, one tenth of China, one thirtieth of the United States." Gina believes tech companies can grow significantly to occupy more of that GDP space, making LatAm one of the most exciting frontiers for startup growth.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gina-gotthilf.md]

### Career Resilience Comes From Understanding the Long Arc
Drawing on her own rocky start (fired, laid off, visa problems), Gina emphasizes that a career is longer than most people realize. What feels like a disaster at 26 is often just a chapter in a much longer story. The key skills are resilience, the ability to tell your story effectively, and understanding how other people perceive value. "It's not just about doing things that actually matter, and learning. It's about being able to tell the story, and it's about understanding what other people perceive as valuable."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gina-gotthilf.md]
