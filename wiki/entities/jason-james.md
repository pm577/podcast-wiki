# Jason James

Co-Founder @ Tezi and one of the leading product minds in the valley. Previously VP Product @ Instacart (scaling to $40B valuation) and Head of Product and Design @ Thumbtack.

## Key Views

### 1. Why Most CPOs are Bad

Most CPOs fail because they see themselves as process managers rather than value creators. They optimize for shipping features instead of shipping customer value. The best CPOs are ruthlessly focused on outcomes, not output — they kill projects that don't move the needle even if the team has invested months of work.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2025-08-29-20product-why-most-cpos-are-b.md]

### 2. Why You Do Not Need PMs in a World of AI

AI is reshaping product development so dramatically that traditional PM roles become redundant. Engineers with AI tools can go from concept to working product faster than a PM can write a spec. The bottleneck shifts from "who writes the PRD" to "who has the clearest vision of what to build."

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2025-08-29-20product-why-most-cpos-are-b.md]

### 3. Why the Design Stage is Dead and Vibe Coding Replaces It

With modern AI coding tools, the design phase collapses. You can go from concept to working prototype in hours. "Vibe coding" — using AI to turn rough ideas into functional software — replaces the traditional handoff from design to engineering.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2025-08-29-20product-why-most-cpos-are-b.md]

### 4. The Three Roles All Founders End Up Firing on Repeat

Founders repeatedly hire and fire the same three roles — the person who can't execute, the person who executes but can't think strategically, and the person who thinks strategically but can't communicate. The rare fourth type who can do all three is what every founder is really looking for.

^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2025-08-29-20product-why-most-cpos-are-b.md]
