# Evan Spiegel

Evan Spiegel appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss why distribution has become the most important moat.

## Key Views

**Distribution > Product** — Most consumer startups obsess over product-market fit but ignore distribution. 'So much of consumer technology focuses on product-market fit. People don't spend nearly enough time thinking about distribution and figuring out distribution.' Snapchat succeeded because it connected people to their closest friends, not everyone — a distribution insight, not a product insight. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-04-26-evan-spiegel.md]

**Software is Not a Moat** — '15 years ago, we essentially learned that software is not a moat, which is something that everyone is discovering today with AI.' Snap learned this early because competitors easily cloned every feature. The durable moats are ecosystems (creator relationships, developer communities) and hardware (vertically integrated stacks). ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-04-26-evan-spiegel.md]

**Ecosystems Over Features** — Easy to copy software features, hard to replicate a full platform with creators, developers, and community relationships. 'The more that we could build a platform around our business, the harder it would be for folks to just copy that.' This insight drove Snap's investment in AR platform with millions of developer-built lenses. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-04-26-evan-spiegel.md]

**Next Platform Shifts Create Distribution Windows** — Every generational form factor (mobile, now glasses/specs) creates a new surface for consumer companies to get distribution. 'The most exciting times in technology are when there are new platforms that get created — a whole new set of opportunities and a whole new surface for people to build generational consumer companies.' ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-04-26-evan-spiegel.md]

**Humanity Over Technology** — 'We're in an industry where so much of the conversation is focused on technology. Humanity is far more important because humanity dictates how technology is adopted.' Anticipates significant societal pushback on AI changes — the crucible moment for tech. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-04-26-evan-spiegel.md]
