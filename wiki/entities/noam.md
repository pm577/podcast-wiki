# Noam Lovinsky

Noam Lovinsky is the Chief Product Officer at Grammarly and one of the most quietly influential product leaders in tech. He previously led product at YouTube (creator experience, then consumer product), served as CPO at Thumbtack (engineering a turnaround from negative growth), and founded Facebook's New Product Experimentation (NPE) team to incubate 0-to-1 ideas inside a large org. Known for rarely tweeting or publishing, Noam advocates doing the work over building a personal brand.

## Key Views

### Advocate for What's Best for the Business, Even If It Hurts You

Noam argues that the #1 career accelerant is openly advocating for what's best for the organization — even if that means getting your own project killed or demoting yourself. At YouTube, he convinced leadership to disband his 50-person team because the resources were better deployed elsewhere. The result: he was promoted to lead YouTube's creator focus area. "If it is a healthy team that rewards those sorts of decisions and actions, good things will happen. If it's not, that's good to know too."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/noam-lovinsky.md]

### "Growth Masks All Problems"

Drawing from his Thumbtack turnaround experience, Noam warns that hypergrowth hides fundamental product issues. Thumbtack was generating triple-digit growth but burning through demand because of insufficient supply-side liquidity. When SEO headwinds hit, growth went negative for the first time. "You don't really have a true understanding of what is working well and what is not working well when you have incredible growth." The solution: diversify growth channels and fix the core experience.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/noam-lovinsky.md]

### The "Layer Yourself" Career Move

A uniquely vulnerable career strategy: ask to be reorganized under someone else when you're struggling. Noam was reporting directly to YouTube's CEO but found the gap impossible. He proactively asked to report to Hunter Walk instead. "No one has ever come to me in my career and said, 'I would like you to layer me in this other person.' But in that moment I was just like, 'This is how I will do better work.'" The move paid off enormously.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/noam-lovinsky.md]

### Marketplace Principles: Customers First, Pros Second, Thumbtack Last

At Thumbtack, Noam established clear ordering principles: customers first, pros second, the business last. The logic: pros need more customers, so optimizing for customer experience creates the best supply. "Doing things like raising prices because we think it's good for the business, even though it causes liquidity issues in the marketplace might be a local maxima." This framework guided tough prioritization decisions during the turnaround.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/noam-lovinsky.md]

### Stamina Over Strategy: When to Kill a Project

Noam believes most early-stage projects die from running out of stamina, not from being wrong. When a team has been "hitting their head against the wall for a long time," they often just need a change of scene. His rule of thumb: monitor team energy levels. "When you see a team that's sort of down and out... sometimes they just need a change of scene, a change of pace, and they get to a much better situation."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/noam-lovinsky.md]
