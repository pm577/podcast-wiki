# Amol Avasare

Amol Avasare is Head of Growth at Anthropic, where he has led the company's unprecedented growth from $1B to $19B ARR in 14 months. He previously led growth at Mercury and MasterClass, founded a startup, and worked as an investment banker.

## Key Views

### 70% of Growth Work Is Fighting "Success Disasters"
At hypergrowth companies like Anthropic, about 70% of a growth leader's time is firefighting the consequences of success — scaling breaks everything. While all the charts are green, teams are stretched thin and every win creates new emergencies. The remaining 30% is proactive growth work: pricing, packaging, and optimizing funnels. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-04-05-amol-avasare.md]

### Growth Categories Are the Same — Intensity Is Radically Different
The categories of growth work (acquisition, activation, monetization) are the same at Anthropic as at any company — but the intensity is unmatched. "50%, 60%, 70% of how you operate in the past, just throw it out the door." With 10X year-over-year growth, linear charts are "not cool" — everything is log-linear. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-04-05-amol-avasare.md]

### Cold Email Your Way Into Any Role
Amol got his job at Anthropic by cold-emailing CPO Mike Krieger. His playbook: use proven high-open-rate subject lines, find personal emails (not LinkedIn where everyone reaches out), keep it short, and follow up persistently until told to stop. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-04-05-amol-avasare.md]

### The CASH Initiative — Automating Growth with AI
Anthropic's growth platform team is driving CASH (Claude Accelerate Sustainable Hypergrowth) — using Claude to automate growth experimentation. This represents the next frontier: AI not just as a product, but as an internal tool to accelerate growth work itself. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-04-05-amol-avasare.md]

### Model Quality Drives Growth; Growth Team Manages the Consequences
Amol is refreshingly honest: the lion's share of Anthropic's success comes from the research team building the best models. The growth team's job is managing the explosion — converting organic demand and ensuring infrastructure doesn't collapse under the weight of success. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-04-05-amol-avasare.md]
