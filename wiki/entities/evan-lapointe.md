# Evan LaPointe

Evan LaPointe is founder of CORE Sciences, teaching companies how the brain works and translating neuroscience into better collaboration, products, and decision-making. He's a four-time founder including Satellite (4th largest analytics product on the internet, acquired by Adobe), where he later ran product strategy and innovation.

## Key Views

**Your Brain Is a College Campus with Departments** — The brain has different systems: the History department (looking up what you already know — lowest energy), the Science department (open-minded experimentation), the Art department (creative, boundless thinking). Most people default to History because the brain conserves energy. To get better answers, consciously route problems to Science or Art. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/evan-lapointe.md]

**Choose Your Character for Influence** — Influence is like playing a video game: start by choosing your character. Are you "the devil's advocate," "the sledgehammer" (break it and see if it holds), "the empathizer"? Your personality has a natural fit. Playing to your natural style is more effective than trying to adopt someone else's approach. Once you know your character, deploy it intentionally. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/evan-lapointe.md]

**Am I a Miserable Experience?** — Before worrying about skills, expertise, or criticality, ask yourself: "Am I a miserable experience to work with?" If the answer is even partially yes, fix that first. The most talented person who's a terrible collaborator destroys more value than they create. Nothing else matters if people dread working with you. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/evan-lapointe.md]

**The Brain Conserves Energy by Default** — The brain's default mode is energy conservation, which means it reaches for the easiest answer (History department) rather than the best answer. To counter this: create deliberate friction for quick answers (force yourself to wait before responding), and lower the barrier to creative thinking (change your environment, physically move to a different space). ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/evan-lapointe.md]

**Better Relationships Through Brain Awareness** — Understanding that team members' brains are also operating in default energy-conservation mode changes how you interpret their behavior. They're not being difficult — their brain is taking the path of least resistance. Frame requests to activate their Science or Art departments: "Let's experiment with this" instead of "Here's the right answer." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/evan-lapointe.md]
