# Maggie Crowley

Maggie Crowley is VP of Product at Toast, and previously VP and Head of Product at Charlie Health, Senior Director of Product at Drift, Director of Product at BevSpot, and a PM at TripAdvisor. She holds an MBA from Harvard Business School and was an Olympic speed skater.

## Key Views

**Three Traits of Great PMs** — Across startups, scale-ups, and enterprises, three consistent patterns distinguish great PMs: (1) Simplifying — finding the one truly important thing and sticking with it long enough to finish. (2) Following up on results — setting calendar reminders to check metrics weeks and months after launch, then sharing findings. (3) Carrying the water — doing whatever it takes (sales calls, customer implementation, QA, copywriting) rather than saying "that's not my job." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/maggie-crowley.md]

**Product Strategy as a Landscape Document** — A practical template for writing strategy: start with mission and goals, dump the full landscape (business context, competitors, SWOT, key risks), give an honest accounting of current state (what works/doesn't, customer feedback, tech debt), identify the top one or two opportunities, outline challenges ("what has to be true"), and propose a solution in 3 bullets max. Share the whole doc — the logic chain from mission to priority should be visible and debatable. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/maggie-crowley.md]

**"When in Doubt, It's Your Job as a PM"** — Unlike engineers (feasibility) and designers (usability), the PM has no defined deliverable — their job is delivering a business result. This means filling every gap no one else is incentivized to fill. PMs need to be willing to take ownership of the bet — which means accepting the risk that the thing they chose might not work. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/maggie-crowley.md]

**Deep Time Over Job-Hopping for Skill Building** — It took Maggie ~5 years to feel she knew what she was doing as a PM. Staying at a company for multiple cycles (she was at Drift ~4 years) lets you see the consequences of your decisions — which is rare and invaluable. Job-hopping has its place, but grinding it out in one place builds deeper expertise. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/maggie-crowley.md]

**Get the PM Title Stamp, Then Ship** — The most common path into PM is getting that first "Product Manager" title (often via lateral move or startup). Once stamped with the title, the next question is always "what have you shipped?" — and many PMs fail to answer that. The number of shipped products and follow-up learnings compound over time. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/maggie-crowley.md]
