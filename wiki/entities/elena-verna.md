# Elena Verna

Elena Verna is the Head of Growth at Lovable (AI-powered app builder) and one of the most frequently repeated guests on Lenny's Podcast — appearing a record 4 times. She previously led growth at [[Miro]], [[Dropbox]], SurveyMonkey, [[Netlify]], and [[Amplitude]].

## Key Views

### The AI Growth Playbook is Different

Elena argues that **only 30-40% of traditional growth frameworks transfer to AI companies**. Her core thesis: AI-native companies need to spend 95% of their energy on *innovation in growth* and only 5% on *optimization* — a complete inversion of the traditional growth role.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/elena-verna-40.md]

She explains: "If you asked me five years ago, what's your organic marketing strategy, I would've said SEO. If you ask me right now, it's all about social — what is my CEO posting? What is my team posting?"^[raw/transcripts/lenny/elena-verna-40.md]

### Building in Public as a Growth Engine

Lovable's #1 growth strategy is [[building-in-public]] — coupled with employee socials and [[founder-led-socials]]. This serves triple duty:
1. **Acquisition** — constant "noise in the market" draws new users
2. **Reactivation** — "people log into social to see what Lovable shipped today"
3. **Community building** — users feel heard because ship cycles are measured in days

> "Velocity of shipping is our number one core value in development team. We do anything and everything to just keep it going up."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/elena-verna-40.md]

### Activation is Everyone's Job

Unlike traditional SaaS where growth owns the activation funnel, at AI-agent companies the *core product team* owns activation inherently. Elena describes a "race car framework" where the growth team focuses on new growth loops and big bets while the agent team obsesses over that first-generation experience.

### The "Capability → Value → Scale" Framework

Attributed to [[John Cutler]]: software adoption passes through three stages:
1. **Capability** — what's possible (vibe coding's current stage)
2. **Value** — how users derive benefit
3. **Scale** — embed into workflows

Because Lovable's capabilities change every 1-3 months, users constantly come back to re-explore — creating a natural re-engagement loop.
