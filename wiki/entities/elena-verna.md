# Elena Verna

Elena Verna appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss growth leader and former head of growth at amplitude, miro, dropbox, and surveymonkey, on 10 growth tactics that never work, the bullseye framework, and founder-led growth.

## Key Views

**Don't Hire a Head of Growth Too Early** — Wait until you have strong product-market fit, at least $1M in ARR, enough user data to run meaningful experiments, and self-serve revenue (for product-led companies). Until then, founder-led growth is more effective because the entire company stays responsible for growth rather than outsourcing it to a single team. The longer you wait, the better — companies like Stripe didn't create growth teams until $100-200M in ARR. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/elena-verna.md]

**A Growth Team Cannot Fix a Declining Business** — If your core metrics are dropping, focus on addressing fundamental product and marketing issues first. Growth teams can amplify success but cannot reverse declining product-market fit or competition eating your lunch. You need to plateau the decline before bringing in growth, or the head of growth is destined to fail. Growth can at best lift metrics by 10-15%, which is rarely enough to reverse a downtrend. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/elena-verna.md]

**Rebrands and Homepage Redesigns Never Produce Good Performance Results** — They are always a step back in performance that gets treated as a fire drill to fix. Every time Elena has seen a marketing site rebrand, it produced a measurable decline that then required months of optimization just to return to baseline. If you must rebrand, model out the expected hit and budget three to six months of post-launch optimization. Never promise that a homepage redesign will drive more acquisition. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/elena-verna.md]

**Copying Competitors Is the Fastest Path to Mediocrity** — You don't know if what you're seeing is the competitor's actual experience, their tactics are specific to their customers and channels, and you miss the context that led to their current solution. Even inside a company like Dropbox, pages that look polished may be 10-year-old experiments that perform terribly. Use competitor analysis for inspiration and patterns, but do your own research, design, and testing. The only real shortcut is talking to people who have solved your problem before. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/elena-verna.md]

**Your Problems Aren't Unique — Don't Start from Scratch** — Before trying to solve something from scratch, research how others have solved similar challenges and talk to people who have done it before. Elena hired an advisor from Atlassian to help stand up Miro's community, which saved 6-8 months of trial and error. Hiring advisors is one of the biggest career and business amplifiers you can do. Patternize your solutions — figure out frameworks that solve not just one problem but a class of problems. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/elena-verna.md]
