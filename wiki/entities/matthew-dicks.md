# Matthew Dicks

Matthew Dicks is a 59-time Moth Story Slam winner and 9-time Grand Slam champion. He is author of *Storyworthy* (Lenny's all-time favorite book on storytelling), nine other books, and works as an elementary school teacher. He teaches storytelling to companies like Slack, Amazon, Lego, and Salesforce.

## Key Views

**Every Good Story Is About a Five-Second Moment** — All worthwhile stories center on a single moment of transformation or realization. The bulk of the story (98%) is context building to bring that moment into greatest clarity. Whether telling a story live or writing a novel or making a film, there is always one instant where the protagonist flips from one state to another. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/matthew-dicks.md]

**Stories Make You Memorable; Facts Are Forgotten** — The human brain is not designed to remember pie charts, facts, statistics, or platitudes. Without storytelling, you will be forgotten 100% of the time. The risk of telling a story (standing out from the herd) is far less than the risk of being forgettable. At conferences, people remember stories, not data. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/matthew-dicks.md]

**The Five-Second Moment Is a Flip — Not a Gradual Change** — Change feels gradual but actually happens in an instant. The moment a student says "I don't like that cheeky smile of yours" — that's the five-second realization that trust has been built. In Star Wars, it's the moment Luke turns off his targeting computer and uses the Force. The storyteller's job is to bring that moment to life so the audience experiences the flip alongside them. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/matthew-dicks.md]

**Telling Stories Separates You from the Herd** — In business, everyone loves the word "storytelling" but few actually do it because being a storyteller means separating yourself from the herd, which risks being "picked off." The far greater risk is staying in the herd and being forgettable. True storytelling requires vulnerability and a willingness to be different. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/matthew-dicks.md]

**Crafting Stories Requires Active Practice** — Storytelling is a skill that can be taught and practiced. Matthew teaches individuals and teams through his company Speak Up. The process involves identifying the five-second moment, building context around it, and then practicing the delivery until it feels natural and authentic. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/matthew-dicks.md]
