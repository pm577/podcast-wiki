# Dr. Fei-Fei Li

Dr. Fei-Fei Li, known as the "godmother of AI," is a Stanford professor who spearheaded ImageNet — the dataset that sparked the modern AI revolution. She was Chief AI Scientist at Google Cloud, co-founded Stanford's Human-Centered AI Institute, and recently launched the world's first Large World Model called Marble through her company World Labs. She has been named one of Time's 100 Most Influential People in AI.

## Key Views

### ImageNet Was the Spark That Ended AI Winter

In the mid-2000s, Fei-Fei realized that AI needed massive amounts of clean-labeled data to get smarter, not just better algorithms. Her insight was that computers needed to see millions of examples to learn object concepts — the way a child learns. ImageNet became the breakthrough that led to the deep learning revolution. She recalls that in 2015-2016, tech companies avoided using the word "AI" because it was seen as a dirty word — a complete inversion of today's landscape.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/dr-fei-fei-li.md]

### There's Nothing Artificial About AI

In her testimony to Congress, Fei-Fei stated: "There's nothing artificial about AI. It's inspired by people. It's created by people, and most importantly, it impacts people." She emphasizes that AI is a human endeavor and its future is up to us. She is not a utopian — she believes technology is a double-edged sword — but she is fundamentally optimistic about AI as part of humanity's long trajectory of innovation that makes lives better.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/dr-fei-fei-li.md]

### World Models Are the Next Frontier

Fei-Fei is pioneering the shift from language models to world models — AI systems that understand and generate 3D spatial environments, physics, and cause-and-effect. Her company World Labs launched Marble, described as the first Large World Model. This represents a shift from AI that processes text/images to AI that understands the physical world — with profound implications for robotics, AR/VR, and scientific discovery.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/dr-fei-fei-li.md]

### AI's Impact Depends on Human Choices

Fei-Fei rejects both the utopian "AI will save everything" and dystopian "AI will destroy everything" narratives. She believes AI's impact is determined by how society chooses to develop and deploy it. She advocates for human-centered AI — building technology that augments rather than replaces human capability, and ensuring diverse voices shape AI's development. This philosophy underpins Stanford's HAI Institute.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/dr-fei-fei-li.md]

### Visual Intelligence Is the Gateway to Understanding

Fei-Fei's career has been defined by the insight that humans are deeply visual animals, and visual intelligence is the key to AI. From ImageNet (teaching machines to see objects) to World Labs (teaching machines to understand 3D space), her work has consistently focused on making AI spatially and visually intelligent. She believes this path leads to AI that can genuinely interact with and understand the physical world.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/dr-fei-fei-li.md]
