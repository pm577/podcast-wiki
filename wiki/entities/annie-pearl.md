# Annie Pearl

**Role:** Chief Product Officer at Calendly; former CPO at Glassdoor; Director of Product at Box  
**Known for:** Scaling Calendly's product and growth, building a sales team on top of a PLG foundation, product strategy frameworks.

## Key Views

### 1. Strategy is an integrated set of choices — where to play and how to win

Annie's crisp definition: strategy is not a goal or a vision — it's an integrated set of choices that outline how you'll win. The key questions: What's your winning aspiration? Where will you play (markets, segments, personas)? How will you win with that target audience? A good strategy makes these choices explicit and connected.

> "Strategy is really just an integrated set of choices that outline how you're going to win in whatever marketplace you choose." — Annie Pearl

### 2. Calendly's first 1,000 users — the power of founder-led distribution

The origin story: Calendly's founder Tope Awotona personally onboarded the first users, many from his own network. This founder-led sales approach gave them direct feedback on what worked and built early product-market fit conviction before any scaling. The lesson: don't outsource customer discovery until you've done it yourself.

### 3. Building a sales team on top of a PLG company

Annie's experience at Calendly is a case study in adding a sales motion to a product-led growth company. The challenge: maintaining the self-serve magic while adding sales touch for enterprise deals. Her framework: clearly define which segments are self-serve vs. sales-assisted, build the handoff gracefully, and ensure the sales team deeply understands the product rather than selling around it.

### 4. The product strategy cascade — from aspiration to execution

Annie walks through Calendly's product strategy cascade: winning aspiration → where to play → how to win → core capabilities → management systems. Each layer informs the next. This creates alignment from the CPO to individual PMs, ensuring everyone's work ladders up to the same strategic choices.

### 5. Advice for breaking into product management

Annie shares practical advice for aspiring PMs: demonstrate product thinking in your current role regardless of title, build a portfolio of shipped work, learn to communicate with engineers and designers, and be relentless about customer empathy. She values demonstrated ability over pedigree.
