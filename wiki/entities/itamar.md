# Itamar Gilad

Itamar Gilad is a product coach, author, and speaker with over two decades of experience in senior product roles at Google (Gmail, identity, YouTube), Microsoft, and various startups. He is the author of *Evidence-Guided: Creating High-Impact Products in the Face of Uncertainty*, which provides a meta-framework for shifting organizations from opinion-based to evidence-guided decision-making.

## Key Views

### Google+ vs. Gmail Tabs: The Tale of Two Approaches

Itamar's defining career contrast: Google+ was a massive opinion-based project — a top-down mandate to build a social network that consumed ~1,000 people and ultimately failed. Gmail's tabbed inbox, by contrast, was evidence-guided — starting as a small idea no one believed in, tested iteratively with dogfooders, usability studies, and data mining. Result: 1.8 billion users actively use the feature. "Evidence is very empowering for us smaller people in the organization to be challenged by opinions."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/itamar-gilad.md]

### The GIST Framework: Goals, Ideas, Steps, Tasks

Itamar's GIST model provides a complete evidence-guided product process. **Goals**: clear, user-centric metrics. **Ideas**: generated from customer insight, not opinions. **Steps**: experiments and tests to validate (smoke tests, Wizard of Oz, fake door). **Tasks**: execution only when confidence is high enough. The framework forces teams to validate before building at scale.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/itamar-gilad.md]

### Signs You're Not Actually Evidence-Guided

Key telltale signs: unclear or output-focused goals, missing user-facing metrics, excessive time spent on roadmapping, no real experimentation culture, and disengaged engineering teams focused on delivery rather than outcomes. "If the team is disengaged from users and the business, they don't care that much — that's a sign you can fix by adopting a more evidence-guided system."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/itamar-gilad.md]

### The Confidence Meter

Itamar's practical tool: rate ideas on a confidence scale (1-10 based on evidence strength). Low confidence ideas get smaller investments (smoke tests, prototypes). High confidence ideas get full builds. This prevents the common mistake of treating all ideas equally or betting the farm on untested assumptions. "You need to create the environment where the leader comes and says 'here's what we need to do' and you ask, 'where's your evidence?'"^[raw/transcripts/lenny/itamar-gilad.md]

### Evidence Empowers Junior People to Challenge Senior Opinions

Itamar's key insight: data is a democratizing force. "If you come with data, if you run a secret experiment and show them, you usually get one of two results: they get mad (in which case polish your resume) or they're pleasantly surprised." Even Steve Jobs changed his mind when presented with evidence — he was against the iPhone initially and against multitouch, but flipped as evidence accumulated.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/itamar-gilad.md]
