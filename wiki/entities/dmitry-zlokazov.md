# Dmitry Zlokazov

*Head of Product at Revolut ($45B fintech, 50M+ customers, 50+ countries). Architect of Revolut's "Local CEO" product ownership model and ultra-flat org structure with 150+ product owners.*

## Key Views

1. **Product owners are "local CEOs" with genuine end-to-end ownership**: At Revolut, product owners manage cross-functional pods with hiring/firing power and full P&L responsibility. This ultra-flat structure enables 150+ product owners to maintain founder-level quality and velocity across dozens of parallel launches in 50+ countries.

2. **Value raw intellect and hunger over experience**: Revolut deliberately favors raw intellect and unquenched hunger to build over years of experience. Internal transfers from engineering, ops, and other functions often become the company's most successful product leaders. The belief is that drive and cognitive ability matter more than domain familiarity.

3. **Never compromise on quality and UX — even for MVPs**: When a new product isn't getting traction, the question isn't just "is the idea wrong?" but "does your product just suck?" Revolut cuts functionality to the most critical features but never compromises on quality, UX, or aesthetics. Removing the "product quality" variable from the uncertainty equation accelerates learning.

4. **Founders review every single UI shipped**: Revolut's founders personally review every UI that ships. Rather than bottlenecking innovation, this detail obsession scales by creating a shared quality standard across the org. Everyone knows what "good enough" looks like because they see the founders' judgment applied consistently.

5. **A product that's 99% done is closer to 0% than 100%**: The final polish — the last 1% of quality — is what transforms a functional product into a "wow" experience. Revolut treats near-finished products as fundamentally not done, refusing to ship until the craft meets their bar. This philosophy is embedded in their "Deliver WOW" principle.
