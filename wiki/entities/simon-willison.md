# Simon Willison

Simon Willison appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss an ai state of the union: we've passed the inflection point.

## Key Views

**The November Inflection Point** — Late 2025, GPT 5.1 and Claude Opus 4.5 crossed a threshold where coding agents went from 'mostly works with close supervision' to 'almost always does what you asked.' This fundamentally changed software engineering. A lot of engineers woke up in January realizing 'I can churn out 10,000 lines of code in a day and most of it works.' ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-04-02-simon-willison.md]

**Agentic Engineering vs Vibe Coding** — Vibe coding (Karpathy's term) is hands-off prototyping where you don't look at the code. Agentic engineering is the professional discipline of using coding agents to build production-ready software with careful review. 'The art of getting really good results out of this, the art of having them help you build software you could deploy to a million people, that's never going to be easy.' ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-04-02-simon-willison.md]

**Dark Factory Pattern** — The next frontier: applying professional practices and quality expectations to code you're not directly reviewing. Moving from 'review all AI-generated code' to automated quality assurance systems that produce better software, not just faster software. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-04-02-simon-willison.md]

**The Challenger Disaster of AI** — Simon predicts a catastrophic AI failure from overconfidence, analogous to NASA's O-ring disaster. 'Lots of people knew those little O-rings were unreliable, but every single time you get away with launching a space shuttle without them failing, you institutionally feel more confident.' ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-04-02-simon-willison.md]

**95% of Code Not Typed** — Simon writes 95% of his code without typing it himself, often on his phone while walking the dog. 'Using coding agents well is taking every inch of my 25 years of experience as a software engineer. I can fire up four agents in parallel. By 11:00 AM, I am wiped out.' ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/lenny-2026-04-02-simon-willison.md]
