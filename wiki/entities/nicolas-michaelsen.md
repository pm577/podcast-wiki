# Nicolas Michaelsen

Founder & CMO at AirHelp, the leading service for air travel compensation. Y Combinator alumnus who draws back the curtain on the exclusive world of Y Combinator — including the admissions process, the infamous interview, the tutoring available to YC startups, and the effects of YC on startup valuation.

## Key Views

### 1. Inside Y Combinator: Admissions, The Interview, and Valuation Effects

Nicolas shared an inside look at YC's admissions process — how startups get selected, what the infamous 10-minute interview is really like, the quality of tutoring and mentorship available to YC companies, and how YC participation affects startup valuation. His experience at AirHelp (YC-backed) provided a firsthand view of the accelerator's inner workings.^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2015-04-30-20-vc-032-inside-y-combinator.md]

### 2. Key Takeaways from the YC Experience

The most valuable parts of YC for Nicolas were the partner office hours (notably with Kevin Hale, founder of Wufoo and YC Partner), the network of fellow founders, and the structured focus on traction and growth metrics that YC instills.^[raw/transcripts/20vc/20vc-2015-04-30-20-vc-032-inside-y-combinator.md]
