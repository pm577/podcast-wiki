# Mayur Kamat

Mayur Kamat is Chief Product Officer at N26 (a $9B neobank with 7M+ customers). Previously he was Global Head of Product at Binance, VP of Product at Agoda, and a PM at Google and Microsoft. He was the first PM on Google Hangouts.

## Key Views

**Strategy Is Overrated for PMs** — "For most product managers, your strategy should be: how fast can I go from hypothesis to data?" The real discipline of product management is making decisions based on evidence. The moment you build experimentation into your process, you've made it scientific. Strategy talk without rapid testing is just speculation. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/mayur-kamat.md]

**The Hangouts Failure — Don't Take on Projects Beyond Your Control** — Mayur's most spectacular failure was being the first PM on Google Hangouts. Despite thousands of people, the full power of Google, and Larry Page saying they could do anything, they still didn't build a great messaging product. The lesson: don't take on projects that are going to be 6-12 months long because you generally don't have control over the macro environment. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/mayur-kamat.md]

**Experimentation Makes PM a Real Discipline** — "Anybody who uses the product thinks they have ideas. At some point you're like, 'What is my discipline? What is my science?' The moment you build experimentation, you've now made it scientific." Experimentation is what separates product management from opinion-based decision making. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/mayur-kamat.md]

**Hiring Great PMs** — N26 is among the top companies globally for hiring and producing great PMs. Key traits they look for: hypothesis-driven thinking, ability to move fast, comfort with ambiguity, and deep curiosity about user behavior. The best PMs don't just execute — they question assumptions rigorously. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/mayur-kamat.md]

**Career Advice: Start on the West Coast US** — For PMs early in their career, starting on the West Coast of the US provides the best foundation — the density of talent, the pace of innovation, and the depth of product culture is unmatched. Compensation early in your career doesn't matter as much as the learning environment. Asia and Europe offer different advantages later. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/mayur-kamat.md]
