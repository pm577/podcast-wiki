# Nir Eyal

Nir Eyal is the author of two bestselling books: *Hooked: How to Build Habit-Forming Products* and *Indistractable: How to Control Your Attention and Choose Your Life*. He writes, consults, and teaches at the intersection of psychology, technology, and business. His books have sold over 1 million copies in over 30 languages.

## Key Views

### The Opposite of Distraction Is Traction, Not Focus

Nir's foundational insight: "The opposite of distraction is not focus. The opposite of distraction is traction." Both words come from the Latin *trahere* (to pull) and end in *actio* (action), reminding us that distraction is something we do, not something that happens to us. Traction pulls you toward your goals; distraction pulls you away. What separates them is intent. "Anything you do with intent is traction. Anything else is distraction."^[raw/transcripts/lenny/nir-eyal.md]

### 90% of Distractions Come from Internal Triggers

Nir's research found that only 10% of distractions are caused by external triggers (pings, dings, rings). The other 90% come from internal triggers: boredom, loneliness, fatigue, uncertainty, anxiety. "Whether it's too much news, too much booze, too much Facebook, you are always going to get distracted from one thing or another unless you understand the root cause of the problem." Step one to becoming indistractable: master those internal triggers.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/nir-eyal.md]

### The Four-Step Indistractable Framework

Nir's complete framework: (1) Master internal triggers — identify the discomfort you're trying to escape. (2) Make time for traction — turn your values into time blocks. (3) Hack back external triggers — not just phones but meetings, emails, Slack. (4) Prevent distraction with pacts — firewalls as a last resort. The strategy matters more than any individual tactic.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/nir-eyal.md]

### Work Distraction Is the Worst Kind

Nir warns about the most pernicious form of distraction: doing work tasks that aren't what you intended. "Just because it's a work-related task doesn't mean it's not a distraction. In fact, that's the worst kind of distraction. Far worse than playing Candy Crush because then at least it's obvious." Checking email when you planned to do deep work is still distraction — it's prioritizing the urgent and easy over the hard and important.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/nir-eyal.md]

### The Hook Model: How Habit-Forming Products Work

Nir's foundational Hook model from *Hooked* describes how products create habits through a four-step cycle: Trigger (internal or external) → Action (the simplest behavior in anticipation of reward) → Variable Reward (the core of the hook) → Investment (where the user puts something in, increasing the likelihood of returning). Understanding this model helps both product builders and those seeking to control their own habits.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/nir-eyal.md]
