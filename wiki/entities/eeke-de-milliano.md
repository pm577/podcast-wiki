# Eeke de Milliano

*Head of Product at Retool. Former longtime PM at Stripe (one of their first PMs). Built Stripe Checkout, Stripe Connect, Stripe Radar, and Chargeback Protection. Started at Stripe as their first account executive.*

## Key Views

1. **Process is variance-reducing — it flattens both tails**: The cost of process is that while it brings low performers up to the average, it also brings high performers (most creative thinkers) down to the average. The tension is that as companies grow, they introduce more process, but the people who don't need it are often the ones driving the most innovation.

2. **Sales experience is an undervalued path into PM**: Eeke joined Stripe as their first account executive — a role she had "no business doing" — but it taught her to talk to customers all day, understand their pain points, and figure out how the product could solve them. Those are exactly the skills a great PM needs. Her advice: go get a sales job.

3. **Co-create products with early customers via shared Slack channels**: At Stripe, they built products like Stripe Billing by working directly with companies like Figma and Slack — sharing Slack channels, showing early builds, getting real-time feedback, and not releasing until the alpha group was "super, super happy." This is the model for product-minded engineering.

4. **The two-sided marketplace payment model was the catalyst for Stripe Connect**: Talking to customers building marketplaces (Airbnb, Uber, Lyft) revealed complex payment flows — one payer to multiple recipients, multiple payers to one, etc. These conversations directly led to Stripe Connect, one of Stripe's foundational products. User conversations that uncover genuine structural needs are the best source of big product ideas.

5. **Innovation requires protecting high performers from excessive process**: As head of product at Retool, Eeke thinks constantly about how to maintain the creative spark of high performers while the company scales. The key is being intentional about which processes are necessary and which are cargo-culted from bigger companies.
