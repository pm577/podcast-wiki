# Gokul Rajaram

Gokul Rajaram is an executive at DoorDash (leading Caviar), serves on the boards of Coinbase, Pinterest, and The Trade Desk, and is a prolific angel investor. He previously was a product director at Facebook (mobile advertising transition), Google AdSense, and Square. He is widely considered one of the top angel investors in the startup ecosystem.

## Key Views

### Great Careers Are Built on Serendipity, Not Linear Plans

Gokul's career-defining lesson: "I think many opportunities don't come linearly just working in your job and making sure you get promoted. Great careers are built by knowing a lot of people doing great work so they know and want you on their teams, and just waiting for serendipity and then seizing it." He discovered AdSense by wandering into engineers' office after hours; he joined Square through informal advising that led to an inbound from Jack Dorsey.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gokul-rajaram.md]

### Paying It Forward Builds a Reservoir of Goodwill

Gokul is a strong advocate of paying it forward. Helping others — through advising, intros, or sharing knowledge — builds "a reservoir of goodwill that comes back to help you in different ways." He cites Lenny's content-creation approach as a perfect example: the value you give freely creates unexpected returns. This philosophy underpins his own prolific angel investing and mentorship.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gokul-rajaram.md]

### Choose Problems, Not Products

When evaluating opportunities, Gokul advises focusing on the problem you're solving and who you're solving it for, not the specific product. He gets energy from problems that affect relatable people — small businesses, consumers, small publishers. Big enterprise problems don't energize him. Finding your customer segment is more important than finding the perfect product idea because the problems will evolve but the customer stays.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gokul-rajaram.md]

### The AdSense Origin Story: Be Curious and Open

Gokul's most famous story: working on Google syndication, he wandered into an unknown office after hours, found engineers working on "reversing Google's search index" for ad targeting, and offered to be their PM on nights and weekends. Within three months, the project (AdSense) was big enough that his boss moved him full-time. The lesson: do your core job well, but equally important is curiosity about what else is happening.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gokul-rajaram.md]

### Founders Should Live the Mission Authentically

For angel investing, Gokul's #1 filter is founder authenticity. "I think it's very important for founders to really live and breathe the mission themselves in an authentic way." He avoids founders who seem in it for the money. The most successful investments come from founders who can't imagine doing anything else — the mission is personal, not transactional.^[raw/transcripts/lenny/gokul-rajaram.md]
