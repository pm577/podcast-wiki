# Amjad Masad

**Role:** Co-founder and CEO of Replit — browser-based AI-powered coding platform with 34M+ users  
**Known for:** Democratizing software development, pioneering AI-assisted coding, vision of "everyone is a developer."

## Key Views

### 1. AI shifts the bottleneck from building to ideation

Amjad's core insight: historically, you were bottlenecked by your ability to make things — ideas couldn't come to life because building was slow and hard. AI removes that bottleneck. Now the constraint becomes how fast you can generate and validate good ideas. Product managers and founders who can think, ideate, and prioritize quickly will have the advantage over those who rely on pure execution skill.

> "Typically, you're bottlenecked where your ideas are not fitting in because they need to be made quickly. Now you open up that bottleneck. You become limited by how fast you can generate ideas." — Amjad Masad

### 2. The billion-dollar company with zero employees is coming

Amjad predicts that within five years, someone will run a billion-dollar company with zero human employees — where support, development, and operations are all handled by AI. This isn't dystopian; it's a natural extension of the trend where AI agents handle increasingly complex workflows. The role of the founder shifts from manager to creator and orchestrator.

> "I could imagine five years from now, someone running a billion-dollar company with zero employees — support handled by AI, development handled by AI — and you're just building and creating this thing." — Amjad Masad

### 3. Making software is still too hard — Replit's mission

Replit's founding insight: software development is fragmented across IDEs, runtimes, package managers, deployment pipelines, and hosting. Each piece is a separate hurdle, especially for beginners. Replit collapses all of this into a single browser-based environment. The goal isn't just to make coding easier for existing developers — it's to enable people who never considered themselves developers to build software.

### 4. People don't realize how far AI coding has come

A recurring theme: the gap between public perception of AI coding capabilities and what Replit users experience daily is huge. Amjad demonstrates live coding that most people find hard to believe. His advice to product teams: try the latest tools yourself. You can't design for an AI-augmented future if you haven't experienced what's possible today.

### 5. The future of product management in an AI world

As AI reduces the cost of building to near zero, PMs and founders need to shift from "what should we build?" to "what *shouldn't* we build?" and "what's the right vision?" The ability to say no, to maintain product coherence, and to deeply understand user needs becomes more valuable, not less. The PM's role evolves from requirements-writer to taste-maker and strategic prioritizer.
