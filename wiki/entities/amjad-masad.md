# Amjad Masad

Amjad Masad appeared on Lenny's Podcast to discuss amjad masad (replit ceo) on why the roi of learning to code doubles every six months.

## Key Views

**Amjad's Law — ROI of learning to code doubles every six months** — The return on investment for learning just a bit of coding (prompting AI, reading code, basic debugging) is doubling every six months as AI coding tools improve. You don't need traditional computer science education — an AI-native coding school would skip most CS fundamentals and teach only app structure, prompting, and debugging. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/amjad-masad.md]

**Ideas become the bottleneck as AI eliminates the production bottleneck** — When AI makes building software dramatically cheaper and faster, the constraint shifts from production to ideation. The key future skill is being generative — rapidly generating, testing, and iterating on ideas. As Amjad puts it: "You become limited by how fast you can generate ideas." ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/amjad-masad.md]

**The Jevons Paradox of software — lower cost drives massively higher consumption** — As the cost of building software falls (from days and dollars to minutes and cents), total consumption of software explodes. People build ideas they'd never have hired a developer for — back-office tools, internal dashboards, side projects. This mirrors how cheaper electricity led to more total spend, not less. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/amjad-masad.md]

**Working prototypes as the universal language that breaks organizational silos** — AI-generated working prototypes replace low-bandwidth communication (Slack, specs, handoffs) between PMs, designers, and engineers. A live prototype is the shared artifact everyone can react to, reducing misinterpretation and enabling Figma-to-code workflows that directly translate designs into running React apps. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/amjad-masad.md]

**Humans as the gaps — and those gaps are shrinking fast** — Currently, humans fill the gaps AI agents can't handle (complex database migrations, large iterations, scaling architecture). But with models improving on a ~6-month cadence, Amjad envisions a future within 5 years where a person could run a billion-dollar company with zero employees — AI handling support, development, and operations. ^[raw/transcripts/lenny/amjad-masad.md]
