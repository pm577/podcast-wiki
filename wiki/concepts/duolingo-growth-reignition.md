# Duolingo Growth Reignition

## Synthesis

1. **Duolingo turned around single-digit DAU growth to 4.5x over four years.** Jorge Mazal (former CPO) shares the complete story: from stalled growth in 2018 to a successful IPO, driven by a handful of product changes rooted in an innovative growth model.

2. **Early failures taught critical lessons.** The Gamification Team's first attempt — copying Gardenscapes' moves counter — was neutral. It failed because Duolingo lessons don't involve strategic decision-making like match-3 games; a moves counter was just a nuisance. A referral program inspired by Uber delivered only a 3% bump — because the best users already had Super Duolingo subscriptions and couldn't be incentivized. Lesson: adopt features only after understanding why they work in their original context and how they'll translate to yours.

3. **The growth model that changed everything.** Jorge's team created a MECE user segmentation model with buckets (New, Current, Reactivated, Resurrected, At-risk WAU, At-risk MAU, Dormant) connected by retention rates. By running sensitivity analysis — moving each rate 2% quarterly for three years — they discovered CURR (Current User Retention Rate) had 5x the DAU impact of any other lever. This became their North Star.

4. **Leaderboards drove the first breakthrough.** Borrowed from Zynga's FarmVille 2, the key adaptation: matching users with similarly engaged competitors (not friends), and making advancement frictionless (automatic opt-in, no extra tasks). Results: overall learning time up 17%, highly engaged learners tripled.

5. **Push notifications required discipline.** Duolingo learned from Groupon's cautionary tale: aggressively A/B testing notifications destroys the channel as users opt out permanently. Their rule: protect the channel at all costs — no quantity increases without strong justification and CEO approval. Dozens of small optimizations (timing, templates, images, localization) compounded year after year.

6. **Streaks became the most powerful engagement mechanic.** An APM discovered that users who reach a 10-day streak have dramatically lower churn. Optimizations followed: streak-saver notifications, calendar views, animations, streak freeze changes, streak rewards. The compounding magic: each day a user returns, they care more about coming back the next day than they did the day before.

7. **CURR improved 21%, reducing daily churn of best users by over 40%.** Combined with other bets (international expansion, social features, TikTok virality, influencer marketing), this drove the 4.5x DAU growth. The share of DAU with a 7+ day streak increased nearly 3x to over half of all daily users.

## Key Disagreements

- **Gamification ceiling:** Whether gamification mechanics have diminishing returns or can keep compounding through new feature vectors.
- **CURR vs. new-user acquisition:** Whether mature products should prioritize retention of current users or invest in new acquisition channels — Duolingo chose CURR first, then expanded.
