# B2B

## Synthesis

1. **B2B sales is about selling outcomes, not features.** Jen Abel's enterprise sales playbook emphasizes that B2B buyers make purchasing decisions based on strategic advantage ("alpha"), not feature checklists. The most effective B2B sales motion demonstrates how the product makes the buyer's company more competitive.

2. **B2B pricing should reflect value, not cost-plus.** Multiple guests across 20VC and Lenny's podcast agree that B2B companies dramatically underprice. The right approach is value-based pricing: charging a fraction of the value you create for the customer. If your product saves a company $1M/year, charging $100K is reasonable — even if it costs $10K to deliver.

3. **Enterprise sales cycles are getting faster but still require patience.** Joe Floyd's 20VC episode discusses red flags in B2B startups: sales cycles longer than 9 months, multiple stakeholders with conflicting priorities, and "evaluation paralysis." The best B2B companies compress these cycles by creating urgency through trials, proof-of-concepts, and executive relationships.

4. **Product-led growth is transforming B2B acquisition.** Simon Menashy (MMC Ventures) notes that the most successful B2B startups are those that let users try the product before talking to sales. Bottom-up adoption through individual users, teams, and departments creates pull from within the organization that dramatically reduces the enterprise sales burden.

5. **B2B marketplaces have different dynamics than B2B SaaS.** Bob Ghoorah differentiates between selling to businesses (B2B SaaS) and building a platform that connects businesses (B2B marketplace). Marketplaces face a chicken-and-egg problem that SaaS companies don't — they must acquire both buyers and sellers simultaneously.

## Key Disagreements

- **Self-serve vs. sales-led:** Whether B2B should be self-serve (like Slack, Notion) or sales-led (like Salesforce, Workday).
- **Vertical vs. horizontal:** Some argue vertical B2B (one industry) is easier to sell; others prefer horizontal (across industries) for scale.
- **PLG vs. enterprise sales:** The degree to which product-led growth can replace traditional enterprise sales motions.
