# PM Second Brain with ChatGPT

## Synthesis

1. **A "second brain" is an AI knowledge repository that offloads context from your working memory.** Amir Klein (monday.com) describes dumping his product context (Slack channels, Notion pages, decks, CSV exports, support docs, competitor research) into a ChatGPT Project. The key insight: PMs spend enormous mental energy just holding context — AI can store and retrieve it instantly, freeing cognitive capacity for actual reasoning and judgment.

2. **Step 1: Create its personality.** Give the ChatGPT Project detailed custom instructions defining how it should think, what role it plays, and how it should interact — including requesting it to challenge you, push back on weak ideas, and think creatively. Use ChatGPT itself to help draft these instructions.

3. **Step 2: Feed it everything.** Everything is text: PRDs, decks (exported as PDFs), websites (saved as PDFs), Slack channel exports, CSV files of user research, Reddit threads. Each file adds depth. The key realization is that once you see everything as text, you realize how much context you can give your second brain.

4. **Step 3: Let it cook.** Use the second brain for tasks that previously consumed hours: drafting sign-up forms with context-appropriate questions, generating Lovable/Bolt prompts for working prototypes from Figma screenshots, and producing tailored communications for different audiences (marketing, leadership, sales enablement, user testing) without reloading context each time.

5. **Context is the new interface.** When all context lives in your head, output is limited by working memory. When context lives outside your head — intact and retrievable — your thinking expands. The breakthrough is not just remembering more, but reasoning better. The second brain surfaced a key insight at monday.com: users weren't blocked by AI capability but by confidence — they needed scaffolding, not features.

6. **The same principles apply across tools.** ChatGPT Projects, Claude Projects, and Gemini Gems all support the same pattern: custom instructions, file uploads, and persistent context across conversations.

## Key Disagreements

- **Single-context vs. multi-project brains:** Whether one monolithic second brain or separate projects per initiative is more effective.
- **AI dependency risk:** Whether relying on an external second brain atrophies native memory and intuition, or simply frees capacity for higher-order thinking.
