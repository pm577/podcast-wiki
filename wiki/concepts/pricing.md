# Pricing

## Synthesis

1. **Value-based pricing requires finding the right metric.** Naomi Ionita (Menlo Ventures) explains that the highest-leverage pricing decision is choosing the value metric — the unit that naturally scales with customer value. This creates alignment: as customers derive more value, they pay more organically.

2. **"Beautifully simple" pricing wins early.** Madhavan Ramanujam (Simon-Kucher) advises startups to prioritize simplicity over optimization. Complex pricing creates friction in the sales conversation and delays time-to-revenue. The litmus test: can a customer understand your pricing in 30 seconds?

3. **Pricing should be revisited every 12-24 months, but with AI that cycle accelerates.** Madhavan Ramanujam notes that pricing strategy previously needed revisiting every two years; with AI, the cycle is compressing to months as costs and capabilities shift rapidly.

4. **Pricing is an emotional decision internally, but a logical one externally.** Ramanujam's key insight: resistance to price increases is almost always internal and emotional, not external and logical. Teams that overcome this internal resistance capture significantly more value.

5. **Usage-based pricing is growing but requires predictability.** Multiple guests discuss the tension between usage-based pricing (aligns with value, but creates unpredictable bills) and subscription pricing (predictable, but may not capture full value). The emerging solution is hybrid models — base subscription + usage overage.

## Key Disagreements

- **Usage-based vs. seat-based:** Some advocate for usage-based as the purest expression of value-based pricing; others argue it creates customer anxiety and churn due to unpredictability.
- **AI pricing models:** Debate over whether AI products should charge per-token, per-output, per-seat, or per-outcome — each has different incentive alignment properties.
