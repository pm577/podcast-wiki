# Sales

## Synthesis

1. **Sell the alpha, not the feature — the enterprise sales playbook from $1M to $10M ARR.** Jen Abel (State Affairs, Jellyfish) argues that enterprise buyers don't care about features; they care about outcomes. The most effective sales motion demonstrates "alpha" — evidence that your product gives the buyer a strategic advantage. She recommends structuring demos around customer-specific use cases rather than feature walkthroughs.

2. **AI agents are transforming sales team composition.** Jason Lemkin (SaaStr) replaced his entire sales team with 20 AI agents and documented the results. The key finding: AI agents excel at structured sales workflows — lead qualification, follow-up sequencing, and meeting scheduling. However, complex enterprise negotiations and relationship-building still require human judgment.

3. **Product-led sales is replacing traditional enterprise sales.** Jeanne DeWitt Grosser describes how Vercel and Stripe built sales motions that complement product-led growth rather than replace it. The modern approach: let users discover value through the product first, then deploy sales resources for expansion and enterprise deals. This reduces customer acquisition costs by 30-50%.

4. **The sales-development-to-account-executive handoff is the most fragile moment in the funnel.** Multiple guests highlight that misalignment between SDRs and AEs destroys pipeline velocity. The best organizations define explicit lead qualification criteria, use shared metrics, and rotate team members across functions to build empathy.

5. **Pricing and packaging are the highest-leverage sales tools.** Jen Abel and Jason Cohen both emphasize that sales success starts with getting pricing right. A well-structured pricing page can do more for conversion than an entire sales team. The most common mistake: pricing that's too complex or that doesn't align with the value different customer segments perceive.

## Key Disagreements

- **Full AI replacement vs. human augmentation:** Lemkin advocates fully automated AI sales teams; others argue critical deals need human relationship-building.
- **Land and expand vs. enterprise-first:** Some recommend starting with smaller deals and expanding; others say enterprise contracts from day one build better unit economics.
- **Product-led vs. sales-led:** The debate continues over whether PLG or enterprise sales is the superior motion for different product categories.
