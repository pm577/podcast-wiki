# Onboarding

## Synthesis

1. **Onboarding's goal is reaching the "aha moment" faster.** Lauryn Isford (Airtable) shows that the highest-leverage onboarding work is reducing time-to-value — getting users to the moment they experience the product's core value. Every step added before the aha moment is a potential drop-off point.

2. **Self-serve onboarding is a leading indicator of product-market fit.** Sean Ellis argues that if users can get through a product's onboarding and experience value without hand-holding, it signals strong product-market fit. If onboarding requires heavy assistance, the product itself may not be intuitive enough.

3. **Improving onboarding consistently delivers 2-3x ROI.** Petra Wille (Strong Product People) notes across many studies that onboarding improvements consistently outperform feature investments for both signup conversion and long-term retention.

4. **Onboarding quality determines retention more than any post-signup intervention.** Rahul Vohra (Superhuman) shows that Superhuman's obsessive focus on the first-time user experience — measuring time-to-aha and optimizing every click — created retention rates that competitors couldn't match through feature parity.

5. **AI products need different onboarding than traditional SaaS.** Aishwarya Naresh Reganti notes that AI products face the "blank canvas" problem: users don't know what to ask or expect. Effective AI onboarding includes prompt suggestions, example outputs, and progressive disclosure of capabilities.

## Key Disagreements

- **Interactive vs. passive onboarding:** Some advocate for interactive walkthroughs that guide users step-by-step; others argue users should explore freely with contextual help available when needed.
- **Time-to-value vs. feature discovery:** The tension between getting users to value fast vs. exposing them to the product's full capability set early.
