# SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity)

A two-page legal document created by Y Combinator (Paul Graham, Jessica Livingston) that standardized early-stage startup funding. Published online for free, it became the de facto standard for seed-stage investment.

## Why It Was Revolutionary

Before the SAFE, venture capital was fragmented — every firm had its own deal structure, founders had to negotiate complex terms. The SAFE introduced a simple, founder-friendly standard that reduced transaction costs to near zero. It was a pivotal moment in Silicon Valley history — analogous to standardizing AC/DC for electricity or the grid for energy distribution.

^[raw/transcripts/stanford-ai-frontier/garry-tan-diana-hu-yc-stanford-ai-frontier.md]

## Broader Lesson

Institutions that set and enforce standards unblock entire ecosystems. YC's SAFE is an example of systems design applied to capital markets.
