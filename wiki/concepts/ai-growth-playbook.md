# AI Growth Playbook

A set of emerging frameworks for how AI-native companies drive growth differently from traditional SaaS. Most comprehensively articulated by [[Elena Verna]] (Head of Growth at Lovable) but echoed across multiple episodes.

## Core Thesis

Only 30-40% of traditional growth frameworks apply to AI companies. The rest must be unlearned or reinvented. The key difference: **optimization matters less than innovation** in fast-moving AI categories.

| Traditional SaaS | AI-Native |
|---|---|
| 80% optimization, 20% innovation | 5% optimization, 95% innovation |
| Growth owns the activation funnel | Core product team owns activation (agent quality = activation) |
| SEO is primary organic channel | Social is primary organic channel (founder + employee socials) |
| Marketing owns messaging | Every engineer is a marketer (ship + announce culture) |
| Category creation takes decades | Category creation can happen in weeks (vibe coding) |

## Key Frameworks

### Ship Velocity as Growth (Elena Verna / [[Gaurav]] at [[Mirage]])

"Ship a marketable feature every week." Velocity of shipping is the #1 core value. This creates:
- Constant market noise (acquisition)
- Natural re-engagement (users check what's new)
- User feedback loop (short idea-to-ship cycles make users feel heard)

### Capability → Value → Scale ([[John Cutler]])

Software adoption passes through three stages:
1. **Capability** — what's possible (vibe coding: "what can I build?")
2. **Value** — how do I get value from this
3. **Scale** — embed into workflows

AI products at the Capability stage get natural re-engagement because what's possible changes every 1-3 months.

### Building in Public

Multiple guests ([[Elena Verna]], [[Anton Osika]], [[Gaurav]]) cite [[building-in-public]] as the #1 growth channel. Requires:
- Founder-led socials (X, LinkedIn) with authentic personality
- Employee socials — every team member is a marketer
- Vulnerability and authenticity over corporate scrubbing

### The Race Car Framework

Used at Lovable: you're moving fast enough that growth work is about "putting oil in the wheels" and removing barriers, not generating demand. The market is pulling; your job is to not hit a wall.

## When This Differs

This playbook **does not apply** to:
- Traditional SaaS with long sales cycles (enterprise, on-prem)
- Marketplace businesses (two-sided network effects dominate)
- Late-stage growth where optimization matters again
- Non-AI categories with stable capabilities
