# Taste (as a Durable Moat)

The cost of shipping code is approaching zero. What does not go to zero is **taste** — the ability to discern what is good, what users want, and what should exist.

## Why It Matters

- Generic benchmarks (MMLU) don't tell you if your product works
- The ultimate judge is whether users want it
- Eval = taste operationalized: does it follow instructions? Is the answer correct? Does it preserve customer trust? Does it hit business goals?

## How to Cultivate It

1. Capture all traces (context-dependent per product)
2. Convert failure cases into evals
3. Replay constantly → system self-heals → improves automatically
4. Human in the loop labels what's wrong

Cross-modal evaluation (using frontier models to judge sub-agents) is the next evolution — Garry Tan is building this into Gstack.
