# AI Product

## Synthesis

1. **AI products should start with the user problem, not the technology.** Stewart Butterfield (Slack) emphasizes that the best AI products are those that solve real user problems without requiring users to understand AI. The technology should be invisible. The most successful AI products — like ChatGPT, Grammarly, and Notion AI — integrate AI into existing workflows rather than creating entirely new interaction paradigms.

2. **Prototype AI products with JSON, not design.** Ravi Mehta (Tinder, Reforge) describes a counterintuitive approach: start with data structures (JSON schemas) before building UIs. AI products are fundamentally about data transformation, and getting the data model right is more important than pixel-perfect design. Once the data flow works, design around it.

3. **Non-technical PMs can now build AI products directly.** Zevi Arnovitz (Meta) provides a complete workflow for non-technical product managers to build functional AI products using Cursor and similar tools. This democratization changes the PM role from "spec writer" to "builder." PMs who can build their own prototypes ship faster and build better products.

4. **AI agents are products, not features.** Jason Lemkin (SaaStr) argues that companies should build dedicated AI agent products rather than bolting AI onto existing products. The sales team that was replaced by 20 AI agents wasn't using "AI features" — they were using purpose-built AI agent products designed from the ground up for specific workflows.

5. **Speed of iteration is the AI product advantage.** Cat Wu (Anthropic) describes how Anthropic's product team moves faster by shipping early and iterating based on real usage. AI products are uniquely suited to this approach because they improve with usage data. The companies that win in AI are those with the fastest feedback loops between product changes and user behavior.

## Key Disagreements

- **AI feature vs. AI product:** Whether AI should be a feature within existing products or a standalone product category.
- **Safety-first vs. speed-first:** Some argue AI products require extensive safety testing before launch; others advocate shipping fast and learning.
- **Consumer vs. enterprise AI:**
Whether the biggest AI product opportunities are in consumer applications or enterprise workflows.
