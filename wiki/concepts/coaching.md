# Coaching

## Synthesis

1. **Executive coaching is a learnable skill, not a mystical art.** Rachel Lockett democratizes coaching by sharing techniques previously reserved for executives paying tens of thousands of dollars. The core skill: shifting from problem-solving mode to curiosity mode. When a team member brings a problem, ask questions instead of providing answers.

2. **Communication is a practiced skill, not a learned concept.** Christian Idiodi (SVPG) emphasizes that you can't learn to be a better communicator by attending a class — you must practice in a safe environment with feedback. The best coaches create psychological safety where team members can practice difficult conversations and receive honest feedback.

3. **Coaching trees are the sign of great leaders.** Bangaly Kaba introduces the concept of "coaching trees" — analogous to coaching trees in sports (Coach K, John Calipari). Great leaders are measured not just by what they build but by the leaders they develop. The best product leaders build coaching trees that propagate their approach across the industry.

4. **Hard truths are the most valuable coaching currency.** Keith Rabois (Khosla Ventures) emphasizes that the most effective coaches deliver hard truths with empathy but without dilution. The Silicon Valley culture of "brutal honesty" works when paired with genuine care for the person's development. Sugar-coating feedback is the enemy of growth.

5. **Coaching is especially critical during transitions.** Multiple guests note that the moments when coaching adds most value are during transitions: new role, new team, new stage of company, or new personal circumstances. Executives who excel at transitions are those who proactively seek coaching rather than waiting for a crisis.

## Key Disagreements

- **Internal vs. external coaches:** Whether coaching is more effective from managers within the organization or external professional coaches.
- **Directive vs. non-directive coaching:** Debate over whether coaches should give direct advice or facilitate self-discovery.
- **Measurability of coaching ROI:** Some argue coaching impact is unmeasurable; others track promotion rates, retention, and 360 feedback scores.
