# AMA (Ask Me Anything)

## Synthesis

1. **AMAs surface the questions your audience actually has — not what you think they should ask.** Jason Cohen discusses how the best AMAs reveal gaps in your content strategy. The questions people ask in an AMA format are often completely different from what product teams assume users care about. These questions become a roadmap for future content and product improvements.

2. **Structured AMAs produce higher quality than open-ended ones.** Albert Cheng (Duolingo) recommends providing a theme or category framework for AMA questions rather than a completely open "ask me anything." Topic-based AMAs — "ask me about growth," "ask me about building teams" — generate more specific, actionable questions than generic AMAs.

3. **AMAs build community through direct access.** Rachel Lockett notes that AMA formats create a sense of direct access between leaders and community members. This access builds trust and loyalty beyond what one-to-many content (blog posts, newsletters) can achieve. The format signals that the leader is accessible and values the community's questions.

4. **Written AMAs have more enduring value than live AMAs.** Multiple guests observe that written AMA responses (on forums, in newsletters, on blogs) accumulate search value and can be referenced years later. Live video AMAs are engaging in the moment but have shorter shelf lives. The best approach: live AMA for engagement, transcribed and edited for enduring content.

5. **AMA responses should be honest, not polished.** The most trusted AMA responses are those that acknowledge uncertainty ("I don't know, but here's how I'd think about it") rather than projecting false confidence. Audiences can detect inauthenticity — a raw, honest AMA builds more trust than a carefully crafted set of talking points.

## Key Disagreements

- **Live vs. async AMAs:** Whether real-time video AMAs or written async AMAs produce better engagement and insights.
- **Moderation approach:** Some curate questions heavily; others take questions as they come for authenticity.
- **Frequency:** Whether AMAs should be weekly, monthly, quarterly, or event-driven.
