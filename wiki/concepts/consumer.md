# Consumer

## Synthesis

1. **Behavioral science explains why consumer products succeed or fail.** Kristen Berman (Irrational Labs) provides the "three Bs" framework: Behavior = Motivation + Ability + Trigger. Most consumer products fail because they over-index on motivation (willpower) and under-invest in making the behavior easy (ability) and creating effective triggers.

2. **The "first mile" experience determines consumer product success.** Scott Belsky (Adobe) argues that the first experience a consumer has with a product is disproportionately important — it shapes their entire mental model. Consumer products that invest in a delightful, frictionless first mile dramatically outperform those that don't.

3. **The hierarchy of engagement predicts long-term retention.** Sarah Tavel's (Benchmark, Pinterest) framework shows that consumer products need to progress users through stages: accidental use → habitual use → meaningful use → community/identity. Products that don't move users up this hierarchy face inevitable churn.

4. **Surprise and delight are underused consumer product patterns.** Scott Belsky notes that Hollywood uses surprise constantly, but most consumer products don't. Micro-surprises and moments of delight in the product experience create emotional attachment that functional features alone cannot.

5. **Consumer AI products face unique challenges.** Kayvon Beykpour (Twitter) discusses how consumer products require speed and simplicity that AI sometimes complicates. The best consumer AI products hide the AI complexity behind a simple interface — the magic should feel like magic, not engineering.

## Key Disagreements

- **Consumer vs. enterprise-first for AI products:** Whether AI-native companies should start with consumer (large TAM, low switching costs, viral potential) or enterprise (higher ACV, longer relationships, data moats).
- **Engagement vs. utility as product north star:** Some consumer products optimize for time spent (engagement); others optimize for task completion speed (utility). The right choice depends on the product category and business model.
