# Marketing

## Synthesis

1. **World-class GTM in 2026 is about channel specialization and AI orchestration.** Jeanne DeWitt Grosser (Vercel, Stripe, Google) argues that modern marketing requires owning specific channels deeply rather than spreading thin. At Stripe, she built distinct GTM teams for different developer segments — each with its own marketing motion, metrics, and content strategy. The era of one-size-fits-all marketing is over.

2. **Marketing channels must be evaluated methodically when growth stalls.** Jason Cohen (WP Engine) recommends a five-step framework when growth stops: assess logo retention, pricing power, net revenue retention, marketing channel efficiency, and total addressable market. His key insight: most companies blame marketing for growth problems that are actually product or pricing issues.

3. **Content marketing is the startup's secret weapon for customer acquisition.** Jay Acunzo (NextView Ventures) emphasizes that startups should market through education, not interruption. The 20VC episode on the Ultimate Marketing Guide stresses that great content builds trust before the first sales conversation — and that trust compounds over time as the content library grows.

4. **AI is reshaping marketing from campaign-based to always-on.** Howie Liu (Airtable) describes restructuring Airtable's entire organization around AI capabilities, including marketing. AI enables personalized content at scale, real-time campaign optimization, and predictive lead scoring. The marketing teams that adapt fastest will have an unfair advantage.

5. **The best marketing feels like product, not promotion.** Across episodes, a consistent theme emerges: the most effective marketing strategies embed value into the marketing itself. Notion's templates, Canva's design tutorials, and Slack's product-led growth all demonstrate that when marketing helps users do their job better, conversion follows naturally.

## Key Disagreements

- **Channel concentration vs. diversification:** Some guests advocate going deep on one or two channels until they saturate (Grosser); others recommend testing many channels early to find product-channel fit.
- **Brand vs. performance marketing:** Debate over whether early-stage companies should invest in brand awareness or focus exclusively on measurable performance marketing.
- **AI in marketing:** Some see AI as a force multiplier for creative work; others worry about homogenization of content as everyone uses the same tools.
