# Marketplace

## Synthesis

1. **Liquidity is the fundamental marketplace challenge.** Benjamin Lauzier (Lyft, Thumbtack) explains that marketplaces need enough density on both sides simultaneously for a good experience. Early marketplaces typically subsidize one side (usually supply) to build liquidity, but this creates a dependency that must be broken as the marketplace matures.

2. **Managed marketplaces are the dominant emerging model.** Benjamin Lauzier describes "managed marketplaces" where the platform controls more of the transaction — vetting supply, setting prices, guaranteeing quality. This model (Thumbtack, Uber) creates better user experiences than pure peer-to-peer (Craigslist) but requires more operational investment.

3. **Commission rates are rising over time.** Dan Hockenmaier (Thumbtack) documented a clear trend: newer marketplaces charge higher commissions than older ones. Modern marketplaces charge 20-30% vs. the 5-10% of earlier generations. This reflects growing marketplace power and willingness to pay for curation and trust.

4. **The hierarchy of engagement applies differently to marketplaces.** Sarah Tavel's framework (accidental → habitual → meaningful → identity) shows that marketplace retention depends on moving users beyond transactional relationships. Marketplaces that create community or identity (Etsy, Airbnb) have dramatically better retention than pure transaction platforms.

5. **Marketplaces must find PMF on both sides.** Benjamin Lauzier emphasizes that marketplace PMF is doubly hard — you need enough demand to attract supply and enough supply to satisfy demand. Most marketplace failures come from solving only one side of the equation. The key insight: start by manually creating supply to test demand, then automate.

## Key Disagreements

- **Commission vs. subscription for marketplace monetization:** Some marketplaces charge per-transaction commissions (aligns with value); others charge seller subscriptions (predictable revenue). The choice affects seller behavior and marketplace liquidity.
- **Full-stack vs. pure marketplace:** Whether platforms should own the full service (Uber, DoorDash) or remain a pure matchmaker (Airbnb, Etsy). Full-stack offers more control but requires more capital.
