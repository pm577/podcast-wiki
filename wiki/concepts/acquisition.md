# Acquisition

## Synthesis

1. **Channel concentration beats channel diversification for early-stage startups.** Jeanne DeWitt Grosser argues that successful companies dominate one or two acquisition channels before expanding. At Stripe, they mastered developer evangelism and content before adding paid acquisition. Spreading too thin across channels dilutes expertise and makes it impossible to find product-channel fit.

2. **Product-led acquisition is the most efficient growth model.** Jeetu Patel (Cisco, Box, DocuSign) emphasizes that the best acquisition strategy makes the product itself the primary acquisition channel. When users invite colleagues, share outputs, or embed your product, acquisition costs approach zero. The holy grail is building virality into the core product experience.

3. **Acquisition channels have lifecycles — they saturate and decay.** Guy Kawasaki (Apple) noted early that every acquisition channel eventually saturates as competitors enter and costs rise. The key is to establish a new channel before your current one peaks. This requires constant experimentation — trying channels that fail before finding the next winner.

4. **Vibe coding and AI are creating new acquisition patterns.** Lazar Jovanovic (Lovable) describes how AI-generated products are changing customer acquisition. When users can create custom solutions quickly, the acquisition model shifts from selling a fixed product to enabling user creativity. The product becomes a platform for user-generated outcomes.

5. **Attribution is broken for multi-channel acquisition.** Multiple guests note that most companies can't accurately attribute customer acquisition to specific channels. The solution: incrementality testing (holdout groups to measure true channel contribution) rather than last-click attribution models that consistently overvalue bottom-of-funnel channels.

## Key Disagreements

- **Paid vs. organic:** Debate over whether paid acquisition is a tax on bad product or a legitimate scale lever.
- **Channel concentration vs. diversification:** Some advocate dominating one channel before expanding; others recommend testing many channels early.
- **Self-serve vs. sales-assisted:** Whether product-led or sales-led acquisition produces better unit economics at scale.
