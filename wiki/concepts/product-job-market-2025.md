# State of the Product Job Market 2025

## Synthesis

1. **Open PM and engineering roles are trending up significantly.** There are over 6,000 open PM roles globally — 53.6% above the 2023 bottom and up 11% since the start of 2025. This is the highest count in over two years, contradicting recession and AI-displacement narratives.

2. **AI PM roles are exploding.** There are 688 open AI PM roles, and the broader "AI roles" category (including AI-native companies and AI-specific roles at traditional companies) has seen explosive growth. Nearly a third of all AI-related roles are based in the Bay Area.

3. **Layoffs are slowing.** The data shows 2025 is on track for fewer layoffs than any of the past four years, though the risk remains ever-present even at respected companies.

4. **Remote work opportunities continue to decline.** At peak (late 2022), ~35% of PM roles were remote-friendly. Today only ~23% have a remote option, and this appears to be approaching a new baseline of ~20%.

5. **The Bay Area dominates opportunity.** Nearly 20% of all open PM roles and 17% of engineering roles are Bay Area-based. Berlin and Austin entered the top 10 PM cities for the first time; Boston and LA fell out.

6. **Capital investment is surging.** GPU/data center investment is up 29% from last year and 227% from five years ago — though it's unclear whether this translates directly to more jobs or mainly more hardware.

7. **Emerging trends to watch:** The rise of the "product engineer" role (415 open roles) and how AI copilot tools are changing PM interviewing.

## Key Disagreements

- **Remote work's future baseline:** Whether the ~20% remote-friendly share is a stable floor or will continue to erode further.
- **Capital investment → job creation:** Whether the massive increase in infrastructure investment will create proportional knowledge-worker jobs or mostly benefit hardware and construction.
