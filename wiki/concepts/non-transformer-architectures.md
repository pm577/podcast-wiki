# Non-Transformer Architectures

Alternative neural network architectures to the transformer, including Mamba (state-space models) and others. Debated as potential successors to the dominant transformer architecture.

## The Scaling-Transformers Argument

Scaling transformers is **working** — there's a simple, proven recipe to make them smarter. More likely that AI will tell us what the better architecture is (by continuing to scale) than humans discovering it first.

^[raw/transcripts/stanford-ai-frontier/yash-bottla-applied-compute-stanford-ai-frontier.md]

## The Anti-Transformer Argument (Ilya, LeCun, others)

Humans don't need internet-scale data to learn. Therefore, there must be a better architecture. The investments in transformer infrastructure (compute scale-outs, chip optimization) are enormous — hard to turn that ship.

## Current State

Frontier labs invest more in transformer architecture. Non-transformer research is experimental. The chips themselves may be optimized for transformer compute.
