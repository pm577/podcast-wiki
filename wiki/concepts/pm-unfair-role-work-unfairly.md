# PM Is an Unfair Role — Work Unfairly

## Synthesis

1. **Product management is structurally "unfair" — and getting harder.** PMs are expected to operate on both maker and manager schedules, can't hire or fire but must deliver, and absorb organizational dysfunction. The "great flattening" (fewer ICs owning more product surface) and AI-accelerated expectations compound this. Fairness isn't coming.

2. **Do action items live in the meeting, not after it.** Tal Raviv's core productivity tactic: instead of adding post-meeting tasks to a to-do list, execute them while screensharing during the meeting itself — writing the summary, creating the Jira ticket, scheduling the follow-up in real time with everyone watching. Colleagues appreciate the immediacy.

3. **Use 59-second Looms to replace meetings.** Half of 30-minute meetings can be pivoted to async video. Key technique: film two takes, aim for under a minute, and pitch the time ROI explicitly. Treating Loom as a core PM communication skill gives a huge productivity advantage.

4. **Hide, ignore, and automate Slack with deliberate rituals.** Don't open Slack for the first half of the day. Use Slack sections to compartmentalize, hide read channels to reduce noise, and use Slack reminders as a "robot executive assistant" to never drop the ball.

5. **Cultivate a team that operates without you.** "Turn everyone into a mini-PM." When teammates do PM-ish work (QA analyzing data, designers going to stakeholders, engineers interacting with customers), shower them with positivity. DMs are the devil — redirect to public channels.

6. **Product scrapbooking: collect signals before you need them.** Maintain a lightweight Notion database of screenshots, support tickets, Gong clips, and Slack threads organized by swim lane (monetization, onboarding, etc.). When quarterly planning hits, you already have the raw material.

7. **Let AI write for you, but don't let it read for you.** Use speech-to-text + GPT to draft PRDs, user stories, and blog posts. But avoid letting LLMs summarize raw customer data — the PM's competitive advantage is staying in the weeds of customer insights and building product intuition.

8. **PM your own brain's freshness.** Disconnect with the same discipline you bring to work. Uninstall Slack mobile, change your display name to "OOO until [date]" while away, and choose physical transitions (walk, stretch, skateboard) instead of doomscrolling.

## Key Disagreements

- **AI as a reading tool vs. writing tool:** Raviv argues AI should write but not read for you; others advocate using AI for summarization of research.
- **Productivity systems are personal:** What works for one PM (no Slack in the morning) may not work for another with different team dynamics or time zones.
