# PR

## Synthesis

1. **Traditional PR is being replaced by founder-led content and direct audience building.** Multiple 20VC guests from 2015-2016 observed that the startup PR playbook was shifting away from press releases and toward founder-written thought leadership. Bob Ghoorah emphasized that startups should pitch "the narrative, not the news" — reporters cover stories, not feature launches.

2. **"Friend-raising before fund-raising" is the PR principle for startups.** Sharon Wienbar's 20VC episode describes how startups should build relationships with journalists and influencers before needing coverage. The same logic applies to analyst relations, partner introductions, and conference speaking — invest in relationships before you need something.

3. **PR amplifies product-market fit; it doesn't create it.** A consistent theme across episodes is that the best PR in the world can't save a product that doesn't work. Stewart Butterfield emphasizes that Slack's early press coverage worked because the product was genuinely loved by users. PR accelerates existing momentum; it doesn't manufacture it.

4. **Crisis communications capability is a non-negotiable for startups.** Brandon Lipman (TechCrunch) discusses how startups often ignore crisis communications until it's too late. Every startup should have a crisis playbook: who speaks for the company, what the escalation path is, and how to communicate transparently without over-sharing.

5. **The modern PR stack includes podcasts, newsletters, and social media.** Guests note that the most influential tech coverage now comes from podcasts (like Lenny's, 20VC), newsletters, and social media — not just traditional tech publications. Startups should build relationships across this entire ecosystem, not just with traditional tech journalists.

## Key Disagreements

- **Hire a PR agency vs. DIY:** Some guests recommend agencies for their relationships; others say founder-led PR is more authentic and cost-effective.
- **Press release value:** Debate over whether press releases still matter or are purely for SEO.
- **When to hire first PR person:** Some say after Series A; others argue PR should start on day one through founder activity.
