## Overview

This comparison examines two dominant go-to-market (GTM) strategies in SaaS: **Product-Led Growth (PLG)** — where the product itself drives acquisition, retention, and expansion through a self-serve model — and **Enterprise Sales** — a top-down, relationship-driven motion where dedicated sales teams close high-value contracts with large organizations. Drawing on insights from Jeanne DeWitt Grosser (Stripe, Vercel), Elena Verna (ex-SurveyMonkey, Reforge), Hila Qu (GitLab, Reforge), and Geoffrey Moore, this page synthesizes when and how to use each.

## Comparison Table

| Dimension | PLG | Enterprise Sales |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary motion** | Bottom-up, user self-serve | Top-down, executive relationship |
| **Sales cycle** | Minutes to days | Months to quarters |
| **Contract value** | Low to medium ($10–$50K ACV) | High ($100K+ ACV) |
| **Customer acquisition** | Viral, freemium, trial | SDR/BDR outbound, field sales |
| **GTM cost** | Low marginal cost per user | High (sales team comp, marketing) |
| **Org structure** | Growth team, product-led sales | Sales, SDRs, solution engineers |
| **Key metric** | Activation, time-to-value, NPS | Pipeline velocity, ACV, quota attainment |
| **Product requirement** | Self-serve onboarding, low friction | Demo-ready, compliance, support |
| **Best for** | SMB, mid-market, API-first products | Enterprise, compliance-heavy, Global 2000 |

## Synthesis

**Jeanne DeWitt Grosser (Vercel, Stripe)** on the Lenny podcast: "PLG makes sense for a lot of companies at the outset, unless you are very explicitly building a product for enterprise. Sierra as an example — they are very clearly going after Global 2000 or something close to that. PLG is not going to be overlaid there." She argues most companies should start PLG-first and add enterprise sales as their product moves upmarket, a hybrid model she calls "product-led sales."

**Elena Verna** (Lenny podcast, 2023): Distinguishes between starting PLG and adding a top-down sales layer vs. starting sales-led and adding PLG. "If you start as a sales motion dominated org, your competitors will add PLG and benefit from attracting end users who become cases to the employer." She argues the hybrid "product-led sales" motion — where product handles acquisition and activation, then sales closes expansion — is the optimal end state for most B2B SaaS.

**Hila Qu (GitLab, Reforge)**: Emphasizes organizational structure — PLG requires a dedicated growth product team, a head of growth marketing, and a head of product-led sales working in concert. Without that alignment, companies fracture between the self-serve and sales motions.

**Geoffrey Moore** (Lenny podcast, 2024): Notes that PLG works best in the "Main Street" phase — "product-led growth is really good at expand because it prompts the user to get more involved. But there's got to be no risk." He frames PLG as ideal for low-risk, high-frequency purchases, while enterprise sales is required for crossing the chasm into mainstream enterprise adoption.

**Howie Liu (Airtable CEO)** on Lenny: Describes restructuring Airtable's entire org around AI, blending PLG with a more consultative enterprise motion as the product matured from a spreadsheet tool into a full platform serving large organizations.
