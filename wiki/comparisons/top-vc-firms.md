## Overview

Benchmark, Andreessen Horowitz (a16z), and Sequoia Capital are the three most iconic venture capital firms in Silicon Valley, yet they operate with fundamentally different models. This comparison draws on 20VC episodes featuring Pat Grady (Sequoia), Ev Randle (Benchmark), David George (a16z), Alex Rampell (a16z), and insights from Brian Halligan (HubSpot/Sequoia CEO coach) to understand what makes each firm distinct.

## Comparison Table

| Dimension | Benchmark | a16z | Sequoia |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Approach** | Small team, high conviction | Platform-as-a-service, full-service | Deep operator mentorship, endurance |
| **Firm size** | ~5 partners, very lean | ~100+ partners, massive platform | ~25 partners, medium structure |
| **Fund size** | ~$500M–$1B per fund | ~$15B+ across multiple funds | ~$8B+ across stages |
| **Stage focus** | Primarily early (Seed–A) | Seed to growth (multi-stage) | Seed to growth (multi-stage) |
| **Brand strategy** | Stealth/prestige, limited partners | Loud, content-forward, "media+" | Quiet authority, founder networks |
| **Thesis style** | Narrative-driven, concentrated bets | Sector specialization, 5 verticals | Pattern recognition, long relationships |
| **Key metric** | Ownership % and total return | Assets under management | Durable returns across decades |
| **Founder fit** | Self-sufficient founders, less hand-holding | High-touch services, PR, hiring help | CEO coaching, playbook sharing |

## Synthesis

**Pat Grady (Sequoia)** on 20VC (2024): Detailed the Sequoia investment process, sharing frameworks from Doug Leone, Roelof Botha, and Alfred Lin. Sequoia's edge is pattern recognition accumulated over 50+ years — they analyze founders on dimensions like "IQ × EQ × endurance." Grady noted that Sequoia's greatest value add is "the framework for thinking about building a lasting company," not just capital. The firm's biggest threat is becoming complacent with its own playbook.

**Ev Randle (Benchmark)** on 20VC (2025): Benchmark's newest GP argued that margins matter less in AI and that mega funds (large AUM) will not produce good returns. Benchmark's model is deliberately small — 5 partners, no associates, no platform team. They bet on a few companies with large ownership positions. Randle contrasted this with the "spray-and-pray" model of larger firms, arguing concentration is how venture capital works best.

**David George (a16z)** on 20VC (2025): Explained how billion-dollar funds can still 5x — a16z's thesis is that the TAM of technology is expanding so fast that massive funds can still generate outsized returns if they invest in the right sectors. He also described a16z's controversial bet on crypto and web3 as a "10-year thesis" that most firms were too early on. A16z's platform (recruiting, BD, marketing, regulatory) is unmatched in VC.

**Alex Rampell (a16z)** on 20VC (2026): Discussed a16z's $15B fundraise and the philosophy that "the best companies have hostages, not customers" — meaning high switching costs and platform lock-in. He argued that mid-sized funds ($500M–$2B) will die, squeezed between angels/seed and mega funds.

**Brian Halligan (HubSpot co-founder, Sequoia CEO coach)** on Lenny (2026): Described his work as a Sequoia CEO coach — helping portfolio company founders transition from founder-mode to CEO-mode. Sequoia invests heavily in CEO development, recognizing that the hardest part of scaling is not the product but the leader.
