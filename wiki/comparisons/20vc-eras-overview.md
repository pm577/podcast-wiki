# 20VC Across 11 Years: How VC Changed

A comparison of how startup fundraising, founder concerns, and VC dynamics evolved across 20VC's 1,462 episodes from 2015 to 2026.

## At a Glance

| Dimension | 2015-2016 | 2020-2021 | 2025-2026 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Avg seed round** | $500K-$2M | $2M-$5M | $3M-$8M+ |
| **Time to Series A** | 18-24 months | 12-18 months | 6-12 months |
| **Top founder concern** | Finding PMF, building a team | Scaling efficiently, remote work | AI moat, agentic workflows |
| **VC posture** | "Show us traction" | "Growth at all costs" | "Show us profitability path" |
| **Hot sectors** | SaaS, fintech, marketplaces | Remote tools, digital health, SPACs | AI agents, foundation models, defense tech |
| **Guests per year** | 128-156 | 93-100 | 148-64 (2026 ongoing) |

## Era Overview

### Post-Financial Crisis Recovery (2015-2016)
*128 episodes in 2015, 156 in 2016*

- **Dominant topics:** Seed funding, London tech scene, angel investing, crowdfunding
- **Typical guests:** UK-based angels, early-stage VCs, accelerator founders
- **The vibe:** "VC was a relationship business, not a data business"
- **Key guests:** Guy Kawasaki (ep 1), Christian Claussen, Stefan Glaenzer
- **Notable:** The podcast launched. Guests debated whether VC was worth it. Crowdfunding was the hot new thing.

### SaaS Scaling Era (2016-2018)
*151 episodes in 2017, 97 in 2018*

- **Dominant topics:** SaaS metrics, ARR, sales efficiency, growth at scale
- **Typical guests:** US-based growth investors, SaaS founders, operators
- **The vibe:** "Metrics became the language of fundraising"
- **Key guests:** Jason Lemkin (first appearance), Beezer Clarkson, Bill Clerico

### Late-Stage Boom (2019-2021)
*96 episodes in 2019, 100 in 2020, 93 in 2021*

- **Dominant topics:** Mega-rounds, unicorns, SoftBank effect, SPACs
- **Typical guests:** Late-stage VCs, public market investors, crossover funds
- **The vibe:** "Record valuations, maximum optimism, FOMO-driven rounds"
- **Key guests:** Tony Fadell, Palmer Luckey, Arlan Hamilton

### COVID Era (2020-2021)
*Overlaps with late-stage boom — 100+20vc episodes in 2020, 93 in 2021*

- **Dominant topics:** Remote work, digital transformation, "we're all remote now"
- **Typical guests:** Founders navigating COVID, Zoom boom companies
- **The vibe:** "Digital acceleration, SPAC frenzy, WFH is the future"
- **Key guests:** Steve Blank, Palmer Luckey, Ilkka Paananen (Supercell)

### Correction & Efficiency (2022-2023)
*138 episodes in 2022, 146 in 2023*

- **Dominant topics:** Down rounds, layoffs, profitability, unit economics, efficiency
- **Typical guests:** Operators who survived 2008, efficiency-focused founders
- **The vibe:** "Growth at all costs is dead. Show me the unit economics."
- **Key guests:** Jason Lemkin (recurring), Alex Bouaziz (Deel), David Sacks

### AI Inflection (2023-2026)
*148 episodes in 2024, 148 in 2025, 64 in 2026 (ongoing)*

- **Dominant topics:** AI agents, foundation models, LLMs, reasoning models, agentic workflows
- **Typical guests:** AI founders, frontier lab executives, AI-native company builders
- **The vibe:** "The biggest platform shift since mobile. Everything is being rewritten."
- **Key guests:** Vlad Tenev, Aaron Levie, Steeve Morin, Micha Kauffman, Andrew Feldman (Cerebras)

## The Arc

1. **2015:** VC is a relationship business. Raise $500K, build for 18 months, pray.
2. **2018:** SaaS metrics rule. Show me your ARR growth rate.
3. **2021:** Growth at all costs. SPACs, mega-rounds, $20M seed rounds.
4. **2023:** Efficiency era. Down rounds, layoffs, "show me profitability."
5. **2026:** AI native. One-person companies, $200/month coding agents replacing $4M teams.

## What Stayed the Same

- "Sell the alpha, not the feature" — Jen Abel's maxim holds across all eras
- Founder quality > market timing > idea (most guests agree)
- The best time to raise is when you don't need to (Jason Lemkin, every appearance)

## What Changed

- Seed rounds went from $500K to $5M+ — same advice, 10x the dollars
- The phrase "boil the ocean" flipped from warning to rallying cry
- Guest origin shifted from 60% UK / 40% US to 30% UK / 70% US
