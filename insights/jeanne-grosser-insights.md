# Jeanne Grosser — Insights

*Extracted from podcast appearances.*

### Insight: 80% of customers buy to avoid pain or reduce risk as opposed to increased upside...
**Claim:** 80% of customers buy to avoid pain or reduce risk as opposed to increased upside. Founders should emphasize risk avoidance in sales, especially to enterprises, rather than just the art of the possible.
**Audience:** founder | **Actionability:** 9/10 | **Confidence:** 0.95
**Source:** lenny-2025-11-30-jeanne-grosser
**Context:** Discussion on customer buying motivations and how to position products.

### Insight: Create a customer buying journey that feels like a unique experience because if ...
**Claim:** Create a customer buying journey that feels like a unique experience because if products are only different at the margin, the experience of being sold to can differentiate and drive buying decisions.
**Audience:** operator | **Actionability:** 7/10 | **Confidence:** 0.9
**Source:** lenny-2025-11-30-jeanne-grosser
**Context:** Lenny asks about go-to-market as a product; Jeanne explains the importance of the buying experience.

### Insight: Sales teams should be so technically credible that engineers can't distinguish t...
**Claim:** Sales teams should be so technically credible that engineers can't distinguish them from product managers. This builds trust with technical buyers.
**Audience:** operator | **Actionability:** 8/10 | **Confidence:** 0.95
**Source:** lenny-2025-11-30-jeanne-grosser
**Context:** Lenny mentions Jeanne's superpower of building a sales org that doesn't feel like a sales org to engineers.

### Insight: Integrate all go-to-market functions (marketing, sales, customer success, suppor...
**Claim:** Integrate all go-to-market functions (marketing, sales, customer success, support, partnerships) into a single lifecycle with a unified segmentation framework to avoid misalignment.
**Audience:** founder | **Actionability:** 6/10 | **Confidence:** 0.85
**Source:** lenny-2025-11-30-jeanne-grosser
**Context:** Jeanne defines go-to-market as any function that touches a customer or makes a dollar, and advocates for integration.

### Insight: Use forward-deployed engineering (a rebrand of professional services) to embed w...
**Claim:** Use forward-deployed engineering (a rebrand of professional services) to embed with customers, understand their needs, and feed learnings back to product. This is especially relevant in AI where customers don't know what they need.
**Audience:** operator | **Actionability:** 7/10 | **Confidence:** 0.9
**Source:** lenny-2025-11-30-jeanne-grosser
**Context:** Jeanne discusses how go-to-market has become more consultative and the rise of forward-deployed engineering.

### Insight: Hire go-to-market engineers to bring technical prowess and AI to re-architect wo...
**Claim:** Hire go-to-market engineers to bring technical prowess and AI to re-architect workflows, enabling personalized customer experiences at scale. Example: Stripe built a data-driven outbound function with only 4 SDRs by creating a company universe database.
**Audience:** operator | **Actionability:** 8/10 | **Confidence:** 0.9
**Source:** lenny-2025-11-30-jeanne-grosser
**Context:** Discussion on the rise of the go-to-market engineer and the Stripe example of Project Rosland.

### Insight: Use AI to create personalized outbound emails by filling in a template based on ...
**Claim:** Use AI to create personalized outbound emails by filling in a template based on company attributes (industry, business model, persona) rather than manually crafting each email.
**Audience:** operator | **Actionability:** 8/10 | **Confidence:** 0.9
**Source:** lenny-2025-11-30-jeanne-grosser
**Context:** Jeanne describes creating a 'mad libs' email template at Stripe in 2017 that was 80% fill-in-the-blank based on customer attributes, and now at Vercel they are redoing it with AI and it works.

### Insight: Hire a go-to-market engineer (GTM engineer) to build AI agents that encode human...
**Claim:** Hire a go-to-market engineer (GTM engineer) to build AI agents that encode human workflows for sales prospecting, rather than just a systems configurator.
**Audience:** founder | **Actionability:** 7/10 | **Confidence:** 0.85
**Source:** lenny-2025-11-30-jeanne-grosser
**Context:** Jeanne contrasts her 2017 team (data scientist + systems person) with her current team at Vercel (data scientist + GTM engineer building agents with Vercel workflows).

### Insight: Build a 'company universe' database with rows as companies and columns as attrib...
**Claim:** Build a 'company universe' database with rows as companies and columns as attributes to enable targeted selling, and layer AI on top to automate prospecting.
**Audience:** operator | **Actionability:** 6/10 | **Confidence:** 0.8
**Source:** lenny-2025-11-30-jeanne-grosser
**Context:** Jeanne describes the A-DNA project at Stripe as a massive database of companies with attributes, and notes that Ben Salzman is productizing this concept with AI.

### Insight: AI will eventually eliminate outbound prospecting by automatically matching comp...
**Claim:** AI will eventually eliminate outbound prospecting by automatically matching companies and products, so invest in AI-native GTM tools now.
**Audience:** investor | **Actionability:** 5/10 | **Confidence:** 0.7
**Source:** lenny-2025-11-30-jeanne-grosser
**Context:** Jeanne shares Ben Salzman's view that AI will get to the point where outbound prospecting is unnecessary because AI will do company-product matching.
