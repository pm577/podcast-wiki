*Extracted from podcast appearances.*

## Insight 1: A bad name with a really great company with great company strategy, great market...
**Claim:** A bad name with a really great company with great company strategy, great marketing is going to be great over time. And a good name is just going to help you, but I don't think a bad name is going to kill a good company.
**Audience:** founder | **Actionability:** 7/10 | **Confidence:** 0.95
**Source:** arielle-jackson
**Context:** Discussion about naming strategies for startups

## Insight 2: The positioning for Square Stand was for brick and mortar businesses, particular...
**Claim:** The positioning for Square Stand was for brick and mortar businesses, particularly quick serve coffee, donuts, sandwich shops. The foil was the ugly old point of sale (cash register). The benefit was turn your iPad into a point of sale. The differentiator was about one unified experience that you can do everything you can on your cash register and more, and you'd be proud to have it out on your counter.
**Framework:** Positioning framework (foil, benefit, differentiator)
**Audience:** operator | **Actionability:** 9/10 | **Confidence:** 0.9
**Source:** arielle-jackson
**Context:** Discussion about positioning the Square Stand product

## Insight 8: Write a naming brief that includes: what you are naming, what you want the name ...
**Claim:** Write a naming brief that includes: what you are naming, what you want the name to communicate, what to avoid, names of competitors, and other considerations (e.g., pronounceability).
**Framework:** Naming Brief
**Audience:** founder | **Actionability:** 9/10 | **Confidence:** 0.95
**Source:** arielle-jackson
**Context:** Arielle describes the naming brief: 'you write a naming brief and it's really simple. What are you naming? ... What do you want the name to communicate? ... What do you want to avoid? ... What are the names of competitive and related products? And then are there other considerations?'

## Insight 9: A good name is suggestive or evocative: not descriptive, but when you hear what ...
