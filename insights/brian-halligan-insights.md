## Insight 1: Hire slow and fire fast. People hire fast and fire slow. If I had to guess, with...
**Claim:** Hire slow and fire fast. People hire fast and fire slow. If I had to guess, with 18 months after you hire a C-level exec, at least 50% of the time they're gone.
**Audience:** founder | **Actionability:** 9/10 | **Confidence:** 0.95
**Source:** lenny-2026-02-15-brian-halligan
**Context:** Discussion on hiring and interviewing for C-level roles.

## Insight 2: Use blind references and ask hard questions like 'Would you enthusiastically reh...
**Claim:** Use blind references and ask hard questions like 'Would you enthusiastically rehire this person for that role?' or 'On a scale of 1 to 10, how likely is it that you'll try to rehire this person back from me down the road?'
**Audience:** founder | **Actionability:** 10/10 | **Confidence:** 0.95
**Source:** lenny-2026-02-15-brian-halligan
**Context:** Advice on improving hiring decisions through better reference checks.

## Insight 3: Parker Conrad's hack: Before a C-level interview, have the candidate sign an NDA...
**Claim:** Parker Conrad's hack: Before a C-level interview, have the candidate sign an NDA, send them the last board deck, and schedule a half-hour chat. If they are just complimentary, it's a red flag; you want someone who will challenge you.
**Audience:** founder | **Actionability:** 10/10 | **Confidence:** 0.9
**Source:** lenny-2026-02-15-brian-halligan
**Context:** Brian sharing a specific interviewing technique from Parker Conrad.
