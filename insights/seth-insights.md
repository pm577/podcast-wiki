# Seth — Insights

*Extracted from podcast appearances.*

### Insight: AI very soon is going to stop being a feature the same way electricity is not a ...
**Claim:** AI very soon is going to stop being a feature the same way electricity is not a feature. What AI companies and all companies need to do is say, what's in this for the user? What promise do I want to make a difficult promise, a remarkable promise, and then how do I keep it?
**Audience:** founder | **Actionability:** 7/10 | **Confidence:** 0.95
**Source:** seth-godin
**Context:** Discussion on how AI companies can build brands and differentiate.

### Insight: Tension is at the heart of every art form and every innovation. What we do when ...
**Claim:** Tension is at the heart of every art form and every innovation. What we do when we launch a new product, we say, we have this thing that can do X, and now the person is imagining what their life might be like if that were true. If they fall in love with that possibility, now there's tension. Did you tell the truth? Is it going to work for them?
**Audience:** operator | **Actionability:** 6/10 | **Confidence:** 0.9
**Source:** seth-godin
**Context:** Discussion on strategy and tension in product launches.

### Insight: I define good taste as knowing what other people want just before they do. So if...
**Claim:** I define good taste as knowing what other people want just before they do. And people, whether they're jazz musicians or fabric designers are seen as having good taste when they bring something to the world that the world didn't necessarily expect but is glad to see.
**Audience:** general | **Actionability:** 5/10 | **Confidence:** 0.9
**Source:** seth-godin
**Context:** Answering how to know if you have good taste and how to build it.

### Insight: Quality is not luxury. Quality is not perfection. Quality means meeting spec, an...

**Source:** seth-godin
**Context:** Discussion on standards and quality in product development.

### Insight: We weren't there to make perfect for us. We were there to delight the person who...
**Claim:** We weren't there to make perfect for us. We were there to delight the person who was buying the software. And our standards were untouchable. We said, 'If that person, the one we're describing is delighted with what we shipped, it's good. And if they're not, it doesn't matter what kind of excuses we have.' ### Insight: If you're a barista and you've chosen to be a barista, maybe because it's the be...
