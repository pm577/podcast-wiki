# Henrik Karlsson — Insights

*Extracted from podcast appearances.*

## Insight 1: When a subordinate brings a problem to a manager and the manager says 'Let me th...
**Claim:** When a subordinate brings a problem to a manager and the manager says 'Let me think about it,' the monkey (responsibility) jumps from the subordinate to the manager. Managers should avoid taking on monkeys and instead ensure the next move belongs to the subordinate.
**Framework:** Who's Got the Monkey
**Audience:** operator | **Actionability:** 9/10 | **Confidence:** 0.95
**Source:** essential-reading-for-product-builders-part-1
**Context:** Discussion of the essay 'Who’s Got the Monkey' about delegation and responsibility management.

## Insight 2: Agency is an amalgamation of autonomy (formulating autonomous goals) and efficac...
**Claim:** Agency is an amalgamation of autonomy (formulating autonomous goals) and efficacy (ability to pursue those goals). It is often gentle, like a river finding the path of least resistance, not hard-edged or aggressive.
**Audience:** general | **Actionability:** 6/10 | **Confidence:** 0.9
**Source:** essential-reading-for-product-builders-part-1
**Context:** Discussion of Henrik Karlsson's essay 'On agency'.

## Insight 3: Creative work requires doing a huge volume of work to close the gap between your...
**Claim:** Creative work requires doing a huge volume of work to close the gap between your taste and your output. Put yourself on a deadline to finish one story every week or month.
**Audience:** general | **Actionability:** 9/10 | **Confidence:** 0.95
**Source:** essential-reading-for-product-builders-part-1
**Context:** Discussion of Ira Glass's talk 'On building good taste'.

## Insight 4: Actual learning often feels bad, while pretending to learn feels good. Avoid act...
**Claim:** Actual learning often feels bad, while pretending to learn feels good. Avoid activities that make you feel high status but don't lead to real understanding.
**Audience:** general | **Actionability:** 7/10 | **Confidence:** 0.9
**Source:** essential-reading-for-product-builders-part-1
**Context:** Discussion of Andy Masley's essay 'Strategies for learning'.

## Insight 5: For new products, pick three key attributes or features, get them very right, an...

**Audience:** founder | **Actionability:** 8/10 | **Confidence:** 0.95
**Source:** essential-reading-for-product-builders-part-1
**Context:** Discussion of Paul Buchheit's essay 'If your product is great, it doesn't need to be good'.

## Insight 6: By focusing on only a few core features in the first version, you are forced to ...
**Claim:** By focusing on only a few core features in the first version, you are forced to find the true essence and value of the product.
**Audience:** founder | **Actionability:** 8/10 | **Confidence:** 0.95
**Source:** essential-reading-for-product-builders-part-1
**Context:** Discussing the iPod and Gmail launches as examples of starting with minimal features.

**Source:** essential-reading-for-product-builders-part-1
