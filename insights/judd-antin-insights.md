# Judd Antin — Insights

*Extracted from podcast appearances.*

## Insight 1: User-centered performance refers to customer obsession or user-centered practice...
**Claim:** User-centered performance refers to customer obsession or user-centered practice that is symbolic rather than focused on learning. It's hugely common. Every time a PM comes to a researcher at the end of a product process and says, 'Can you just run a quick user study just to validate our assumptions,' that's user-centered performance. It's too late to matter.
**Audience:** operator | **Actionability:** 8/10 | **Confidence:** 0.95
**Source:** judd-antin
**Context:** Judd Antin describing a common dysfunctional pattern where research is used to validate decisions already made, rather than to inform them early.

## Insight 2: We don't validate, we falsify. We are looking to be wrong. Many PMs, many design...
**Claim:** We don't validate, we falsify. We are looking to be wrong. Many PMs, many designers are not in that place. They do not want to be wrong. They're looking to validate, and that's user-centered performance.
**Audience:** operator | **Actionability:** 7/10 | **Confidence:** 0.9
**Source:** judd-antin
**Context:** Judd Antin explaining the mindset shift needed for effective research: seeking disconfirmation rather than confirmation.

## Insight 3: Researchers need to be more profit focused. There are a lot of people out there ...

**Audience:** founder | **Actionability:** 6/10 | **Confidence:** 0.85
**Source:** judd-antin
**Context:** Judd Antin responding to critiques of his article, arguing that research must align with business goals to be valued.

## Insight 4: We're just doing too much of what I would consider the wrong type of research. I...
**Claim:** We're just doing too much of what I would consider the wrong type of research. I have this framework: macro, middle range, and micro research. Macro is big picture, strategic, business focused. Micro is technical usability and A/B test analysis. Middle range is where a lot of user understanding questions fall, and it's devastatingly not impactful enough for the business.
**Framework:** Macro, Middle Range, Micro Research
**Audience:** operator | **Actionability:** 7/10 | **Confidence:** 0.9
**Source:** judd-antin
**Context:** Judd Antin explaining his framework for categorizing research types and arguing that too much effort goes into middle-range research that lacks impact.
