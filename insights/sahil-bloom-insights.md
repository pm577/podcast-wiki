# Sahil Bloom — Insights

*Extracted from podcast appearances.*

## Insight 1: The gap between what people think business is and what actually happens is the m...
**Claim:** The gap between what people think business is and what actually happens is the messy middle, the time in between starting and the outcome is 99% of the process of business and startups and entrepreneurship.
**Framework:** Messy Middle
**Audience:** founder | **Actionability:** 7/10 | **Confidence:** 0.95
**Source:** teaser_2021
**Context:** Greg Isenberg discussing the reality of startups versus the romanticized view.

## Insight 2: The biggest gap between what people think business is versus what it really is, ...
**Claim:** The biggest gap between what people think business is versus what it really is, is that aspect of just crawling through the river of shit that exists between where you start and where you finish. That willingness to get punched in the face, to get knocked down and to get back up, no one talks about that.
**Audience:** founder | **Actionability:** 6/10 | **Confidence:** 0.9
**Source:** teaser_2021
**Context:** Sahil Bloom expanding on the messy middle concept.

## Insight 3: What frustrates me most about the current state of podcasts is that it's one sid...
**Claim:** What frustrates me most about the current state of podcasts is that it's one sided. It all ends as soon as you finish the conversation during the interview. The opportunity is changing that, making it a conversation, following up, digging deeper, going further with all the insights.
**Audience:** general | **Actionability:** 8/10 | **Confidence:** 0.85
**Source:** teaser_2021
**Context:** Sahil Bloom criticizing traditional podcast format and proposing interactive community-driven podcasting.

## Insight 4: I believe the current state of podcasting sucks. A few people have microphones a...
**Claim:** I believe the current state of podcasting sucks. A few people have microphones and it's just not fair. What if you allowed a bunch of people to have microphones so they could connect, collaborate, and build community.
**Audience:** general | **Actionability:** 8/10 | **Confidence:** 0.85
**Source:** teaser_2021
**Context:** Greg Isenberg advocating for democratizing podcasting through community participation.

## Insight 5: The moment that changed my mind about business and opportunity was the first tim...
**Claim:** The moment that changed my mind about business and opportunity was the first time I ever went to Silicon Valley. I realized they weren't smarter than us. They might have had better connections, more money, gone to better schools, but that was it. That was when I realized I could do anything.
**Audience:** general | **Actionability:** 5/10 | **Confidence:** 0.9
**Source:** teaser_2021
**Context:** Greg Isenberg sharing a personal realization that success is not about innate superiority.

## Insight 6: I got invited by a mentor to a fundraiser event. I was 24. I was in conversation...
**Claim:** I got invited by a mentor to a fundraiser event. I was 24. I was in conversations with impressive people and realized there was nothing different about them versus me, but they were in these rooms constantly. That was the moment I realized I had to just find a way to be in those rooms.
**Audience:** general | **Actionability:** 7/10 | **Confidence:** 0.9
**Source:** teaser_2021
**Context:** Sahil Bloom describing the importance of being in the right rooms to generate insights.

## Insight 7: The biggest part of it would've been the people I met. The relationships I built...
**Claim:** The biggest part of it would've been the people I met. The relationships I built with other community members with the hosts, with the guests. That is what is the force multiplier for your career, those networks, how that compounds over time, just as well as any financial investment.
**Audience:** general | **Actionability:** 8/10 | **Confidence:** 0.9
**Source:** teaser_2021
**Context:** Sahil Bloom emphasizing the value of network and relationships from community participation.
