# Geoffrey Moore — Insights

*Extracted from podcast appearances.*

## Insight 1: The tendency when you're in the chasm is, 'I just need more customers. I should ...
**Claim:** The tendency when you're in the chasm is, 'I just need more customers. I should take any customer I could find,' because we need revenue. It's like taking a match and running it back and forth under a log. It's not going to light the log. So how do you start a fire? Well, you start it by putting a little kindling, little crumpled up paper, and you hold the match in one place until the fire starts. That's why adjacency is so important.
**Framework:** Crossing the Chasm
**Audience:** founder | **Actionability:** 9/10 | **Confidence:** 0.95
**Source:** geoffrey-moore
**Context:** Discussing the importance of focusing on a narrow beachhead segment rather than trying to serve many customers at once.

## Insight 2: The Crossing the Chasm model is, 'Can you create a viable repeatable business?' ...
**Claim:** The Crossing the Chasm model is, 'Can you create a viable repeatable business?' And in order to do that, you need to have an ecosystem of partners work with you in order to consolidate your position. Well, why would an ecosystem work with some startup that nobody's ever heard of? The answer would be if you had consolidated a market segment where you were number one.
**Framework:** Crossing the Chasm
**Audience:** founder | **Actionability:** 8/10 | **Confidence:** 0.95
**Source:** geoffrey-moore
**Context:** Explaining why achieving market leadership in a niche segment attracts partners and builds power.

That would cause partners to go, 'Whoa, if we're going to serve that segment, we got to work with these guys.'
**Framework:** Crossing the Chasm
**Audience:** founder | **Actionability:** 9/10 | **Confidence:** 0.95
**Source:** geoffrey-moore
**Context:** Describing the 'fish to pond ratio' and how to choose a beachhead segment.

## Insight 4: Before the chasm, the customers you work with are people who say, 'We believe wh...
**Claim:** Before the chasm, the customers you work with are people who say, 'We believe what you believe.' So in other words, they're on the same side. After the chasm, they say, 'I'm not sure about that, but we need what you have.' So transitioning from, 'We believe what you believe,' which is how you sell to visionaries to, 'We need what you have,' which is how you sell to pragmatists. That's the shift.
**Framework:** Crossing the Chasm
**Audience:** operator | **Actionability:** 8/10 | **Confidence:** 0.95
**Source:** geoffrey-moore
**Context:** Explaining the difference in messaging between early adopters and mainstream customers.

## Insight 5: Pragmatic people buy what they see their peers are buying. So if peer one is buy...

**Framework:** Crossing the Chasm
**Audience:** operator | **Actionability:** 7/10 | **Confidence:** 0.95
