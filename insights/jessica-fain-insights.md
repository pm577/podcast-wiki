# Jessica Fain — Insights

*Extracted from podcast appearances.*

## Insight 1: As product managers, one of our best sets of skills is curiosity and empathy and...
**Claim:** As product managers, one of our best sets of skills is curiosity and empathy and trying to understand our users. But the moment that we're talking to an executive, we forget those skills and those talents.
**Audience:** operator | **Actionability:** 7/10 | **Confidence:** 0.9
**Source:** lenny-2026-03-22-jessica-fain
**Context:** Discussing the common mistake of not applying user empathy to executives when pitching ideas.

## Insight 2: People completely misunderstand how executives make decisions. What is going on ...
**Claim:** People completely misunderstand how executives make decisions. What is going on in the head side? Describe an executive's calendar as a stroke light going off. You wake up at 8:00 AM, you've already got a huge list of urgent things going on. They have not had the time, the energy, the wherewithal to center your problems.
**Audience:** operator | **Actionability:** 8/10 | **Confidence:** 0.9
**Source:** lenny-2026-03-22-jessica-fain
**Context:** Explaining why pitches fail: executives are overwhelmed and not focused on your problem.

## Insight 5: One of the biggest things you can do to build trust is kill things, deprioritize...
**Claim:** One of the biggest things you can do to build trust is kill things, deprioritize things. If you're thinking about how do you be more senior, how do you show up in a way that is in a leadership mindset, you get paid to be a domain expert. Your executive is looking for you to be the deepest person in the room. Bringing your expertise to bear is absolutely crucial. You have to act like a CPO.
**Audience:** operator | **Actionability:** 9/10 | **Confidence:** 0.9
**Source:** lenny-2026-03-22-jessica-fain
**Context:** Discussing how to build trust with executives by showing strategic judgment and domain expertise.
