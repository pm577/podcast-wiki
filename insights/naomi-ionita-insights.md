## Insight 1: Do not set it and forget it. I see companies do this, where they labor over desi...
**Claim:** Do not set it and forget it. I see companies do this, where they labor over designs and features. And they build this perfect product that's delightful to use. And then pricing's sort of plucked out of thin air, and then they don't revisit it. This was Evernote. It was many, many years before we went back and overhauled the pricing. So, think about your pricing just like you do your roadmap. Every 6 to 12 months, there's probably something meaningful that you're launching for users. So, treat that as an opportunity to revisit your monetization strategy and making sure you're compensated appropriately.
**Audience:** founder | **Actionability:** 9/10 | **Confidence:** 0.95
**Source:** naomi-ionita
**Context:** Discussing common missteps in pricing, specifically the mistake of not revisiting pricing after initial set.

## Insight 2: One challenge that Evernote really struggled with was this evolution from single...
**Claim:** One challenge that Evernote really struggled with was this evolution from single-player to multiplayer to team to enterprise. It's a chasm that a lot of bottom-up SaaS businesses struggle to cross. Evernote was philosophically antisocial. It was meant to be your second brain, kind of your personal tool. And I think that capped the company's growth potential. I always used to say you can't retrofit collaboration. You have to be collaboration-first.
**Audience:** founder | **Actionability:** 7/10 | **Confidence:** 0.9
**Source:** naomi-ionita
**Context:** Reflecting on why Evernote didn't become Notion, highlighting the importance of building collaboration from the start.

## Insight 3: If companies do that well, it benefits every metric. That bridge from single-pla...
**Claim:** If companies do that well, it benefits every metric. That bridge from single-player to multiplayer. Acquisition goes up. You grow organically through referrals and shared workflows. Retention goes up because now you have these shared workflows that are incredibly sticky. Employees are accountable to each other to say, 'This is how work gets done.' Design in Figma, roadmap planning and ticketing in Jira are linear. It just becomes the default platform. And modernization goes up. Revenue scales with usage.
**Audience:** founder | **Actionability:** 8/10 | **Confidence:** 0.9
**Source:** naomi-ionita
**Context:** Explaining the benefits of crossing from single-player to multiplayer in SaaS products.
