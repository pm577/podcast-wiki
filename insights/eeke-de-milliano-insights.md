*Extracted from podcast appearances.*

## Insight 1: Process, by definition, is variance reducing. You're introducing it because you ...
**Claim:** Process, by definition, is variance reducing. You're introducing it because you worry that the variance in your org is too high. You want people to sort of meet a certain standard. And the cost of that is obviously, while you are reducing the standard and bringing folks up to the average, you're also bringing other folks down to the average. And oftentimes, the folks you're bringing down are your highest performers, your most creative thinkers.
**Audience:** operator | **Actionability:** 7/10 | **Confidence:** 0.95
**Source:** eeke-de-milliano
**Context:** Discussion about the trade-offs of introducing process in organizations as they grow.
