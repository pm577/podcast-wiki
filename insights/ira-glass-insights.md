# Ira Glass — Insights

*Extracted from podcast appearances.*

## Insight 1: When a subordinate brings a problem to a manager and the manager says 'Let me th...
**Claim:** When a subordinate brings a problem to a manager and the manager says 'Let me think about it,' the monkey (responsibility) leaps from the subordinate's back to the manager's. Managers should avoid taking on monkeys that belong to subordinates.
**Framework:** Who's Got the Monkey
**Audience:** operator | **Actionability:** 9/10 | **Confidence:** 0.95
**Source:** essential-reading-for-product-builders-part-1
**Context:** Discussion of the essay 'Who's Got the Monkey' by Oncken and Wass, illustrating how managers inadvertently take on subordinates' responsibilities.

## Insight 2: Agency is an amalgamation of autonomy (formulating autonomous goals) and efficac...
**Claim:** Agency is an amalgamation of autonomy (formulating autonomous goals) and efficacy (ability to pursue those goals). It is often gentle, like a river finding the path of least resistance, not necessarily aggressive.
**Framework:** Agency
**Audience:** general | **Actionability:** 7/10 | **Confidence:** 0.9
**Source:** essential-reading-for-product-builders-part-1
**Context:** Discussion of the essay 'On agency' by Henrik Karlsson, defining agency and contrasting it with aggressive interpretations.

## Insight 3: Creative work requires doing a huge volume of work to close the gap between your...
**Claim:** Creative work requires doing a huge volume of work to close the gap between your taste and your output. Put yourself on a deadline to finish one story every week or month.
**Audience:** general | **Actionability:** 10/10 | **Confidence:** 0.95
**Source:** essential-reading-for-product-builders-part-1
**Context:** Discussion of Ira Glass's talk on building good taste, emphasizing the need for volume of work to improve.

## Insight 4: Pretending to learn feels good but actual learning often feels bad. Avoid activi...
**Claim:** Pretending to learn feels good but actual learning often feels bad. Avoid activities that make you feel high status but don't result in real learning.
**Audience:** general | **Actionability:** 8/10 | **Confidence:** 0.9
**Source:** essential-reading-for-product-builders-part-1
**Context:** Discussion of Andy Masley's 'Strategies for learning', warning against performative learning.

## Insight 5: For new products, pick three key attributes or features, get those very right, a...
**Claim:** For new products, pick three key attributes or features, get those very right, and forget about everything else. The rest is noise.
**Framework:** Three Key Attributes
**Audience:** founder | **Actionability:** 9/10 | **Confidence:** 0.95
**Source:** essential-reading-for-product-builders-part-1
**Context:** Discussion of Paul Buchheit's essay 'If your product is great, it doesn't need to be good', using iPod and Gmail as examples.

## Insight 6: By focusing on only a few core features in the first version, you are forced to ...
**Claim:** By focusing on only a few core features in the first version, you are forced to find the true essence and value of the product. If your product needs ‘everything’ in order to be good, then it’s probably not very innovative.
**Audience:** founder | **Actionability:** 8/10 | **Confidence:** 0.95
**Source:** essential-reading-for-product-builders-part-1
**Context:** Discussion of product launch strategy using examples of iPod and Gmail

## Insight 7: In a great market—a market with lots of real potential customers—the market pull...
**Claim:** In a great market—a market with lots of real potential customers—the market pulls product out of the startup. The product doesn’t need to be great; it just has to basically work.
**Framework:** Market is most important factor
**Audience:** founder | **Actionability:** 7/10 | **Confidence:** 0.9
**Source:** essential-reading-for-product-builders-part-1
**Context:** Marc Andreessen's essay on team, product, market

**Claim:** The secret sauce of Elon Musk is accessible to everyone and right there in front of us—if we can just wrap our heads around it.
**Audience:** general | **Actionability:** 3/10 | **Confidence:** 0.7
